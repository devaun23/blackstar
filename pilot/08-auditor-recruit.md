## Operating procedure first 24 hours

1. Reply to every comment within 2 hours.
2. Hard questions get honest answers. "Is the rate fair?" — yes, $75/hr is
   below industry medical writing rates ($100-150/hr) but the work is
   bounded and low-overhead. State it.
3. "How do I know you're not collecting questions for free?" → link the
   pilot/07-auditor-sow.md, mention the NDA, offer to do a 10-min vetting
   call before they commit.
4. "Why not just hire MDs?" → the audience is *Step 2 takers*; recent
   high-scoring students have the exact mental model of an MS3-MS4 sitting
   the exam this year. MDs years out have rusted on the test-taking layer.
5. Don't argue with skeptics. Concede true points. Cite the SOW for
   workflow questions.

## Funnel

```
Reddit/Discord post → Tally intake form → 10-min vetting call (verify score,
discuss conflicts, walk through SOW) → NDA signed → 5-item trial batch (paid)
→ 25-item full batch (paid) → optional ongoing role
```

5-item trial is the calibration step — if their flags align with your own
audit (and they catch ≥1 thing the AI judge missed), they're hired for
the full batch. If their flags miss obvious issues or disagree with no
articulable reason, end politely and pay the trial rate.

---

## Acceptance criteria for the cohort

- 2-3 auditors onboarded within 2 weeks of posting
- All have signed NDAs on file before any item content is shared
- At least 1 auditor's flags overlap ≥60% with your own on the trial batch
  (signal that the rubric is reproducible)
- At least 1 disagreement between you and an auditor that updates the SOW
  or the pipeline (signal that audit is doing real work)

If none of your auditors disagree with you, they're rubber-stamping. End
the cohort and try a different filter.

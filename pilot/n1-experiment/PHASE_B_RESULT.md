## Synthesis — what this experiment showed

**The pipeline works end-to-end, the judge works, and the adversarial tester works** — and together they surfaced a real, generalizable construct-validity flaw in the current generator. That's a useful result, even though Phase B "failed" the pass gate.

What the new components contributed:
- **Best-of-N + judge** picked candidate 2 over candidate 0 (medical-incongruent enoxaparin detail), candidate 1 (patient-voiced diagnosis telegraphing), and candidate 3 (Wells-score ambiguity from trace edema). The judge's reasoning was specific and quote-anchored, not generic. Three of the four candidates had different defects; the judge correctly identified the most fixable one.
- **Adversarial student tester** caught a defect the rubric path can miss: even with D-dimer as a distractor and the correct answer (CTPA) identifiable, the weak-student persona explicitly cited the stem's telegraphing sentence ("no other diagnosis explains this presentation better than pulmonary embolism") as the reason to skip D-dimer. This is the **same sentence the judge flagged** under `diagnosis_given_away` as a hard-gate concern. Two independent signals converged on the same problem — high confidence the defect is real.

What was learned about the generator (vignette-writer):
- It **does** include the intended cognitive-error distractor (D-dimer) when generating from this transfer rule — that's a relief, since one possible failure mode was that the targeted distractor never makes it into the option set.
- It **also** inserts an explicit "diagnosis is X" sentence into the stem when justifying the Wells-score components. That sentence collapses the hinge and defeats the entire reason the question exists. This is a generator-prompt issue, not a pipeline-architecture issue.
- The medical_validator caught related defects (Wells arithmetic accuracy, threshold consistency) but did NOT flag the telegraphing sentence as a key-integrity issue. The judge did; the adversarial tester confirmed it.

What was learned about the pipeline path:
- 5 separate runs all hit the same case-planner enum-mismatch failure mode on D-dimer/PE questions ("diagnostic_test", "diagnostic_test_selection", "diagnostic_sequencing", "test_selection") — the LLM consistently picks values outside the cognitive_operation enum. Patched in `src/lib/factory/agents/case-planner.ts` with explicit aliases + fuzzy fallback.
- The pre-existing TODO in CURRENT_STATE.md (4 source-firewall provenance columns missing from `item_draft`) caused all 5 runs to fail at vignette-writer's insert until patched. Patched with the same retry-without-columns pattern used at `pipeline-v2.ts:1202` for `review_status`.
- The PE source pack (`PACK.AHA.PE.2024`) failed the source-sufficiency gate's "competing pathways" check because 3 of 4 treatment_steps had no `condition` field. Patched two treatment_steps with semantically-defensible conditions ("High clinical suspicion, awaiting confirmatory imaging" / "Confirmed PE, hemodynamically stable").

What "best-of-N alone won't help" looks like in practice:
- The first 4 Phase B candidates (run before the patches) all died at case-planner with schema validation errors. **N=4 was no rescue against a systematic prompt+schema mismatch.** This is the failure-mode telltale called out in the plan ("LLM mode-collapsing on this prompt; N doesn't help").
- After the patches landed, N=4 produced 4 distinct candidates with different defects (candidate-level diversity restored), and the judge picked a defensible winner.

## Phase C proposal

The experiment showed that **the new components (best-of-N orchestrator + cross-family judge + adversarial student tester) work**, but they exposed a single dominant generator defect: the vignette-writer inserts a "diagnosis is X" telegraphing sentence to justify the Wells score in the stem. Before extending to more transfer rules (Sepsis, ACS, AKI, GI Bleed), fix that defect:

1. **Generator prompt change.** Update the vignette-writer's system prompt to forbid narrator-voiced diagnosis statements in the stem. The Wells-score components should be presented as raw clinical findings (surgery date, vitals, exam) without an explicit "PE is the best explanation" sentence — that conclusion is what the test-taker is supposed to reach.
2. **Validator change.** Promote the `diagnosis_given_away` check from a judge-only concern to a hard-gate validator. The adversarial tester confirmed it's load-bearing for construct validity.
3. **Re-run this experiment** after those two changes. If the weak student now correctly falls into the D-dimer trap, `overall_construct_valid=true` and Phase B's pass criteria are met.
4. **Only then extend to Sepsis / ACS / AKI / GI Bleed** — one transfer rule at a time, with the same n=1 baseline + n=4 best-of-N + adversarial protocol. Don't generalize the architecture until it produces a construct-valid winner on at least one rule.

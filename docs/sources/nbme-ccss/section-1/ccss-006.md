# Item 6 — Cornual Ectopic Pregnancy

## Clinical Scenario
30yo primigravid woman at 11 weeks' gestation presents to ED with 3-day history of increasingly severe midline to right lower quadrant abdominal pain and increasing vaginal spotting. Also has pain with intercourse. No serious illness history, only prenatal vitamins. While waiting to be examined, she has onset of dizziness. Temp 35.7C, pulse 110, resp 18, BP 90/50. Uterus consistent with 10-week size, tender to palpation. Tilt test positive. Transvaginal US shows intramural gestational sac to the right of midline extending into the myometrium. Free fluid in the cul-de-sac, no adnexal masses.

## Decision Point
Most likely diagnosis?

## Options
- A) Ampullar pregnancy
- B) Complete abortion
- **C) Cornual pregnancy** ✓
- D) Incomplete abortion
- E) Inevitable abortion
- F) Missed abortion
- G) Threatened abortion

## Correct Answer: C
This patient has an ectopic pregnancy in the fundus of her uterus, near where her fallopian tube meets the uterine body — a cornual (or interstitial) pregnancy. Ectopic pregnancies present with vaginal bleeding, abdominal pain, and hemodynamic instability. The key finding is the US showing an intramural gestational sac to the right of midline extending into the myometrium. A cornual pregnancy is located in the interstitial portion of the fallopian tube, within the myometrium rather than in the ampulla. Cornual pregnancies can grow larger before rupturing (due to the thicker myometrium), making rupture particularly dangerous with significant hemorrhage.

## Educational Objective
Ectopic pregnancy is an abnormal extrauterine pregnancy occurring in an anatomic location that cannot support a pregnancy. A cornual or interstitial pregnancy is an ectopic pregnancy in the myometrium near the area where the fallopian tube meets the uterine cavity. Complications include rupture and bleeding with consequent hemorrhagic shock. Serum B-hCG concentrations do not increase as expected.

## Common Errors
- **A (Ampullar pregnancy)**: Most common location for ectopic pregnancy (in the ampulla of the fallopian tube). However, this patient's gestational sac is located to the right of midline and extends into the myometrium — cornual, not ampullar.
- **B (Complete abortion)**: No products of conception passed, active bleeding and hemodynamic instability present.
- **D (Incomplete abortion)**: Still has a visible gestational sac on US, not consistent with partial passage of products.
- **E-G (Other abortion types)**: The intramural location of the gestational sac and hemodynamic instability points to ectopic pregnancy, not any form of spontaneous abortion.

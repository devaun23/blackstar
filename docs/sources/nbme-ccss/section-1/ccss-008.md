# Item 8 — Decompensated Heart Failure Diagnosis

## Clinical Scenario
68yo woman, 4-day history of progressive shortness of breath. PMH: COPD, T2DM, HTN, osteoarthritis. Meds: metformin, losartan, lovastatin, acetaminophen. Continuous home oxygen therapy. Temp 37C, pulse 70, resp 36, BP 100/88. Pulse ox on room air 82%. S3 gallop heard. 4+ pitting edema of lower extremities. Diffuse bilateral crackles on auscultation.

## Decision Point
Which of the following is most likely to confirm the diagnosis?

## Options
- A) Chest x-ray
- B) CT scan of the chest
- **C) Echocardiography** ✓
- D) 12-Lead ECG
- E) Ventilation-perfusion lung scans

## Correct Answer: C
This patient presents with dyspnea, hypoxia, diffuse crackles, and pitting edema — suggestive of decompensated congestive heart failure. Heart failure is categorized by having either reduced ejection fraction (systolic) or preserved ejection fraction (diastolic). The S3 gallop and bilateral crackles are classic findings. Heart failure is diagnosed with echocardiography, which assesses chamber size, valve structure and function, and pressure — providing the most comprehensive diagnostic information.

## Educational Objective
Patients with heart failure classically present with dyspnea, orthopnea, lower extremity edema, and jugular venous distension. Physical examination may disclose pulmonary crackles and an S3 or S4 heart sound on auscultation. Heart failure is diagnosed with echocardiography.

## Common Errors
- **A (CXR)**: Shows volume overload (pulmonary edema, effusions) and guides early management but is not a confirmatory test for heart failure — doesn't assess EF or valve function.
- **B (CT chest)**: Good for diagnosing lung abscess, structural abnormalities, and staging; CT scans show pulmonary edema in HF but are not the diagnostic test for HF.
- **D (12-Lead ECG)**: Diagnoses cardiac ischemia (STEMI). ECG will show ST elevation in contiguous leads with reciprocal depression. This patient's symptoms developed over 4 days, not consistent with acute MI.
- **E (V/Q scan)**: Evaluates for pulmonary embolism in patients unable to undergo CTA. This patient lacks risk factors for PE and her presentation is consistent with HF.

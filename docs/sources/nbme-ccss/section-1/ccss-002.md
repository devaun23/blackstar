# Item 2 — Transient Synovitis

## Clinical Scenario
7yo boy, 1-day history of right hip pain and limp. Fell off bed 5 days ago wrestling. Cold resolved 3 weeks ago. No meds, immunizations up-to-date, 50th percentile. Temp 37.6C, pulse 100, resp 18, BP 98/66. No swelling/warmth/erythema of hips/knees. Passive ROM of right hip produces moderate pain. Labs: WBC 10,000, segs 52%, bands 3%, eos 3%, lymphs 37%, monos 5%, ESR 24, CRP 3000 (N=67-1800), ANA negative. Hip X-rays normal. US shows mild joint effusion. Arthrocentesis yields clear fluid, Gram stain negative.

## Decision Point
Most likely long-term complication of this patient's current condition?

## Options
- A) Hip flexion contracture
- B) Leg length discrepancy
- C) Pauciarticular arthritis
- D) Polyarticular arthritis
- **E) There are no long-term complications** ✓

## Correct Answer: E
Transient synovitis is an inflammatory condition of the synovial lining of weight-bearing joints (hip most commonly affected) in children recovering from a viral infection. It is self-limited and NOT associated with long-term complications. Key features: pain on weight bearing, antalgic gait, refusal to ambulate, tenderness over the joint. Vital signs and labs generally normal except mildly elevated inflammatory markers (ESR, CRP). Antecedent viral infection reported in up to 30% of cases. Normal X-rays, mild effusion on US, clear joint fluid with negative Gram stain all support this diagnosis over septic arthritis.

## Educational Objective
Transient synovitis is an inflammatory condition involving the synovial lining of weight-bearing joints, with the hip frequently affected. Symptoms are transient, treatment is supportive (rest, NSAIDs), and there are no long-term complications. It is important to rule out emergent/urgent conditions such as cellulitis, septic arthritis, osteomyelitis, and fracture.

## Common Errors
- **A (Hip flexion contracture)**: Seen in cerebral palsy or prolonged immobility. Transient synovitis resolves in 1-2 weeks.
- **B (Leg length discrepancy)**: Can develop after fracture, osteomyelitis, or malignancy. Not associated with transient synovitis.
- **C (Pauciarticular arthritis)**: JIA with <=4 joints. HLA-B27 associated. This patient's acute presentation post-viral illness is more consistent with transient synovitis.
- **D (Polyarticular arthritis)**: JIA with >=5 joints. More common in females. Not consistent with this presentation.

# Item 3 — Recurrent UTI in Pregnancy

## Clinical Scenario
37yo primigravida at 12 weeks' gestation. 1-day history of urinary frequency, moderate pain and burning with urination. No fever, abdominal/flank pain, or abnormal vaginal discharge. Five UTIs in the past year, all beginning within 3 days of sexual intercourse, each resolving after 1 day of ciprofloxacin or TMP-SMX. No serious illness history, no meds. Sexually active with one male partner. Normal vitals, normal pelvic exam. UA shows numerous WBCs and bacteria. Prescribed 7-day course of nitrofurantoin.

## Decision Point
Most appropriate next step in management for the remainder of this patient's pregnancy?

## Options
- A) Observation only
- B) Postcoital ciprofloxacin therapy
- **C) Postcoital nitrofurantoin therapy** ✓
- D) Daily ciprofloxacin therapy
- E) Daily levofloxacin therapy

## Correct Answer: C
This patient has recurrent UTIs reliably triggered by sexual intercourse. She is pregnant. Two key considerations: (1) UTIs must be treated in pregnancy due to risk of pyelonephritis and pregnancy complications; (2) fluoroquinolones (ciprofloxacin, levofloxacin) are contraindicated in pregnancy because they can damage fetal bone, cartilage, and tendons. Nitrofurantoin reliably treats cystitis and is safe in pregnancy. Since her UTIs are triggered by intercourse, postcoital prophylaxis is appropriate rather than daily therapy.

## Educational Objective
UTIs are more common in women due to shorter urethra. Sexual intercourse can introduce bacteria, leading to recurrent UTIs. Patients with frequent, recurrent UTIs triggered by sexual intercourse are candidates for postcoital antibiotic prophylaxis. For pregnant patients, nitrofurantoin is a common antibiotic that reliably treats cystitis and is safe to use during pregnancy.

## Common Errors
- **A (Observation only)**: Inappropriate — UTIs in pregnancy must be treated to prevent pyelonephritis and complications.
- **B (Postcoital ciprofloxacin)**: Fluoroquinolones cross the placenta and can damage fetal bone, cartilage, and tendons. Contraindicated in pregnancy.
- **D, E (Daily fluoroquinolones)**: Both contraindicated in pregnancy. Also, daily therapy is unnecessary when UTIs are reliably triggered by a specific event (intercourse).

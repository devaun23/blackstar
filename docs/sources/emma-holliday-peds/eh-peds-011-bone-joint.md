# Kid with a Limp

- Most common cause overall? Trauma

## Developmental Hip Dysplasia

- 18 mo F w/ asymmetric gluteal folds on exam -> Developmental hip dysplasia
- RF? 1st born F, +FH, breech position
- Dx and Tx? Clunk on Barlow. U/S of hip if unsure. Tx w/ Pavlik harness, surgery

## Legg-Calve-Perthes Disease

- 5 y/o M initially w/ painless limp now has pain in his thigh -> Legg-Calve-Perthes Disease (avascular necrosis)

## Transient Synovitis

- 5 y/o M initially w/ a cold 1 wk ago now presents w/ a limp & effusion in the hip. X-rays normal, ESR is 35, T = 99.8, WBCs = 10K -> Transient Synovitis
- Next best step? Bed rest for 1 wk + NSAIDs

## SCFE (Slipped Capital Femoral Epiphysis)

- 14 y/o lanky M w/ nagging knee pain and decreased ROM of the hip on exam -> SCFE
- Remember they're not always fat!
- Tx? Surgically close and pin the epiphysis to avoid osteonecrosis

## Osgood-Schlatter

- 14 y/o basketball player has knee pain and swelling of the tibial tubercle -> Osgood-Schlatter
- Overuse injury from jumping

# JRA (Juvenile Rheumatoid Arthritis)

- 12 y/o F w/ 2 wk history of daily fevers to 102 and a salmon colored evanescent rash on trunk, thighs and shoulders. Left knee and right knee swollen -> JRA
- Good prognostic factor? +ANA
- Bad prognostic factor? +RF, also polyarticular and older age at onset
- Tx? 1st line = NSAIDs, 2nd line = methotrexate, 3rd = steroids

# Kawasaki Disease

- 2 y/o F w/ 2 wk history of daily fevers to 102 and a desquamating rash on the perineum. Swollen hands and feet, conjunctivitis and unilateral swollen cervical lymph node -> Kawasaki
- Other lab findings? Increased plts (wk 2-3), increased urine WBC, increased LFTs, increased CSF protein
- Best 1st test? 2D echo and EKG. Repeat the echo after 2-3 wks of tx
- Tx? Acute = IVIG + high dose aspirin. Then aspirin + warfarin
- Most serious sequelae? Coronary artery aneurysm or MI

# Bone Tumors

## Ewing Sarcoma

- If <10, more likely. M>F. More common if hx of retinoblastoma or previous radiation -> Ewing Sarcoma
- X-ray: "Onion skinning" (layers of periosteal development)
- Tx? Rads and/or surgery

## Osteogenic Sarcoma

- If >10, more likely. M>F -> Osteosarcoma
- X-ray: "Sunburst" and "Codman's triangle"
- Tx? Chemo and/or surgery

## Leukemia

- More diffuse bone pain in a patient w/ petechiae, pallor and increased infections -> Don't forget bone pain can be presenting sx for leukemia

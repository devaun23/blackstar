# Growth & Nutrition

- Newborns lose 10% of birth weight in 1st week -> diuresis of extravascular fluid
- Should regain birth weight by 2 weeks
- Should double weight by 6 months
- Should triple weight by 1 year
- Increased 50% of length by 1 year
- Double length by 5 years
- Breast milk is best for babies
- Contraindications to breast-feeding -> Galactosemia, PKU, HIV, HSV on the breast, chemo, Li, Iodide, alcohol
- Breast milk vs Formula -> Breast milk is whey dominant, more lactose, more LCFA, less Fe but its better absorbed

# Abnormal Growth

## Constitutional Growth Delay

- 14 y/o boy, always been below 5% in height. Parents are tall & were "late bloomers" -> Constitutional Growth Delay
- Bone age < Real age. Child is likely to have normal final adult height.

## Familial Short Stature

- Same story, but father is 5'2" and mom is 4'10" -> Familial Short Stature
- Bone age = Real age

## Obesity

- 14 y/o boy, 50% in height, 97% for weight -> Obesity
- Bone age > Real age
- Other causes of advanced bone age? Precocious puberty, CAH, Hyperthyroidism

## Pathologic Short Stature

- 14 y/o boy, starts out in 50% for height, in the past 2 years is now between the 5%-10% -> Pathologic Short Stature
- Consider: craniopharyngioma (vision problems, check CT), hypothyroidism (check TFTs), hypopituitarism (check IGF-1), Turner's (check karyotype)

# Primitive Reflexes

| Reflex | Stimulus | Response | Duration |
|--------|----------|----------|----------|
| Moro | Head extended | Arms and legs both flex | Birth - 4/6mo |
| Grasp | Finger in palm | Flexes hand | Birth - 4/6mo |
| Rooting | Rub cheek | Head turns to that side | Birth - 4/6mo |
| Placing | Stimulate dorsum of foot | Steps up | Birth - 4/6mo |
| Tonic neck | Neck turned to one side | Opposite arm flexes, ipsilateral arm extends | Birth - 4/6mo |
| Parachute | Fall simulated | Arms are extended | 6-8mo - por vida |

- CNS origin of these reflexes? Brainstem and vestibular nuclei

# Developmental Milestones

| Age | Gross Motor | Fine Motor / Social |
|-----|-------------|---------------------|
| 2mo | Sustains head in plane of body | Social smile, start to coo, follows object 180deg |
| 6mo | Roll over, sits w/ support, creep/crawl | Stranger anxiety |
| 9mo | Sit unsupported | Pincer grasp, walks w/ hand held, object permanence, peek-a-boo & bye-bye |
| 15mo | Walk alone | Builds 3 cube tower, scribbles w/ crayon |
| 24mo | Runs well | 1/2 of speech comprehensible, 2-3 word sentences, builds 7 cube tower, holds spoon |
| 30mo | Walks upstairs w/ alternating feet, stands on 1 foot | Knows name, refers to self as "I" |
| 36mo | Walks downstairs, jumps w/ both feet | Copies a circle, knows age and sex |
| 48mo | Hops on 1 foot, throws ball overhead | Copy cross and square, group play, goes to toilet alone |
| 60mo | Skips | Copies a triangle, draws person w/ 8-10 parts, understands taking turns, counts to 3 |

# Potty Training

## Enuresis

- Urinary continence should be attained by 5 years
- Primary if continence never achieved, Secondary if after a 6mo period of dryness
- Medical causes to r/o? UTI (do a UA), constipation (disimpact) or diabetes (check sugar)
- Tx? 1st behavioral (reward system, pee before bed, bell-alarm pad). 2nd pharmacological (DDAVP or imipramine)

## Encopresis

- Fecal continence should be attained by 4 years
- Most common cause? Constipation, fecal retention
- Tx? Disimpact, stool softeners, high fiber diet
- Behavioral modification? Post-prandial toilet sitting

# Immunizations

- Due at birth -> HepB (give HepB IV if mom is HBsAg+)
- Due at 2mo, 4mo and 6mo -> HepB, Rota, DTaP, HiB, PCV and IPV
- Starting at 6mo and then yearly -> Influenza
- Contraindications to flu vac? Egg allergy (also CI for yellow fever vac)
- Due at 12mo -> MMR, varicella, HepA (live vaccines not for kiddos <12mo)
- Contraindications to MMR? Neomycin or streptomycin allergy
- Due before age 2 -> DTaP and 2nd HepA (6mo after the 1st one)
- Due before kindergarten -> Last IPV, DTaP, MMR and varicella
- Due at age 12 -> Tdap booster, meningococcal vaccine, and HPV (girls)

# Hematuria

- Best 1st test? Urinalysis
- Dysmorphic RBCs or RBC casts -> Glomerular source

## Nephritic Syndrome

- Definition: Proteinuria (but <2g/24hrs), hematuria, edema and azotemia

### IgA Nephropathy (Berger's Disease)

- 1-2 days after runny nose, sore throat & cough -> Berger's Disease (IgA nephropathy)
- MC cause of nephritic syndrome

### Post-Strep Glomerulonephritis

- 1-2 weeks after sore throat or skin infection -> Post-strep GN
- Smoky/cola urine
- Best 1st test is ASO titer. Subepithelial IgG humps

### Goodpasture's Syndrome

- Hematuria + hemoptysis -> Goodpasture's Syndrome
- Antibodies to collagen IV

### Alport Syndrome

- Hematuria + deafness -> Alport Syndrome
- XLR mutation in collagen IV

# Kidney Stones

- Flank pain radiating to groin + hematuria
- Best test? CT

| Type | Clue | Treatment |
|------|------|-----------|
| Calcium oxalate | Most common type | HCTZ |
| Cysteine | Kid w/ family hx of stones. Can't resorb certain AA | Alkalinize urine |
| Struvite (Mg/Al/PO4) | Chronic indwelling foley and alkaline pee. Proteus, staph, pseudomonas, klebsiella | Remove stone |
| Uric acid | Leukemia being treated w/ chemo | Alkalinize urine + hydration |
| Pure oxalate | Post bowel resection for volvulus. Ca not reabsorbed by gut | Restrict oxalate |

- Stones <5mm -> will pass spontaneously. Just hydrate
- Stones >2cm -> open or endoscopic surgical removal
- Stones 5mm-2cm -> extracorporeal shock wave lithotripsy

# Proteinuria

- Best 1st test? Repeat test in 2 weeks, then quantify w/ 24hr urine

## Nephrotic Syndrome

- Definition: >3.5g protein/24hrs, hypoalbuminemia, edema, hyperlipidemia (fatty/waxy casts)
- MC in kiddos? Minimal change disease -> fusion of foot processes
- Tx? Prednisone for 4-6 weeks
- Most common complication is infection -> make sure immunized against pneumococcus and varicella

## Renal Vein Thrombosis

- Nephrotic patient suddenly develops flank pain -> suspect renal vein thrombosis!
- 2/2 peeing out ATIII, protein C and S
- Do CT or U/S stat!

## Orthostatic Proteinuria

- MC in school aged kids. Normal while supine, increased when standing.

# Viral Exanthems

## Roseola (HHV-6)

- 2 y/o w/ a fever to 105, 3 days later gets a pink, maculopapular rash on trunk, arms and legs -> Roseola (HHV-6)

## Fifth Disease / Erythema Infectiosum (Parvovirus B19)

- 2 y/o w/ a low grade fever, lacy reticular rash on cheeks and upper body (spares palms/soles) -> Fifth Disease (Parvovirus B19)
- Who is this bad for? Pregnant women, sickle cell, thalassemia patients

## Scarlet Fever (Group A Strep)

- Fine, maculopapular desquamating rash begins on chest and spreads to neck, trunk, & extremities + strawberry tongue. Sore throat 1-2 wks prior -> Scarlet Fever
- Tx? PCN prevents rheumatic fever (won't help reduce chances of APSGN)

## Measles (Paramyxovirus)

- Cough, runny nose, fever -> macular rash begins behind ears & spreads down. Gray spots on buccal mucosa (Koplik spots) -> Measles
- Tx? Vitamin A + supportive care

## Rubella (Paramyxovirus)

- Sore throat, joint pain, fever, pinpoint rash on the face and spreads down. Rose spots on the palate -> Rubella
- Complications? Congenital rubella syndrome

## Hand-Foot-and-Mouth Disease (Coxsackie A16)

- Baby with poor feeding. Vesicles in the mouth, on palms and soles + rash on buttocks -> Hand-Foot-and-Mouth Disease

## Mumps (Paramyxovirus)

- 16 year old M with swollen parotid glands, fever & headache -> Mumps
- Complications? Orchitis and sterility

# Tick-Borne Diseases

## Lyme Disease

- 6 y/o kid from central PA, went camping. Had fever -> Lyme Disease (Borrelia burgdorferi)
- Tx? Amoxicillin for this kid. Doxy if >8
- Complications? Arthritis, heart block, meningitis, Bell's palsy

## Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever

- 6 y/o kid from coastal NC, went camping. Had fever, myalgias, abd pain -> RMSF (Rickettsia rickettsii)
- Tx? Doxycycline no matter what age
- Complications? Vasculitis and gangrene

# Skin Infections

## Scabies

- 8 y/o kid, multiple excoriations on arms. Itchy at night -> Scabies
- Tx? 5% permethrin for whole family!

## Impetigo

- Honey-colored crusted plaque on face -> Impetigo. MC bug is staph if bullous
- Tx? Topical mupirocin if localized

## Staph Scalded Skin Syndrome

- Inflamed conjunctiva and multiple blisters. Nikolsky's positive -> Staph Scalded Skin Syndrome (exfoliative toxin)
- Tx? IV oxacillin or nafcillin

# Meningitis

- Most common bugs? Strep Pneumo, H. Influenza, N. meningitidis (tx w/ ceftriaxone and vanco)
- In young & immunosuppressed? Add Listeria (tx w/ ampicillin)
- In people w/ brain surgery? Add Staph (tx w/ vanco)
- Randoms? TB (RIPE + steroids) and Lyme (IV ceftriaxone)
- Best 1st step? Start empiric treatment (+ steroids if you think it is bacterial). Then check CT if signs of increased ICP. Then do LP.
- LP findings: +Gram stain, >1000 WBC is diagnostic. High protein and low glucose support bacterial
- Roommate of the kid with bacterial meningitis and petechial rash? Rifampin prophylaxis!

# Ear Infections

## Otitis Media

- 2 y/o w/ fever to 102, tugging on right ear. TM is red and bulging -> Otitis Media
- Most sensitive dx test? Limited mobility on insufflation or air-fluid level
- RF? Decreased SES, Native Americans, formula fed, tobacco smoke, around kids
- Tx? Amoxicillin or azithromycin for 10 days. If no improvement in 2-3 days, switch to amox-clav
- Complications? Effusion -> place tubes if bilateral effusion >4mo or if bilateral hearing loss

## Otitis Externa

- 12 y/o in summer swim league has pain when adjusting goggle straps behind ear. Thick exudates and tender posterior auricular nodes -> Otitis Externa
- Tx? Topical ciprofloxacin
- Complications? Malignant external otitis can invade temporal bone -> facial paralysis, vertigo. Need CT and IV abx. May need surgery

# Sore Throat

## GABHS Pharyngitis

- 7 y/o w/ exudative pharyngitis w/ tender cervical lymph nodes and fever to 102 -> GABHS pharyngitis
- Best 1st test? Rapid strep antigen. If negative and clinical suspicion is high -> do culture
- Tx? PCN or erythromycin (prevents rheumatic fever)

## Peritonsillar Abscess

- Child presents w/ "hot potato voice" and upon throat exam, uvula is deviated -> Peritonsillar abscess
- Tx? Aspiration or I&D + abx, tonsillectomy if recurrent
- Indications for tonsillectomy? >5 episodes of strep/year for 2 years or >3 episodes/year for 3 years

## Retropharyngeal Abscess

- Child presents w/ "muffled voice", stridor and refuses to turn head -> Retropharyngeal abscess
- Tx? I&D for C&S. GAS + anaerobes. 3rd gen ceph + amp or clindamycin
- Complications? Retropharyngeal space communicates w/ mediastinum

## EBV (Mono)

- Older kiddo w/ fever, fatigue, generalized adenopathy and splenomegaly (anterior and posterior cervical nodes) -> EBV
- What happens if you give ampicillin or amoxicillin? Maculopapular rash (immune mediated vasculitic)
- Dx? Blood smear shows lymphocytosis w/ atypical lymphs. +Heterophile antibody (Monospot) test
- Tx? Rest and symptomatic therapy
- Precautions? Splenic hemorrhage or rupture (most in 2nd week). No contact sports until splenomegaly resolves.

# Respiratory Distress

## Croup

- 1 y/o w/ fever to 100.5 & "barking" cough and loud noises on inspiration -> Croup
- Most common bug? Parainfluenza virus
- X-ray buzzword? "Steeple sign"
- Tx? Mist, epinephrine neb, steroids

## Epiglottitis

- 2 y/o w/ fever to 104 & drooling w/ intercostal retractions and tripod position -> Epiglottitis
- Most common bug? H. Flu B only in unimmunized. Also strep pyo, strep pneumo, staph
- X-ray buzzword? "Thumbprint sign"
- Next best step? Go to OR and intubate
- Tx? Anti-staph abx + 3rd generation cephalosporin

# Pneumonia

## Acute Bronchitis

- Kid w/ cough productive of yellow-green sputum, runny nose, T = 100.8. Lung exam only reveals some coarse rhonchi -> Acute bronchitis
- Tx? Supportive tx w/ anti-pyretic, antitussives, antihistamines

## Pneumonia by Age

- MC cause in neonates <28 days? GBS, E. Coli, Listeria
- MC cause 1mo-3mo? C. trachomatis, RSV, parainfluenza, strep pneumo
- Specific findings for chlamydia pneumonia? Staccato cough, eosinophilia
- MC cause in kids 4mo-5 y/o? VIRAL! (RSV) then S. pneumo
- MC cause in kids >5 y/o? Mycoplasma, S. pneumo

## Bronchiolitis

- 9 mo infant w/ runny nose, wheezy cough, T = 101.5, RR = 60. Retractions visible and pulse ox 91% -> Bronchiolitis
- Most common bug? RSV. Confirm w/ swab
- CXR findings? Hyperinflation w/ patchy atelectasis
- Tx? Hospitalize if respiratory distress. Albuterol nebs. NO steroids
- Who needs vaccine? Palivizumab for premies, CHD, lung disease, immune disease

## Whooping Cough (Pertussis)

- 9 mo infant with severe coughing spells with loud inspiratory whoops and vomiting afterwards. 2 weeks ago had runny nose and dry cough -> Whooping cough
- Responsible bug? Bordetella pertussis
- Lab findings? CBC shows lymphocytosis
- Tx? Erythromycin for 14 days
- Family members and kids in daycare? Erythromycin for 14 days (prophylaxis)

# UTI

- In neonates sxs are vague: fever, dehydration, fussy
- If fever is present -> it's pyelo. Cystitis has NO fever
- Before age 1, boys are more likely than girls to get UTI
- Anatomic risk factor? Vesicoureteral reflux. Need abx prophylaxis
- Diagnosis? Clean catch or cath sample, UA and Culture (>10K CFU)
- Need ultrasound if any febrile UTI for anatomy, abscess or hydronephrosis
- Tx of UTI? PO trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole or nitrofurantoin
- Tx of pyelo? 14 days of IV ceftriaxone or amp & gent
- Follow up? Test of cure to confirm sterility
- Who needs VCUG? All males, females <5, any pyelo, females >5 w/ 2nd UTI
- Role of Tc-labeled DMSA scan? Most sensitive and accurate study of scarring and renal size, but not first line

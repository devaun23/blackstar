# Benign Murmurs

- >30% of kiddos have them
- Good characteristics: systolic, <II/VI, soft, vibratory and musical
- Still's murmur -> systolic, <II/VI, soft, vibratory and musical, heard best @ lower mid-sternum
- Venous hum -> best heard in anterior neck, disappears when jugular vein is compressed
- Never normal: anything DIASTOLIC, anything >II/VI -> get an echo

# Cyanotic Heart Disease

## Transposition of the Great Arteries

- Newborn is cyanotic @ birth, O2 does not improve -> Transposition of the Great Arteries
- Most common in infants of diabetic mothers
- Associated murmur? NONE! (unless PDA or VSD)
- Immediate tx? PGE1 to keep PDA patent

## Tetralogy of Fallot

- 2 y/o child who gets cyanotic and hyperpneic while playing, squats down -> Tetralogy of Fallot
- VSD + RA hypertrophy + overriding aorta + pulmonary stenosis
- Associated murmur? Harsh SEM + single S2
- Tx? O2 and knee-chest position, surgical correction

## Ebstein Anomaly

- Bipolar woman gives birth to a child w/ holosystolic murmur worse on inspiration -> Ebstein Anomaly
- Tricuspid insufficiency 2/2 TV displacement into RV
- Associated arrhythmia? Wolff-Parkinson-White

## Tricuspid Atresia

- Cyanosis @ birth with holosystolic murmur, depends on VSD or ASD for life. EKG shows LVH -> Tricuspid atresia
- Give PGE1 until surgery

## Truncus Arteriosus

- Heart defect associated with DiGeorge syndrome. CXR shows increased pulm blood flow and bi-ventricular hypertrophy -> Truncus arteriosus
- Eisenmenger develops early. Do surgery in 1st few weeks of life

# Acyanotic Heart Disease

## VSD

- #1 congenital heart lesion. Harsh holosystolic murmur over LL sternal border, loud P2 -> VSD
- If II/VI in a 2mo old? If no sxs, continue to monitor. Most close by 1-2yr
- Gold standard dx test? Echo
- Surgery indicated when: FTT, 6-12mo w/ pHTN, >2yrs w/ Qp/Qs >2:1
- Is louder better or worse? Better. Means the defect is small. Most often membranous. More likely to spontaneously close.

## ASD

- Loud S1 w/ fixed and split S2. Older child w/ exercise intolerance -> ASD

## Endocardial Cushion Defect

- Most common defect in Down Syndrome baby. Fixed & split S2 + SEM w/ diastolic rumble -> Endocardial cushion defect
- Tx? At risk for early Eisenmenger's. Surgery before pHTN @ 6-12mo.

## PDA

- Continuous machine-like murmur w/ bounding pulses and wide pulse pressure -> PDA
- Associations? Prematurity, congenital rubella syndrome
- Tx? If not closed by 1wk, give indomethacin or surgically close

## Coarctation of the Aorta

- Most common defect in Turner's baby -> Coarctation of the Aorta
- Decreased femoral pulses, "reverse 3 sign", "notching" @ inferior rib border 2/2 increased collateral
- May see asymmetry in arm BPs

# Other Cardiac Diseases

## HOCM

- 15 year old athlete complains of occasional palpitations, angina and dizziness. Last week he fainted during the 1st inning of his baseball game -> HOCM
- Murmur? SEM, better w/ increased preload (squat, handgrip), louder w/ valsalva, standing, exercise (decreased preload)
- Tx? Beta blockers or CCB (no diuretics or dig — they decrease preload)
- Restrictions? No sports or heavy exercise!

## Acute Rheumatic Fever

- 7 year old girl w/ vague chest pain, pain in several different joints, rash. ESR elevated. EKG shows PR prolongation -> Acute Rheumatic Fever
- Tx? Oral PCN (erythromycin) for 10 days, then prophylactic till 20
- Complications? Mitral stenosis (then aortic or tricuspid involvement)

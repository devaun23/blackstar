# APGAR

- Pulse of 130, acrocyanotic, grimaces to stimulation, moving all extremities and crying -> Score = 8
- 2pts for pulse, 1 for color, 1 for irritability, 2 for tone and 2 for respiration
- APGAR tells you general info about how the newborn tolerated labor (1min) and the newborn's response to resuscitation (5min)
- APGAR does NOT tell you what to do next (does not guide therapy) or how the baby will turn out (does NOT predict neurologic outcome)

# Physical Exam Findings

## Erb-Duchenne Palsy

- When assessing Moro on an LGA newborn, the right arm remains extended and medially rotated -> Erb-Duchenne C5-C6
- Klumpke palsy is C7-C8 + T1
- Refer if not better by 3-6mo for neuroplasty

## Clavicular Fracture

- When palpating the clavicles on a LGA newborn, you feel crepitus and discontinuity -> Clavicular Fracture
- Will form a callus in 1wk. No tx needed. Can use figure of 8 splint.

## Scalp Findings

| Finding | Key Feature | Crosses Suture Lines? |
|---------|-------------|----------------------|
| Caput succedaneum | Edema | Yes |
| Cephalohematoma | Fluctuance | No |

## Benign Skin Findings

- Mongolian spots -> benign blue-gray patches
- Nevus simplex (salmon patch) -> benign vascular birthmark
- Milia -> white papules on face
- Erythema toxicum -> benign rash of newborn
- Strawberry hemangioma -> benign, usually involutes
- Neonatal acne -> benign

## Nevus Sebaceous

- Area of alopecia with orange colored nodular skin -> Nevus sebaceous
- Tx? Remove before adolescence b/c it can undergo malignant degeneration

## Seborrheic Dermatitis

- Thick, yellow/white oily scale on an inflammatory base -> Seborrheic dermatitis (cradle cap)
- Tx? Gently clean w/ mild shampoo

# Neonatal Screening

- Two disorders screened for in every state because they are disastrous if not caught early (and contraindication to breast feeding) -> PKU and Galactosemia

## PKU

- Deficient phenylalanine hydroxylase
- Sxs = MR, vomiting, athetosis, seizures, developmental delay over 1st few months
- Signs = fair hair, eyes, skin, musty smell
- Tx? Low phenylalanine diet

## Galactosemia

- Deficient galactose-1-phosphate uridyltransferase. G1p accumulates to damage kidney, liver, brain
- Sxs = MR, direct hyperbili & jaundice, hypoglycemia, cataracts, seizures
- Predisposed to E. coli sepsis
- Tx? No lactose for life

# Jaundice

## Physiologic Jaundice

- 3 days old, bili @ 10, direct is 0.5. Eating & pooping well -> Physiologic jaundice
- Gone by 5th DOL. Liver conjugation not yet mature.

## Breast Feeding Jaundice

- 7 days old, bili @ 12, direct is 0.5. Dry mucous membranes, not gaining weight -> Breast feeding jaundice
- Decreased feeding = dehydration = retain meconium & re-absorb deconjugated bili

## Breast Milk Jaundice

- 14 days old, bili @ 12, direct is 0.5. Baby regained birth weight, otherwise healthy -> Breast milk jaundice
- Breast milk has glucuronidase and de-conjugated bili

## Pathologic Jaundice

- 1 day old, bili @ 14, direct is 0.5 -> Pathologic jaundice
- Pathologic = on 1st DOL, bili >12, d-bili >2, rate of rise >5/day
- Next best test? Coombs
- If positive -> Rh or ABO incompatibility
- If negative -> twin/twin or mom/fetus transfusion, IDM, spherocytosis, G6PD deficiency

## Biliary Atresia

- 7 days old. Dark urine, pale stool. Bili @ 12, dbili is 8. LFTs elevated -> Biliary atresia
- Bile ducts cannot drain bile. Causes liver failure. Need surgery.
- Other causes of direct hyperbilirubinemia: always r/o sepsis, galactosemia, hypothyroid, choledochal cyst, CF

## Inherited Causes

- Random inherited causes of indirect hyperbili -> Gilbert (decreased glucuronyl transferase level), Crigler-Najjar (type 1, total deficiency)
- Random inherited causes of direct hyperbili -> Dubin-Johnson (black liver), Rotor (no black liver)

## Treatment

- Indirect bili can cross BBB, deposit in BG and brainstem nuclei and cause kernicterus (esp if bili >20)
- Tx? Phototherapy ionizes the unconjugated bili so it can be excreted
- Double volume exchange transfusion if phototherapy doesn't work

# Respiratory Disorders

## Diaphragmatic Hernia

- Baby born w/ respiratory distress, scaphoid abdomen -> Diaphragmatic hernia
- Biggest concern? Pulmonary hypoplasia
- Tx? If dx prenatally, plan delivery at place w/ ECMO. Let lungs mature 3-4 days then do surgery

## TE Fistula

- Baby born w/ respiratory distress w/ excess drooling -> TE fistula
- Best diagnostic test? Place feeding tube, take xray, see it coiled in thorax
- Associated anomalies? VACTERL -> Vertebral, Anal atresia, Cardiac, Tracheo-Esophageal, Radial and Renal

## Choanal Atresia

- 1 week old baby becomes cyanotic when feeding but pinks up when crying -> Choanal atresia
- Associated anomalies? CHARGE -> Coloboma, Heart defects, Retarded growth, GU anomalies, Ear anomalies and deafness

## RDS (Respiratory Distress Syndrome)

- 32 wk premie has dyspnea, RR of 80 w/ nasal flaring -> RDS
- Pathophys? Surfactant deficiency, can't keep alveoli open
- Prenatal dx? L/S ratio <2, give antenatal betamethasone
- Tx? O2 therapy with nasal CPAP to keep alveoli open

## TTN (Transient Tachypnea of the Newborn)

- 38 wk LGA infant born by C/S to an A2GDM has dyspnea/grunting -> TTN
- Pathophys? Lung fluid not squeezed out, retained
- Usually minimal O2 needed. Self-resolves in hours to days.

## Meconium Aspiration Syndrome

- 41 wk AGA infant born after ROM yielded greenish-brown fluid -> Meconium aspiration syndrome
- Next best step? Intubate & suction before stimulation
- Complications? Pulmonary artery HTN, pneumonitis

# GI Disorders

## Gastroschisis

- Defect lateral (usually R) of the midline, no sac -> Gastroschisis
- Will see high maternal AFP
- Associated w/ other disorders? Not usually.
- Complications? May be atretic or necrotic requiring removal. Short gut syndrome.

## Omphalocele

- Defect in the midline. Covered by sac -> Omphalocele
- Associated w/ Edwards & Patau
- Associated w/ Beckwith-Wiedemann Syndrome = big baby w/ big tongue, hypoglycemia, ear pits

## Umbilical Hernia

- Defect in the midline. No bowel present -> Umbilical hernia
- Associated w/ congenital hypothyroidism (also big tongue)
- Repair not needed unless persists past age 2 or 3

## Pyloric Stenosis

- 4 wk old infant w/ non-bilious vomiting and palpable "olive" -> Pyloric stenosis
- Metabolic complications? Hypochloremic metabolic alkalosis
- Tx? Immediate surgical referral for myotomy

## Intestinal Atresia

- 2 wk old infant w/ bilious vomiting. Pregnancy complicated by polyhydramnios -> Intestinal atresia (or annular pancreas)
- Associated w/ Down Syndrome (esp duodenal)

## Malrotation and Volvulus

- 1 wk old baby w/ bilious vomiting, draws up his legs, has abd distension -> Malrotation and volvulus
- Pathophys? Doesn't rotate 270 degrees ccw around SMA
- Ladd's bands can kink the duodenum

## Intussusception

- 2 mo old baby has colicky abd pain and currant jelly stool w/ a sausage-shaped mass in the RUQ -> Intussusception
- Barium enema is both diagnostic and therapeutic

## Hirschsprung's Disease

- 3 day old newborn has still not passed meconium -> DDx: Meconium ileus or Hirschsprung's
- Meconium ileus -> consider CF if +FH. Gastrograffin enema is dx & tx
- Hirschsprung's -> DRE = explosion of poo. Bx showing no ganglia is gold standard

## Necrotizing Enterocolitis

- 5 day old former 33 weeker develops bloody diarrhea -> NEC
- Xray? Pneumatosis intestinalis (air in the wall)
- Tx? NPO, TPN (if needed), antibiotics and resection of necrotic bowel
- Risk factors? Premature gut, introduction of feeds, formula

# GU Disorders

## Cryptorchidism

- Newborn male with no palpable testes -> Cryptorchidism
- Associated w/ prune belly syndrome
- Usually in inguinal canal
- Next best test? Ultrasound if not palpable
- Surgery if not descended by 1yr to avoid sterility/cancer

## Hypospadias

- Newborn male with urethral opening on the ventral surface -> Hypospadias
- Do NOT circumcise! Foreskin is used in eventual repair.

## Congenital Adrenal Hyperplasia

- Newborn child with ambiguous genitalia. One month later has vomiting & decreased Na, increased K and acidosis -> CAH
- MC cause? 21 hydroxylase deficiency (autosomal recessive)
- Definitive test? 17-OH progesterone before and after ACTH bolus
- Tx? Hydrocortisone and fludrocortisone (increase doses in times of stress)

# Infants of Diabetic Mothers

## Pre-existing Diabetes (esp Type 1)

- Control glc in 1st trimester & take 4mg folate/day
- Complications: Placental insufficiency/IUGR, congenital heart dz, NTD, caudal regression syndrome, small left colon syndrome

## Gestational Diabetes

- LGA -> increased risk of birth trauma (clavicle, Erbs), C/S & TTN
- Hypoglycemia -> maternal hyperglycemia causes fetal hyperinsulinemia. Feed frequently if <40. IV dextrose if <20
- Hypocalcemia -> neonatal seizure (always check Ca!)
- Polycythemia -> big baby needs more O2, hypoxia increases EPO. Complications: renal or splenic vein thromboses
- Jaundice -> more RBCs to break down. Risk for kernicterus
- RDS -> increased insulin interferes w/ cortisol surge prior to birth that normally stimulates lung maturation. Check L/S ratio >2

# Neonatal Fever

- If a baby <28 days has a fever >100.4 = sepsis until proven otherwise
- Sxs might include irritability, poor feeding
- Tests: CBC w/ diff, CXR, blood cultures, urine cultures (use catheter), LP
- Risk factors: prematurity, chorioamnionitis, intrapartum fever, maternal leukocytosis, prolonged ROM (>18hrs), GBS+ mom
- Most common bugs? Group B Strep, E. Coli, Listeria monocytogenes
- Empiric tx? Amp + gent until 48hr cultures are negative. Cefotaxime + Amp if meningitis suspected

# TORCH Infections

| Infection | Key Features | Treatment |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Syphilis | Maculopapular rash on palms/soles, snuffles, periostitis | PCN |
| Toxoplasmosis | Hydrocephalus, intracranial calcifications, chorioretinitis | Sulfadiazine + leucovorin |
| Rubella | Cataracts, deafness, heart defects (PDA, VSD), extramedullary hematopoiesis | No tx |
| CMV | Microcephaly, periventricular calcifications, deafness, thrombocytopenia, petechiae | Ganciclovir (won't prevent MR) |
| Congenital Varicella | Limb hypoplasia, cutaneous scars, cataracts, chorioretinitis, cortical atrophy | VZIG if mom exposed 5d before-2d after delivery |

# Neonatal Conjunctivitis

| Timing | Cause | Treatment |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| DOL 1-3 | Chemical (silver nitrate drops) | Self-limited (rare now, we use erythromycin) |
| DOL 3-5 | Gonococcal (bilateral purulent, can cause corneal ulceration) | Topical erythromycin + IV 3rd gen cephalosporin |
| DOL 7-14 | Chlamydia (mucoid discharge, lid swelling) | Oral erythromycin. Complication: chlamydial pneumonia |

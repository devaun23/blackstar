# Sickle Cell Disease

## Presentations

- Swollen, painful hands and feet -> Dactylitis. 2/2 necrosis of small bones
- Excruciating pain in the extremities, ulcers, hip pain -> Pain crises. Ischemic damage 2/2 sickling
- Point tenderness on femur, fever, and malaise -> Osteomyelitis. MC bug is salmonella
- Things seen on blood smear? Increased retics, normal MCV, sickles, targets, Howell-Jolly bodies
- Acute drop in HCT with decreased retics -> Aplastic crisis. Parvovirus B19
- Recurrent RUQ pain after meals -> Pigment gallstones. Do cholecystectomy
- Respiratory distress & emergent tonsilectomy -> Waldyer Ring hyperplasia
- Proteinuria and increased creatinine + recurrent UTIs -> Kidney infarcts due to sickled RBCs

## Complications

- Most common cause of sepsis? Strep Pneumo
- Fever, cough, chest pain, chills, SOB -> Acute chest syndrome. Pulmonary infarction. MC cause of death.
- Tx? O2, abx and exchange transfusion
- Acute confusion and focal neurologic deficits -> Stroke. Tx? Exchange transfusion (NOT tPA!)
- Assessing risk? Transcranial doppler (v <200cm/sec), keep HbS <30%

## Prevention & Treatment

- Vaccination: 23-valent pneumococcal vaccine @ age 2 + H. flu and N. meningitides
- PCN prophylaxis from age 2mo until age 6yrs
- If fatigue and megaloblastic anemia -> most likely folate deficiency (higher need 2/2 increased retics)
- Tx? Hydroxyurea increases production of HbF. Tx infections aggressively and manage pain.
- Bone marrow transplant cures but has 10% post-op mortality

# Pediatric Anemias

## Physiologic Anemia

- Physiologic drop in H&H for 1st 2-3mo -> not a big deal
- Transient Erythroblastopenia occurs later (3mo-6yrs) = immune suppression after viral infection (not B19)

## Iron Deficiency Anemia

- 18 mo kiddo, picky eater, drinks lots of cow's milk -> Fe-deficiency
- Decreased H&H, MCV 75, decreased ferritin, increased TIBC
- Tx w/ oral ferrous salts

## Thalassemia

- 18 mo kiddo, eats a varied diet. Mom is Italian -> Thalassemia
- Decreased H&H, MCV 60, decreased RDW
- Varying degrees. Tx w/ transfusion & deferoxamine
- Can see expanded medullary space

## Folate Deficiency

- 8 mo kiddo is irritable, has glossitis & FTT. Picky eater, drinks lots of goat's milk -> Folate deficiency
- See low serum (RBC) folate. Tx w/ daily folate.

## Diamond-Blackfan Anemia

- 4 mo pale baby, normal plts, WBCs but hemoglobin is 4. Increased RBC ADA and low retics. Triphalangeal thumbs -> Diamond-Blackfan Anemia
- Tx? Corticosteroids, transfusions, stem cell transplant

## Fanconi Anemia

- 18 mo baby presents w/ low plts, low WBCs and profound anemia. Cafe-au-lait spots, microcephaly, and absent thumbs -> Fanconi Anemia
- Dx? Bone marrow shows hypoplasia, cytogenetic studies for chr breaks
- Tx? Corticosteroids, androgens, bone marrow transplant
- Complications? Increased risk for AML and other cancers

## Lead Poisoning

- 2 y/o baby presents w/ hyperactivity, impaired growth, abdominal pain and constipation -> Lead Poisoning
- Blood smear shows basophilic stippling
- Dx? Venous blood sample, check lead level
- Tx? >45 tx w/ succimer. >70 admit and tx w/ EDTA + dimercaprol
- Screening? Test blood lead levels btwn 12-24mo if low SES, live in old house (<1960)

# Thrombocytopenia

## ITP

- 15 y/o F recurrent epistaxis, heavy menses & petechiae. Decreased plts only -> ITP
- Tx w/ IVIG for 1-2 days, then prednisone, then splenectomy. NO platelets!!

## Von Willebrand Disease

- 15 y/o F recurrent epistaxis, heavy menses, petechiae, normal plts, increased bleeding time and PTT -> VWD
- DDAVP for bleeding or pre-op. Replace factor VIII (contains vWF) if bleeding continues.

## Hemophilia

- 7 y/o M recurrent bruising, hematuria, & hemarthroses, increased PTT that corrects w/ mixing studies -> Hemophilia
- If mild, tx w/ DDAVP, otherwise replace factors

## Vitamin K Deficiency

- 1 wk old newborn, born at home, comes in with bleeding from the umbilical stump & bleeding diathesis -> Vitamin K deficiency
- Decreased II, VII, IX and X. Same in CF kid with malabsorption
- Tx w/ FFP acutely + vitamin K shot
- 1st factor depleted? VII, so PT increases 1st
- 2 factors not depleted? VIII and vWF b/c they are made by endothelial cells

## Liver Failure

- 9 y/o F with Wilson's disease developed fulminant liver disease -> all factors depleted except VIII and vWF

# HUS (Hemolytic Uremic Syndrome)

- 3 y/o child with petechiae, abdominal pain, vomiting, lethargy. Bloody diarrhea 5 days ago after eating hamburgers -> HUS
- Labs: thrombocytopenia and increased creatinine
- Most common cause? E. Coli O157:H7, Shigella, Salmonella, Campylobacter
- Tx? NO platelets! Aggressive nutrition (TPN) and early peritoneal dialysis. Don't give abx for bloody diarrhea -> can increase risk of HUS

# HSP (Henoch-Schonlein Purpura)

- 5 y/o child with purpura on legs and buttocks, abdominal pain, joint pain, currant jelly stool. Normal smear, normal coags. IgA and C3 deposited in skin -> HSP
- Most common cause? Usually follows a URI
- Tx? Symptomatic treatment. Can use steroids for GI or renal disease.

# Pediatric Tumors

## Brain Tumors

- New onset seizure, ataxia and HA worse in the AM with vomiting for a month -> brain tumor. Most likely infratentorial
- Most common -> Pilocytic astrocytoma of cerebellum. Resect. ~90% survive.
- 2nd most common, worse prognosis -> Medulloblastoma. Vermis, obstructs 4th ventricle

## Craniopharyngioma

- Adolescent with height in 5th%, w/ bitemporal hemianopsia. See calcifications in sella turcica -> Craniopharyngioma
- Suprasellar. A remnant of Rathke's pouch.

## Wilms Tumor

- 2 year old hypertensive child with asymptomatic abdominal mass -> Wilms tumor
- Associations? Aniridia, GU anomalies, hemihypertrophy, Beckwith-Wiedemann
- Best test? Abdominal CT. Do CXR to check lung involvement
- Tx? Surgery, chemo, rads

## Neuroblastoma

- 4 year old with jerking movements of eyes and legs, bluish skin nodules and a tender abdominal mass -> Neuroblastoma
- Diagnostic tests? Increased urine homovanillic or vanillylmandelic acid

# Leukemia & Lymphoma

## ALL

- 3 year old girl w/ a limp & left leg pain, T = 99.9, HSM, petechiae, & pallor. Cells are CALLA and TdT+ -> ALL
- Best test? Bone marrow biopsy -> >30% lymphoblasts
- Tx? VDP + CNS tx w/ intrathecal methotrexate
- Poor prognostic factors? <1 or >10 years old, >100K WBC

## Hodgkin Lymphoma

- 14 y/o boy w/ enlarged, painless, rubbery nodes, drenching fevers, and 10% weight loss -> Hodgkin Lymphoma
- Best test? Excisional biopsy. Then staging CT or laparoscopy (determines tx)
- Tx? Chemo + Rads. 90% cure if stage I or II

## Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma

- 7 year old girl with non-productive cough and large anterior mediastinal mass on CXR -> Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma
- Best test? Biopsy of mass, bone marrow bx for staging
- Tx? Surgical excision if abdominal tumor. Can use anti-CD20 if B-cell tumor. Rads for some.

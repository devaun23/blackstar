# Hydrocephalus

- Anytime you see a meningocele or myelomeningocele -> Do a head CT looking for hydrocephalus (Arnold Chiari II)
- Anytime you see an infant with head circumference >95th% -> Consider hydrocephalus. Also bulging fontanelle, increased DTRs, HA, vomiting
- Noncommunicating -> Stenosis of cerebral aqueduct, tumor/malformation near 4th ventricle
- Communicating -> SAH, pneumococcal/TB meningitis, leukemia

## Dandy-Walker Malformation

- Infant with increasing head size, prominent occiput, cerebellar ataxia and delayed motor development -> Dandy-Walker malformation
- CT or MRI shows cystic expansion of 4th ventricle. Can see agenesis of cerebellar vermis.

# Seizures

## Febrile Seizure

- 1 y/o develops fever to 102.4. Four hours later has a 3-4 minute tonic-clonic seizure -> Febrile Seizure
- Next best step? Give acetaminophen. NO increased risk for epilepsy

## Absence Seizure

- 8 year old boy always "staring into space". Episodes last only seconds, have lip smacking, goes right about his business after -> Absence Seizure
- Common EEG finding? 3 Hz spike and wave discharge
- Best Tx? Ethosuximide or valproic acid

## Infantile Spasms

- 6 mo old brought in for multiple symmetric contraction episodes of neck, trunk and extremities that occur in spells -> Infantile Spasms
- Common EEG finding? Hypsarrhythmia = asynchronous, chaotic, bilateral
- Best Tx? ACTH. Prednisone is 2nd line.

# Neurodegenerative Disorders

## Friedreich Ataxia

- 8 y/o w/ difficulty w/ balance while walking, no DTRs, bilateral Babinski and "explosive, dysarthric speech" -> Friedreich Ataxia
- AR, trinucleotide repeat
- Most common cause of death? HOCM -> CHF

## Metachromatic Leukodystrophy

- 2 y/o w/ gait disturbance, loss of intellectual function, nystagmus and optic atrophy. Cresyl violet metachromatic staining -> Metachromatic leukodystrophy
- AR
- Pathophys? Deficiency of arylsulfatase A -> accumulation of cerebroside sulfate

## Adrenoleukodystrophy

- 12 y/o w/ decreased school performance, behavior changes, ataxia, spasticity, hyperpigmentation, increased K, decreased Na, acidosis -> Adrenoleukodystrophy
- XLR
- Prognosis? Death within 10 years

## Tay-Sachs Disease

- 9 mo who had previously been reaching milestones starts to lag. Seizures, hypotonia, cherry red macula -> Tay-Sachs
- Pathophys? Deficiency of hexosaminidase A -> accumulation of GM2 ganglioside

# Neuromuscular Disorders

## Werdnig-Hoffmann Disease (SMA Type 1)

- 3 mo infant lays in the "frog-leg" position, <5th% 2/2 feeding difficulties, hypotonic, fasciculations of the tongue and absent DTRs -> SMA 1 (Werdnig-Hoffmann Disease)
- Prognosis? Most die before age 2

## Charcot-Marie-Tooth Disease

- 6 y/o brought in 2/2 "clumsiness" and frequent falls. Lower leg has decreased muscle bulk and appears "stork-like". Multiple small injuries on hands and feet. Pes cavus and claw hand -> Charcot-Marie-Tooth Disease
- Tests? Decreased motor/sensory nerve velocity, sural nerve biopsy. CPK is normal
- Tx? Stabilize ankles w/ surgical fusion. Usually normal lifespan and most remain ambulatory.

# Mood Disorders

## Bipolar Disorder

Decreased sleep, hypersexuality, pressured speech, grandiosity, impulsive spending → Manic Episode
Manic episode + depressive episodes → Bipolar I Disorder
Bipolar I incidence in the population → ~1%
MZ twin concordance for Bipolar I → 80-90%
First manic episode in a 75 y/o → Look for a medical cause, especially right frontal hemisphere stroke
SSRIs and TCAs should be avoided in bipolar → can trigger mania

## Lithium

Tx: Acute mania → Haloperidol or clonazepam for agitation/delusions. Lithium, valproic acid or carbamazepine for maintenance
Patient on lithium takes NSAIDs and develops n/v/d, coarse tremor, ataxia, confusion, slurred speech → Lithium Toxicity
Lithium toxicity precipitated by NSAIDs → Better pain meds are aspirin or sulindac
Lithium toxicity EKG findings → T-wave flattening or inversion + U waves
Tx: Lithium toxicity → Fluid resuscitation. Emergent dialysis if level >4 or kidney disease
MOA: Lithium → Suppresses inositol triphosphate
Lithium therapeutic levels → 0.6-1.2
Lithium monitoring → Li level q4-8wks, TFTs q6mo, Cr, UA, CBC, EKG
Lithium major side effects → Weight gain, acne, GI irritation, cramps
C/I: Lithium → Severe renal disease, MI, diuretics or digoxin, myasthenia gravis, pregnancy or breastfeeding
Lithium in pregnancy → Ebstein's anomaly (malformed tricuspid valve, atrializes part of RV). Risk highest if taken during 1st trimester

## Mood Stabilizers in Special Populations

Tx: Bipolar in pregnancy → Clonazepam, especially during 1st trimester
Bipolar + elevated LFTs and hepatitis → Suspect valproate side effect
Bipolar + Stevens-Johnson Syndrome → Suspect lamotrigine (less likely carbamazepine)
Bipolar + agranulocytosis → Suspect carbamazepine. Check CBC regularly
C/I: Carbamazepine ANC <2000 → Monitor closely with weekly CBC
C/I: Carbamazepine ANC <1000 → Discontinue the medication
Bipolar + elevated AFP in 20wk pregnant woman → Could be valproate or carbamazepine causing neural tube defect. Reproductive-age females should take 4g folate daily
Most common complication of carbamazepine → Rash
Valproate therapeutic levels → 60-120
Carbamazepine therapeutic levels → 6-12
Valproate side effects → n/v/d, skin rash, elevated LFTs, hepatitis

## Major Depressive Disorder

Decreased appetite, weight loss, anhedonia, insomnia, decreased energy, poor concentration, guilt for 2 weeks → Major Depressive Disorder
Most important first question in depression → Assess for suicidal ideation
#1 RF for completed suicide → Prior attempt
Suicide risk factors → Age >45, white, male, serious illness, detailed plan, no support, ETOH/drugs
MDD polysomnogram findings → Shortened REM latency, more frequent REM
MDD atypical lab test → Dexamethasone suppression test shows failure to suppress cortisol
Medications that cause depression → IFN, beta-blockers, alpha-methyldopa, L-dopa, OCPs, ETOH, cocaine/amphetamine withdrawal, opiates
Medical diseases that cause depression → HIV, Lyme, Hypothyroidism, Porphyria, Uremia, Cushing's Disease, Liver disease, Huntington's, MS, Lupus, L-MCA stroke

## Atypical Depression

Hyperphagia, weight gain, hypersomnia, leaden paralysis → Atypical Depression
Atypical depression features → Hypersensitive to rejection, can affect social functioning
Tx: Atypical depression → MAOIs are best treatment

## Bereavement vs Adjustment Disorder

1 month after death of child, mother feels guilty, can't sleep/concentrate/eat → Uncomplicated Bereavement (V-code)
Bereavement features → No suicidal ideation (other than wanting to be with loved one), no psychosis (other than hearing/seeing loved one). Rarely treat with antidepressants
4 months after death of chihuahua, woman still has depressive symptoms → Adjustment Disorder
Adjustment disorder criteria → Symptoms within 3 months of stressor, out of proportion. Cannot persist longer than 6 months
Tx: Adjustment disorder → Psychotherapy is best treatment

# Psychopharmacology — Antidepressants

## SSRIs

Tx: MDD first line → SSRIs. Also indicated for OCD, bulimia, anxiety, PTSD, premature ejaculation
SSRI with most drug-drug interactions → Paroxetine
SSRI that doesn't require tapering → Fluoxetine (long half-life)
SSRI with fewest drug-drug interactions → Citalopram
SSRI discontinuation syndrome → HA, n/v/d, dizziness and fatigue when stopping suddenly. Most common with sertraline and fluvoxamine
SSRI + MAOI → Serotonin syndrome: myoclonic jerks, tachycardia, high BP, hyperreflexia, n/v/d
SSRI causing sexual dysfunction → Switch to bupropion (DA/NE reuptake inhibitor)
C/I: Bupropion → Bulimia, alcoholics, epileptics

## Other Antidepressants

Erection lasting >3 hours → Likely caused by trazodone (priapism)
Good antidepressant for old, skinny, sad ladies → Mirtazapine (increases appetite and sleep)
Antidepressant to avoid in hypertensive patients → Venlafaxine (SNRI). Don't take with St. John's Wort
Pounding head, flushing, nausea, myoclonus after eating cheese, drinking red wine, taking decongestant or meperidine → Hypertensive crisis with MAOI
Tx: MAOI hypertensive crisis → 5mg IV phentolamine

## Tricyclic Antidepressant Toxicity

Kid ingests pills: dry mouth, tachycardia, vomiting, urinary retention, seizures, widened QRS, prolonged QT → Tricyclic Antidepressant overdose
TCA toxicity anticholinergic effects → Dry mouth, tachycardia, urinary retention
#1 cause of death in TCA overdose → Arrhythmia (torsades, v-fib)
Tx: TCA overdose → Activated charcoal if ingestion within 1-2hrs. Give IV sodium bicarbonate (helps metabolic acidosis and is cardioprotective)

# Psychotic Disorders

## Schizophrenia

Decline in functioning, flat affect, no motivation, paranoid delusions, auditory hallucinations for >6 weeks → Schizophrenia, Paranoid type (most common and best prognosis)
Schizophrenia prevalence → 0.5-1%
MZ twin concordance for schizophrenia → 50%
Sibling risk for schizophrenia → 10%
Positive symptoms neurobiology → Excess dopamine in limbic area binding D2 receptors
Negative symptoms neurobiology → Decreased dopamine in prefrontal cortex/mesocortical tract. This is why typical antipsychotics make negative symptoms worse

## Psychotic Disorder Differential

Delusions, hallucinations, and flat affect for >1 week but <1 month → Brief Psychotic Disorder
Delusions, hallucinations, and flat affect for >1 month but <6 months → Schizophreniform Disorder
Persecutory delusions for 3 years + develops MDD symptoms → Schizoaffective Disorder (delusions/hallucinations present for >2 weeks in absence of mood symptoms)
Tx: Schizoaffective → Atypical antipsychotics + SSRI if depression, + lithium if manic
MDD for 3 years + hears voices saying he is worthless → MDD with Psychotic Features (delusions are typically mood congruent)
Tx: MDD with psychotic features → Atypical antipsychotic + SSRI, or ECT (especially in pregnant patients)
Man convinced Miley Cyrus is in love with him, otherwise functional → Delusional Disorder, Erotomanic type (non-bizarre)
Tx: Delusional disorder → Therapeutic relationship + medications

# Antipsychotics

## Typical Antipsychotics

Tx: Acute agitation or psychosis → IM haloperidol
MOA: Typical antipsychotics → D2 receptor antagonist at mesolimbic tract, helps positive symptoms
Typical antipsychotic causes hyperprolactinemia and EPS → D2 receptor blockade
Low potency typicals (chlorpromazine, thioridazine) → Less EPS, more anticholinergic effects
High potency typicals (haloperidol, fluphenazine) → More EPS
Medication non-adherence in psychosis → Give decanoate forms every 2-4 weeks
Purple-grey metallic rash over sun-exposed areas and jaundice → Chlorpromazine side effect
Prolonged QTc and pigmentary retinopathy → Thioridazine side effect

## Extrapyramidal Side Effects

Eyes "stuck" looking up or head "stuck" turned to side → Acute Dystonia (<12 hours)
Tx: Acute dystonia → Benztropine or diphenhydramine
Feeling like you "always have to move" → Akathisia (30-90 days)
Tx: Akathisia → Propranolol (1st line) or benzodiazepine
Coarse resting tremor, masked facies, unsteady gait, bradykinesia → Drug-induced Parkinsonism (>6 months)
Tx: Drug-induced parkinsonism → Benztropine/diphenhydramine, amantadine or bromocriptine. NOT L-dopa
Involuntary tongue movements and grimacing after years on antipsychotic → Tardive Dyskinesia (>years)
Tx: Tardive dyskinesia → Stop antipsychotic and switch to atypical or clozapine

## Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome

Within hours of haloperidol injection: elevated CPK, T=103F, rigidity, autonomic instability, delirium → Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome
Tx: NMS → 1st discontinue offending medication. 2nd cooling blankets and dantrolene sodium or bromocriptine (2nd line)
Medications that can cause NMS → Metoclopramide, compazine, droperidol

## Atypical Antipsychotics

Atypical with highest risk for EPS and elevated prolactin → Risperidone (but comes in depot shot)
Weight neutral atypical but prolongs QTc → Ziprasidone
Weight neutral atypical but increases akathisia → Aripiprazole
Atypical most associated with weight gain → Olanzapine (but #1 side effect is sedation)
Atypical that causes orthostasis and cataracts → Quetiapine (alpha-blocking properties)
Tx: Treatment-refractory schizophrenia → Clozapine
Clozapine most common side effects → Sedation, weight gain, increased blood sugar and lipids
Clozapine most dangerous side effects → Agranulocytosis, decreased seizure threshold
Clozapine monitoring → CBC with ANC weekly for 6 months, then every 2 weeks for next 6 months. D/c if WBCs <3000 or ANC <1500

# Anxiety Disorders

## Panic Disorder

SOB, palpitations, chest pain with recurrent attacks and avoidance behavior → Panic Disorder with Agoraphobia
Panic disorder workup → EKG, cardiac enzymes, echocardiogram, TSH or T4, urine drug screen
Tx: Panic disorder → Alprazolam or clonazepam low dose PRN short term, but SSRIs are preferred long-term
C/I: Benzodiazepines → Drug addicts, COPD, restrictive lung disease
Acute benzo withdrawal → Temp 101, convulsions, confusion, hypertension (similar to DTs)
Tx: Benzo withdrawal → Diazepam or chlordiazepoxide + haloperidol if psychotic

## Phobias and Social Anxiety

Deathly fear of flying that inhibits functioning → Specific Phobia
Tx: Specific phobia → CBT with flooding or exposure/extinction. Can give benzos for situational use
Deathly fear of presenting in grand rounds, afraid of being laughed at → Social Phobia
Tx: Social phobia → Propranolol to stop hyperarousal + benzo
Keeps to herself, afraid peers will laugh at her → Avoidant Personality Disorder
Tx: Avoidant PD → CBT

## GAD

Difficulty falling asleep, worrying about failing, worrying boyfriend will leave, symptoms >6 months → Generalized Anxiety Disorder
Tx: GAD → Buspirone (5HT-1a partial agonist), but must give benzos to bridge because buspirone takes >3 weeks to work

## OCD

2-3 hours scrubbing in shower daily, knows it's excessive, can "feel bacteria" → Obsessive Compulsive Disorder
OCD comorbid conditions → High prevalence of vocal-motor tics, 5-7% have full-blown Tourette's
Tx: OCD gold standard → Clomipramine
Tx: OCD first line → SSRIs

## PTSD

Recurrent nightmares of trauma, avoidance, hypervigilance for >1 month → PTSD
Tx: PTSD → Sertraline or paroxetine combined with CBT. Prazosin for nightmares
Same symptoms but only 3 weeks → Acute Stress Reaction
Same symptoms but in response to a bad breakup → Adjustment Disorder

# Personality Disorders

## Cluster A — Odd/Eccentric

Constantly accuses wife of cheating, feuding with neighbors, suspicious of others → Paranoid PD
Tx: Paranoid PD → Low-dose antipsychotics can help paranoid behavior
Never married, no close friends, works as night security guard, prefers solitary hobbies → Schizoid PD
Schizoid vs Avoidant → Schizoid patients don't WANT relationships
Never married, no friends, magical thinking, reads books on communicating with animals → Schizotypal PD
Schizotypal vs Schizoid → Distinguished by magical thinking/interests
Schizotypal vs Schizophrenia → Distinguished by lack of delusions/hallucinations

## Cluster B — Dramatic/Erratic

Court-mandated counseling for beating girlfriend, kicked out of high school for fighting, history of stealing → Antisocial PD
Antisocial PD comorbidity → 2/3 have substance abuse
Unstable relationships, superficial wrist cuts, impulsive spending, impulsive sexual behavior → Borderline PD
Borderline PD defense mechanism → Commonly use splitting
Dresses provocatively, tries to seduce professor, dramatic behavior → Histrionic PD
Histrionic PD comorbidity → Look for substance abuse or eating disorder
Grandiosity, feels special, refuses to deal with anyone but the president → Narcissistic PD
Narcissistic vs hypomania → Can be confused due to grandiosity
Tx: Narcissistic PD → Individual therapy

## Cluster C — Anxious/Fearful

No friends, avoids coworkers because fears ridicule and rejection → Avoidant PD
Tx: Avoidant PD → Beta-blocker or SSRI for social phobia symptoms
Jumps from relationship to relationship, "doesn't do well alone", calls friends 20x/day for input → Dependent PD
Dependent PD comorbidity → Look for comorbid depression and anxiety. SSRI
Spends more time color-coding notes than studying, makes lists 3 times daily, people call her "anal" → Obsessive Compulsive PD
OCPD vs OCD → OCPD actions are "ego-syntonic" (feels right to patient)

# Eating Disorders

## Anorexia Nervosa and Bulimia

No menstrual cycle for 3 months, BMI 17, eroded teeth, Russell sign (calluses on knuckles) → Anorexia with purging features
Anorexia vital signs → Hypotension, bradycardia, hypothermia
Anorexia CBC → Leukopenia
Anorexia chemistry → High HCO3, low Cl, low K, high carotene, high LFTs and amylase
Anorexia TFTs → Normal
Anorexia lipid profile → High cholesterol
Anorexia hormones → High cortisol, low LH/FSH, low estrogen
Anorexia long-term complications → Osteoporosis
#1 cause of death in anorexia → Heart disease. Then suicide
Tx: Anorexia → Admit to maximize nutrition. SSRIs help bulimia, anorexia needs intensive counseling
Refeeding syndrome → Low PO4, low Mg, low Ca and fluid retention

# Sleep Disorders

## Sleep Architecture

Stage 2 sleep EEG → Sleep spindles and K-complexes
Slow wave sleep (Stage 3-4) → Delta waves. Sleepwalking, sleep talking, night terrors occur here
REM sleep → Skeletal muscle paralysis, dreaming

## Sleep Disorder Differential

Trouble falling/staying asleep causing impaired function for >1 month → Insomnia
Tx: Insomnia → Sleep hygiene education first, then benzos (reduce sleep latency, increase SWS and REM). Zolpidem, zaleplon, eszopiclone are GABA-A receptor agonists
Creepy-crawly sensation on legs when falling asleep, better with movement → Restless Leg Syndrome (Dyssomnia NOS)
RLS workup → Rule out iron-deficiency anemia or chronic kidney disease. Neuropathy
Tx: RLS → Ropinirole or pramipexole (dopamine agonists)
Daytime sleepiness and depression in an obese patient with a large neck → Obstructive Sleep Apnea
Dx: OSA → Polysomnogram showing >10 hypopneic/apneic episodes per hour
Tx: OSA → CPAP to reduce pulmonary hypertension
Irresistible attacks of refreshing (REM) sleep, cataplexy with intense emotion, hypnagogic hallucinations → Narcolepsy
Tx: Narcolepsy → Scheduled naps and modafinil

# Substance Use Disorders

## Alcohol

Tonic-clonic seizures, BP 180/110, HR 118, T 100.1 in a known alcoholic → Alcohol withdrawal seizures
Alcohol withdrawal seizures timing → ~12-24 hours since last drink (bimodal peak at 8 and 48 hours)
Delirium tremens timing → ~48-72 hours since last drink
DTs symptoms → Confusion, fluctuations in consciousness, tactile hallucinations (formication)
Blood alcohol level 225mg/mL → Takes ~9 hours to clear (zero-order kinetics, 25mg/hr)
Beta-blockers in alcohol withdrawal → Mask autonomic hyperactivity; follow hyperreflexia to dose benzos instead
Tx: Alcohol withdrawal → Diazepam or chlordiazepoxide (long half-lives: 80 and 120 hours respectively)
Tx: Alcohol withdrawal in Child's class C cirrhotic → Lorazepam, oxazepam, or temazepam (glucuronidated, not hepatically metabolized)
Most specific test for alcohol consumption in past 10 days → Carbohydrate-deficient transferrin. Less specific: elevated GGT and AST more than twice ALT
Confusion, ataxia, ophthalmoplegia → Wernicke Encephalopathy (thiamine deficiency)
Tx: Wernicke → Give thiamine FIRST, then glucose-containing fluids
Wernicke progressing to apathy, anterograde/retrograde amnesia, confabulation → Korsakoff's syndrome (irreversible damage to mammillary bodies). Can see mammillary body atrophy on MRI

## Opioids

Unresponsive, BP 100/60, HR 50, RR 6, track marks → Opioid overdose
Tx: Opioid overdose → Intubate first, then IV or IM naloxone (full mu-opioid antagonist)
Pupils may be dilated in opioid OD → Hypoxia from respiratory depression can cause pupil dilation
Opioid withdrawal symptoms → Joint/muscle pain, photophobia, goosebumps, diarrhea, tachycardia, HTN, GI cramps, dilated pupils, anxiety/depression
Tx: Opioid withdrawal → Clonidine for autonomic symptoms, ibuprofen for muscle cramps, loperamide for diarrhea
Tx: Long-term opioid dependence → Methadone, buprenorphine, or naltrexone

## Stimulants and Hallucinogens

Horizontal nystagmus, dilated pupils, ataxia, acute psychosis → PCP intoxication
Tx: PCP psychosis → Haloperidol for acute psychosis
Injected conjunctiva, sedation, increased appetite → Cannabis intoxication
Suicidal ideation, hypersomnia, depression, anergia → Cocaine/Amphetamine withdrawal
Dilated pupils, seizure, tachycardia, hypertension → Cocaine/Amphetamine intoxication
Cocaine intoxication workup → EKG first, then urine tox screen
Tx: Cocaine-induced seizure → Lorazepam
Tx: Cocaine-induced HTN and tachycardia → Calcium channel blocker. Beta-blockers are CONTRAINDICATED

# Delirium and Dementia

## Delirium

Nursing home patient: sleeps during day, agitated at night, seeing green men, UTI symptoms → Delirium
Delirium workup → UA and culture, glucose, Na, blood cultures, B12, RPR. Check medication list (benadryl, opiates, benzos)
#1 RF for delirium → Age. Underlying dementia is 2nd biggest
Common cause of delirium → Acute substance withdrawal. Look for it on 2nd or 3rd post-op day in alcoholic
Delirium EEG → Diffuse background slowing. Psychosis has normal EEG
Tx: Delirium → Reduce excessive stimuli, calendar and clock for orientation, stop unnecessary meds. Give haloperidol if agitated

## Alzheimer's Disease

Memory loss with aphasia, apraxia, getting lost while driving → Alzheimer's Dementia (most common type)
Alzheimer's MMSE → Prompting does not improve recall
Alzheimer's pathology → Global brain atrophy, beta-amyloid plaques, tau neurofibrillary tangles
Alzheimer's genetics → APP on chromosome 21, ApoE E2
Tx: Alzheimer's → Donepezil, rivastigmine, galantamine (side effect: diarrhea). Memantine

## Other Dementias

Memory loss with sexual disinhibition and apathy → Frontotemporal Dementia (Pick's Disease)
FTD pathology → Lobar atrophy, intraneuronal silver-staining inclusions (Pick bodies)
Tx: FTD → Olanzapine for severe disinhibition
Fluctuating consciousness, visual hallucinations, shuffling gait → Lewy Body Dementia
LBD pathology → Intracytoplasmic alpha-synuclein inclusions in neocortex
Tx: LBD → Acetylcholinesterase inhibitors. NOT L-dopa. Avoid neuroleptics
Sudden, stepwise decrease in memory/cognition → Vascular Dementia
Loss of vibration sense, labile affect, Argyll Robertson pupil (accommodates but doesn't react) → Tertiary Syphilis (Neurosyphilis)
Dx: Neurosyphilis → Positive RPR/VDRL. Spinal tap to look for spirochetes
Tx: Neurosyphilis → IV penicillin. If pen-allergic, must desensitize
Myoclonus, startle response, seizures, history of corneal transplant → Creutzfeldt-Jakob Disease
CJD pathology → Spongiform encephalopathy
CJD EEG → Triphasic bursts
Incontinence, gait disturbance/frequent falls, rapidly developing dementia → Normal Pressure Hydrocephalus
Dx: NPH → CT/MRI shows hydrocephalus, spinal tap shows normal opening pressure
Tx: NPH → Ventriculoperitoneal shunt improves cognitive function in 50-67% of patients

# Factitious Disorders and Malingering

54 y/o RN with 2 months of diarrhea, presented to 4 hospitals, colonoscopy shows colonic pigmentation → Munchausen Syndrome (factitious disorder with laxative abuse)
Munchausen vs factitious → Munchausen is more severe because they actually induce symptoms. Done for primary gain
Concerned mother with 15-month baby having recurrent seizures, requests invasive testing → Munchausen Syndrome by Proxy (form of child abuse)
Munchausen by proxy mortality → 10% of children die before adulthood
Involved in car accident, sues for nerve damage, video shows dancing the night before → Malingering (V-code)
Malingering association → Associated with antisocial personality disorder. Done for secondary gain

## Intellectual Disability

IQ average and standard deviation → Mean 100, SD 15
Mild intellectual disability → IQ 55-70
Moderate intellectual disability → IQ 40-55
Severe intellectual disability → IQ 25-40
Profound intellectual disability → IQ <25

## Genetic Syndromes

Macrocephaly, long face, macroorchidism, IQ ~50 → Fragile X Syndrome
Fragile X inheritance → X-linked dominant, CGG repeats with anticipation
Fragile X complications → Seizures, MVP, aortic dilation, tremors, ataxia, ADHD-like behavior
Fragile X → Most common cause of inherited intellectual disability
Decreased tone, oblique palpebral fissures, simian crease, macroglossia, Brushfield spots → Down Syndrome
Down Syndrome IQ → Mild-moderate intellectual disability
Down Syndrome cardiac → VSD, endocardial cushion defects
Down Syndrome GI → Hirschsprung's, intestinal atresia, imperforate anus, annular pancreas
Down Syndrome endocrine → Hypothyroidism
Down Syndrome MSK → Atlanto-axial instability
Down Syndrome neuro → Increased risk of Alzheimer's by age 30-35 (APP on chromosome 21)
Down Syndrome cancer → 10x increased risk of ALL
Cafe-au-lait spots, seizures, large head, autosomal dominant → Neurofibromatosis
Coarse facies, short stature, cloudy cornea, autosomal recessive → Hurler Syndrome
Broad square face, short stature, self-injurious behavior, deletion on chromosome 17 → Smith-Magenis Syndrome
Hypotonia, hypogonadism, hyperphagia, skin picking, aggression, deletion on paternal chromosome 15 → Prader-Willi Syndrome
Seizures, strabismus, sociable with episodic laughter, deletion on maternal chromosome 15 → Angelman Syndrome
Elfin appearance, friendly, increased empathy and verbal reasoning, deletion on chromosome 7 → Williams Syndrome
ADHD-like symptoms, microcephaly, smooth philtrum → Fetal Alcohol Syndrome (most common cause of intellectual disability overall)
Seizures, chorioretinitis, hearing impairments, periventricular calcifications, petechiae at birth, hepatitis → Congenital CMV infection
Seizures, hearing impairments, cloudy cornea/retinitis, heart defects, low birth weight → Congenital Rubella Syndrome
Abnormal muscle tone, unsteady gait, seizures, intellectual disability → Cerebral Palsy from birth asphyxia
IUGR, hypertonia, distinctive facies, limb malformation, self-injurious behavior, hyperactive → Cornelia de Lange Syndrome
Coloboma, heart defects, choanal atresia, growth retardation, GU anomalies, ear deformity/deafness, chromosome 8 → CHARGE Syndrome
Autism spectrum symptoms, heart disease, palate defects, hypoplastic thymus, hypocalcemia, chromosome 22 deletion → DiGeorge Syndrome
Vomiting, seizures, lethargy, coma, acidosis with stress/illness → Maple Syrup Urine Disease

## Pervasive Developmental Disorders

Exclusively in girls, normal development 6-8 months then regression, hand-wringing, loss of speech and hand use → Rett Syndrome (X-linked dominant, MECP2 deletion)
Normal development until age 2 then major loss of verbal/social skills with autistic behavior → Childhood Disintegrative Disorder
Lack of mother-child eye contact, language delay/repetitive language, preoccupation with parts of toys, before age 3 → Autism
Problems with social skills (usually recognized in preschool) with preserved verbal ability → Asperger Syndrome

## ADHD

7 y/o told several times to complete chores, can't focus on homework, loses belongings → Normal age-appropriate behavior
ADHD next best step → Get information from child's school/teacher
ADHD risk factors → 77% heritability, LBW, tobacco/ETOH exposure
ADHD comorbid conditions → ODD/CD in 30-50%

## ADHD Medications

| Medication | MOA | Side Effects |
| Methylphenidate (Concerta, Ritalin) | Blocks DA reuptake | Nausea, decreased appetite, increased HR and BP, stunted growth |
| Amphetamine (Adderall) | Blocks DA/NE reuptake and stimulates release | Similar to methylphenidate |
| Atomoxetine (Strattera) | NE reuptake inhibitor (non-stimulant) | Dry mouth, insomnia, decreased appetite |
| Clonidine, Guanfacine | Alpha-2 agonists, reduce peripheral SNS | Decreased BP, causes sedation |

## Conduct Disorder and ODD

14 y/o steals, sets fires, truancy for 5 years, assaulted schoolmate → Conduct Disorder (need symptoms for 6 months)
CD comorbidity → Substance abuse. May progress to antisocial personality disorder
14 y/o argumentative, disrespectful to teachers, defies rules, deliberately annoys grandmother → Oppositional Defiant Disorder (need symptoms for 12 months)
ODD vs CD → ODD stops just short of breaking the law or physically harming others

## Tourette Syndrome

Loud grunting and hand movements, at least once daily for 1 year without tic-free period >3 months → Tourette Syndrome
Tourette comorbidity → Look for OCD compulsions
Tx: Tourette first line → Clonidine (relatively benign side effect profile)
Tx: Tourette most effective → Haloperidol or pimozide (dopamine-receptor antagonists)

## Other Childhood Disorders

7 y/o with frequent abdominal pain and missed school, never on weekends or summer → Separation Anxiety Disorder
6 y/o adopted child, no bond with adoptive parents, inhibited and hypervigilant → Reactive Attachment Disorder
18 month old regurgitating and re-chewing food, previously eating normally → Rumination Disorder. Check lead levels
6 y/o soiling clothes every 2 weeks → Encopresis
Encopresis workup → Check for fecal retention
Tx: Encopresis → Behavioral modification with positive reinforcement only
6 y/o wetting clothes daily → Enuresis
Enuresis workup → UA and urine culture
Tx: Enuresis → Alarm and pad for 6 weeks. TCAs reduce bedwetting but relapse common. DDAVP has same problem + side effects (headaches, nausea, hyponatremia)

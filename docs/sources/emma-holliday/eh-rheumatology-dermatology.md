## Arthritis — Chronic

- Knee pain, DIP involvement, no swelling or warmth, worse @ the end of the day, crepitus -> OA
- DIP joint involvement, rash w/ silvery scale on elbows and knees, pitting nails and swollen fingers -> Psoriatic Arthritis
- PIP and wrists bilaterally, worse in the AM, low grade fever -> RA
- Symmetric, bilateral arthritis, malar rash, oral ulcers, proteinuria, thrombocytopenia. Arthritis is not erosive or have lasting sequelae -> SLE

## Arthritis — Acute Swollen Joint

- 1st best test? -> Tap it!
- WBCs >50K -> Septic arthritis
- 30 yr old who "travels a lot for work" -> Gonococcal. Cx may be negative. Look also for tenosynovitis and arm pustules. Tx w/ ceftriaxone.
- 70 yr old nun -> Staph aureus. Tx w/ nafcillin or vanco.
- WBCs 5-50K -> Inflammatory. If no crystals, think RA, ank spon, SLE, Reiter's
- Needle shaped, negatively birefringent crystals -> Gout. Monosodium Urate. Acute TX? Indomethacin + colchicine (steroids if kidneys suck). Chronic TX? Probenecid if undersecreter. Allopurinol if overproducer.
- Rhomboid shaped, positively birefringent crystals -> Pseudogout. Calcium pyrophosphate.
- WBCs 200-5K -> OA, hypertrophic osteoarthropathy, trauma
- WBCs <200 -> Normal

## Antibodies to Know

- If negative, rules out SLE? -> ANA (peripheral/rim staining). Anti-dsDNA or Anti-Smith
- Most sensitive for SLE? -> ANA
- Drug induced lupus? (hydralazine) -> Anti-histone
- Sjogren's Syndrome? -> Anti-Ro (SSA) or Anti-La (SSB)
- CREST Syndrome? -> Anti-centromere
- Systemic Sclerosis? -> Anti-Scl-70, Anti-topoisomerase
- Mixed connective tissue disease? -> Anti-RNP
- 2 tests for RA? -> RF (against Fc of IgG) and Anti-CCP (cyclic citrullinated peptide)

## Skin Signs of Systemic Diseases

- Sign of Leser-Trelat -> Sudden eruption of multiple seborrheic keratoses. Associated with internal malignancy.
- Dermatomyositis -> Heliotrope rash (violaceous eyelid), Gottron papules. Associated with underlying malignancy.
- Dermatitis Herpetiformis -> Intensely pruritic vesicles on extensor surfaces. Associated with Celiac disease.
- Erythema Multiforme -> Target lesions. Associated with HSV, Mycoplasma, drugs.
- Acanthosis Nigricans -> Velvety hyperpigmentation in skin folds. Associated with insulin resistance, DM, GI malignancy.
- Seborrheic Dermatitis -> Greasy, scaly patches. Associated with Parkinson's, HIV.

## Skin Signs of Systemic Diseases Part 2

- Porphyria Cutanea Tarda -> Photosensitive blistering. Associated with Hep C, ETOH, iron overload.
- Erythema Nodosum -> Tender red nodules on anterior shins. Associated with sarcoidosis, IBD, strep, TB, OCPs.
- Necrolytic Migratory Erythema -> Erythematous scaling rash. Associated with glucagonoma.
- Pemphigus Vulgaris -> Flaccid bullae, + Nikolsky sign. IgG against desmoglein (intercellular).
- Bullous Pemphigoid -> Tense bullae, negative Nikolsky sign. IgG against hemidesmosomes (basement membrane).
- Behcet's Syndrome -> Oral and genital ulcers, uveitis, pathergy.

## Other Skin Randoms

- Acrodermatitis enteropathica -> Zinc deficiency. Perioral and acral dermatitis.
- Actinic Keratosis -> Precursor to squamous cell carcinoma. Tx w/ 5-FU or excision.
- Dermatitis of Pellagra -> Niacin deficiency. Diarrhea, Dermatitis, Dementia.
- Tinea Capitis -> Fungal scalp infection. Tx w/ oral griseofulvin or terbinafine.
- Kaposi Sarcoma -> HHV-8. Purple/red vascular lesions. Associated with AIDS.
- Bacillary Angiomatosis -> Bartonella henselae. Looks like Kaposi but responds to ABX.

## Skin Cancer

- Basal Cell Carcinoma -> Pearly, translucent papule with telangiectasias. Shave or punch bx then surgical removal (Mohs)
- Squamous Cell Carcinoma -> AK is precursor lesion (tx w/ 5FU or excision) or keratoacanthoma. Excisional bx at edge of lesion, then wide local excision. Can use rads for tough locations.
- Melanoma -> Superficial spreading (best prog, most common), Nodular (poor prog), Acrolentiginous (palms, soles, mucous membranes in darker complected races), Lentigo Maligna (head and neck, good prog). Need full thickness biopsy b/c depth is #1 prognostic factor. Tx w/ excision: 1cm margin if <1mm thick, 2cm margin if 1-4mm thick, 3cm margin if >4mm. High dose IFN or IL2 may help.

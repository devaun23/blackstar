# ACG Acute Pancreatitis Guidelines (2024)

## Source Identity

- **Full title:** American College of Gastroenterology Guidelines: Management of Acute Pancreatitis
- **Organization:** American College of Gastroenterology (ACG)
- **Publication year:** 2024
- **Journal:** American Journal of Gastroenterology
- **URL:** https://journals.lww.com/ajg/fulltext/2024/01000/american_college_of_gastroenterology_guidelines.14.aspx
- **Access method:** Journal subscription / institutional access
- **Source pack ID:** PACK.ACG.AP.2024

## Scope

Diagnosis, severity classification, and management of acute pancreatitis in adults.

## Key Recommendations Extracted

### Diagnosis
- Diagnosis requires ≥2 of 3 criteria: (1) characteristic abdominal pain, (2) serum lipase or amylase >3× upper limit of normal, (3) characteristic findings on cross-sectional imaging
- Lipase preferred over amylase (higher sensitivity and specificity)
- CT not required for diagnosis if clinical + biochemical criteria met; reserve for diagnostic uncertainty or complications

### Severity Classification (Revised Atlanta)
- **Mild:** No organ failure, no local/systemic complications
- **Moderately severe:** Transient organ failure (<48h) OR local/systemic complications without persistent organ failure
- **Severe:** Persistent organ failure (>48h)

### Fluid Resuscitation
- Goal-directed fluid resuscitation with lactated Ringer's (LR) preferred over normal saline
- Initial rate: 1.5 mL/kg/hr, adjusted to clinical response
- Aggressive fluid resuscitation (>250-500 mL/hr bolus) is NOT recommended — associated with worse outcomes
- Monitor BUN at 12-24h as marker of adequate resuscitation (goal: decreasing BUN)
- Hematocrit and BUN trends guide fluid titration

### Pain Management
- Multimodal analgesia; opioids are safe and effective
- NSAIDs as adjunct when not contraindicated
- No evidence that opioids worsen pancreatitis or cause sphincter of Oddi spasm at therapeutic doses

### Nutrition
- Early oral feeding (within 24h) as tolerated — low-fat solid diet preferred
- Enteral nutrition (nasojejunal or nasogastric) for patients who cannot tolerate oral intake
- Enteral >> parenteral nutrition (reduces infection, mortality, organ failure)
- TPN only if enteral route not feasible

### ERCP
- Urgent ERCP (<24h) indicated for acute cholangitis complicating gallstone pancreatitis
- ERCP NOT indicated for predicted mild gallstone pancreatitis without cholangitis
- ERCP within 72h for persistent biliary obstruction without cholangitis

### Cholecystectomy
- Same-admission cholecystectomy for mild gallstone pancreatitis (reduces recurrence)
- Delay cholecystectomy for severe/necrotizing pancreatitis until inflammation resolves

### Complications
- Infected pancreatic necrosis: step-up approach (antibiotics → percutaneous drainage → minimally invasive necrosectomy)
- Sterile necrosis does NOT require antibiotics or intervention
- Prophylactic antibiotics NOT recommended

### Red Flags
- Organ failure >48h → severe pancreatitis → ICU
- SIRS criteria at admission predict severity
- Rising BUN despite fluids → reassess volume status and consider complications

## Normalization Notes

- Fluid rates reflect 2024 guideline shift away from aggressive resuscitation
- Revised Atlanta classification is the standard severity framework
- Lipase >3× ULN threshold is the most board-relevant diagnostic criterion

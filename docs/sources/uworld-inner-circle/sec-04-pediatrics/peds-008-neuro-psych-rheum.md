# NERVOUS SYSTEM

## CONGENITAL
- Intellectual Disability: Down (upslanted palpebral fissures), Fragile X (CGG repeats, MCC inherited, long face, macroorchidism), FAS (small palpebral fissures, thin lip, smooth philtrum)

## PREMATURITY
1. IVH: Acute neuro changes, bulging fontanelle, increasing head circumference; Dx: Cranial US
2. SIDS: RF: Maternal substance abuse, <20yo, Prematurity, Prone sleeping, Bed sharing

## NORMAL DEVELOPMENT
1. Breath-Holding Spells: Brief apnea with emotional trigger → LOC
   - Cyanotic (crying → cyanosis), Pallid (minor injury → pallor)
   - Association: IDA; Management: Treat IDA, Reassurance, Resolves by 5yo
2. Head Circumference: Benign Familial Microcephaly (<2nd %ile with FH) or Macrocephaly (>98th with FH) → Reassurance

## SUPERIOR BRAIN
- Vitamin K deficiency: Reduced coagulation → intracranial hemorrhage → Obstructive hydrocephalus → Bulging fontanelle

## INFERIOR BRAIN
1. Arnold Chiari I (MC): Cerebellar tonsils through foramen magnum; Asymptomatic → Symptomatic with Valsalva
   - Complication: Syringomyelia (cervical)
2. Arnold Chiari II: Cerebellar tonsils + Vermis + Medulla + Myelomeningocele

## HEADACHE
1. Medulloblastoma: MC malignant brain tumor in children; Cerebellum → ventricular occlusion → ICP
2. Migraine: Equal until puberty then F>M; Triggers: Sleep deprivation, stress, menses
   - Bilateral (younger)/Unilateral pulsatile + Photo/phonophobia + N/V + Aura
   - IMAGING NOT REQUIRED unless ataxia/papilledema/waking from sleep
   - Tx: NSAIDs, Triptans, Antiemetics, Ergots
3. IIH: Obese F; Drugs: Retinoids, Tetracyclines, Growth Hormone
   - CF: HA, Vision loss, Pulsatile tinnitus, Diplopia (CN6), Papilledema
   - Dx: LP opening pressure >250; Tx: Weight loss, Acetazolamide

## EXTREMITIES
- Acute Hemiplegia: Ischemic stroke (prothrombotic, SCD), ICH (trauma, hemophilia), Todd paralysis (post-seizure, resolves 36hrs), Hemiplegic migraine

### Friedrich Ataxia: AR, GAA repeats → Frataxin; Dysarthria, Loss DTR, Gait ataxia, Cardiomyopathy, DM
### Rett Syndrome: MECP2 mutation, F>M; Normal development → regression 6-18mo; Microcephaly, Seizures
### Progressive Infantile SMA: AR, SMA mutation; Bilateral symmetric weakness; Progresses over months

## NEUROCUTANEOUS
1. NF1: AD; Cafe-au-lait, Freckles, Neurofibromas, Lisch nodules, Optic glioma
2. Tuberous Sclerosis: Angiofibromas, Ash-leaf spots, Shagreen patches, Renal angiomyolipoma

# PSYCHIATRIC/BEHAVIORAL

## Secondary Acute Onset Psychosis Causes
1. CNS: Trauma, Space-occupying lesion, Infection, Stroke, Epilepsy
2. Metabolic: Acute Intermittent Porphyria, Hypoglycemia, Na/Ca/Mg disturbance, Renal/liver failure
3. Wilson Disease (AR): ATP7B mutation → hepatic copper accumulation
   - CF: Hepatic (liver failure, cirrhosis), Neuro (Parkinsonism, Dysarthria), Psych (Depression, Psychosis)
   - Dx: Low Ceruloplasmin, High Urinary Cu, Kayser-Fleischer rings; Tx: D-penicillamine, Zinc
4. SLE: Psychosis + Arthralgia + Thrombocytopenia + Hematuria/Proteinuria
5. Substance Abuse: Hallucinogens, Marijuana, Sympathomimetics, Alcohol withdrawal
6. Medications: Anticholinergics, Alpha-adrenergics, Serotonin syndrome, Corticosteroids, INH

## SUBSTANCE ABUSE
- Inhalants: Perioral dermatitis, Cerebellar dysfunction with chronic use
- Cocaine: Weight loss, Behavioral changes, Nasal mucosal erythema
- FAS: Microcephaly, Thin lip, Short palpebral fissures, Smooth philtrum
- Heroin/Methadone: IUGR, SIDS, Neonatal Abstinence Syndrome (high-pitched cry, autonomic dysfunction)

## DEVELOPMENT DISORDERS
1. Autism: Deficits in social communication + Restricted/repetitive behaviors; Tx: Early multimodal intervention
2. Global Developmental Delay: <5yo; Social responsiveness and effort to communicate (vs ASD)
3. Intellectual Disability: Deficits in intellectual and adaptive functioning
4. Specific Learning Disorder: Academic difficulties → anxiety, school refusal; Dyslexia

## BEHAVIORAL DISORDERS
1. ADHD: Inattention + Impulsivity + Hyperactivity in >2 settings, >6mo, before 12yo
   - Tx: Stimulants (Methylphenidate), Atomoxetine (NE reuptake inhibitor), Alpha-2 agonists, Bupropion
2. Generalized Anxiety: >6mo; Restlessness, Fatigue, Difficulty concentrating, Sleep disturbance; Tx: CBT, SSRI
3. Selective Mutism: Intact communication but don't speak in specific settings (school)
4. OCD: Obsessions + Compulsions >1hr/day; Tx: SSRI + CBT (exposure prevention)
5. Trichotillomania: Recurrent hair pulling; Irregular patches, variable shaft lengths; Tx: CBT
6. Tourette: Multiple motor + >1 vocal tics, >1yr, <18yo; Tx: Habit reversal, Antidopaminergic, Alpha-2 agonists
7. Conduct Disorder: Violates societal norms/rights of others
8. ODD: Deliberately disobedient/hostile toward authority

## MENTAL HEALTH
- Magical thinking (2-7yo), Imaginary friend (3-6yo): Normal
- PTSD: Distressing dreams, Reenactment in play, Emotional dysregulation
- Reactive Attachment Disorder: Interrupted bonding → Poor social responsiveness, Limited affect
- Disinhibited Social Engagement: Overfamiliarity even with strangers (vs RAD withdrawal)

## Key Conditions
1. Acute Rheumatic Fever (5-15yo): Major: Joints, Carditis, Nodules, Erythema marginatum, Chorea; Dx: 2 Major + 1 Minor
2. JIA: Fever + Arthritis + Salmon rash + LAD; Morning stiffness; Tx: NSAIDs, Methotrexate
3. Acute Osteomyelitis: S. aureus; Pain, difficulty weight bearing; Increased uptake on bone scan
4. Septic Arthritis: Infants: Fussiness, Hip flexed/abducted/ER; Lab: Elevated WBC/ESR/CRP; Tx: Aspiration + Abx
5. Transient Synovitis: MCC atraumatic hip pain 3-8yr; Self-limiting; Bilateral effusions → Transient; Unilateral → Septic

## BONE/JOINT CONDITIONS
1. DDH: RF: Breech, FH; (+) Ortolani; <4mo → US; >4mo → Xray; Tx: Pavlik harness
2. Legg-Calve-Perthes: Osteonecrosis of femoral head; Antalgic gait; Quadriceps atrophy; Tx: Avoid weight bearing
3. Osgood-Schlatter: Tibial tubercle osteochondritis; Anterior knee pain; Pain with squatting/extension
4. SCFE: Obese pre-teen/teen; Decreased abduction and IR; Hip ER during passive flexion; Bilateral hip Xray
   - RF: Earlier onset with Hypothyroidism; Tx: Non-weight bearing, Surgical pinning
5. Sickle Cell Crisis: Presents at 6mo when defective HbA production increases
6. Toddler's Fractures (<3yo): Twisting motion minor fall; Hairline; Tx: Immobilization
7. Genu Varum (Birth-2yo): Symmetric bowing → Reassurance
8. Growing Pains (2-12yo): Bilateral, primarily at night → Observation

## TUMORS
- Ewing Sarcoma: Osteolytic, onion skin/moth-eaten appearance
- Osteosarcoma (13-16yo): Sunburst periosteal reaction; Elevated ALP/LDH; Association: RB1, TP53
- Langerhans Cell Histiocytosis: Lytic lesions; Central DI; Skin lesions; Tx: Prednisone + Vinblastine

## NECK/BACK
1. Congenital Torticollis: SCM dysfunction; Ipsilateral head tilt + Contralateral chin deviation; Tx: Tummy time, PT
2. Atlantoaxial Instability: DOWN SYNDROME; UMN findings (Babinski, hyperreflexia)
3. Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis: >10yo; Asymmetric prominence on forward bending; Spine Xray

## FOOT
1. Calcaneal Apophysitis (Sever): Heel pain in active kids; Tx: NSAIDs, activity limitation
2. Congenital Clubfoot: Rigid medial/upward deviation; Tx: Serial casting → Surgery
3. Metatarsus Adductus: Flexible → Reassurance

## SOCIAL SCIENCES
- Sexual Abuse: Imitation of sexual acts, Coercive behaviors, Excessive preoccupation
- Normal: Toddler touching genital region without above features

# HEME ONC

## ANEMIA
- MCV classification; RI (Reticulocyte Index): >2% destruction, <2% production issue

### Microcytic
1. Anemia of Chronic Disease: Increased inflammatory cytokines → decreased iron availability; High Ferritin, Low TIBC
2. Iron-Deficiency
   - Causes: Prematurity, Lead exposure, Exclusive breastfeeding >6mo, Cow milk >24oz/day, Chronic GI bleed
   - Labs: Low Fe/Ferritin/Transferrin sat, High TIBC, High RDW; Reactive thrombocytosis
   - Screening: Hb at age 1 (Hb<11); Tx: Empiric Fe supplementation
3. Thalassemia: Normal/Low RDW, Target cells, Basophilic stippling
   - Alpha: Normal Hb electrophoresis → Genetic testing
   - Beta-major: Absent HbA, Elevated HbA2/HbF; Lifelong transfusions + chelation
   - Hint: Unchanged iron studies despite months of Fe therapy → thalassemia trait

### Normocytic
1. Anemia of Prematurity: Impaired EPO production
2. Aplastic Anemia: Low RI, Pancytopenia; BM: Hypocellular with fat; Triggers: Autoimmune, Drugs, Viral hepatitis, HIV
3. Sickle Cell Trait: Mild hemolysis, MC complication: Hematuria (renal medulla sickling)
4. Sickle Cell Disease: AR, Glu→Val mutation; Howell-Jolly bodies
5. G6PD: X-linked; Heinz bodies, Bite cells
6. Hereditary Spherocytosis: AD; Spherocytes; FH similar symptoms
7. PNH: Fatigue, dark urine, dyspnea
8. HUS/TTP: E. coli O157:H7 prodrome → Anemia + Thrombocytopenia + AKI; Schistocytes

### Macrocytic
1. Megaloblastic: B12 (neuro symptoms worsen with folate alone), Folate (Phenytoin, MTX, TMP, Chronic hemolysis)
2. Fanconi Anemia: Defective DNA repair, Pancytopenia, Hypoplastic thumbs; Tx: HSCT
3. Diamond-Blackfan: Pure red cell aplasia, Triphalangeal thumbs; Tx: CST or Transfusion

## PANCYTOPENIA
- 1st step: BM evaluation
- Poor reticulocyte response → BM failure (aplastic) or infiltrative disease
- Impaired Production: Fanconi, Aplastic Anemia, PNH, SLE, Nutritional (B12/Folate)
- Infiltration: Acute leukemia (blast cells), Lymphoma, Myelofibrosis
- Enhanced Destruction: DIC, TTP (intravascular), Hypersplenism (extravascular)

## PLATELET DISEASE
1. ITP: IgG-coated platelets, Isolated thrombocytopenia; Tx: IV Ig
2. TTP: ADAMTS13 deficiency; Pentad: Anemia, Thrombocytopenia, Renal, Neuro, Fever; Normal PT/PTT; Tx: Plasma exchange
3. HIT: Heparin >5 days + Platelet drop >50% + Paradoxical thrombosis
4. Pseudothrombocytopenia: EDTA-induced platelet clumping; Correct with heparin or sodium citrate tube

## COAGULATION
1. vW Disease: AD, Impaired platelet-endothelial binding; Elevated PTT, Normal PT/Platelets
   - Mucosal bleeding: nose, mouth, menorrhagia; May cause deep bleed via F8 deficiency
2. Hemophilia A (F8 deficiency): X-linked; Prolonged PTT; Hemarthrosis
   - Baby: Excessive bleeding post-circumcision; Child: Joint pain after minor trauma
   - Dx: Prolonged PTT → Rule out vWD → Mixing study (normalizes = factor deficiency)
   - F8 Inhibitor: Tx with Recombinant F7 (bypasses intrinsic pathway)
   - Tx: Mild: Desmopressin; Mod-severe: F8 concentrate
3. Hemophilia B (F9): Similar to A
4. Vitamin K Deficiency
5. Antiphospholipid Antibody Syndrome: High PTT; LA prevents phospholipid-induced coagulation in assay

## LEUKEMIAS

### ALL (MC childhood cancer)
1. CF: Nontender LAD, Hepatosplenomegaly, Testicular enlargement, Mediastinal mass (T-cell)
2. Labs: Pallor (anemia), Bruising (thrombocytopenia), Initial leukocytosis, Bone pain (refusal to walk)
3. Dx: BM >20% blasts; LP for CNS involvement

### AML → MC acute adult leukemia; APML: DIC + Atypical promyelocytes
### CML vs Leukemoid: CML has Absolute basophilia + Low L-ALP; Leukemoid has High L-ALP
### CLL → Warm AIHA (IgG autoantibodies against rbc)

## LYMPHOMAS

### Hodgkin
1. Bimodal 15-35 and >60; Association: EBV
2. Painless LAD (supraclavicular suspicious), Mediastinal mass, B symptoms, Pruritis
3. Labs: Eosinophilia, Lymphopenia, Elevated LDH; Dx: LN biopsy → Reed-Sternberg cells
4. COD: Radiation-induced solid organ malignancy; Nonmalignant: CVD

### Non-Hodgkin
- Follicular (MC NHL): Painless, peripheral LAD
- DLBCL: Locally invasive
- Burkitt (t8;14): African/EBV, "Starry sky"
- Chronic autoimmune disease (SLE, Sjogren) → Increased NHL risk
- HIV-related: EBV-related NHL

## TUMORS
1. Neuroblastoma (<2yo): Adrenal/sympathetic chain; Raccoon eyes, Opsoclonus-myoclonus; Elevated catecholamine metabolites; N-myc
2. Wilms Tumor (2-5yo): MC renal malignancy; Unilateral; HTN + Hematuria + Mass
   - WAGR: Wilms + Aniridia + GU abnormality + Intellectual disability (11p deletion)
   - Screening: Abdominal US every 3mo starting infancy
3. Tumor Lysis Syndrome: High tumor burden → Hyperuricemia + Hyperphosphatemia + Hyperkalemia
   - Tx: IV hydration, Rasburicase/Allopurinol, Electrolyte correction

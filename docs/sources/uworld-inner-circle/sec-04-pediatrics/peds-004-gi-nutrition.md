# GI & NUTRITION

## Food Allergies
- Atopic Triad: Atopic dermatitis, Asthma, Food/environmental allergies
- Management: Exclusively breastfeed for 6mo

## Neonatal Indirect Hyperbilirubinemia
- Kernicterus: unconjugated bilirubin deposition in basal ganglia, pons, cerebellum → hearing loss
- Increased Production (Coombs+): Rh isoimmunization, ABO incompatibility
- Increased Production (Coombs-): Spherocytosis, G6PD, Cephalohematoma, Polycythemia
- JAUNDICE <24HRS
- Decreased Clearance: Physiologic jaundice (days 2-4), Biliary atresia, Gilbert, Crigler-Najjar
- Increased Enterohepatic: Lactation failure jaundice, Breastmilk jaundice

## Emesis
### Non-Bilious Non-Projectile
- Normal post-feeding; GER: Immature LES, spit-up with normal weight gain → upright feeding

### Projectile Bilious (distal to ligament of Treitz)
1. Malrotation: Failure of rotation → narrow mesenteric base; Xray: Double bubble + Normal gas
   - Complication: Volvulus → Small bowel obstruction + Shock
2. Duodenal Atresia: Bilious vomiting in first 2 days; Polyhydramnios; Association: Down syndrome
   - Xray: Double bubble + NO distal gas
3. Annular Pancreas: Failure of apoptosis; Association: Down syndrome
4. Intestinal Atresia: In-utero vascular accident (cocaine); Xray: Multiple air-fluid levels
5. NEC: Gas-producing bacteria → bowel wall inflammation/necrosis
   - RF: Preemie, Congenital heart disease; Xray: Pneumatosis intestinalis, Pneumoperitoneum

### Projectile Non-Bilious
1. TEF (Type C): Blind esophagus + Fistula; Gurgling; Association: VACTERL, CHARGE
2. Pyloric Stenosis (wks 2-8): Olive-shaped mass; Labs: Hypochloremic Hypokalemic Met Alkalosis
   - Tx: Fix electrolytes FIRST → Pyloromyotomy

## GI BLEEDING
1. NEC: Feeding intolerance + bilious emesis + abdominal distention; Tx: NPO, IVF, TPN, Abx
2. Intussusception: 6-36mo, episodic pain, KNEE-CHEST position, RUQ sausage mass, currant jelly stool
   - Dx: Air-Enema (best test); Tx: Air-Enema or Surgery; 25% have Meckel's as lead point
3. Meckel's Diverticulum: Painless hematochezia + IDA; <2yo, Rule of 2s
   - Dx: Technetium-99 scan; Tx: Surgical resection
4. Anal Fissures: Visual inspection → Reassurance (will heal spontaneously)

## Failure to Pass Meconium (no BM in 48hrs)
1. Imperforate Anus: Never check rectal temp first; VACTERL association
2. Meconium Ileus = Cystic Fibrosis: Dilated loops; Tx: Water-enema; Confirm: Sweat chloride
3. Hirschsprung's: Lack of inhibitory neurons in distal colon
   - 90%: Explosive with DRE; 10%: Overflow incontinence
   - Dx: Barium enema + Biopsy (lack of plexus); Tx: Resect; Association: Down syndrome

## Functional Constipation >2yo
- Voluntary Holding: Overflow incontinence, Encopresis; Tx: Bowel regimen + Behavior
- Allergic Proctocolitis (<6mo): Loose stools with mucus/blood → Hydrolyzed formula
- Solid food introduction: Pellet-like stools → Prune/Apple/Pear puree

## Diarrhea
1. C. Jejuni: Pseudoappendicitis (infectious ileocecitis)
2. Yersinia: Pseudoappendicitis
3. Rotavirus, Norovirus, Adenovirus
4. Salmonella: Avoid antibiotics (carrier state)
5. Lactose Intolerance: Primary (persistent) vs Secondary (transient post-infection); Dx: Breath hydrogen test

## Dehydration
- Faster in children: Immature kidneys, Higher BMI, Cannot indicate thirst
- Types: Isotonic (equal loss), Hypotonic (Na loss > water, pyloric stenosis), Hypertonic (water > Na, correct in 48hrs)
- Mild 5%: Alert, normal fontanelle, Tx: ORT + BRAT
- Moderate 5-10%: Irritable, depressed fontanelle, Tachycardia, cap refill 2-3s
- Severe >10%: Lethargic, Hypotension, Tx: Na HCO3 for acidosis
- Maintenance: 3.5-10kg → 100mL/kg; 11-20kg → 1000 + 50mL/kg over 10; >20kg → 1500 + 20mL/kg over 20

## Malabsorption
1. Celiac Disease: Growth delay, IDA; Association: T1DM, Autoimmune thyroiditis, Down syndrome
   - Dx: Anti-tTG, Anti-endomysial; Biopsy: Villous blunting; Tx: Gluten-free diet
2. Cystic Fibrosis: FTT + Recurrent sinopulmonary; CFTR mutation; Sweat chloride test
   - MC organism kids: S. aureus; Adults: Pseudomonas
   - Complications: Nasal polyps, Pancreatic insufficiency (Vit ADEK), Male infertility, Cor pulmonale
3. Congenital Hypothyroidism: After maternal thyroxine wanes → FTT

## Nutritional Deficiency
- Breastfed infants: Cholecalciferol supplementation, Iron supplementation
- IDA: #1 nutritional deficiency; RF: Preemie, exclusive breastfeeding >6mo, cow milk <12mo
- Vegan: Risk B12 and Vit D/Ca/Iron/Zinc deficiency

## Vitamin Deficiency
- Vit A: Bitot spots, Night blindness, Keratomalacia; Excess: IIH, Hepatic toxicity
- Vit C (Scurvy): Follicular hyperkeratosis, Perifollicular hemorrhage, Poor wound healing
- Vit D Toxicity: Hypercalcemia, Shortened QT

## Mineral Deficiency
- Chromium: Impaired glucose control
- Copper: Hypochromic microcytic anemia, Neurological dysfunction; Cause: Bariatric surgery, Excessive Zinc
- Zinc: Hypogonadism, Impaired taste/wound healing, Alopecia

## Esophagus
- Eosinophilic Esophagitis: Dysphagia, food impaction + atopy; No PPI response; >15 eosinophils/HPF

## Hernia
- Congenital Umbilical: Soft reducible mass; Observe, Surgery at 5yo if large
- Beckwith-Wiedemann: Hyperinsulinemic hypoglycemia, Macroglossia, Visceromegaly, Omphalocele

## Biliary
1. Biliary Atresia: 2-8wks, Pale stools, Direct hyperbilirubinemia; Dx: RUQ US → Cholangiography; Tx: Kasai
2. Biliary Cyst: Complication: Pancreatitis, Cholangitis

## Liver
- Reye Syndrome: ASA in children during viral infection → Cerebral edema + Acute liver failure
  - Labs: High AST/ALT/Ammonia, High PT/INR, Low Glucose; Tx: Supportive

## Spleen
- Atraumatic Splenic Rupture: RF: EBV infection; Avoid sports 3-4 weeks
- Asplenia: Increased sepsis risk (encapsulated organisms), Howell-Jolly bodies

## HY Imaging
1. Air in bowel wall and portal veins → NEC
2. Air in stomach/duodenum but no distal air → Duodenal Atresia
3. Narrow rectosigmoid with dilation → Hirschsprung
4. Thickened pylorus → Pyloric Stenosis
5. Hemorrhage into lateral ventricles → IVH

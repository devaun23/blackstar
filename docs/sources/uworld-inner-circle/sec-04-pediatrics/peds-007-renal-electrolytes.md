# RENAL, URINARY SYSTEMS & ELECTROLYTES

## NEONATE
1. Creatinine: Freely crosses placenta, may be elevated from maternal renal failure; Normalizes in 2wks
2. Neonatal AKI: Oliguria <1 void/day of life; Eval: Renal and bladder US

## ELECTROLYTES
- Hyperkalemia: Tx: Calcium Gluconate/Chloride first; Hemodialysis for severe
- Acute Hyponatremia with seizures: RF: Hypotonic fluid (D5W); Tx: Return Na to normal

## ACID BASE

### Metabolic Acidosis — Normal AG (<12) = NAGMA (Loss of Bicarbonate)
1. Severe Diarrhea
2. Renal Tubular Acidosis
   - Type 1 (Distal): Hypokalemia, Urine pH >5.5 (ABNORMALLY HIGH IN ACIDOSIS); Causes: Genetic, Sjogren, RA; Tx: NaHCO3
   - Type 2 (Proximal): Hypokalemia, Urine pH <5.5; Causes: Fanconi Syndrome, Acetazolamide
   - Type 4: Hyperkalemia, Urine pH <5.5; Causes: Obstructive uropathy, CAH, Poorly controlled DM
3. Excess Saline; Intestinal/Pancreatic fistula

### Metabolic Acidosis — Elevated AG (>12) = Unmeasured Acid
- MUDPILES: Methanol (blindness), Uremia, DKA, Propylene glycol, INH/Iron, Lactic acidosis, Ethylene glycol (oxalate crystals → ARF), Salicylates (tinnitus, early resp alkalosis → late met acidosis, NEAR-NORMAL pH)
- Winter's Formula: 1.5(HCO3) + 8 ± 2

### Metabolic Alkalosis
- UCl <10 (Volume Responsive): Diuretics, Dehydration, Emesis/NG suction → Contraction alkalosis
  - Emesis: HCl/KCl loss → Hypokalemia/Hypochloremia → RAAS activation → maintains alkalosis
  - Tx: IV normal saline + K supplementation
- UCl >10 + HTN: Hyperaldosteronism, Renal artery stenosis
- UCl >10: Bartter, Gitelman

## GLOMERULAR DISEASE

### Nephrotic Syndrome
- Fatty casts; Complications: Hypercoagulability (AT3 loss, Renal vein thrombosis MC), Hyperlipidemia, HTN, Infection, Iron-resistant microcytic anemia, Vit D deficiency, SBP
1. Minimal Change Disease (MCC in children): Dysfunctional TC → podocyte injury → proteinuria
   - Excellent steroid response; EM: Foot process effacement
2. Membranous GN: Primary idiopathic or Secondary (HBV, HCV, Lupus, Drugs)
3. FSGS: Blacks, HIV, Heroin; Features: HEMATURIA, HTN; Steroid-resistant
4. Amyloidosis: AA (chronic inflammation) or AL (myeloma)
5. IgA Nephropathy (Berger): MCC glomerular hematuria; Gross hematuria post URI/Exercise
6. Alport Syndrome: X-linked Type 4 Collagen mutation; Hematuria + SNHL + Anterior lenticonus

### Nephritic Syndrome
1. PSGN (MC GN in children): 2wks post URI/skin infection; LOW COMPLEMENT (C3, CH50)
   - Antistreptolysin-O; Tx: Self-limited, Loop diuretics for volume overload
2. Lupus: Anti-DNA immune complexes; GN + Nephrotic
3. Goodpasture: Linear immunofluorescence; Lung disease before kidney; Tx: Plasmapheresis
4. Diabetic Nephropathy: Microalbuminuria → CVD risk

## HEMATURIA
1. Glomerular: Brown urine, edema, HTN, proteinuria, RBC casts → Complement levels
   - Low C3 → PSGN vs Lupus nephritis (differentiate with ASO and ANA)
2. NonGlom Symptomatic: Trauma → CT; UTI → Urine culture; Kidney stone → Renal US
3. NonGlom Asymptomatic: Renal US + Urine culture + Urine Ca:Cr

## KIDNEY INJURY
1. HUS: E. coli O157:H7 prodrome → Anemia + Thrombocytopenia + AKI; Tx: Supportive; Avoid Abx/Antimotility agents
2. IgA Vasculitis: Purpura + Joint pain + Intussusception + Scrotal pain
3. Renal Artery Thrombosis: Complication of umbilical artery catheterization → HTN
4. NSAIDs: Pre-renal azotemia (BUN/Cr >20:1) via PG inhibition
5. ATN: Aminoglycosides, Prolonged ischemia, Sepsis

## LOWER URINARY TRACT

### Enuresis
1. Primary Nocturnal (>5yo): Delayed bladder maturation, Low ADH, Dec bladder capacity
   - Tx: Treat constipation first; Restrict fluids; Enuresis alarm; Desmopressin
2. Secondary Nocturnal (>6mo dry then relapse): UTI, CKD (daytime+nighttime), DM (polyuria), DI, OSA
   - OSA: Snoring, Hyperactivity → Polysomnography

### Posterior Urethral Valves (Males only)
- MCC chronic renal insufficiency in male children → Urinary reflux
- Complication: Potter Sequence (flat facies, pulmonary hypoplasia, limb deformities)

### Recurrent Cystitis: RF: Functional constipation → Impacted stool compresses bladder → Urinary stasis
### UTI: Infants → UA + Culture via catheterization; Tx: 1-2wks Cephalosporin; Renal US after 1st febrile UTI
- VUR: #1 RF for chronic pyelonephritis; Grade 4-5 → Daily Abx prophylaxis

## STONES
1. Calcium (MC): RF: Dehydration, Excess Na/Oxalate; Tx: Thiazides, Potassium citrate
2. Struvite (Mg-Ammonium-Phosphate)
3. Uric Acid (Radiolucent): RF: DM, Gout; Tx: Alkalinize urine
4. Cystine (Hexagonal, Radiolucent): Impaired COLA transport; Dx: Positive cyanide nitroprusside

## TUMORS
1. Beckwith-Wiedemann: Increased risk Wilms, Hepatoblastoma, Neuroblastoma
2. Neuroblastoma (<2yo): Adrenal/sympathetic chain; Raccoon eyes, Opsoclonus-myoclonus; N-myc
3. Wilms (2-5yo): MC renal malignancy; WAGR (11p deletion); Screening: US q3mo
4. Tumor Lysis Syndrome: Hyperuricemia + Hyperphosphatemia + Hyperkalemia → AKI

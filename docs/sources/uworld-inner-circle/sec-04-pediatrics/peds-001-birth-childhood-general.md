# BIRTH AND NEWBORN EXAMINATION

Step 1: Mom's History
1. CBC
2. Coombs + or -
3. STI screening
4. Strep Screening: GBS + (3 doses Amoxicillin 3 hrs before delivery)
5. Hepatitis B Screening: Mom HbsAg positive— Baby receives Gammaglobulin + Hep B vaccine

Step 2: Birth History
1. Vaginal or C-section (Repeat C-section, Intrauterine asphyxia, Intrauterine stress)
2. Breach or Cephalic presentation
   - Breach complication: hip dislocation (Barlow's or Ortolani) or congenital instability
3. Meconium aspiration syndrome: Intrauterine stress → meconium enters lungs

Step 3: When leakage of Amniotic fluid started
1. 48-72hrs: Amniotic membrane break introduces vaginal bacteria → Chorioamnionitis
2. Rupture >16hrs: Cultures → Baby placed on Abx

Step 4: APGAR
Step 5: Gestational Age (AGA, SGA, LGA: Diabetes)

Step 6: Physical appearance
1. Cyanosis: Peripheral/Acrocyanosis (physiological) vs Central (disease)
2. Dystocia: Shoulder dystocia → Erb's palsy

Step 7: Exam
1. CVS first (pericardium, valves, femoral pulse)
2. Lungs (Laryngomalacia, Diaphragmatic Hernia, Foreign body)
3. Head: Fontanelle closes at 18mo; Caput Succedaneum (crosses midline, resolves 48hrs) vs Cephalohematoma (subperiosteal, doesn't resolve)
4. Ears: Low set → Down syndrome, Turner, Trisomy 18

# POSTNATAL

1. Congenital infection: Symmetric growth restriction + Hepatosplenomegaly
2. Still murmur: Systolic, musical, grade 1-2 at LL sternal border → Reassurance
3. Higher WBC in neonates: 9,000-30,000/mm3
4. Transient tachypnea of newborn: Retained fetal lung fluid; CXR: Hyperinflation, Fluid in fissures
5. Initiation of respirations: 1st Tactile stimulation + O2; 2nd Bag-mask or ETT
6. Down syndrome: AV septal defect, Duodenal atresia, Hirschsprung, Hypothyroidism, Acute Leukemia, Atlantoaxial instability

# CHILDHOOD

1. Child Abuse: RF: unstable family; CF: Metaphyseal corner fractures, Posterior rib, Retinal hemorrhage; Eval: Skeletal survey
2. Constitutional Growth Delay: Short stature with deceleration that normalizes after 2-3yo
3. Growth Chart: <2nd percentile for weight or length in <2yo
4. Lymph nodes: Reassuring (localized, soft, mobile, <2cm) vs Worrisome (supraclavicular, firm, >2cm, systemic sx)
5. Thymus: Atrophies after puberty; Absence → DiGeorge; Presence post-puberty → Lymphoma

# IMAGING
1. Ultrasound: safe <4months
2. Xray: >4months (wait for ossification)
3. Lumbosacral US: Sacral dimple or tuft of hair → MRI if abnormal

# POISONING & ENVIRONMENTAL

## Injury Prevention
- Drowning MCC injury-related death 1-4yo → 4-sided fence

## Carbon Monoxide
1. Displaces O2 → carboxyhemoglobin, left-shift O2 curve
2. Mild: HA, confusion; Severe: Cerebral hypoxia, Lactic acidosis, MI (1/3), Pulmonary edema
3. Dx: ABG carboxyhemoglobin; Tx: High flow O2; Severe: Hyperbaric O2

## Important Antidotes
1. Acetylcysteine → Acetaminophen
2. Amyl Nitrite → Cyanide
3. Atropine → Organophosphate
4. Deferoxamine → Iron toxicity
5. Dimercaprol → Arsenic, Mercury
6. Hyperbaric O2 → CO
7. Lorazepam → PCP intoxication
8. Methylene blue → Methemoglobinemia (Nitrites, Dapsone, Benzocaine)
9. Naloxone → Opiate overdose (also Clonidine, Loperamide in children)

## Cyanide
- Nitroprusside releases NO and Cyanide ions; RF: Renal insufficiency
- CF: AMS, lactic acidosis, seizures, coma

# ALLERGY & IMMUNOLOGY

## Transient Hypogammaglobulinemia of Infancy
1. Self-limited, Low IgG, Normal leukocytes and vaccine response
2. Maternal IgG wanes at 6mo → physiological nadir may be prolonged
3. Vs Common Variable Immunodeficiency (abnormal Ab response to vaccines)

## Anaphylaxis
1. Mast cell activation → Prompt IM Epinephrine
2. Biphasic: initial → asymptomatic 1-30hr → recurrence; Protracted: hours to days
3. Early intubation for UPPER AIRWAY OBSTRUCTION (stridor, hoarseness)

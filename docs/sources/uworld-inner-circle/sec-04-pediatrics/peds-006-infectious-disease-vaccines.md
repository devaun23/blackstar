# INFECTIOUS DISEASE

## CONGENITAL
Nonspecific: Hepatosplenomegaly, rash, jaundice, symmetric growth restriction

1. Toxoplasmosis: Chorioretinitis, Hydrocephalus, Diffuse intracranial calcifications
   - Tx: Pyrimethamine + Sulfadiazine + Folinic acid
   - Note: Toxo and CMV both cause chorioretinitis/HPS/seizures/microcephaly; Only Toxo causes Macrocephaly (hydrocephalus)

2. Rubella: Cataracts, Heart defects (PDA), Sensorineural hearing loss

3. CMV: Periventricular calcifications, Microcephaly, Sensorineural hearing loss

4. HIV: Diffuse LAD, Lethargy, Thrush, P. Jirovecii

5. HSV 2: Vesicular/ulcerative rash

6. Syphilis: Rhinorrhea, Skeletal anomalies, Desquamating rash (palms/soles)
   - Late: Saddle nose, Notched teeth, Saber shins, SNHL
   - Dx: VDRL/RPR + Darkfield microscopy; Tx: Penicillin

7. Neonatal Abstinence Syndrome (Substance Abuse): 1st week; Irritability, poor feeding, sneezing, seizures

## NEONATAL

### Neonatal Conjunctivitis

| Type | Onset | Findings | Treatment |
|------|-------|----------|-----------|
| Chemical | <24hr | Mild irritation after Silver Nitrate | Eye lubricant |
| Gonococcal | 2-5D (Wk 1) | Marked swelling + purulent discharge; Most severe | IM Cefotaxime; Prophylaxis: Topical Erythromycin |
| Chlamydial | 5-14D (Wk 2) | Mild swelling, Watery/mucopurulent discharge | Oral Erythromycin (SE: Pyloric stenosis) |
| HSV | Wk 2-3 | Vesicular/ulcerative lesions | |

### Early Neonatal Sepsis (<72hrs)
- Pathogens: GBS, E. coli, Listeria
- GBS IS MCC OF NEONATAL SEPSIS, PNEUMONIA AND MENINGITIS
- S/S: Temperature instability, Abnormal WBC/bandemia, Lethargy, Poor feeding, Respiratory distress, Jaundice
- Sepsis Workup (complete): LP + CBC + Blood Cx + Urine Cx; >3yr: excludes LP
- Tx: Ampicillin + Gentamicin; GBS confirmed: Penicillin G alone
- Prevention: Intrapartum Abx prophylaxis for GBS+ mothers at 36wks

### GBS in Neonates
| | Early (<1wk) | Late (>1wk) |
|---|---|---|
| PP | Vertical transmission | Horizontal transmission |
| CF | Sepsis, Pneumonia, Meningitis | Bacteremia, Cellulitis-Adenitis, Meningitis |

### Neonatal Tetanus: Trismus, Spasms, Hypertonicity; Tx: Supportive + Abx + TIG
### Neonatal Botulism: Rapid hypotonia, weakness, constipation, poor feeding

## MENINGITIS
- <1mo: GBS, E. coli, Listeria, HSV
- >1mo: S. pneumoniae, N. meningitidis
- Tuberculous meningitis; Mumps (aseptic)

## ENT

### Mastoiditis: MC suppurative complication of AOM; Tender protruded auricle
### Cholesteatoma: Chronic painless otorrhea; Pearly white mass in superior TM
### Acute Bacterial Rhinosinusitis: H. influenzae (50%), S. pneumoniae (25%), M. catarrhalis (25%)

### Cervical Lymphadenitis
| Pathogen | CF |
|----------|-----|
| S. aureus, S. pyogenes | Acute, suppurative; Tx: Clindamycin |
| Anaerobic (Prevotella) | History of periodontal disease |
| Mycobacterium avium | Chronic, nontender, violaceous |
| Bartonella henselae | Cat scratch; Tx: Azithromycin |
| Bilateral viral | Adenovirus (acute), EBV/CMV (subacute) |

### Vesicular Oral Infections
- Anterior (HSV1): Buccal mucosa, tongue, gingiva, hard palate → Oral Acyclovir
- Posterior (CoxA): Palatine pillars, tonsils, soft palate → Supportive (Herpangina)

# VACCINATIONS

## Administration
- IM: Mid-Anterolateral Thigh (Vastus lateralis) up to 18mo; 22-25 gauge
- Wait 15 min post-vaccine before discharge

## Contraindications
- Previous severe reactions (Anaphylaxis, Febrile seizures)
- Pregnant: No live vaccines (MMR, Varicella, Yellow Fever, IN Influenza)
- Hypersensitivity: Egg (Influenza, MMR, Yellow Fever), Mercury (HepB, Tdap, Meningococcal), Neomycin (MMR), Gelatin (MMR, Td)
- Rotavirus: Intussusception risk

## Vaccine Types
1. Inactivated: Polio, Hep A, IM Influenza
2. Toxoid: Diphtheria, Tetanus
3. Live Attenuated: IN Influenza, Rotavirus, MMR, Varicella
4. Subunit/Conjugate: Hep B, Pertussis, Hib, Pneumococcal, Meningococcal (age 11/12 + booster 16), HPV (ages 11-12)

## Special Groups
- Premies (<35wks): Same schedule based on chronological age; RSV: Palivizumab monthly x4
- Sickle Cell: Pneumococcal vaccine especially important (asplenia)

## Tetanus Wound Management
- <3 lifetime doses: Clean → Tdap; Dirty → Tdap + IG
- >3 lifetime doses: Clean + >10yr → Tdap; Dirty + >5yr → Tdap

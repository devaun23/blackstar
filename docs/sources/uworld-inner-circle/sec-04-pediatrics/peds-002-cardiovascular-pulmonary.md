# CARDIOVASCULAR

## SYNCOPE
- Exertional Syncope: Cardiac → ECG; Neurological → EEG

### Psychogenic Pseudosyncope
- Prolonged LOC (vs 1-2min syncope), Normal vitals, La belle indifference, High frequency >1/day

### Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy
1. AutoDominant sarcomere mutation → asymmetric hypertrophy → LVOT obstruction
2. Murmur: Midsystolic crescendo-decrescendo at L sternal border or apex
3. Increased with: Valsalva, Standing (Dec Preload); Decreased with: Handgrip, Squatting, Leg raise (Inc Volume)
4. Complication: Anterior mitral valve leaflet motion → LVOT obstruction

### HCM in Infant of Diabetic Mother
- Transient hypertrophy from glycogen/fat deposition; Regresses by age 1

### Long QT Syndrome → Arrhythmia → syncope, <1-2min

### Vasovagal Syncope → Prodrome (nausea), <1-2min

## TACHYARRHYTHMIA

### A Fib
- Association: Hyperthyroid (5-15%); High CHA2DS2VASc ≥2 → Anticoagulate (NOAC or Warfarin)

### SVT (narrow complex)
1. PP: Dual AV node pathways (slow/fast) → reentry circuit (AVNRT)
2. Baby HR 220-280bpm: Pallor, irritability, poor feeding
3. Triggers: WPW, Alcohol, Nicotine, Caffeine, Pseudoephedrine
4. HD Unstable → Cardioversion; HD Stable → 1st Vagal maneuvers → 2nd Adenosine

### QT Prolongation
- Acquired: Macrolides, Fluoroquinolones, Ondansetron, Azoles, Antipsychotics, TCAs, Class 1A/III
- Electrolyte: Low Mg, Low K
- Congenital: Romano-Ward (AutoD); Jervell & Lange-Nielsen (AutoR + sensorineural hearing loss)
- Tx: Avoid triggers, Nonselective BB (Propranolol, Nadolol)

## CYANOSIS (R→L SHUNTS)

### Tetralogy of Fallot
1. Harsh crescendo-decrescendo systolic murmur at LU sternal border (pulmonic stenosis)
2. Tet spells: Exertion/feeding → increased PVR → R→L shunt → acute hypoxemia/cyanosis
3. Single S2; CXR: Boot-shaped heart
4. Tx: Knee-chest positioning (Inc SVR), Inhaled O2

### PDA

### Persistent Pulmonary HTN of Newborn
- R-L shunting across PDA; RF: Lung hypoplasia, Meconium aspiration, Infection
- CXR: Clear lungs with decreased pulmonary vascularity; Tx: Inhaled NO

### Coarctation of Aorta
1. Thickening of aortic arch → decreased flow to descending aorta
2. Worse after PDA closure ~5 days
3. CF: Differential pulses UE vs LE; Neonates: HF, Cardiogenic shock; Children: LE claudication, Secondary HTN
4. Association: Turner Syndrome, Bicuspid Aortic Valve
5. Complications: Aortic Aneurysm/Dissection, Cerebral aneurysm/SAH
6. Tx: PGE1 neonates; Surgical repair

### VSD
1. Holosystolic murmur LL sternal border, palpable thrill
2. Postnatal: L→R shunt; Infancy: Pulmonary over-circulation → HF with feeding
3. Late: Eisenmenger (pulmonary arterial thickening → R→L shunt)

### Eisenmenger's Syndrome
- Chronic pulmonary blood influx → Pulm HTN → RVH → DOE + Cyanosis

### Hypoplastic Left Heart Syndrome

| Dx | Exam | CXR | Management |
|----|------|-----|------------|
| Transposition | Single S2, Cyanosis <24hrs | Egg-on-a-string | PG to maintain PDA |
| Tricuspid Atresia | Single S2, VSD murmur | Minimal pulm flow | |
| Truncus Arteriosus | Single S2, Systolic ejection murmur | Inc pulm flow | |
| TOF | Harsh PS murmur | Boot-shaped (RVH) | |
| TAPVR with Obstruction | Severe cyanosis | Pulm edema, Snowman sign | |

## VASCULITIS

### SLE
- Dx requires 4 of 11 criteria
- Lupus Nephritis: Hematuria, proteinuria, rising Cr → Renal Biopsy

### IgA Vasculitis (Henoch-Schonlein Purpura)
1. Palpable purpura on LE WITHOUT THROMBOCYTOPENIA
2. Arthritis → REFUSAL TO WALK
3. Abdominal pain/intussusception (ileoileal)
4. Renal disease (IgA nephropathy)
5. SCROTAL PAIN (1/3 boys)
6. Dx: Clinical (>2 of: arthralgia, abdominal pain, renal disease)
7. Stable → NSAIDs; Unstable → Glucocorticoids

### Kawasaki Disease
1. Fever >5 days + >4 of: Conjunctivitis, Oral changes (strawberry tongue), Polymorphous rash, Distal extremity changes, Cervical LAD >1.5cm
2. Lab: Elevated Plt/WBC/CRP/AST/ALT; Decreased Hb; Sterile pyuria
3. Tx: IVIG + Aspirin
4. Complications: Coronary artery aneurysm; Lymphocytic myocarditis → HF

# PULMONARY & CRITICAL CARE

## PREMATURITY

### Apnea of Prematurity
- 99% <28wks; Central apnea; Cessation >20sec with bradycardia/desaturation
- Tx: Caffeine (stimulates drive until 34-37wks)
- Complication: Cerebral palsy (intermittent hypoxia → watershed insult)

### Bronchopulmonary Dysplasia
- Chronic lung disease from prolonged ventilation/O2
- CXR: Coarse markings with cystic changes
- Surfactant reduces mortality but doesn't prevent BPD
- Complication: Pulm HTN → cor pulmonale

### Neonatal Respiratory Distress Table

| Condition | PP | RF | CF | CXR/Tx |
|-----------|----|----|-----|--------|
| TTN | Retained fetal lung fluid | C-section, Prematurity | Inc work of breathing | Fluid in fissures |
| RDS | Surfactant deficiency | Prematurity | V/Q mismatch | Ground glass, Air bronchogram; Surfactant |
| PPHN | Persistent high PVR | Prematurity | Severe cyanosis, Clear lungs | Dec pulm vascularity; Inhaled NO |

### Tension Pneumothorax
- Sudden respiratory distress, decreased unilateral breath sounds
- Cause: Trauma, Mechanical ventilation; Tx: Immediate thoracostomy

## CONGENITAL
### Congenital Diaphragmatic Hernia
- Intrathoracic bowel loops, displaced cardiac silhouette
- Tx: ETT, NG decompression, Surgery

## AIRWAY

### ABPA → RF: Asthma, CF; CXR: Fleeting infiltrates; Tx: Albuterol, Dexamethasone
### ARDS → Sepsis/aspiration/trauma → noncardiogenic pulm edema → Mechanical ventilation
### Bronchiolitis (RSV, <2yo) → Wheezing, NO consolidation; Tx: Supportive; Prevent: Palivizumab

## ASTHMA

### Management Ladder
1. SABA: Albuterol
2. LABA: Fluticasone/Salmeterol
3. CST: Beclomethasone, Prednisone
4. LTRA: Montelukast
5. Anticholinergic: Ipratropium (short), Tiotropium (long)

### Persistent Asthma → Scheduled Inhaled CST + LABA
### AERD → Triad: asthma + nasal polyposis + NSAID sensitivity; Tx: Clopidogrel, LTRA
### Exercise-Induced → Pre-exercise bronchodilator or bronchoprovocation; Tx: Inhaled CST or LTRA before exercise
### Occupational → Serial PEFR measurements
### Comorbid GERD → Micro-aspiration → increased vagal tone; Tx: PPI trial

### Acute Exacerbation
1. Albuterol + Ipratropium + Prednisone
2. Severe indicator: Normal PaCO2 (respiratory fatigue)
3. Severe Tx: Neb Albuterol/Ipratropium + IV CST + IV Mag + NIPPV → intubation if no improvement
4. Greatest RF for severe: Prior intubation/ICU

# DERMATOLOGY

## BENIGN NEONATAL RASHES

| Diagnosis | Onset | CF | Management |
|-----------|-------|-----|------------|
| Erythema toxicum | Birth-3days | Pustules with erythematous base on trunk | Observation |
| Milia | Birth | Firm, white papules on face | Observation |
| Milia rubra | Birth | Erythematous papular rash on occluded areas | Avoid overheating |
| Neonatal pustular melanosis | Birth | Nonerythematous pustules → hyperpigmented macule | Observation |
| Neonatal cephalic pustulosis | 3wks | Erythematous papules/pustules on face/scalp | Observation, daily cleansing |

## CHILDHOOD PIGMENTED LESIONS
1. Cafe-au-lait spots
2. Mongolian Spot (CDM): Melanocytes in dermis, resolves after 10yo
3. Congenital Melanocytic Nevus: Benign, INCREASED DENSITY dark coarse hairs; Biopsy if change
4. Vascular: Nevus Flammeus (port-wine, no recess); Nevus Simplex (fade); Hemangioma (proliferate then involute)

## BLISTERS
1. SSSS (S. aureus exfoliative toxin): (+) Nikolsky, DIFFUSE RASH AND SHEDDING; Tx: Nafcillin/Vancomycin
2. Bullous Impetigo: Localized SSSS; well-appearing; honey-colored crust
3. Erysipelas (Strep): Well-demarcated, superficial dermis
4. TEN (Medication): (+) Nikolsky + mucosal involvement, >30% BSA

## HEAD
- Tinea Capitis: Hair loss, kerion; Tx: Oral Griseofulvin or Terbinafine; treat close contacts

## EXTREMITIES
1. Atopic Dermatitis
   - RF: FH atopy, Filaggrin gene mutation
   - Acute: Pruritic erythematous patches; Chronic: Lichenified plaques
   - Associations: Eosinophilic Esophagitis, Langerhans cell histiocytosis
   - Tx: Emollients, Topical CST, Calcineurin inhibitors (Pimecrolimus)
   - Complications: Impetigo (S. aureus), Eczema Herpeticum (HSV1, PAINFUL, life-threatening), Molluscum, Tinea

2. Guttate Psoriasis: Triggered by Strep; (+) Auspitz sign, Koebner phenomenon; Nail pitting/onycholysis

3. Serum Sickness vs SS-like reaction
   - Common: Fever, Pruritic rash (spares mucous membranes), Arthralgias, Onset 1-2wks
   - SS: Foreign trigger (antivenom, monoclonal Ab); High titer immune complex
   - SS-like: Medication (Cefaclor, Penicillin, TMP-SMX); Low-grade fever, LYMPHADENOPATHY; D/C drug

4. Tinea Corporis: Scaly annular lesion with central clearing; Dx: KOH; Tx: Topical/Oral antifungals

## FOLLICLES/GLANDS
1. Keratosis Pilaris: Retained keratin plugs, posterior upper arm
2. Seborrheic Dermatitis: GREASY yellow scales; Scalp, face, umbilicus; Tx: Emollient, antifungals

## INSECT BITES
- Bed bug: LINEAR GROUP, central hemorrhagic punctum
- Scabies: Pruritic burrows in intertriginous areas

# ENT

## NOSE
1. Allergic Rhinitis: Transverse crease, Turbinate hypertrophy
2. Choanal Atresia: Blue with feeds, pink during crying; Dx: Failed catheter; Tx: Surgery
3. Epistaxis: Lean forward; Anterior → cauterize; Posterior → packing
4. Foreign Body: Unilateral bacterial infection

## THROAT
1. Laryngomalacia: Collapse of supraglottic structures, chronic inspiratory stridor worse supine, resolves 18mo
2. Foreign Body: Wheezing not improved by albuterol; Hyperinflation of affected lung; Tx: Rigid Bronchoscopy
3. Complications: Recurrent pneumonia, Bronchiectasis

# OPHTHALMOLOGY
1. Well-child eye exam: Routinely starts at age 4
2. Retinoblastoma <2yo
3. Amblyopia <5yo (vision still developing)
4. NF1 <6yo: Optic pathway glioma → decreased visual acuity, optic disc pallor, proptosis
5. Optic Nerve injury: Normal red reflex, Decreased color vision, Relative afferent pupillary defect

# ENDOCRINE, DIABETES & METABOLISM

## Congenital Adrenal Hyperplasia
- Is 2nd number a "1" → virilization; Is 1st number a "1" → HTN
1. 21-hydroxylase: Dec Mineralocorticoids (salt wasting), Dec Glucocorticoids (hypoglycemia), Inc Androgens (virilization)
   - Dx: Elevated 17-Hydroxyprogesterone
   - First days: Normal lytes (maternal hormones); 1-2wks: Salt abnormalities
2. 11b-hydroxylase: virilization + HTN (11-deoxycorticosterone has mineralocorticoid activity)
3. 17a-hydroxylase: HTN only

## Congenital Hypothyroidism
- MCC: thyroid agenesis
- After maternal thyroxine wanes: Lethargy, Enlarged fontanelle, Protruding tongue, Umbilical hernia, Constipation
- Dx: High TSH, Low T4; Tx: Levothyroxine; Normal if treated neonatally

## DiGeorge Syndrome (22q11.2 deletion)
- Conotruncal cardiac defect (ToF, Truncus arteriosus), Thymic hypoplasia, Cleft palate, Hypocalcemia

## Neonatal Thyrotoxicosis
- Transplacental maternal anti-TSH receptor Ab; Self-resolves 3mo; Tx: Methimazole + BB

## MEN Syndromes
- Type 1: HyperPTH + Pituitary + Pancreatic
- Type 2A: HyperPTH + Medullary Thyroid + Pheochromocytoma
- Type 2B: RET mutation → Mucosal neuromas + Medullary Thyroid (prophylactic thyroidectomy) + Pheo

## Precocious Puberty (Girls <8, Boys <9)
- Advanced Bone Age + High LH → Central (MRI); Low LH + GnRH stim → Low LH: Peripheral
- Central Tx: GnRH (desensitizes pituitary, suppresses FSH/LH)
- Normal Bone Age: Premature Thelarche or Adrenarche

## Galactosemia (AR)
- GALT deficiency → Galactose accumulation
- CF: Jaundice, hepatomegaly, vomiting, hypoglycemia, cataracts (1st few weeks), E. coli sepsis
- Dx: Newborn screening, Absent RBC GALT; Tx: Soy formula

## Glycogen Storage Disease (Von Gierke)
- Glucose-6-phosphatase deficiency → glycogen accumulation
- CF at 3-4mo: Hypoglycemia seizures, Lactic acidosis, Hepatomegaly, "Doll-like" face

## Gaucher Disease (AR)
- Glucocerebrosidase deficiency → Glucocerebroside in macrophages
- CF: Severe splenomegaly, Anemia, Thrombocytopenia, Bony pain; Tx: Enzyme replacement

## Infant of Diabetic Mother
- 1st TM: Congenital heart disease, NTD, Small left colon
- 2nd-3rd TM: Macrosomia, Organomegaly, Neonatal hypoglycemia (most common), Polycythemia
- Transient hypertrophic cardiomyopathy from glycogen/fat deposition

## DKA
- RF: Infection (catecholamines, cortisol, glucagon)
- Labs: Glucose >200, HCO3 <15, pH <7.3, AG >14, Ketones
- Serum K normal/high despite total body K depletion (acidosis shifts K extracellular)
- Tx: 10mL/kg isotonic fluid → Insulin + Potassium; Complication: Cerebral Edema

## Growth/Nutrition
1. Constitutional Delay: FH "late bloomers", Short with normal velocity after 3yo, Delayed bone age
2. PKU (AR): Cannot convert PHE→Tyrosine; Musty odor, Intellectual disability, Hypopigmentation
3. Nutritional Rickets: Exclusively breastfed without Vit D; Craniotabes, Widened wrists, Bowing; Tx: Vit D + Ca

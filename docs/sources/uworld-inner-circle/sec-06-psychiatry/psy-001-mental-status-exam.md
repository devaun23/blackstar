# Mental Status Exam

## Mini Mental Exam Components

1. Orientation: Person, Place, Time (year), Situation (why are you here)
2. Appearance: Neat/clean, Tattoo/piercing, Malodorous
3. Behavior/Attitude: Cooperative, Resistant, Evasive, Suspicious, Energetic
4. Speech
   - Speed: Slow, Normal, Rapid
   - Volume: Soft, Loud
   - Latency: How long it takes for patient to speak, pressured speech
   - Prosody (musical tone): Normal, Monotone
   - Mute, Word Salad
5. Insight: Good or Poor
6. Judgement: Good or Poor
7. Impulse Control: Good or Poor
8. Mood: Elevated/Elated/Euphoric, Sad
9. Affect (how patient displays mood on face)
   - Congruent vs Incongruent
   - Stable vs Labile (up and down)
   - Range: Normal/Full, Restricted (depressed), Blunted/Flat (psychosis)
   - Reactivity: Are they reacting
10. Thought Process
    - Logical/Illogical
    - Organized/Disorganized
    - Intact/Loose association
    - Flight of ideas
    - Tangential with loose association
    - Circumferential
11. Thought Content
    - Suicidal ideation
    - Homicidal ideation
    - Hallucinations
    - Delusions
12. Cognition: Attention/Concentration/Memory
    - Mini-Cog exam: Repeat (Cat, Tree, Pen), Spell World backwards, Draw clock (10:20am)
    - Poor score: Cannot draw clock or cannot repeat any words
    - Inattentive (cannot concentrate at all) vs Distracted (attentive until external stimulus)

# History Taking

## 1-liner Format

- Age, Gender, Ethnicity; living situation; working situation; PMH; Presentation/Brought by; in context of substance abuse/medical compliance

## HPI Structure

1. According to charts (previous admission, diagnosis, medications)
2. UDS and blood alcohol levels
3. At time of evaluation: cooperative, eye contact
4. Differential assessment for: Depression, Mania, Psychosis, Anxiety, Trauma, Suicidal ideation, Homicidal ideation, Appetite, Sleep, Substance abuse details

## Remaining History

- Past psychiatric history: Outpatient psychiatrist, Medications, Seizure disorder
- PMH, Brain injury/TBI, Substance abuse/Methadone program
- Social history: Smoking, Alcohol, Criminal history, Living arrangement, Academic history
- Developmental history, Family history, Trauma
- Mental Status Exam -> Assessment -> Plan

## How to Diagnose (Hierarchy)

1. Any organic cause (Hyperthyroidism, HTN)
2. Any psychosis
3. Any mood disorder (depression, mania)
4. Any anxiety or trauma
5. Any substance abuse
6. Any other disorder

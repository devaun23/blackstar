# OCD

- Most recognized behavior is EXCESSIVENESS
- Excessive worrying and loss of time
- Pathophysiology: Cortico-striato-thalamo-cortical circuit; structural abnormalities in orbitofrontal cortex and basal ganglia
- Obsession: Recurrent persistent intrusive thoughts/urges/images causing marked anxiety
- Compulsion: Repetitive behaviors/mental acts in response to obsession
- Time consuming or cause significant distress/impairment
- Special case: Postpartum OCD — new mothers reluctant to disclose symptoms; mild cases treated with CBT
- Severity: Yale-Brown Obsessive-Compulsive Scale (Y-BOCS)
  - Time spent, Interference, Distress, Resistance, Control (0 = no symptoms, 4 = extreme)
- Treatment
  1. CBT (Exposure and Response prevention)
  2. SSRI (Sertraline, Paroxetine, Fluoxetine, Fluvoxamine preferred)
  3. SNRI (Venlafaxine)
  4. TCA (Clomipramine — not 1st line due to SE: sedation, dry mouth, constipation, urinary retention, hypotension)

## Hoarding Disorder

- Difficulty discarding possessions regardless of actual value
- Treatment: CBT

# Impulse Control Disorders

## Gambling Disorder

- Persistent maladaptive gambling -> impairment or distress
- Nonsubstance behavioral addiction
- Dx: >=4 criteria including preoccupation, chasing losses, damaged relationships, reliance on others for losses, gambling when distressed, escalating stakes, irritability when cutting back, repeated failed attempts to quit, concealment
- Treatment: CBT, Self-help programs

## Intermittent Explosive Disorder

- Unable to restrain aggressive impulses -> verbal/physical aggression out of proportion
- Episodes <30 minutes with initial relief but subsequent remorse/dysphoria/embarrassment
- Note: Aggressive outbursts in Bipolar Disorder last days-months with manic symptoms
- Treatment: CBT, SSRI

## Kleptomania

- Inability to resist impulse to steal objects of low monetary value or not needed
- Temporary relief with theft but subsequent guilt/shame
- Items may be returned
- Treatment: CBT

# Personality Disorders — Weird, Wild, Worried

- Note: Limited insight into problematic nature of behavior

## Cluster A — Weird

### Paranoid PD

- Suspicious, distrustful, hypervigilant
- Pattern of distrust and suspiciousness — NO clear delusions
- Believes being exploited/deceived; interprets benign comments as threats; bears grudges; questions partner loyalty without justification
- DDx: Delusional disorder (delusions only >=1 month), Schizophrenia (delusions + hallucinations + disorganization + negative symptoms), Schizotypal PD (eccentric behavior, unusual perceptions)

### Schizoid PD

- Prefer to be a loner, detached, unemotional
- Lack of desire for close relationships
- Emotionally detached with flat affect; indifference to praise or criticism

### Schizotypal PD

- Unusual thoughts, perceptions, and behavior
- Odd beliefs/magical thinking
- Eccentric appearance
- Idiosyncratic speech
- Limited capacity for intimate relationships
- Paranoid beliefs without evidence
- Differentiated from Primary Psychotic Disorders by absence of well-formed persistent delusions or frank hallucinations

## Cluster B — Wild

### Antisocial PD

- Age >=18
- Violates rights of others, social norms, laws
- Impulsive, irritable, aggressive
- Lack of remorse; failure to accept responsibility
- Evidence of Conduct Disorder before age 15
- Management: Psychotherapy for mild forms; treat comorbid disorders

### Borderline PD

- Dx: Pervasive pattern of unstable relationships, self-image/affect/marked impulsivity with >=5 of:
  1. Frantic efforts to avoid abandonment
  2. Unstable intense interpersonal relationships
  3. Markedly unstable self-image
  4. Impulsivity in >=2 potentially self-damaging areas
  5. Recurrent suicidal behaviors/threats of self-mutilation
  6. Mood instability (marked mood reactivity lasting minutes-hours, vs days in primary mood disorders)
  7. Chronic feelings of emptiness
  8. Inappropriate intense anger
  9. Transient stress-related paranoia or dissociation
- Treatment: DBT (1st line) targeting unstable moods, impulsivity, suicidality; adjunct pharmacotherapy; antidepressants for comorbid mood/anxiety
- Association: History of childhood trauma, insecure attachment, dissociative symptoms
- Joint treatment as medical team can minimize splitting

### Histrionic PD

- Superficial, theatrical, attention-seeking
- Express empathy; excessive emotionality with rapidly shifting emotions; impressionist speech
- Overestimate closeness of relationships
- Use seductive behavior to get needs met

### Narcissistic PD

- Grandiosity, lack of empathy
- Sense of entitlement

## Cluster C — Worried

### Avoidant PD

- Avoidance due to fears of criticism and rejection
- Occupational dysfunction; turning down promotions due to fear of criticism
- Desire relationships (unlike Schizoid)

### Dependent PD

- Submissive, clingy, needs to be taken care of
- Avoid taking initiative due to feelings of inadequacy
- May experience depressed mood/anxiety about managing on their own

### Obsessive-Compulsive PD

- Preoccupied with orderliness, details, scheduling, rigid rules
- Perfectionism interferes with efficacy and task completion

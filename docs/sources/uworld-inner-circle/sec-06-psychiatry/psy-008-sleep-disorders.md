# Sleep Disorders

## Advanced Sleep-Wake Phase Disorder

- Circadian rhythm disorder
- Inability to stay awake in the evening (by 7pm) and early morning insomnia

## Delayed Sleep-Wake Phase Disorder

- Circadian rhythm disorder ("night owls")
- Inability to fall asleep at traditional bedtimes -> sleep-onset insomnia and excessive daytime sleepiness
- Sleep normal if allowed to follow internal circadian rhythm and sleep until late morning

## Enuresis

- Bedwetting

## Insomnia

### Chronic Insomnia

- Treatment
  1. CBT (more effective than medication)
  2. Non-benzo receptor agonist
  3. Orexin receptor antagonist
  4. Silenor (Doxepin)
  5. Ramelteon

## Narcolepsy

- Type 1: Low hypocretin (Orexin) levels
- Clinical features
  - Excessive daytime sleepiness
  - Cataplexy: Transient loss of muscle tone

## Restless Leg Syndrome

- Risk factors and associations
  - Iron deficiency, Uremia, Pregnancy
  - DM (especially with neuropathy)
  - Multiple Sclerosis, Parkinson disease
  - Drugs: Antidepressants, Antihistamines, Antipsychotics, Antiemetics
- Manifestations
  - Uncomfortable urge to move legs with
    - Unpleasant sensations in legs
    - Onset with inactivity or at night
    - Relief with movement
    - Dysesthesia: Crawling and itching feeling
- Dx: Clinical; serum iron studies obtained in all patients
- Nonpharmacologic: Limit caffeine/alcohol, regular moderate exercise, warm/cold soaks
- Medications
  1. Supplemental iron (if serum ferritin <75 ng/mL)
  2. Mild intermittent: Carbidopa-levodopa PRN
  3. Frequent/daily: Gabapentin, Pregabalin (alpha-2-delta calcium channel ligands)
  4. Dopamine agonists (pramipexole, ropinirole) NOT preferred

## Sleep Terror Disorder (NREM sleep)

- Features
  - Abrupt arousals from sleep (panicked scream, terror, autonomic arousal, unresponsive to comfort)
  - Little or no dream recall
  - Amnesia of episodes
- Management
  - Reassurance
  - Resolve spontaneously within 1-2 years
  - Low-dose benzo at bedtime if frequent, persistent, and distressing

# Anxiety and Trauma-Related Disorders

## Overview — GPASSS

1. Generalized Anxiety Disorder
2. Panic Disorder
3. Agoraphobia
4. Specific Phobia (MC anxiety disorder)
5. Social Anxiety
6. Selective Mutism
7. Separation Anxiety

## Acute Stress Disorder

- Duration: >=3 days, <1 month
- Clinical features
  - Exposure to actual or threatened trauma
  - Intrusive symptoms: Nightmares, flashbacks
  - Avoidance: Avoid reminders of trauma, avoid distressing thoughts
  - Dissociation: Emotional detachment, decreased interest, amnesia to details
  - Negative mood: Negative worldview
  - Hyperarousal: Sleep disturbance, hypervigilance, easy startle, irritability
- Management
  - Trauma-focused brief CBT
  - If reluctant for therapy: Provide education on common responses to normalize stress response
  - Consider medication for insomnia, intense anxiety
  - Monitor for PTSD

## PTSD

- Duration: >=1 month
- Risk factors: Military combat, Sexual trauma, Conflict/displacement, Physical injury, Medical illness (MI, Stroke, ICU), Childhood abuse
- Clinical features: Same symptom clusters as ASD but >=1 month duration
- Treatment
  - Trauma-focused psychotherapy/CBT (correct maladaptive thoughts, exposure-based techniques)
  - Antidepressants: SNRI, SSRI
  - Nightmares: Prazosin (alpha-1 antagonist) at bedtime, requires titration for orthostasis
- Course: Onset within months; 25% have delayed onset (>=6 months); commonly chronic
- Sexual abuse: High risk for developing PTSD, Depression, Suicidality

## Adjustment Disorder

- Duration: <6 months
- Symptoms within 3 months of IDENTIFIABLE STRESSOR
- Marked distress +/- functional impairment
- Does not meet criteria for another DSM-5 disorder
- DDx
  - Normal stress response (not excessive, no significant impairment)
  - Acute stress disorder (traumatic event, intrusive reexperiencing)
  - MDD (>=2 weeks, 5/9 symptoms)
- Management: Psychotherapy (1st line), Adjunctive short-term pharmacotherapy (sleep aid, anxiolytic)

## Generalized Anxiety Disorder

- Prevalent in perinatal population
- Clinical features
  - Excessive, uncontrollable worry (multiple issues) >=6 months
  - >=3 of CRIMS-F (>=1 in children): Concentration difficulty, Restlessness, Irritability, Muscle tension, Sleep disturbance, Fatigue
  - Chronic fluctuating course; academic/occupational dysfunction
- Associated features
  - Physical symptoms (headaches, neck pain); stomachaches in children
  - Perfectionism
  - Comorbid depression, substance use
  - Increased cardiovascular event risk
- Treatment
  1. CBT
  2. SSRI (Sertraline recommended in children if functional impairment; Escitalopram with CBT)
  3. SNRI (Venlafaxine)
  4. Buspirone (FDA-approved nonbenzo anxiolytic; useful in patients with high risk of benzo abuse; NOT for comorbid depression or panic)
  5. Pregnancy: CBT 1st line
  6. 2nd line: Benzo (risk of abuse, dependence, tolerance, withdrawal)
     - Elderly adverse effects: Confusion, cognitive impairment, falls, paradoxical agitation

## Social Anxiety Disorder (Social Phobia)

- Fear of SOCIAL HUMILIATION AND CRITICISM
- No spontaneous panic attacks
- Dx: Marked anxiety >=1 social situation for >=6 months; social situations avoided or endured with intense distress; marked impairment
- Subtype: Performance only (do not fear nonperformance social situations)
- Treatment
  - General: SSRI (Sertraline), SNRI, CBT
  - Performance anxiety: Beta-blocker (Propranolol) for autonomic response; Benzo (avoided if substance use history)

## Specific Phobia

- Duration: >6 months
- Clinical features: Marked anxiety about specific object/situation (flying, heights, animals, injections, blood, thunderstorms)
- Avoidance behavior; generally develops in childhood after traumatic event
- Treatment: CBT with exposure/systematic desensitization (1st line), Short-acting benzo

## Panic Disorder

- Clinical features
  - >=1 month of worry about future events AND untriggered/unexpected panic attacks
  - Symptoms: Dizziness, losing control, chest pain, palpitations, SOB, choking, nausea, trembling, paresthesias, chills, sweating, feeling of impending doom
  - Derealization (detached from surroundings), Depersonalization (detached from body)
  - Fear of losing control or dying
- Associated: Agoraphobia
  - Severe cases: Patients become homebound or leave only when accompanied
- Treatment: SSRI, SNRI, CBT
  - Acute distress: Benzo (avoided 1st line due to abuse risk)

## Agoraphobia

- Duration: >=6 months
- Afraid of not being able to escape
- Situations: Open places, closed places, standing in line/crowds, fear of going outside alone, fear of public transport

## Anxiety Due to Other Medical Condition

- Hyperthyroidism: Important to assess thyroid function in new-onset anxiety before prescribing medications
- Dx: Thyroid function tests

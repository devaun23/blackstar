# Childhood and Early-Onset Disorders

## ADHD

- Dx: Onset before age 12, impairment in >1 setting
- Clinical features
  - Inattentive and hyperactive/impulsive symptoms for >=6 months
  - Subtypes: Predominantly inattentive (MC Adults), Predominantly hyperactive/impulsive (Alpha-2 agonist preferred), Combined (MC Children)
- Treatment
  - Stimulant preferred in school-age children (rapid onset, long safety record)
    - Methylphenidate, Amphetamines
    - SE: Decreased appetite, Insomnia (manage with early dosing and large morning meals)
  - Non-stimulants
    - Children only: Alpha-2 agonist (Clonidine, Guanfacine — limited adult efficacy)
    - Children and adults: SNRI (Atomoxetine), Bupropion, TCA (severe SE: cardiotoxicity, convulsions, coma)
  - Behavioral therapy: Target aggression, improve social and organizational skills
  - Preschool-age: Parent-child behavioral therapy (parent management training); medications NOT recommended

## Autism Spectrum Disorder

- Deficits in social communication and interactions with onset in early development
  - Sharing of emotions/interests
  - Nonverbal communication
  - Developing/understanding relationships
- Restricted, repetitive patterns of behavior
  - Repetitive movements or speech
  - Insistence on sameness/routines
  - Intense fixated interests
  - Adverse responses to sensory input (repeated checking BP cuff, eating selected foods only)
- May occur with or without language/intellectual impairment
  - High-functioning autism: Normal/near-normal intellectual ability that delays diagnosis
- Other features: Lack of initiation/response to interaction, awkward body language, abnormal eye contact, solitary play, impulse/aggressive outbursts
- Management: Early diagnosis and intervention, comprehensive multimodal treatment (speech, behavioral therapy, educational services), adjunctive pharmacotherapy for comorbidities

## Conduct Disorder

- Age <18
- Severe aggressive behavior toward people and animals
- Serious violation of rules (truancy, running away)
- Deceitfulness +/- theft
- Treatment: CBT, Family therapy, Parent management training
- Association: Antisocial Personality Disorder

## Disruptive Mood Dysregulation Disorder (DMDD)

- Onset before age 10

## Oppositional Defiant Disorder (ODD)

- Duration: >=6 months
- Pattern of angry/irritable mood, argumentative/defiant behavior, or vindictiveness
- Arguing, deliberately ignoring others, blaming others for own mistakes
- Easily angered, annoyed, resentful
- Not due to another mental disorder
- Treatment: Parent management training, Psychotherapy (anger management, social skills), Assess for comorbid ADHD

## Disinhibited Social Engagement Disorder

- Due to severe early neglect
- Overfamiliarity with unfamiliar adults

## Reactive Attachment Disorder

- Clinical features
  - Insufficient care: Neglect, abuse, inconsistent caregiving
  - Does not seek or respond to comfort
  - Poor social responsiveness, limited positive affect
  - Unexplained irritability/fear/sadness even during safe encounters
- Associated: Toileting/sleep difficulties, Anxiety, Aggression, Hyperactivity/impulsivity
- DDx: Adjustment disorder, PTSD, Global developmental delay, Intellectual disability, Autism SD, Anxiety/Depressive disorders
- Management
  - Safe, stable, enriching environment with consistent responsive caregiving
  - Psychological services (parenting skills, individual/family counseling)
  - Symptoms improve with consistent attachment figures

## Separation Anxiety Disorder

- Infants/toddlers but can occur at any age; associated with transition/change
- Marked fear of separation from loved one
- School/work absences
- Children: Stomachaches, headaches, repeated nightmares involving separation, difficulty sleeping alone
- Pathological when persistent, impedes development, or causes significant functional impairment

## Tourette Syndrome

- Features
  - Usually preceded by irresistible urges, followed by feelings of relief
  - Exacerbated by stress and fatigue
  - Multiple motor and >=1 vocal tics (not necessarily concurrent, >1 year)
    - Motor: Facial grimacing, blinking, head/neck jerking, shoulder shrugging, tongue protrusion, sniffing
    - Vocal: Grunting, snorting, throat clearing, barking, coprolalia
  - Onset <18 years old
- Treatment
  1. Behavioral therapy (habit reversal training)
  2. Antidopaminergic agents: Tetrabenazine (dopamine depleter), Antipsychotics (Risperidone, Haloperidol)
  3. Alpha-2 agonists (Guanfacine, Clonidine)
- Associations: ADHD, OCD (3-6 years after tics first appear)

## Persistent Motor Tic Disorder

- Either motor or vocal tic lasting >1 year but not both

## Sexual Abuse in Children

- More likely to develop PTSD, Depression, Suicidality
- Abnormal behaviors: Repeated insertion of objects into vagina/anus, sex play involving genital contact, use of force/threats/bribes, age-inappropriate sexual knowledge, repeated UTI without medical cause

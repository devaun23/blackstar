# Mood Disorders

## Bipolar I

- Dx: Manic episode MANDATORY
- Manic episode: 1 week impaired function + hospitalization
  - Elevated/expansive (+3 DIG FAST) or irritable mood (+4 DIG FAST) + increased energy
  - DIG FAST: Distractibility, Indiscretion/poor impulse control, Grandiosity, Flight of ideas, Activity increased (goal-directed), Sleep deficit, Talkativeness/pressured speech
  - Irritable mood may present as uncharacteristic angry outbursts and aggressive behavior
  - Additional features: Hospitalization, Psychotic features
- Depressive episode: 2 weeks (may have occurred years ago)
- Hypomanic episode: 4 days change in function
- Treatment
  - Atypical antipsychotics (anti-dopaminergic)
    - 1st: Olanzapine IM (fast onset)
  - Mood stabilizers
    - Carbamazepine (SE: Aplastic anemia, SIADH)
    - Lithium: Check CMP, UA, EKG, pregnancy test, thyroid function; Toxicity = abdominal pain; SE: Nephrogenic DI, Ebstein anomaly, Arrhythmia, Hypothyroidism, Hyperparathyroidism (elevated Ca); check levels day 4; COX-inhibitors and decreased salt increase levels
    - Valproic acid: Preferred in renal disease; SE: Hepatotoxicity, Thrombocytopenia, Pancreatitis, High ammonia in elderly, Neural tube defects
    - Lamotrigine: SE: Mild rash, Stevens-Johnson Syndrome, Toxic epidermal necrolysis
  - Pregnancy: ECT, Lamotrigine
  - Maintenance (long-term due to high recurrence): Lithium (also decreases suicide risk), Valproate, Quetiapine, Lamotrigine
  - Severe illness (psychosis, aggression, frequent episodes): Lithium/Valproate + 2nd gen antipsychotic (Quetiapine)

## Bipolar II

- Dx: Lack of manic episode
  - Depressive episode (2 weeks) + Hypomanic episode (4 days)
- Important: Differentiate bipolar depression from unipolar depression — bipolar patients NOT treated with antidepressants (risk of inducing mania)
- Treatment (Acute Bipolar Depression)
  - 1st: 2nd gen antipsychotics (Quetiapine, Lurasidone)
  - Lamotrigine, Lithium, Valproate, Olanzapine + Fluoxetine
- Note: Differentiate bipolar psychosis from primary psychotic disorders by lack of psychotic symptoms outside mood episodes

## Cyclothymic Disorder

- Duration: >=2 years
- Features of hypomanic and depressive episodes (not enough criteria for either)

## Major Depressive Disorder

- Duration: 2 weeks
- Dx: 5 of the following + decreased interest (SIG E CAPS)
  1. Sleep disturbance (want to sleep — vs Mania: do not want to sleep)
     - Decreased REM sleep latency, Decreased slow-wave sleep, Increased REM duration, Increased serum cortisol (HPA axis hyperactivity)
  2. Interest decreased (pervasive sadness and anhedonia that do not decrease over time)
  3. Guilt and feelings of worthlessness
  4. Energy decreased
  5. Concentration problems, academic dysfunction, social impairment
  6. Appetite/weight changes
  7. Psychomotor agitation or retardation
  8. Suicidal ideation
- Pediatric: May present with irritability rather than depressed mood (consider MDD if irritability + social withdrawal + academic decline)
- Elderly: Often present with somatic complaints (fatigue, musculoskeletal pain, memory problems)

### MDD with Psychotic Features

- Clinical features: Delusions ("belief dying of cancer without medical indications"), Hallucinations
- Treatment: Antidepressant + Antipsychotic or ECT
  - ECT works faster, preferred in elderly who are malnourished, psychotic, or suicidal
  - ECT has no absolute contraindications
- Vs Schizophrenia: Sleep, appetite, energy are normal in schizophrenia

### Post-Stroke/Cancer/Chronic Illness Depression

- Common cause of underdiagnosed and untreated depression
- Early treatment with antidepressants +/- psychotherapy helps significantly

### Depression-related Cognitive Impairment

- Mimics dementia (previously called pseudodementia)
- Possibly sign of severe depression in elderly
- Reversible with antidepressants (SSRI) and psychotherapy

### MDD Treatment

- Suicide-risk assessment prior to treatment initiation
- 1st line: Antidepressant + CBT; ECT
- Partial response (25-50% improvement): Augment with Bupropion (activating, no weight gain or sexual dysfunction), different MOA antidepressant, 2nd gen antipsychotic (Aripiprazole), Lithium, T3, or psychotherapy
- Treatment resistance: Discontinue and switch to new class (Bupropion)
- Antidepressant-induced hypomania/mania: Screen for mania history before starting; discontinue immediately if symptoms appear; mood stabilizer/antipsychotic if persistent

### PHQ-9 Scoring

- 1-4: Minimal, 5-9: Mild, 10-14: Moderate, 15-19: Moderately severe, 20-27: Severe

### Treatment Duration

- Single-episode: Continue 6 months at remission dose, then taper if complete remission maintained
- >=2 lifetime episodes: Long-term maintenance (1-3 years)
- >=3 lifetime episodes, chronic (>=2 years), strong FH, severe (suicide attempt): Life-long continuation

## Persistent Depressive Disorder (Dysthymia)

- >=2 years with hopelessness, appetite/sleep/energy/concentration disturbance, low self-esteem
- Lacks guilt or suicidal ideation

## Atypical Depression

- Clinical features: Eat more, Sleep more, Mood reactivity (positive response to pleasant events), Hypersensitivity to criticism, Leaden paralysis ("arms and legs are heavy")
- Treatment: MAO-I, Light therapy

## Catatonia

- Most commonly occurs in context of mood disorder
- Clinical features: Immobility or excessive purposeless activity, Mutism, Stupor, Negativism (resistance to instructions), Waxy flexibility (initial resistance then maintenance of new posture), Echolalia, Echopraxia
- Dx: Lorazepam challenge test (IV Lorazepam -> partial temporary relief within 5-10 minutes)
- Treatment: Benzodiazepine (Lorazepam), ECT

## Persistent Complex Bereavement Disorder

- Prolonged grief >12 months
- Persistent yearning, Difficulty accepting death
- Treatment: Psychotherapy

## Adjustment Disorder with Depressed Mood

- Within 3 months of identifiable stressor
- Does not meet criteria for another disorder
- Treatment: Psychotherapy (targets maladaptive stress response)

## Suicidal Behavior

- Associated with low 5-hydroxyindoleacetic acid in CSF (primary metabolite of serotonin)
- RF: #1 History of prior suicide attempt, Active substance use (disinhibition), Plan for imminent risk -> involuntary admission
- Treatment: Lithium

## Medication-induced Mood Disorder

- Corticosteroid use: May lead to euphoria, irritability, restlessness, anxiety, sleep disturbance; prolonged use may lead to depression, hypomania, mania, psychosis, confusion

## Bipolar Depression Maintenance

- Lithium, Lamotrigine, Lurasidone, Quetiapine, Cariprazine, SSRI + Antipsychotic

## Postpartum

- Postpartum blues: Treatment = Reassurance and monitoring
- Postpartum depression: Treatment = SSRI (Sertraline preferred in breastfeeding)
  - Assessment includes questions regarding thoughts of suicide or harming the baby

## FDA-approved Bipolar Depression

- Lithium, Lurasidone (Latuda), Lamotrigine, Quetiapine (Seroquel), Olanzapine + Fluoxetine (Symbyax)

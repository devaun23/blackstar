# Learning and Developmental Disorders

## ADHD

- Dx: Onset of symptoms before age 12 with impairment in more than one setting (school and home)
- Clinical features
  - Inattentive and hyperactive/impulsive symptoms for >=6 months
  - Inattentive: Difficulty focusing, distractible, does not listen/follow instructions, disorganized, forgetful, loses objects
  - Hyperactive/impulsive: Fidgety, unable to sit still, "driven by a motor", hypertalkative, interrupts, blurts out answers
  - Subtypes: Predominantly inattentive (MC in Adults), Predominantly hyperactive/impulsive, Combined (MC in Children)

## Autism Spectrum Disorder

- Limited social interest
- Restricted interests
- Repetitive behavior

## Disruptive Mood Dysregulation Disorder (DMDD)

- Dx after age 6 (temper tantrums common in younger children)

## Global Developmental Delay

- Deficits in multiple areas of intellectual functioning

## Language Disorder

- Persistent difficulty in acquisition and use of language
- Clinical features
  - Difficulty explaining oneself in multiple settings
  - Inability to name colors or letters
  - Limited vocabulary, inaccurate sentence structure
  - Frustration expressed nonverbally: Temper tantrums, physical aggression
- Association: May lead to Specific Learning Disorder (requires standardized testing)

## Specific Learning Disorder

- Risk factors
  - Prematurity, Prenatal alcohol exposure
  - Poverty/understimulating environment
  - Psychiatric conditions: ADHD, Depression
  - Chronic medical/neurologic conditions: Diabetes, Seizures
  - Chromosomal disorders: Fragile X, Turner syndrome
  - Family history of learning disabilities
- Clinical features: Academic skills/achievement below intellectual potential
  - Come to attention with stress of increasing academic demands
  - Can contribute to negative self-concept, social anxiety, behavioral issues
  - Types: Reading (dyslexia), Writing, Math
- Evaluation and Management
  - Education testing
  - Hearing/vision assessment
  - Rule out psychiatric/medical disorders
  - Quality instruction/academic support/accommodations

## Childhood-onset Fluency Disorder (Stuttering)

- Impairment in fluency of speech production

## Social (Pragmatic) Communication Disorder

- Persistent difficulty in social use of verbal and nonverbal communication (eye contact, body language)

## Selective Mutism

- Patients do not speak in specific settings
- Dx: >=1 month failure to talk in certain situations (school) despite normal verbal communication elsewhere
- Association: Social Anxiety Disorder (fear of negative evaluation)
  - May lead to other anxiety disorders
- Treatment: CBT with graded exposure, Family therapy, SSRI

# Normal Development

## Imaginary Friend

- Common in preschool and early school-age years, decreases after age 6
- Provides comfort in times of distress or fear
- No evidence of social or academic dysfunction

## Separation Anxiety (Normal)

- Commonly develops at 9-18 months, resolves by age 2
- Recurs during transitions requiring separation (e.g., attending school)
  - Normal if child settles quickly and engages with other children
  - Further assessment if persistent anxiety/distress, repeated somatic complaints, or school refusal

## Stranger Anxiety

- Commonly develops at 6 months, peaks at 8-9 months, resolves by age 2
- Normally cry when unfamiliar person approaches even with primary caregiver present

# Ethics and Social Sciences

## Agitated/Nonviolent Patients in ER

- Maintain distance of 2 arm lengths; avoid direct eye contact
- Interview with door open, clinician closer to door
- Utilize verbal de-escalation including offering food or drink
- Chemical and physical restraints when verbal de-escalation fails and violence is imminent

## Confidentiality

- Adolescent nonsuicidal self-injury: Involve patient in creating plan to inform parents
- Exceptions to confidentiality: Suicidal/homicidal ideation, Potentially dangerous self-harm, Abuse, Neglect

## Informed Consent

- Adolescent: Parent-requested drug testing without adolescent's knowledge is not recommended
  - Instead ask for detailed behavioral changes and examine privately
  - Drug testing usually unnecessary when patients are forthcoming

## Medical Futility

- No Escalation of Treatment (NEOT) orders: Specify predetermined limits, minimize guilt
- Useful when family members are reluctant to withdraw care

## Professional Conduct — Gifts

- Not accepted from patients experiencing mood episodes due to change in judgement
- Acceptance depends on cost, type, timing, and motivation

## Treatment Adherence

- Involve patient in selecting medication formulation and dosing frequency from options

# Defense Mechanisms

## Immature

1. Acting out
2. Denial
3. Displacement: Transferring feelings to less threatening object/person
4. Intellectualization: Focusing on nonemotional aspects to avoid distressing feelings
5. Passive aggression
6. Projection
7. Rationalization
8. Reaction formation: Transforming unacceptable feelings into the opposite
9. Regression
10. Splitting

## Mature

1. Altruism
2. Sublimation: Channeling impulses into socially acceptable behaviors
3. Suppression: Putting unwanted feelings aside to cope with reality

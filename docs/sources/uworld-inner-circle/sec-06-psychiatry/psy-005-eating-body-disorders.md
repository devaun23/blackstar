# Eating Disorders

## Anorexia Nervosa

- Clinical features
  - BMI <18.5 kg/m2
  - Intense fear of gaining weight
  - Amenorrhea
  - Distorted views of body weight and shape
  - Dermatological: Hair loss, scaly skin, lanugo (fine downy hair)
  - Restricting subtype: Fast and/or exercise excessively
  - Purging subtype: Compensate with laxatives or vomiting
  - Euthyroid-sick syndrome: Low T3/T4 that normalizes with improved diet (thyroid replacement NOT recommended)
- RF: High-achieving, type-A personality; participation in activities (ballet, running)
- Treatment
  - CBT
  - Nutritional rehab
  - Severe/refractory: Olanzapine
- Indications for Hospitalization (Unstable vitals/electrolytes)
  - Bradycardia (<40/min), Dysrhythmia
  - Hypotension (<80/60), Orthostasis
  - Hypothermia
  - Electrolyte disturbance, marked dehydration
  - Organ compromise (Renal, Hepatic, Cardiac)
  - <70% expected weight (BMI <15 kg/m2)

## Bulimia Nervosa

- Recurrent binge eating episodes
- Clinical features
  - Binge eating with inappropriate compensatory behavior (vomiting, excessive exercise, starvation)
  - Excessive worrying about body weight/shape
  - Normal weight to overweight
  - Hypokalemia due to self-induced vomiting
- Treatment
  - CBT
  - Nutritional rehab
  - SSRI: Fluoxetine (reduces frequency of binging/purging)
  - Bupropion contraindicated (increased seizure risk)

## Binge-eating Disorder (Most Common Eating Disorder)

- >=1 episode per week of binge eating over 3 months
- Clinical features
  - Recurrent binge eating episodes
  - No inappropriate compensatory behaviors
  - Lack of control during eating
  - Eating alone due to embarrassment; distress, depression, disgust, or guilt after overeating
- Treatment: CBT, Behavioral weight loss therapy, SSRI, Lisdexamfetamine (DA/NE release), Topiramate

## Body Dysmorphic Disorder

- Clinical features
  - Preoccupation with >=1 perceived physical defects
  - Defects not observable or appear slight to others
  - Repetitive behaviors (mirror checking, concealing, excessive grooming, skin picking)
  - Significant distress or impairment
  - Variable insight (good, poor, absent/delusional beliefs)
- Approach
  - Acknowledge distress; do NOT refer to perceived flaws as "imagined"
  - Determine level of insight by exploring perceptions of how others see them
  - Reassurance about normal appearance rarely helpful
  - Focus on alleviating distress and functional impairment
  - Avoid inappropriate cosmetic/medical/surgical treatments
- Treatment: SSRI, CBT

## Pica

- Eating non-food substances

# Gender Dysmorphia

- Clinical features
  - Persistent (>=6 months) incongruence between assigned and felt gender
  - Desires to be another gender
  - Dislikes own anatomy, desires sexual traits of another gender
  - Feels significant distress/impairment
- Initial management
  - Assessment of safety
  - Support; psychotherapy
  - Referral to specialist services

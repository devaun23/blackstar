# Somatoform Disorders

## OARS Method

- Open-ended questions
- Affirming
- Reflective listening
- Summarizing

## Deception Absent

### Somatic Symptom Disorder

- >=1 unexplained symptoms
- Excessive thoughts, anxiety, and behaviors in response to symptoms
- Management: Regular visits every 4-8 weeks

### Illness Anxiety Disorder (formerly Hypochondriasis)

- >6 month duration
- Minimal to NO symptoms
- Preoccupation with idea of having a serious illness
- Treatment: Regular visits, limit diagnostic tests and referrals, focus on coping and functional improvement

### Conversion Disorder (Functional Neurologic Symptom Disorder)

- Neurologic symptoms incompatible with anatomy or pathophysiology
- Example: Psychogenic nonepileptic seizures (pseudoseizures)
  - Clinical features
    - Witnessed events during wakefulness
    - Long, fluctuating course
    - Asynchronous thrashing
    - No loss of consciousness or postictal period
  - Comorbidities: Depression, Anxiety, Physical/sexual abuse, Epilepsy
  - Dx: Video electroencephalogram (differentiate epileptic vs nonepileptic)
  - Treatment: CBT

## Deception Present (conscious production of symptoms)

### Factitious Disorder (primary gain)

- Falsification of symptoms/inducing injury in absence of obvious external rewards (gain more medical attention/treatment)
- Factitious Disorder Imposed on Self = Munchausen Syndrome
- Factitious Disorder Imposed on Another = Munchausen Syndrome by Proxy

### Malingering (secondary gain)

- Falsification of illness for obvious external rewards (avoiding work, financial compensation, obtaining drugs)
- Suspected when
  - Medicolegal context
  - Marked difference between claimed stress/disability and objective findings
  - Lack of cooperation during eval and in complying with regimen
  - Presence of antisocial personality disorder

# Intrapartum Fetal Assessment

## Biophysical Profile

1. Fetal breathing: >=1 breathing episode for >=30 seconds
2. Gross body movements: >3 general body movements
3. Fetal tone: >1 episode of extremity extension/flexion of fetal limbs or spine
4. Amniotic fluid index or single fluid pocket >=2cm x 1cm

### Oligohydramnios

- AFI <5cm or single fluid pocket <2cm
- Causes: Preeclampsia, Abruptio placentae, Uteroplacental insufficiency, Renal anomalies, NSAIDs
- Complications: Breech presentation, Meconium aspiration, Preterm delivery, Umbilical cord compression

### Polyhydramnios

- AFI >=24cm or single fluid pocket >=8cm
- Causes: Esophageal/duodenal atresia, Anencephaly, Multiple gestation, Congenital infection, DM (uterine-size discrepancy)
- Complications: Fetal malposition/Breech, Umbilical cord prolapse, Preterm labor, Preterm PROM
- Clinical features
  - Increased uterine size -> Abdominal discomfort
  - Maternal lung compression -> Dyspnea
  - Increased intrauterine pressure -> Preterm contractions
- Management
  - Asymptomatic at term: Expectant management (outcomes unchanged by intervention)
  - Symptomatic preterm: Increased risk for preterm labor and preterm prelabor ROM; may benefit from amnioreduction

### Non-stress Test (NST)

- Indication: Decreased fetal movement (hypoxemia/acidemia may cause compensatory blood flow diversion from extremities to CNS)

### BPP Scoring

- Five measurements, rated 0 or 2
- 8-10: Reassuring
- 6: Equivocal -> Deliver if >36wks, repeat BPP in 24hrs if <36wks
- <=4: Abnormal -> Fetal hypoxia -> Immediate intervention, prompt delivery

## Fetal Heart Rate Monitoring (FHR)

### Heart Rate

- Normal: 110-160 bpm
- Baseline FHR: HR during a 10-minute segment excluding periods of marked variability, periodic or episodic changes; minimum baseline duration >=2 minutes

### Reactivity

- 95% of fetuses reactive by 34 weeks

#### Reassuring

- HR 110-160 bpm
- Moderate variability
- >=2 Accelerations: 15 bpm increase lasting 15 seconds
  - Accelerations require mature sympathetic nervous system (develops at 26-28 weeks)
  - Extremely premature fetuses (<28 weeks) often do not demonstrate reactivity
- Early Deceleration
  - Coincide with uterine contraction ("mirror image")
  - Physiological: Pressure on fetal head -> vagus nerve stimulation -> reflex bradycardia

#### Non-reassuring

- Absent variability: Neuroaxial (epidural) anesthesia-caused hypotension
- Fetal Tachycardia: FHR >160 bpm
  - Not a sign of fetal distress if good variability present
  - Causes: Maternal fever, Amnionitis, Fetal hypoxia, Fetal anemia, Fetal tachyarrhythmia (>200 bpm with abrupt onset, little/no variability), SVT 200-240 bpm, Fetal heart failure
- Fetal Bradycardia: FHR <110 bpm for >10 minutes
  - Not a sign of compromise in absence of other non-reassuring patterns
  - Causes: Heart block, Occiput posterior/transverse position, Serious fetal compromise, Maternal beta-blockers

### Late Decelerations

- At least 15 bpm lasting >15 seconds post contraction
- Etiology: Uteroplacental insufficiency
- Causes
  - Excessive uterine contractions / Hyperstimulated uterus (inadequate recovery time)
  - Neuroaxial anesthesia-caused hypotension (Tx: Phenylephrine, Ephedrine)
  - Maternal hypotension, Maternal hypoxemia
  - Reduced placental exchange: Hypertensive disorder, Diabetes, IUGR, Abruption
  - Late-term pregnancy (>=41 weeks): Increased placental vascular resistance -> intermittent fetal hypoxemia
    - Increased risk of stillbirth due to placental aging
    - Progressive placental dysfunction -> Chronic hypoxemia = uteroplacental insufficiency -> CNS depression
    - To preserve CNS: Fetal blood distribution increases to brain, decreases to kidneys
    - Decreased urine production = Oligohydramnios (single deepest pocket <2cm)
    - Decreased blood supply to peripheral muscles -> Decreased fetal activity
- Management
  1. Place patient on side, discontinue oxytocin, correct hypotension (Phenylephrine/Ephedrine), IV hydration
  2. If late decelerations persist >30 minutes: Fetal scalp pH indicated (>7.25 reassuring)
  3. Perform BPP for further evaluation of fetal oxygenation
  4. BPP <=4: Immediate intervention, prompt delivery

### Variable Decelerations

- Appear as a "V", unrelated to contractions
- Sharp dip in FHR with quick recovery
- Etiology: Uterine contraction compresses umbilical cord
  - Cord compression increases fetal SVR and BP -> reflexive rapid decrease in FHR
  - May be due to change in maternal position compressing cord
  - ROM can lead to umbilical artery compression, oligohydramnios, fetal cord compression
- Causes: Oligohydramnios, Cord prolapse, Nuchal cords
- Recurrent: Umbilical cord compression with >50% contractions -> Hypoxemia and acidosis
- Management
  1. Shift maternal position or Amnioinfusion (for ruptured membranes)
  2. Amnioinfusion = instillation of saline into intrauterine cavity
- Note: Uterine rupture may cause Bradycardia, Variable decelerations (cord compression from ruptured uterine scar), and Late decelerations (fetal hypoxia)

### Sinusoidal Tracing

- Associated with increased morbidity/mortality and severe fetal anemia (severe hypoxia and Rh disease)
- Regular amplitude and frequency; excluded from definition of variability

### Variability

- Baseline variability: Fluctuations in FHR of more than 2 cycles per minute
- Persistently minimal or absent FHR variability = most significant intrapartum sign of fetal compromise
- Decreased variability causes: Fetal metabolic acidosis, CNS depressants, Fetal sleep cycles, Congenital anomalies, Prematurity, Fetal tachycardia, Hypoxia, Opioids, Magnesium
- Minimal variability: Fetal sleep state (<6 bpm from baseline) -> wait until baby wakes up

## Electronic Fetal Heart Rate Monitoring (EFM) Tracing Interpretation

### Acceleration

- Abrupt increase in FHR above baseline; onset to peak <30 seconds and <2 min duration
- Adequate acceleration (= Reactive NST)
  - <32 wks: >10 bpm above baseline for >10 seconds
  - >32 wks: >15 bpm above baseline for >15 seconds and <2 minutes
- Prolonged acceleration: Increase lasting 2-10 minutes
- Fetal scalp stimulation can induce accelerations (not used in decelerations)

### Deceleration Patterns

- Episodic: Not associated with uterine contractions
- Periodic: Associated with uterine contractions
  - Early: Gradual decrease, onset to nadir >30 seconds, nadir occurs with peak of contraction
  - Late: Gradual decrease, onset to nadir >30 seconds, onset occurs after contraction, nadir after peak
  - Variable: Abrupt decrease >15 bpm, onset to nadir <30 seconds, lasts >15 seconds and <2 minutes
- Types
  - Gradual: Decrease and return to baseline with onset to nadir >30 seconds
  - Abrupt: Decrease >15 bpm with onset to nadir <30 seconds

### Uterine Contractions

- Normal: <=5 contractions in 10-minute window averaged over 30 minutes
- Tachysystole: >5 contractions in 10 minutes
  - Contractions temporarily interrupt intervillous blood flow
  - Excessive contractions cause inadequate recovery time -> decreased uteroplacental blood flow
  - RF: Induced or augmented labor (uterotonic agents)
  - Complication: Uterine rupture
  - Management: Lateral repositioning, Tocolysis, Discontinue uterotonic agents until decelerations resolve

### EFM Categories

#### Category I: Normal

- All criteria for Reactive NST
  - Baseline FHR 110-160 bpm
  - Moderate FHR variability
  - +/- Accelerations, Early decelerations
  - No late or variable decelerations
- Strongly predictive of normal acid-base status; routine care

#### Category II: Indeterminate

- Not Category I or III
- Includes: Tachycardia, bradycardia without absent variability, minimal variability, absent variability without recurrent decelerations, marked variability, absence of accelerations after stimulation, recurrent variable decelerations with minimal/moderate variability, prolonged deceleration >2 min but <10 min, recurrent late decelerations with moderate variability, variable decelerations with slow return to baseline/"overshoot"
- Not predictive of abnormal fetal acid-base status; requires continued surveillance and reevaluation

#### Category III: Abnormal

- >=1 of: Sinusoidal pattern, Absent variability with recurrent late decelerations, Absent variability with recurrent variable decelerations, Absent variability with bradycardia
- Complications: Increased risk of fetal hypoxia, hypoxic brain injury, demise
- Management
  1. Maternal repositioning
  2. Amnioinfusion (if variable decelerations)
  3. Improve uteroplacental blood flow: Oxygen, IV fluids, D/C uterotonics
  4. C-section if not completely dilated (10cm)
  5. Operative vaginal delivery if complete cervical dilation

# PREGNANCY DISORDERS

## Uterine Size-Date Discrepancy
- Causes: Incorrect dating, Multiple gestation, Polyhydramnios, Molar pregnancy, Fibroids, GDM

## Ectopic Pregnancy
1. 75% Ampulla; Cornual/Interstitial: Life-threatening
2. CF: Elevated hCG, Abdominal pain, Amenorrhea, Vaginal bleeding; Rupture → Hypovolemic shock
3. RF: Previous ectopic, Prior tubal surgery, PID, IVF
4. Evaluation: (+) hCG → TVUS
   - Adnexal mass → Methotrexate (if stable); Intrauterine pregnancy → Reassurance
   - Non-diagnostic → Serum hCG: <3500 repeat in 48hrs; >3500 repeat + TVUS
   - <35% hCG rise in 48hrs = Ectopic or nonviable; >35-50% rise = Normal early pregnancy
5. Methotrexate CI: Immunosuppression, Liver disease, Pulmonary disease, Breastfeeding
6. Unstable → Surgical: Diagnostic laparoscopy + Salpingectomy

## Molar Pregnancy
1. RF: Extremes of maternal age, Prior mole, Vitamin A deficiency
2. hCG >100,000; US: "Snowstorm"; Theca lutein cysts; Hyperemesis; Preeclampsia <20wks; Hyperthyroidism
3. Tx: D&C; Monitor hCG declining levels for 6 months (rising = GTN → Methotrexate)

## Spontaneous Abortion

| Type | Cervical Os | Fetal Activity | US | Management |
|------|-------------|---------------|-----|------------|
| Threatened | Closed | (+) Cardiac | Normal | Observation |
| Missed | Closed | (-) Cardiac | No fetal pole | D&C or Misoprostol |
| Inevitable | Open | - | Nonviable in lower uterus | D&C or Misoprostol |
| Incomplete | Open | - | Partial POC | D&C or Misoprostol |
| Complete | Closed | - | Empty cavity | Observation, Serial hCG |

## Cervical Insufficiency
- RF: Cervical conization/LEEP, Mechanical dilation, Obstetric injury, Ehlers-Danlos, Uterine anomalies
- CF: Painless dilation, Pelvic pressure, Light bleeding, Increased discharge (no contractions)
- Dx: ≥2 prior painless 2nd TM losses OR Shortened cervix <25mm + prior preterm
- Tx: Cerclage suture; Vaginal Progesterone for shortened cervix

## Hyperemesis Gravidarum
- RF: Molar pregnancy, Multifetal gestation; CF: Severe vomiting, >5% weight loss, Ketonuria
- Labs: Hypokalemic Hypochloremic Metabolic Alkalosis

## HYPERTENSIVE DISORDERS

### Chronic HTN: >140/90 before conception or <20wks
- Tx: Labetalol, Nifedipine ER, Hydralazine, Methyldopa; CI: ACEi, ARB, Nitroprusside

### Gestational HTN: New-onset elevated BP >20wks without proteinuria/end-organ damage

### Preeclampsia
1. New-onset BP >140/90 at >20wks + Proteinuria OR End-organ damage
2. MC symptom: Severe HA (bilateral occipital/frontal, no NSAID/Acetaminophen relief)
3. RF: Prior preeclampsia, CKD, Chronic HTN, T1DM, Multiple gestation, SLE
4. Severe Features (≥1): SBP>160/DBP>110, Plt<100K, Cr>1.1, Elevated transaminases, Ankle clonus, Hemolysis, Pulmonary edema, Vision/Cerebral symptoms
5. Pulmonary edema: #1 cause in pregnancy due to arterial vasospasm + endothelial damage + decreased renal function
6. Management: <37wks no severe → Expectant; ≥37wks or ≥34wks severe → Delivery
   - Severe BP: IV Labetalol (avoid in bradycardia), IV Hydralazine, PO Nifedipine
   - Seizure prophylaxis: Mg Sulfate (also fetal neuroprotection at 24-32wks)

### Eclampsia
- Preeclampsia + Tonic-clonic seizures; CT: Bilateral frontal lobe edema
- Tx: #1 Mg Sulfate → Antihypertensives → Delivery (curative)

### HELLP
1. Thrombocytopenia (platelet consumption) + MAHA (rbc shearing) + Elevated Transaminases (hepatic ischemia)
2. RUQ pain (Glisson capsule inflammation); Tx: Delivery + Mg Sulfate + Hydralazine
3. Complications: Abruption, Subcapsular liver hematoma, Renal failure, DIC

## CONGENITAL DEFECTS
### CMV: Close contact with young children; US: Periventricular calcifications, Microcephaly, Ventriculomegaly, FGR, Hydrops
### HIV: ART ASAP; Viral load <1000 → Vaginal; >1000 → C-section; Infant: Zidovudine or Multi-ART
### HSV: Antiviral at 36wks; (+) Lesion at labor → C-section; (-) Lesion → Vaginal
### Parvovirus B19: Fetal hydrops (HOHF from severe anemia); Ascites, Skin edema, Large placenta
### Syphilis: IM Penicillin G; Penicillin allergy → Desensitization; Screen at 1st visit + 3rd TM
### Toxoplasmosis: RF: Raw meat, Cat feces; Bilateral ventriculomegaly, Diffuse calcifications; Tx: Pyrimethamine + Sulfadiazine
### VZV: Growth restriction, Limb abnormalities, Cortical atrophy
### Congenital Zika: Microcephaly, Craniosynostosis, Spasticity; Aedes mosquito or sexual transmission

### Neural Tube Defects
- RF: Low folic acid, Methotrexate, Antiepileptics, DM
- Screening: 2nd TM MSAFP; Elevated → US; Prevention: Average risk 0.4mg, High risk 4.0mg folic acid

### Trisomy 21 (Down): Low AFP/Estriol, High hCG/Inhibin; Thickened nuchal fold; CAVSD
### Trisomy 18 (Edwards): Low AFP/Estriol/hCG; Rocker-bottom feet, Clenched hands
### Trisomy 13 (Patau): Holoprosencephaly, Midline defects

## AUTOIMMUNE
### SLE in Pregnancy
- RF for flare: Pregnancy, Postpartum, Hydroxychloroquine discontinuation
- Complications: Preterm birth, Preeclampsia, FGR, Fetal demise
- Neonatal Lupus: Anti-SSA/SSB → AV block (ventricular rate = bradycardia), Scalp/periorbital rash

## Fetal Growth Restriction
- Symmetric (1st TM): Chromosomal, Congenital infection
- Asymmetric (2/3rd TM): Uteroplacental insufficiency (HTN, DM), Tobacco, Malnutrition; "Head-sparing"
- Management: Weekly BPP, Serial Umbilical artery Doppler, Serial growth US

## Intrahepatic Cholestasis of Pregnancy
- 3rd TM pruritis worst on hands/feet (no rash); Elevated total bile acids >10; Risk: IUFD if >100
- Tx: Delivery at 37wks; Ursodeoxycholic acid

## Acute Fatty Liver of Pregnancy
- 3rd TM fulminant liver failure; Profound hypoglycemia ~40, Elevated transaminases, DIC
- Management: IMMEDIATE DELIVERY

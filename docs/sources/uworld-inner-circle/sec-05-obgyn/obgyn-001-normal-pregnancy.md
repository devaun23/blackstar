# NORMAL PREGNANCY, CHILDBIRTH, PUERPERIUM

## REPRODUCTION
### Hormones
1. hCG (syncytiotrophoblast): Appears 10 days post-fertilization, Peaks 9-10wks, Maintains corpus luteum
   - High: Twins, Hydatidiform mole, Choriocarcinoma; Low: Ectopic, Threatened/Missed abortion
2. Human Placental Lactogen: Antagonizes insulin → predisposes to glucose intolerance/GDM
3. Progesterone: 1st 6-7wks Corpus luteum → 7-9wks Luteal-placental shift → Post-9wks Placenta
4. Estrogen: Estradiol (reproductive), Estriol (pregnancy/placenta), Estrone (menopause/adipose)

### Fetal Circulation Shunts
- Ductus Venosus (UV→IVC), Foramen Ovale (RA→LA), Ductus Arteriosus (PA→Desc Aorta)

### Lactation
- Prolactin → milk production; Oxytocin (suckling reflex) → milk ejection; Colostrum first (IgA)
- Placenta expulsion → drop in estrogen/progesterone → initiates milk production

## PREGNANCY

### Adaptations
- Cardiac: Inc CO, Inc Plasma Volume, Dec SVR (Progesterone vasodilation → Dec BP in first 20wks)
- Respiratory: Inc TV (Progesterone), Dec FRC; Respiratory Alkalosis with compensatory low HCO3
- Endocrine 1st TM: Inc Total T4, Mild inc Free T4, Dec TSH (hCG stimulates TSH-R)
- Renal: Hydronephrosis (Progesterone → ureteral dilation; Uterus compresses ureters)
- Clinical: Peripheral edema, Inc HR, Systolic ejection murmur, Nocturnal leg cramps

### Prenatal Visits
1. Initial: Hb/Hct, Rh type + Ab screen, HIV/HBsAg/VDRL, Chlamydia/Gonorrhea, Rubella/Varicella immunity, Urine culture, Pap
2. 12-16wks: Screening for bacteriuria (treat asymptomatic → prevent pyelonephritis)
3. 24-28wks: Hb/Hct, Ab screen if Rh(-), 1hr 50g GCT
   - Abnormal GCT >140 → 3hr 100g GTT; 2+ abnormal values → GDM
   - GDM Tx: 1st Dietary modification → 2nd Insulin (does not cross placenta)
4. 28-32wks: Rhogam (if Rh- and baby Rh+); Again within 72hrs postpartum
5. 36-38wks: GBS culture; Prior HSV → Antiviral suppression (Acyclovir/Valacyclovir)

### Rhogam Indications
- 28-32wks + within 72hrs postpartum; Also: Ectopic, Spontaneous abortion, Amniocentesis, Trauma, Bleeding
- KB test quantifies fetomaternal hemorrhage to determine dose

### Gestational Age
- 1st TM US crown-rump length most accurate (±3-5 days between 7-14wks)
- After 20wks: Fundal height in cm ≈ weeks (±2-3cm)

### Amniotic Fluid
- Oligohydramnios (<5cm): Renal anomalies, Preeclampsia, PROM, NSAIDs
  - Complications: Breech, Meconium aspiration, Cord compression
- Polyhydramnios (≥24cm): Esophageal/duodenal atresia, Anencephaly, DM, Multiple gestation
  - Complications: Malpresentation, Cord prolapse, Preterm labor

## CHILDBIRTH

### Labor Stages
1. First: Latent (0-6cm slow) → Active (>6cm, >1cm/2hr)
   - Active protraction (<1cm/2hr): CPD or Inadequate contractions → Oxytocin augmentation
   - Active arrest (no change >4hrs adequate or >6hrs inadequate contractions) → C-section
2. Second: 10cm to delivery; Arrest: >3hrs nulliparous or >2hrs multiparous
3. Third: Placenta delivery
4. Fourth: Recovery

### Fetal Presentation
- Optimal: Occiput Anterior
- Breech: RF: Polyhydramnios, Oligohydramnios, FGR, Multiparity, Placenta previa, AMA, Uterine anomalies
  - Management: ECV at >37wks if no contraindications; C-section if ECV fails or contraindicated
- Vaginal delivery contraindicated: Prior Classical C-section, Placenta previa, Prior extensive myomectomy

### Anesthesia
- Epidural complications: Lengthened 2nd stage, LAST (seizures), HYPOTENSION (sympathetic blockade → vasodilation)
  - Tx for hypotension: Left uterine displacement, IV bolus, Vasopressor
  - Postdural puncture HA: Worse sitting/standing; Tx: Epidural blood patch

## POSTPARTUM
### Normal Findings
1. Transient rigors/chills (Dec Estrogen/Progesterone → 1st 24hrs)
2. Lochia rubra: Endometrial shedding up to 8 weeks
3. Uterine involution: Firm, palpable 1-2cm around umbilicus
4. Breast engorgement: Increased Prolactin; Suckling increases Prolacin + Oxytocin
5. Stress incontinence: Pudendal nerve injury; Conservative if <6wks postpartum

## PROCEDURES
1. Cell-free fetal DNA (≥10wks): 99% sensitivity; Screens for Trisomy 21/18/13 + sex chromosome
2. Chorionic villus sampling (1st TM): Fetal karyotyping
3. Quad test (2nd TM): AFP (NTD), Trisomy 18/21
   - Down: Low AFP, Low Estriol, High hCG, High Inhibin
   - Edwards: Low AFP, Low Estriol, Low hCG, Normal Inhibin
4. Amniocentesis (≥15wks): Fetal karyotype; Complication: Preterm labor/PPROM
5. Amniotomy: Not routinely performed; TAUS before to avoid cord prolapse

## FETAL HEART RATE MONITORING

### Reassuring (Reactive NST)
- Baseline 110-160, ≥2 accelerations (15bpm x 15sec), Moderate variability (6-25bpm)
- Early deceleration: Head compression during contractions → normal

### Non-Reassuring
- Tachycardia (>160): Chorioamnionitis, Fetal anemia, Maternal hyperthyroidism, Medications
- Bradycardia (<100 for >10min): Heart block, Maternal BB, Serious compromise
- Late Deceleration: Placental insufficiency → Uterine tachysystole; Tx: Lateral repositioning, Stop Oxytocin
- Variable Deceleration: Cord compression; Tx: Amnioinfusion
- Absent/Minimal Variability: Most significant sign of fetal compromise
- SINUSOIDAL: FETAL ANEMIA

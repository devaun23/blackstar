# GYNECOLOGY

## AMENORRHEA

### Primary (No menarche >13 without 2nd sexual development, or >15 with development)
1. Uterus Present + High FSH → Karyotype (Turner Syndrome)
2. Uterus Present + Low FSH → Cranial MRI
3. Uterus Absent + 46,XX + Normal Female Testosterone → Mullerian Agenesis (Normal ovaries/estrogen, Absent uterus/FT)
4. Uterus Absent + 46,XY + Male Testosterone → Androgen Insensitivity (Excess T→Estrogen, Breast development, No axillary/pubic hair, Cryptorchid testes)
5. 5-alpha-reductase deficiency (46,XY): Male internal, Female external; Virilization at puberty; No breast development
6. Imperforate Hymen: Cyclic pain without bleeding; Blue-tinged bulging vaginal mass; Tx: Incision
7. Turner Syndrome (45,X): Streak ovaries → Low Estrogen → High FSH/LH; Normal adrenarche; Horseshoe kidneys, Coarctation

### Secondary (No menses >3mo regular or >6mo irregular)
1. PCOS: Increased androgens (Theca cells) → High LH/normal FSH → Anovulation → Unopposed Estrogen
   - Dx: 2 of 3 (Anovulatory oligomenorrhea, Polycystic ovaries, Hyperandrogenism)
   - Tx: Weight loss, OCP (prevents unopposed estrogen), Spironolactone (hirsutism), Letrozole (ovulation), Clomiphene (infertility)
   - Complications: Infertility, Metabolic syndrome, Endometrial hyperplasia/cancer
2. Primary Ovarian Insufficiency (<40yo): Elevated FSH; Causes: Turner, Fragile X (FMR1 premutation), Autoimmune, Chemo, Radiation
3. Functional Hypothalamic: Caloric deficiency/strenuous exercise → Dec GnRH → Dec LH/FSH → Dec Estrogen → Bone loss
4. Asherman Syndrome: Intrauterine adhesions from D&C or endometritis; Normal hormones; No withdrawal bleed with Progesterone
5. Prolactinoma: Elevated Prolactin inhibits GnRH
6. Hypothyroidism: Low T3/T4 → Inc TRH → Prolactin release → inhibits GnRH

## MENSTRUAL DISORDERS
- Menorrhagia: Intramural fibroids; Tx: Tranexamic acid (desire fertility), Progestin-IUD (contraception), Endometrial ablation
- Primary Dysmenorrhea: Excess PG → uterine ischemia; Sexually inactive → NSAIDs; Sexually active → OCP
- PMS/PMDD: Luteal phase symptoms; Tx: SSRI if >2 cycles

## CONTRACEPTION
### Combined OCP
- Benefits: Reduce Endometrial/Ovarian cancer risk
- Absolute CI: Migraine with aura, Severe HTN, Ischemic heart disease, Thrombophilia, >35yo smoker >15/day, Active breast cancer, Severe liver disease
### Progestin-Only (Mini-pill, Injection, IUD)
- Medroxyprogesterone injection: Adolescents with disabilities; SE: Weight gain
### IUD
- Copper (Paraguard): Non-hormonal, Spermicidal; CI: Menorrhagia; Best emergency contraception
- Levonorgestrel (Mirena): Thickened mucus, Thin endometrium; Use: Menorrhagia
- Insertion CI: Active pelvic infection
### Emergency: Copper IUD >99% effective; Ulipristal (progesterone receptor blocker)

## BREAST
- Cyclic Mastalgia: Bilateral, 2wks pre-menses; No eval needed; Tx: Supportive bra, NSAIDs
- Galactorrhea: Hyperprolactinemia (antipsychotics, OCPs); Bilateral → Serum Prolactin + TSH
- Intraductal Papilloma: MCC bloody nipple discharge; Unilateral bloody; Tx: US + Biopsy + Excision
- Fibroadenoma (<30yo): Rubbery, increases with hormones; Dx: US + FNA
- Breast Cancer RF: BRCA1/2, Dense tissue, Nulliparity, OCP, Radiation; Screening: Mammogram (>40yo yearly if BRCA+)

## OVARIAN MASSES
- Premenopausal: Pregnancy test + Pelvic US; Malignant features → Laparoscopy; Benign → NSAIDs + Repeat US 6wks
- Postmenopausal: Pelvic US → Malignant features → Surgery; Benign → CA-125 → Elevated → Surgery; Normal → Observation
- Ovarian Torsion: Sudden severe unilateral pain + Nausea; Absent Doppler flow; RF: Mass >5cm; Tx: Laparoscopy

### Epithelial (CA-125): Serous/Mucinous Cystadenoma; Epithelial Ovarian Carcinoma (solid, thick septations, ascites)
### Germ Cell (LDH, hCG, AFP): Dysgerminoma; Mature Teratoma (dermoid, prone to torsion); Yolk Sac (AFP)
### Sex Cord-Stromal:
- Granulosa Cell Tumor: Inc Estradiol + Inhibin; AUB, Breast tenderness; Complication: Endometrial carcinoma
- Sertoli-Leydig: Inc Testosterone; Rapid virilization, Amenorrhea; ELEVATED TESTOSTERONE, NORMAL DHEAS

## UTERINE CONDITIONS
1. Adenomyosis: Endometrium in myometrium; >40yo multiparous; Dense, boggy uterus; Tx: OCP/IUD or Hysterectomy
2. Fibroids: Monoclonal myocyte proliferation; Heavy menses, Pelvic pain; Growth ↑ with Estrogen; Near menopause → Regression
   - Submucosal → Infertility (impairs implantation); Tx: Hysteroscopic myomectomy
   - Pedunculated: Cervical dilation, "Beefy red tissue at os"
   - Pregnancy: Rapid growth → Outgrow blood supply → Degeneration (pain, tenderness, leukocytosis)
3. PID: RF: Multiple partners, 15-25yo; CF: Cervical motion/uterine/adnexal tenderness; Tx: Ceftriaxone + Doxycycline

## ENDOMETRIAL CONDITIONS
1. Endometriosis: Retrograde menstruation → ectopic implants → inflammation/fibrosis
   - CF: Chronic cyclical pelvic pain, Dyspareunia, Dyschezia, INFERTILITY, Laterally displaced cervix
   - Dx: Diagnostic laparoscopy + Biopsy; Tx: OCP/NSAIDs → GnRH agonist (Leuprolide) → Surgery
2. Endometrial Hyperplasia: UNOPPOSED ESTROGEN (Obesity, PCOS, Tamoxifen, Nulliparity)
   - Tx: Progestin-releasing IUD or Hysterectomy; Repeat biopsy q3mo
3. Endometrial Carcinoma: Same RF as hyperplasia; Tx: TAH-BSO; Smoking DECREASES risk
4. AUB Evaluation: <45yo → Medical management (OCP) → Failed → Endometrial biopsy; Postmenopausal → TVUS (<4mm observe, >4mm biopsy)

## CERVIX
1. Acute Cervicitis: Chlamydia/Gonorrhea; Mucopurulent discharge, Friable cervix; Tx: Ceftriaxone + Doxycycline
2. HPV: Cervical/Vulvar/Vaginal/Anal cancer + Warts; Vaccine ages 11-12
3. CIN 3: Tx: LEEP, Conization; (+) Margins → Repeat conization (fertility) or Hysterectomy
4. Cervical Cancer: Exophytic mass, Painless ulcers, Irregular bleeding; RF: HPV, Tobacco, Early sexual activity
5. Screening: <21yo none; 21-29 Pap alone; 30-65 Pap + HPV cotesting

## VAGINAL/VULVAR
- Vulvovaginal Candidiasis: Pruritus, Thick white discharge, Normal pH; RF: Abx, DM; Tx: Clotrimazole/Fluconazole
- Vaginismus: Pain with any penetration; Tx: CBT, Pelvic floor PT, Vaginal dilators
- Lichen Sclerosis: Intense pruritis, WHITE VULVAR PLAQUES, Lichenification; RF: Autoimmune; Tx: Clobetasol
- Lichen Planus: Erosive lace-like lesions (Wickham striae); Dyspareunia; Tx: High-potency topical CST
- Vulvar Carcinoma: HPV 16/18; Single raised plaque/ulcer; RF: Lichen sclerosis; Dx: Biopsy

## PELVIC ORGAN PROLAPSE
- Cystocele (anterior), Uterine prolapse (apical), Rectocele (posterior — constipation, splinting to defecate)
- RF: #1 Multiparity; Tx: Pelvic floor exercises, Pessary, Surgical repair

## URINARY INCONTINENCE
1. Stress: Urethral hypermobility (weak pelvic floor); Small spurts with cough/sneeze; Tx: Pelvic floor strengthening, Midurethral slings
2. Urge/Overactive Bladder: Uninhibited detrusor contraction; Large volume loss; Tx: Oxybutynin (anticholinergic), Mirabegron (B3 agonist)
3. Overflow: Underactive detrusor or Outlet obstruction; Elevated PVR; Tx: Alpha blockers (Tamsulosin), Bethanechol
4. Urethral Diverticulum: Urethral mucosa herniation; Dysuria, Postvoid dribbling, Anterior vaginal wall mass

## FISTULAS
- Rectovaginal: RF: 3rd-4th degree lacerations; Uncontrollable gas/feces from vagina
- Vesicovaginal: MCC worldwide: Obstructed labor; Painless continuous urine leakage; Dx: Bladder dye test

## MENOPAUSE
1. Cessation of menses >12mo; ~50yo; Dec Estrogen from follicle depletion
2. CF: Hot flashes, Vulvovaginal atrophy (pale mucosa, petechiae, elevated pH >5, Shortened vagina, Dyspareunia), Urinary incontinence
3. Dx: Elevated FSH; Tx: Topical vaginal Estrogen for vulvovaginal atrophy; HRT for osteoporosis
4. HRT SE: Breast cancer risk (Estrogen + Progesterone > Estrogen alone)

## INFERTILITY
1. Definition: <35yo → 12mo; >35yo → 6mo unprotected
2. Evaluation: Male semen analysis first → Tubal patency (Hysterosalpingogram) → Ovulatory function (Day 21 Progesterone) → Ovarian reserve (Day 3 FSH)
3. Causes: Diminished ovarian reserve (normal cycle, decreased fecundability, Elevated FSH); Intrauterine adhesions; PCOS
4. Ovulation induction: Clomiphene (SERM, increases FSH/LH); Complication: Ovarian Hyperstimulation Syndrome (Ascites, Pleural effusions, Hypercoagulable)

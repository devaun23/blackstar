# Parenteral Nutrition

1. ABG: Non-anion gap metabolic acidosis

## Mineral Deficiency (especially patients on parenteral nutrition)

1. Chromium: Impaired glucose control in diabetic
2. Copper: Brittle hair, Skin depigmentation, Neurological (ataxia, peripheral neuropathy), Anemia, Osteoporosis
   1. Hypochromic microcytic anemia (impaired iron absorption), Brittle hair
   2. Skin depigmentation, Osteoporosis
   3. Neurological dysfunction (ataxia, peripheral neuropathy similar to subacute combined degeneration of B12 def)
   4. Cause
      1. Gastric surgery — bariatric surgery
      2. Excessive Zinc — competes with copper
   5. Dx
      1. Low serum copper and ceruloplasmin levels
3. Iron: Microcytic anemia
4. Selenium: Thyroid dysfunction, CM, Immune dysfunction
5. Zinc (duodenum, jejunum): Hypogonadism, impaired taste, impaired wound healing, alopecia, skin rash with perianal involvement

# Candidemia

1. MCC: Candida albicans
2. RF: Central venous catheters, GI surgery/perforation, Intensive care unit stays, Immunocompromised, TPN
3. Blood culture: Budding yeast
4. Management
   1. Empiric antifungals:
      1. Echinocandins: Micafungin
   2. Source control (remove central venous catheter)
      1. Candida produces glycocalyx — protective, sticky biofilm that prevents eradication
   3. Evaluation of metastatic infection
      1. All patients with candidemia require ophthalmologic consultation for endophthalmitis evaluation
         1. Epi
            1. Transient/persistent fungemia --> hematogenous dissemination to the choroid --> invasion of retina and fluid chambers
         2. RF: Central venous catheters, GI surgery/perforation, Intensive care unit stays, Immunocompromised, TPN, BS Antibiotic use
         3. CF
            1. Unilateral floaters and progressive vision loss
            2. Pain rare until late
            3. Fever may be absent and patient may otherwise feel well
         4. Dx
            1. Blood cultures (often negative)
            2. Vitreous fluid sampling and culture
         5. Tx
            1. Systemic antifungal therapy
            2. Vitrectomy + vitreous injection (if fluid chamber involved)
      2. Echocardiogram required for persistent positive blood cultures to rule out infective endocarditis

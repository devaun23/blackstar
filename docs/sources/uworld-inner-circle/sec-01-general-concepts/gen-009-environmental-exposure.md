# Arsenic poisoning

1. PP:
   1. Binds to sulfhydryl groups
   2. Disrupts cellular respiration and gluconeogenesis
2. Sources:
   1. Pesticides/insecticides
   2. Contaminated water often from wells
   3. Pressure-treated wood (fence worker)
3. CF:
   1. Acute:
      1. Garlic breath, vomiting, watery diarrhea, QTc prolongation
      2. Profuse — Pancytopenia, Hepatitis
   2. Chronic:
      1. Hypo/hyperpigmentation
      2. Hyperkeratosis — characteristic are Mees lines (horizontal striation of fingernails)
      3. Stocking-glove neuropathy — burning, painful hypersensitivity; distal weakness; hyporeflexia
4. Tx: Chelation
   1. Dimercaprol (British anti-Lewisite)
   2. DMSA (Meso-2,3-dimercaptosuccinic acid, succimer)

# Anticholinergic Toxicity

1. Etiology: Competitive inhibition of muscarinic ACh receptors in CNS, ciliary body, heart, smooth muscle, secretory glands
2. RF
   1. Antihistamines: Diphenhydramine, Cyproheptadine, Doxylamine most readily available
      1. May lead to abrupt, rapidly progressive memory impairment in elderly
   2. Psychotropics:
      1. 1st gen: Chlorpromazine, Haloperidol
      2. 2nd gen: Clozapine
      3. TCA: Amitriptyline, Clomipramine
   3. Antiparkinson: Benztropine, Trihexyphenidyl
   4. Antimuscarinic:
      1. Ipratropium (bronchodilator)
      2. Tiotropium
      3. Oxybutynin, Darifenacin (overactive bladder)
      4. Dicyclomine, Hyoscyamine (antispasmodics)
      5. Scopolamine (antiemetic)
   5. Mydriatics: Atropine, Cyclopentolate
   6. Plant-based: Muscarinic mushroom species, Jimson weed, Nightshade species
3. CF
   1. Hallucinations
   2. Sympathetic hyperactivity: Mydriasis, Hyperthermia, Tachycardia
   3. Dry skin and mucous membranes
   4. Ileus, urinary retention
4. Further Work up
   1. Electrocardiogram to evaluate for QRS interval or QTc prolongation
5. Dx
   1. Based on clinical findings, no confirmatory laboratory tests
6. Tx
   1. Supportive
   2. Activated charcoal
   3. Benzo for agitation and seizures
   4. Physostigmine (AChEi) for severe central and peripheral symptoms

# Bites

## Clenched-fist bite injury "fight bite"

1. Puncture (tooth) through soft tissue overlying MCP joint, can result in septic arthritis
   1. Joint pain, erythema, swelling, fluctuant, painful range of motion
2. Dx: All septic joints require drainage
   1. Larger joints are drained via arthrocentesis or arthroscopy
   2. Smaller ones via surgical irrigation and debridement
3. Tx: Urgent surgical irrigation and debridement and antibiotic therapy
   1. IV antibiotics (ampicillin/sulbactam) after joint cultures
   2. Wound is left open to drain and heal by secondary intention

## Snake bite

1. Antivenom
   1. Crotalidae polyvalent immune Fab — used with high risk only due to anaphylaxis risk

## Spider bite

1. Loxosceles reclusa — brown recluse
   1. Small, red papule that can form larger necrotic wound
   2. Complication: Necrosis and eschar
   3. Found in warm, dry areas like attics, woodpiles, cellars
   4. Most resolve with local wound care and cold packs (decrease venom phospholipases that cause tissue necrosis)

# Caustic ingestion

1. Injury to upper aero digestive tract — pain, dysphagia, oropharyngeal erythema and ulcerations
2. Conscious and alert patient

# Exercise associated collapse

1. Occurs in conditioned athletes immediately after cessation of exercise due to sudden decrease in cardiac preload
2. Tx: Oral hydration, Trendelenburg positioning

# Heat exhaustion

1. Similar symptoms to Heat stroke but lacks neurological symptoms

# Exertional Heat Stroke

1. RF:
   1. Strenuous activity during hot and humid weather
   2. Elderly (impaired thermoregulatory capacity)
   3. Dehydration, poor acclimatization
   4. Lack of physical fitness, obesity
   5. Meds:
      1. Anticholinergics
      2. Antihistamines "Allergy meds"
         1. Working outside in hot weather worsens symptoms
         2. Impaired ability to thermoregulate through sweat and vasodilation
      3. Phenothiazine
      4. TCA
      5. Antipsychotics: Quetiapine
2. CF:
   1. Core T >40C (104F) with CNS dysfunction (AMS, seizures)
      1. Warm, dry skin
      2. Hypernatremia
   2. Organ or tissue damage:
      1. Renal or hepatic failure, DIC, ARDS
      2. Rhabdomyolysis (prolonged unconsciousness): positive urine blood dipstick with few rbc
3. Management:
   1. Rapid cooling: ice water immersion
   2. Fluids, electrolyte correction — No role for antipyretic therapy

# Hypothermia (Accidental)

1. Classification
   1. Mild: 32-35C (90-95F)
      1. Tachypnea, Tachycardia
      2. Ataxia, dysarthria, increased shivering
   2. Moderate: 28-32C (82-90F)
      1. Bradycardia, lethargy, hypoventilation, decreased shivering, atrial arrhythmia
   3. Severe: <28C (82F)
      1. Coma, CV collapse, ventricular arrhythmia
2. Labs: Metabolic acidosis due to lactic acidosis
3. Tx
   1. General: Warmed (107F) crystalloid for hypotension, Endotracheal intubation in comatose
   2. Rewarming:
      1. Mild: Passive external warming (remove wet clothing, cover with blankets)
      2. Moderate: Active external warming (warm blankets, heating pads, warm baths)
      3. Severe: Active internal warming (warmed pleural or peritoneal irrigation, warmed humidified oxygen)

# Frostbite

1. CF
   1. Superficial pallor and anesthesia
   2. Blistering, eschar formation
   3. Deep tissue necrosis and mummification
2. Management
   1. Rapid rewarming in water bath 98.6-102.2F (37-39C)
   2. Analgesia and wound care
   3. Angiography or Tech99 scan to assess for thrombosis if rewarming is unsuccessful
   4. Thrombolysis in severe, limb-threatening cases

# Lead poisoning

1. RF: Occupational exposure — battery manufacturing, plumbing, home restoration, distillation of alcohol through parts with lead soldering
2. CF
   1. GI: Abdominal pain, constipation, anorexia
   2. Neuro:
      1. Short-term memory loss "forgetful"
      2. Peripheral neuropathy — Weakness with dorsiflexion of wrists and feet
   3. Heme: Microcytic anemia 99% of lead bound to erythrocytes and impairs heme production
   4. Hyperuricemia (impaired purine metabolism)
3. Lab
   1. Anemia
   2. inc Venous lead level
   3. inc Serum Zinc protoporphyrin level
   4. Basophilic stippling on peripheral smear
   5. Impaired purine metabolism — Hyperuricemia
4. Dx: Blood lead levels, CBC with blood smear, x-ray fluorescence to measure bone lead concentration
5. Tx: Chelation — Calcium disodium EDTA

# Malignant Hyperthermia (within 24 hours)

1. Genetic mutation alters control of intracellular calcium
2. Triggered by succinylcholine, inhalation gases and excessive heat
   1. Calcium accumulation causes muscle rigidity and increased cellular metabolism --> Hypercarbia resistant to increased minute Ventilation and Tachypnea
   2. Sustained muscle contraction, Tachycardia, Myoglobinuria
   3. Hyperthermia (later, sustained contractions generating more energy than can be dissipated)
3. Tx: Dantrolene

# Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome

1. RF: Antidopaminergic drugs (Antipsychotics, Metoclopramide)
   1. #1 cause: Dopamine antagonism
      1. Central dopamine receptor blockade in hypothalamus — autonomic dysregulation and hyperthermia
      2. Nigrostriatal pathway — tremor, diffuse lead-pipe rigidity "increased muscle tone in all extremities"
      3. Haloperidol (antipsychotic)
      4. Promethazine (antiemetic)
      5. PD drug withdrawal
2. CF: Malignant FEVER
   1. Myoglobinuria
   2. Fever
   3. Encephalopathy
   4. Vitals unstable
   5. Elevates enzymes (CK)
   6. Rigidity

# Organophosphate poisoning

1. PP: AChE inhibitors --> cholinergic excess
2. Sources:
   1. Pesticide
   2. Chemical weapons (colorless, tasteless, slightly fruity odor — multiple people presenting with same symptoms)
3. CF:
   1. Muscarinic: DUMBELS
      1. Diarrhea/diaphoresis
      2. Urination
      3. Miosis
      4. Bronchospams (diffuse wheezing, scattered rhonchi), bronchorrhea, bradycardia
      5. Emesis
      6. Lacrimation
      7. Salivation — drooling
   2. Nicotinic: Muscle weakness, paralysis, fasciculations
4. Dx: RBC AChE activity
5. Management:
   1. Remove patient's clothes, irrigate skin
   2. 1st: Atropine — Muscarinic reversal (relieves bronchospasm)
   3. 2nd: Pralidoxime — Muscarinic and nicotinic reversal (regenerates AChE)

# Radiation

1. Acute Radiation Syndrome
   1. CF: Tachycardia, Nausea, Vomiting, Diarrhea that may progress to Anemia and Encephalopathy
   2. Dx: Geiger-Muller counter

# Scombroid poisoning

1. Foodborne illness from eating spoiled fish high in histamine levels
2. CF: Flushing, itching, headache and hypotension (=lightheadedness)
3. Note: cooking, smoking or freezing spoiled fish does not eliminate histamine

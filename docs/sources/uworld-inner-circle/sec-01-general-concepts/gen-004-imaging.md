# CARDIAC CATH

1. Use: Gold standard for valvular disease; definitive confirmation of coronary artery narrowing
2. Measures: Valve gradient, Calculate valve area
3. SE:
   1. Avoid dye in renal dysfunction; time consuming
   2. Complication: Retroperitoneal Hematoma
      1. Sudden hemodynamic instability and ipsilateral flank/back pain "diaphoretic and clammy"
      2. Dx: Non-contrast CT of abdomen and pelvis or Ultrasound

## Diagnostic: ANGIOGRAPHY

1. Requires Cardiac Catheterization
2. Tiny balloon at tip of catheter; presses any plaque against artery wall to improve blood flow

## Treatment: ANGIOPLASTY, PCI

1. PCI (PERCUTANEOUS CORONARY INTERVENTION)
   1. Requires Cardiac Catheterization (iodine-based dye injected in coronary arteries)
   2. Nonsurgical improvement in blood flow to heart
   3. Types
      1. Metal
      2. Drug-eluting
2. PTCA (PERCUTANEOUS TRANSLUMINAL CORONARY ANGIOPLASTY)

## CENTRAL VENOUS CATHETER

1. Confirm tip placement with CXR prior to catheter use
2. Complications occur in 15% cases
   1. Small veins — Venous perforation; increased DVT risk (Preoperative LMWH)
   2. Lung puncture — Pneumothorax or Myocardial perforation causing Pericardial Tamponade

# BRONCHOSCOPY

1. Evaluation of accessible pulmonary masses, alarm systems of endobronchial lesion (hemoptysis)

# THORACOSTOMY

1. Minimally invasive procedure in which a thin plastic tube is inserted into the pleural space

# THORACOTOMY

1. Cut is made between the ribs to see and reach the lungs or other organs in the chest or thorax

# IV PYELOGRAPHY

1. Evaluate course of ureters; diagnose anatomical variants; assess structural abnormalities of renal collecting system

# CT

1. CT: Single photon emission
   1. Use: Tumor staging
2. CT contrast: structural abnormality or mass lesions
3. CT non contrast: emergency evaluation of unprovoked seizures, suspected stroke
   1. Dark structures = Hypodense = Low density
   2. Brighter structures = Hyperdense = High density = Hypoattenuation (Blood)
4. Cerebral CT Angio: Subarachnoid hemorrhage

# MRI

1. Visualizes bony anatomy, neural structures and soft tissue
2. Preferred in nonemergency/elective situation for mass lesions
3. MR Angio: Subarachnoid hemorrhage, Trigeminal neuralgia

# BONE SCAN

1. Identifies areas of high bone turnover
2. Use: Metastatic disease, Suspected Fractures and Osteomyelitis

# Upper GI Series = Barium Swallow

1. Esophagogastroduodenoscopy (indication: dysphagia)

# ULTRASOUND

Anechoic: fluid-filled

## ECHO: TTE

1. More posterior to TEE, closer to esophagus
2. Use: Noninvasive, high accuracy compared to cath
   1. Pericardial effusion in patients with acute pericarditis
3. Measures: Peak Velocity (PVel), Mean pressure gradient (MPG) and Aortic Valve area (AVA)

## ECHO: TEE

1. Use: Visualize Thoracic aorta (performed in OR by experienced person)
2. Endoscope, better visualization of valves
3. Use: renal insufficiency; hemodynamic instability

## FAST: focused assessment with sonography

1. Use: Hemodynamically stable patient

## eFAST: extended focused assessment with sonography

# Imaging with Contrast

1. Contrast nephropathy
   1. Risk of this complication decreased by hydration prior to the imaging procedure
   2. Key idea: Many cases of contrast nephropathy will self-resolve, but in serious cases can progress to acute tubular necrosis
   3. Pretreatment with prednisone and diphenhydramine --> Reduces risk of urticarial and anaphylactic drug reactions
   4. Discontinuation of metformin often performed in hospitalized patients due to risk of lactic acidosis (AG metabolic acidosis)

# Tests

1. 24-hr Holter monitoring: outpatient setting, identify intermittent arrhythmia in symptomatic patients (syncope, palpitations)
2. Serum CK: Assesses for muscle damage
3. EMG: Assesses muscle over neuropathy

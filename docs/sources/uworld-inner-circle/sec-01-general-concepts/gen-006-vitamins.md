# Vitamin A

1. Uses
   1. Vitamin A-deficient measles
   2. All-trans Retinoic Acid: APL
   3. Oral Isotretinoin: Severe acne
      1. OCP required to reduce risk of pregnancy in sexually active patients
2. Deficiency
   1. Corneal squamous metaplasia — Bitot spots keratin debris on conjunctiva
   2. Corneal degeneration — keratomalacia
   3. Night blindness
   4. Periductal mastitis — Smoking leads to Vit A deficiency
3. Excess
   1. Chronic toxicity
      1. Alopecia
      2. Idiopathic Intracranial HTN
      3. Hepatic toxicity
      4. Arthralgias
4. Stored in Hepatic Stellate Ito cells

# Thiamine (B1)

1. Used in
   1. Branched-chain ketoacid-D, Alpha-ketoglutarate-D, Pyruvate-D, Transketolase
2. Wet Beriberi
   1. Dilated Cardiomyopathy — High output cardiac failure
   2. Biventricular HF
   3. Edema
   4. Peripheral vasodilation
3. Dry Beriberi
   1. Symmetric muscle wasting
   2. Polyneuropathy
4. Wernicke
   1. Triad: confusion, ophthalmoplegia, ataxia
   2. Life-threatening
   3. Chronic malnutrition — alcohol, systemic malignancy, hyperemesis of pregnancy
      1. Alcohol prevents B1 uptake in SI — low ATP — Lactic Acidosis
   4. Worsened by glucose infusion: Thiamine supplementation before glucose
   5. Dx: Inc in RBC Transketolase post B1 therapy
5. Korsakoff
   1. Confabulation, permanent memory loss, personality change
6. Wernicke-Korsakoff
   1. Medial dorsal nucleus of thalamus — preaqueductal gray matter
   2. Mammillary bodies

# Riboflavin (B2)

1. Cheilosis painful, Corneal Clouding
2. Succinate-D (Complex II)
   1. (-) Succinate --> Fumarate
   2. (-) ETC

# Niacin (B3)

1. B2/B6 necessary
2. Used in
   1. Alpha-ketoglutarate-D, Pyruvate-D, Isocitrate-D, Malate-D, Lactate-D
   2. Cofactor for NAD and NADP
      1. Tryptophan deficiency can lead to less NAD and NADP
   3. Dyslipidemia: lowers VLDL, raises HDL
3. Deficiency: Pellagra (Dermatitis — hyperpigmented sun-exposed skin rash, diarrhea, dementia — ataxia, intention tremor, speech trouble)
   1. Carcinoid Syndrome — high Tryptophan metabolism
   2. Prolonged INH therapy — low B6 competes for B6-R
   3. Hartnup Disease (Tryptophan)
      1. Autosomal Recessive
      2. Amino Acid transporter deficiency in PCT and Enterocytes
      3. Aminoaciduria and low Gut absorption
      4. Low Tryptophan
      5. Tx: Protein, Nicotinic Acid
4. Excess
   1. PG-induced facial flushing Use Aspirin
   2. Hyperglycemia
   3. Hyperuricemia

# Pantothenic Acid (B5)

1. Used in
   1. Coenzyme A
   2. FA Synthase
2. Deficiency
   1. (Nonspecific) Dermatitis, Enteritis
   2. Alopecia, Adrenal insufficiency

# Pyridoxine (B6)

1. Used in
   1. Transamination: ALT, AST
   2. Glycogen phosphorylase
   3. Synthesis of
      1. Glutathione
      2. Cystathionine [Homocysteine — Cystathionine — Cysteine]
      3. Heme
      4. Niacin
      5. Histamine
      6. NTs: DA, NE, Epi, 5-HT, GABA
2. Deficiency
   1. Peripheral Neuropathy — Isoniazid and Oral Contraceptives INH competes for B6-R
   2. Sideroblastic anemia — Impaired Hb synthesis and Fe excess
   3. Homocystinuria
      1. Can't convert Cystathionine --> Cysteine (Cystathionine Synthase+B6), methionine buildup
      2. Marfanoid habitus, dislocated lens (down and in), improvement with B6 supplements
      3. Hyper-coagulability, thromboembolic events

# Biotin (B7)

1. Used in
   1. Pyruvate carboxylase
   2. Acetyl-CoA carboxylase [Acetyl-CoA — Malonyl-CoA] FA Synthesis
   3. Propionyl-CoA carboxylase [Propionyl-CoA — Methylmalonyl-CoA] Odd and Branched chain FA
2. Deficiency
   1. Raw egg: Avidin
   2. LT Antibiotic use
   3. Dermatitis, enteritis, alopecia

# Folate (B9) [Jejunum]

1. Used in
   1. Converted to Tetrahydrofolic acid (THF)
   2. DNA and RNA Nitrogenous base synthesis
2. Deficiency
   1. Macrocytic, Megaloblastic anemia
   2. Hypersegmented polymorphonuclear cells
   3. Inc Homocysteine
   4. Pregnancy
   5. Drugs
      1. Phenytoin (-) B9 absorption
      2. Sulfonamide (-) B9 precursor dihydropterate synthetase
      3. Methotrexate (-) DHF reductase (side effects avoided with concurrent folinic acid)
      4. Alcoholism

# Cobalamin (B12) [Ileum]

1. Used in: Essential for DNA synthesis and myelin formation
   1. Methionine synthase
   2. Methylmalonyl CoA Mutase
2. Deficiency
   1. Symptoms
      1. Paresthesias
      2. Subacute combined degeneration
         1. (1st) SC — Myelinated peripheral nerve damage: Symmetric LE paresthesias
         2. Myelinated fibers in the brain — Neuropsych manifestations: Irritation, Mood changes
         3. Dorsal Columns — Impaired proprioception/vibrioception and sensory ataxia (gait impairment worsens when eyes are closed — (+) Romberg sign)
         4. LCST — (+) Babinski reflex
      3. Megaloblastic Anemia
      4. Note: Some patients may only exhibit neurological symptoms — lack of hematological abnormality does not exclude B12 deficiency
   2. Inc Homocysteine, Methylmalonic acid levels (Methionine Synthase — Homocysteine Methyltransferase)
   3. 2nd Folate Deficiency
   4. Prolonged deficiency — irreversible nerve damage
   5. Causes
      1. Diphyllobothrium latum
      2. Gastric bypass surgery
      3. Crohn Disease — absence of terminal ileum
      4. Intrinsic Factor — Anti-intrinsic factor antibodies are diagnostic
      5. Drugs
         1. Metformin >= 5 years
            1. Alterations in calcium homeostasis causes impaired absorption
         2. Excess Alcohol
         3. Long term antacid (PPI) use leads to achlorhydria and decreases B12 absorption
   6. Heme symptoms masked by Folate supplementation but not neurologic ones

# Vitamin C (Ascorbic acid)

1. Treatment for Methemoglobinemia by reducing Fe3+ to Fe2+
2. Used in
   1. Facilitates Fe absorption by reducing to Fe2+
   2. Collagen synthesis — proline and lysine hydroxylation
   3. Dopamine Carboxylase (DA --> NE)
3. Deficiency — Scurvy
   1. Alcohol use disorder, Substance abuse disorder, Psychiatric illness
   2. CF
      1. Follicular hyperkeratosis "Corkscrew hair"
      2. Perifollicular hemorrhage
      3. Ecchymosis, Petechiae, Hemarthrosis
      4. Gingivitis, Impaired wound healing — Collagen synthesis issue
   3. Dx: Plasma or leukocyte Vit C levels
4. Excess
   1. Calcium oxalate nephrolithiasis
   2. Worsen Fe toxicity (inc absorption) — Hereditary Hemochromatosis, Transfusion-overload

# Vitamin D

1. 25-OH D3 (storage liver) — 1,25-(OH)2 D3 Calcitriol (active kidney)
2. Regulation
   1. Stimulated by: Inc PTH, low Ca, low PO4
   2. PTH — inc Ca reabsorption, dec PO4 reabsorption
3. Deficiency
   1. Rickets
   2. Osteomalacia
   3. Hypocalcemic tetany
   4. Conditions (malabsorption)
      1. Chronic Kidney Disease
      2. Advanced Liver Disease
      3. Cystic Fibrosis
   5. Drugs: induce P450 which degrades VitD to inactive metabolites
      1. Phenytoin
      2. Carbamazepine
      3. Rifampin
   6. Worsened by: pigmented skin, premature birth
4. Excess
   1. Granulomatous disease (Vit D activation by epithelioid macrophages 1alpha-hydroxylase)
   2. Hypercalcemia — Shortened QT interval
   3. Hypercalciuria, stupor, loss of appetite

# Vitamin E [Tocopherol, Tocotrienol]

1. Function — Antioxidant, free radical damage protection
2. Deficiency
   1. Acanthocytosis
   2. Muscle weakness
   3. PC and SC demyelination
3. Excess
   1. Enterocolitis in infants
   2. High dose might alter Vitamin K — enhanced Warfarin anticoagulant effect

# Vitamin K

1. Function
   1. Activated by Epoxide Reductase to reduced form
   2. Cofactor for G-carboxylation of glutamic acid residues on clotting factors
2. Deficiency
   1. Neonatal hemorrhage, high PT, aPTT
   2. Prolonged broad-spectrum antibiotics

# Zinc [Zinc fingers]

1. Deficiency
   1. Male hypogonadism, Dysgeusia (rancid, metallic taste), Anosmia
   2. Acrodermatitis Enteropathica: periorificial, alopecia, diarrhea
   3. Predispose to Alcoholic Cirrhosis

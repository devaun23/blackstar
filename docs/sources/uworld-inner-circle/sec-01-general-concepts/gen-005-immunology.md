# B-Cell Deficiencies

General: ENCAPSULATED BACTERIA (RECURRENT SINOPULMONARY INFECTIONS)
Labs: dec Ig and vaccine response

## X-linked agammaglobulinemia

Present around 6mo when maternal IgG wanes; encapsulated organisms

1. PP: BTK defect (Tyrosine Kinase): gene necessary for all stages of B cell development and proliferation
   1. Abnormal pre-BC maturation
2. CF
   1. Recurrent Sinopulmonary esp with Encapsulated bacteria due to defective opsonization similar for C3 deficiency
   2. Recurrent GI and Enterovirus infections due to IgA deficiency
   3. Small or absent lymphoid tissue "SMALL TONSILS"
3. Labs
   1. dec Ig and Antibody response to vaccine
   2. Flow cytometry: dec CD19 BC and Normal TC
4. Tx
   1. dec or absent concentration of all Ig subclasses --> Monthly infusion of immune globulin
   2. Prophylactic Abx as necessary
5. Live vaccines contraindicated

## CVID

Presents in adolescence with history of infections; encapsulated organisms

1. Impaired differentiation of mature B cells = IgM levels may be low normal
2. CF
   1. Recurrent Sinopulmonary esp with Encapsulated bacteria because Ab needed opsonization required for clearance
   2. Recurrent GI infections due to IgA deficiency
      1. Salmonella, Campylobacter, Giardia, Chronic disease
      2. FAILURE TO THRIVE = LOW HEIGHT AND WEIGHT PERCENTILE
   3. Associated atopic conditions — Asthma, Eczema
   4. Associated autoimmune conditions — Rheumatoid arthritis
3. Labs
   1. Normal B cells and Normal leukocyte count
   2. No response to vaccination, Low IgG, IgA +/- IgM
4. Tx: Ig replacement

## IgA deficiency

1. Asymptomatic or recurrent sinopulmonary infections, otitis media, failure to thrive and small lymph nodes "lack of lymphatic tissue"
2. Anaphylaxis following blood transfusion due to preformed Anti-IgA Ab
3. Low IgA, normal or high IgG and IgM

# Combined TC and BC Deficiencies

General: VIRAL, BACTERIAL AND FUNGAL INFECTIONS — CANDIDA, PNEUMOCYSTIS + FAILURE TO THRIVE
Labs: dec Leukocytes +/- dec Ig and vaccine response

## Severe Combined Immunodeficiency (SCID)

Presents in infancy; Failure to thrive

1. X-linked/Auto recessive
   1. Failure of TC development
   2. Because T cells play a role in BC activation, TC deficiency may also have dec Ab production
2. CF
   1. Severe viral, fungal or opportunistic infections — Pneumocystis "bilateral diffuse crackles" in <1yo
   2. Chronic diarrhea
   3. Failure to thrive
   4. Absent lymphoid tissue "SMALL TONSILS"
3. Labs
   1. Low CD19
   2. Absent CD3 (no TC) = dec CD4 and CD8 — Fungal and opportunistic infections
4. Tx
   1. Stem cell transplant
   2. Antiviral and Antifungal prophylaxis due to impaired cellular immunity
5. Live vaccines contraindicated

## Hyper IgM Syndrome

Presents in infants; Failure to thrive

1. X-linked recessive
   1. Defect in CD40L (found on TC)
   2. Impaired BC class switching
2. CF
   1. Recurrent Sinopulmonary esp with Encapsulated bacteria due to defective opsonization lack Ig
   2. Recurrent GI and Enterovirus infections due to IgA deficiency
   3. Severe viral, fungal or opportunistic infections — Pneumocystis "bilateral diffuse crackles"
3. Labs
   1. Normal BC and TC concentrations (although dysfunctional)
   2. inc IgM, dec of all other Ig
   3. During infection: dec Neutrophils, inc Lymphocytes
4. Tx: Prophylactic Abx + IV Ig

## Wiskott-Aldrich syndrome

1. X-linked recessive mutation in WAS gene, expressed in hematopoietic cells and regulates cytoskeleton remodeling in response to cell signaling
2. Abnormal actin skeleton in wbc and platelets — Microthrombocytopenia
3. CF: Recurrent infections, Atopic dermatitis, Bruising

## Ataxia-telangiectasia

1. Defective DNA repair
2. CF
   1. Cerebellar ataxia
   2. Oculocutaneous telangiectasias — Ocular telangiectasia
   3. Recurrent sinopulmonary infections + Fungal infections
   4. Absent or small lymph nodes

# T-Cell Deficiencies

## DiGeorge/Thymic aplasia

1. Low absolute leukocyte count with lymphopenia
2. Tx: Antiviral and Antifungal prophylaxis due to impaired cellular immunity

## Hyper-IgE syndrome

1. Autosomal Dominant
2. PP: Defective JAK-STAT signaling — impaired Th17
   1. dec Neutrophil proliferation/chemotaxis
3. CF
   1. Severe, chronic eczema
   2. Noninflammatory abscess
   3. Recurrent sinopulmonary infections
   4. Dysmorphic facies: broad nose, prominent forehead
   5. Retained primary teeth
4. Labs
   1. inc IgE
   2. Eosinophilia
   3. Normal leukocyte count with dec Th17
5. Management: Supportive skin care, Antibiotic prophylaxis and treatment

# Phagocytosis Deficiencies

General: SKIN AND SOFT TISSUE INFECTIONS, FUNGAL INFECTIONS, CATALASE-POSITIVE ORGANISMS IN CGD
Labs: Impaired oxidative burst in CGD; NORMAL LEUKOCYTE COUNT AND IG

## CGD (catalase + organisms)

1. X-linked recessive mutation of NADPH oxidase — impaired respiratory burst — dec superoxide production — inhibition of phagocytic intracellular killing
2. Skin and pulmonary infections with FUNGAL AND CATALASE (+) ORGANISMS
   1. Fungal: Aspergillus
   2. Catalase +: S. aureus, Serratia marcescens, B/Pseudomonas cepacia, Nocardia (cavitary pneumonia)
   3. Neutrophils may use hydrogen peroxide from pathogens but catalase (+) organisms can degrade their own reactive oxygen species
   4. Characteristic GRANULOMAS involving many organs
      1. Migration of phagocytes to infection site but inability to degrade inflammatory signals leads to chronic and focal aggregation of inflammatory cells = granulomas
3. Labs
   1. Normal leukocyte count
4. Gram stain
   1. Phagocytic cells filled with bacteria due to ineffective intracellular killing
5. Dx
   1. (Flow cytometry): Measure oxidative burst with dihydrorhodamine 123 testing — colorless when respiratory burst is absent
   2. Nitroblue tetrazolium test
6. Tx:
   1. Prophylaxis: TMP-SMX, Itraconazole, Interferon gamma
      1. Breakthrough infection may occur despite prophylaxis
      2. For new infection — obtain biopsy of active infection prior to Abx therapy — IDENTIFY ORGANISMS FIRST
      3. Ex: Bronchoscopy for biopsy of new lung abscess
   2. Active infection: Culture-based, Antimicrobial therapy
   3. Hematopoietic cell transplant is curative

## Leukocyte adhesion defect

1. Impaired Neutrophil chemotaxis
2. CF: Frequent skin and respiratory infections
3. Labs: Normal leukocyte count or Leukocytosis + Neutrophilia

## Chediak-Higashi

1. Lysosomal trafficking disorder — Neutropenia
2. Recurrent infections: Staph, Strep
3. Oculocutaneous albinism

# Complement Disorders (encapsulated organisms)

General: Neisseria infection, Autoimmune disease
Labs: dec CH50; NORMAL LEUKOCYTES COUNT AND IG

## C1q deficiency

1. Recurrent sinopulmonary bacterial infection
2. Childhood SLE

## Terminal complement (C5-C9) deficiency

1. Recurrent encapsulated bacterial infection especially Neisseria

# Hereditary Angioedema

Most episodes resolve 2-5 days. Acute treatment with C1 inhibitor concentrate to prevent airway involvement.

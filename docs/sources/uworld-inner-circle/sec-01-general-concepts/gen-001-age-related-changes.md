# Age-related changes

## Cardiac

1. Decreased myocyte number
2. Decreased resting and maximal CO
3. Decreased maximum HR
4. Increased contraction and relaxation of heart muscle
5. Increased myocardial stiffness during diastole --> Heart failure with preserved EF
6. Pigment accumulation in myocardial cells

## Orthostatic Hypotension

1. Insufficient constriction of blood vessels in LE on standing (either due to defect in autonomic reflexes, dec intravascular volume or medications)
2. Cause: Decreased baroreceptor sensitivity and defect in myocardial response to this reflex
   1. Affected by arterial stiffness, decreased NE content of sympathetic nerve endings and reduced sensitivity of myocardium to sympathetic stimulation
3. Cause: Intravascular volume depletion due to diuresis

## Lungs

1. Increased lung compliance

## Eyes

1. Age-related sicca syndrome
   1. Age related dry eye syndrome
   2. Exocrine gland atrophy
   3. CF: Decreases visual acuity (corneal epithelial erosions impair vision)
   4. Tx: Artificial tears, Cyclosporine drops (inc tear)

## Ears

1. Presbycusis: Age related hearing loss — bilateral, progressive, sensorineural hearing loss (SNHL)
   1. PP: Damage to sensorineural structures in the inner ear — Cochlear hair loss and cochlear neuron degeneration
   2. High frequency hearing loss first (women, children), Tinnitus (continuous ringing, rushing or buzzing)
   3. Speech discrimination difficulty (impaired hearing with background noise)
   4. Tx: Education of the patient and family, limited background noise, Amplification (hearing aids)

## Neuro

1. Heat Stroke: Impaired thermoregulatory capacity

## HTN

1. Causes increase in glomerular hydrostatic pressure
   1. HTN led nephrosclerosis
2. Concentric hypertrophy
   1. Diastolic LV dysfunction

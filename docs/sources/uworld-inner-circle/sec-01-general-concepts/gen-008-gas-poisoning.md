# Carbon Monoxide

1. Displaces oxygen to create carboxyhemoglobin, reduces O2 carrying capacity, causes left-shift
2. Source
   1. Smoke inhalation
   2. Defective heating system, gas motors operating in poor ventilation
3. Presentation
   1. Mild-moderate
      1. Headache, confusion
      2. Malaise, dizziness, nausea
   2. Severe
      1. Cerebral hypoxia — Drowsiness, seizure, syncope, coma
      2. Lactic acidosis — Elevated lactate (impairs oxygen utilization)
      3. MI
         1. Reported in 1/3 of patients
         2. "Chest pain and sob, was preparing steak on indoor charcoal grill. Elevated trop I, venous lactate, sinus tach with T wave inversion anterior leads"
      4. Pulmonary edema "bilateral infiltrates on CXR"
      5. Arrhythmias
4. Diagnosis
   1. ABG (arterial blood gas): carboxyhemoglobin level
   2. ECG +/- cardiac enzymes
5. Treatment
   1. High flow 100% oxygen
   2. Severe: intubation/hyperbaric oxygen

# Cyanide

1. Presentation
   1. "Patient presents with severe hypertension with acute end-organ complications (renal failure, congestive heart failure). Nitroprusside is given and symptoms improve. But next morning patient is confused, agitated, has general tonic-clonic seizures"
2. Nitroprusside
   1. Parenteral vasodilator with quick onset and offset, used for rapid blood pressure control in HTN emergency
   2. Metabolism releases NO (arteriolar and venous dilation) and Cyanide ions
   3. Most common in renal insufficiency patients or higher doses (prolonged infusion overnight)
   4. Altered mental status, lactic acidosis, seizures, coma

# Methemoglobinemia

1. RF: Dapsone, some Topical anesthetics (Benzocaine)
2. CF: Cyanosis, dyspnea, tachycardia
   1. Phenazopyridine: Pt with dysuria, frequency, taking an OTC med for pain for 3 days and develops perioral cyanosis, shortness of breath --> methemoglobinemia
   2. Methemoglobinemia (Fe 3+) cannot bind oxygen
   3. Management: Stop Phenazopyridine, Start Methylene blue and Vitamin C (activates methemoglobin reductase (Fe 3+ to 2+))
3. Dx: Methemoglobin level test
   1. Pulse ox is falsely elevated because it estimates based on PaO2 only, not on effective Hb-O2 binding
   2. Hypoxia, SpO2 <85% and large oxygen saturation gap (blood gas vs pulse oximetry)
4. Tx: Vitamin C — reduces Fe3+ to Fe2+, Methylene blue

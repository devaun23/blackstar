# Sepsis

1. SIRS: 2 or more of the listed criteria in the table
2. Sepsis: SIRS + Source of infection
3. Severe Sepsis: SIRS + Source of infection + End organ damage + Hypotension + Lactate >4
4. Septic Shock: Severe sepsis that is unresponsive to fluids — persistent hypotension

# CVS

## CHA2DS2VASc — used for Atrial Fibrillation

1. Criteria
   1. CHF (1)
   2. HTN (1)
   3. Age >75 (2)
   4. DM (1)
   5. Stroke/TIA/Thrombo-embolism (2)
   6. Vascular disease — prior MI, PAD, Aortic disease (1)
   7. Age 65-74 (1)
   8. Sex (Female) (1)
   9. Max score (9) — cant count age twice
2. Chronic anticoagulation: Men >=2, Women >=3
3. Oral anticoagulants: Apixaban, Rivaroxaban, Dabigatran or less commonly Warfarin

## TIMI risk score — Thrombolysis in MI risk score

1. 0-2: Stress test
2. >=3: Cath lab

# Resp

## Centor Criteria — Strep Pharyngitis

1. 4+: treat with Penicillin
2. 2-3: rapid strep test
3. Note: Rapid strep test always done in children

## Light's criteria — Pleural effusion

## COPD

1. Home oxygen: If O2 sat <88% or PaO2 <55

## CURB-65 — Pneumonia

1. Confusion
2. BUN >7 mmol/l
3. Respiratory Rate >30
4. SBP <90, DBP <60
5. Age >65
6. Score
   1. 0-1: Outpatient Drugs: Typical — Amoxicillin, Atypical — Azithromycin
   2. >2: Hospitalize + Inpatient Fluoroquinolones

## Well's Criteria — PE

1. >4: CTA or V/Q first if renal insufficiency *in high risk patient with normal V/Q --> CT scan
2. <4: D-dimer

# GI

## Ascites — Spontaneous Bacterial Peritonitis

1. +250 neutrophils in ascitic fluid + fever --> (next step) Paracentesis
2. RF: Cirrhosis

## SAAG: (Serum Albumin) - (Serum Ascites Albumin)

1. >1.1g/dL: portal hypertension — Cardiac ascites, Cirrhosis, Budd-Chiari
2. <1.1g/dL: no port-htn — TB, Peritoneal carcinomatosis, Pancreatic ascites, Nephrotic syndrome

# Renal

## Anion Gap: Na - (HCO3 + Cl)

1. >12
   1. MUDPILES: Methanol, Uremia, DKA, Propylene, INH/Iron, Lactic acidosis, Ethylene, Salicylates
2. <12
   1. Urine Anion Gap: (Na + K) - Cl
      1. (+) Renal Tubular Acidosis
      2. (-) Diarrhea (lost HCO3)

## Calcium (corrected)

1. [0.8 (normal albumin - pt's albumin)] + Serum Ca; Normal albumin = 4
2. Ex: Alb 4, Ca 9 = Cal 9; Alb 3, Ca 9 = Cal 9.8; Alb 2, Ca 9 = Ca 10.6; Alb 5, Ca 9 = Ca 8.2
3. As albumin decreases, Ca previously bound to albumin is now free and leads to higher total Ca levels

## Serum Osm

1. Na + Gluc/18 + BUN/2.8 = 280

# Neuro

## Glasgow Coma Scale

1. <=8: Intubated (endotracheal intubation)

## POUND criteria — Migraine

1. Pulsatile
2. Duration between 4-72h (hOurs)
3. Unilateral

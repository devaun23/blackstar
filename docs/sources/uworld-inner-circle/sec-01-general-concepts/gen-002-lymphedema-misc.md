# Chronic Lymphedema

1. Etiology: Physical disruption of lymphatic drainage
   1. Lymphadenectomy, radiation
   2. Malignant obstruction
   3. Chronic inflammation (Recurrent cellulitis)
   4. Parasitic infection (Filariasis)
   5. Congenital (Turner syndrome)
2. CF
   1. Swelling, heaviness, discomfort
   2. Early: soft skin, pitting edema
   3. Late: firm, dry and thickened skin progressive deposition of collagen and fat, non pitting edema
   4. Stemmer sign: inability to lift skin on the dorsum of an extremity specific
3. Tx
   1. Weight loss, limb elevation
   2. Compression bandages and physiotherapy
   3. Diuretics contraindicated

# Graft Rejection

1. Acute Graft-versus-host disease (GVHD) ~100 days
   1. Maculopapular rash, Profuse/watery diarrhea, Liver inflammation (elevated ALP, Bilirubin, Transaminases)
   2. CD8+ TC-mediated injury

# Vasodilation-induced decreased perfusion

1. Hepatorenal syndrome: Liver dz Patients secrete more NO — decreases peripheral vascular resistance -- decreased RBF
   1. Tx: Splanchnic vasoconstrictors — Midodrine, Octreotide, NE; Liver transplant
2. Shock — IgE led vasodilation and bronchospasm — airway obstruction and circulatory collapse

# Surgery with hx Stroke/MI

1. Usually CI unless in setting of Infective Endocarditis

# Cultures and Treatment

1. Don't wait and treat empirical: Meningitis
2. Flucytosine — Given with Amphotericin B for Cryptococcus meningitis, switch to PO Fluconazole for maintenance

# Immunosuppression (Transplant)

1. Prophylaxis
   1. TMP-SMX for PCP
   2. Ganciclovir/Valganciclovir for CMV
2. Skin lesions
   1. Pseudomonas — Ecthyma gangrenosum (Gram negative bacteremia)
3. Reactive Leukocytosis (levels will be temporarily elevated and then return to normal)
   1. CST: demargination
   2. Stress: releases leukocytes into bloodstream

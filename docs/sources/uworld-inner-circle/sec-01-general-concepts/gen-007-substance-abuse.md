# Alcohol Use Disorder

## Pharmacotherapy

1. Naltrexone
   1. Mu-opioid receptor antagonist
   2. CI — Liver disease, opioid use
2. Acamprosate — used if liver disease or concurrent opioid use
   1. Glutamate modulator
   2. Reduces risk for relapse
   3. Adjustment if renal failure (excreted by kidneys)
3. Disulfiram — used if highly motivated

## Wernicke Encephalopathy

1. "Lateral gaze defect, wide-based gait, confused" = ophthalmoplegia, ataxia, confusion triad
2. Sx mimic alcohol intoxication but alcohol levels are low or undetectable
3. Chronic malnutrition — alcohol, systemic malignancy, hyperemesis of pregnancy
4. Tx: Thiamine

## Alcohol Associations

1. Bingeing + Cocaine --> Acute Alcohol Myopathy and Rhabdomyolysis "LE tenderness and swelling"
   1. Increased risk of Acute Kidney Injury
   2. Acute alcohol myopathy presents as LE tenderness and swelling without sensory sx
      1. Cocaine exacerbates by vasoconstriction and direct myocyte toxicity
      2. Disruption of myocyte membrane — muscle lysis — release heme-containing myoglobin into bloodstream — intravascular depletion — subsequent insult from direct tubular toxicity of heme = pigment nephropathy
      3. Tx of Rhabdomyolysis: Volume resuscitation to replenish intravascular and intramuscular fluid
         1. Note: initial tissue damage may cause acute compartment syndrome
2. Hepatitis
   1. Lab values: AST >2x ALT, Elevated ALT, Elevated GGT, Elevated Ferritin
3. Refeeding Syndrome
   1. Lack of DTR and weakness post glucose and electrolyte infusion
   2. Dx: Hypophosphatemia

# Cocaine

## Cause

1. Potentiates sympathomimetic actions by causing inhibition of norepinephrine reuptake
2. Enhances stimulation of alpha and beta-adrenergic receptors — coronary vasoconstriction, increased HR, systemic BP, and myocardial oxygen demand --> MI
3. Promotes platelet activation and aggregation — Enhances thrombus formation

## Presentation

1. Epistaxis, rhinitis (intranasal use)
2. Symp hyperactivity: tachycardia, hypertension, dilated pupils
3. Coronary vasoconstriction-led chest pain
4. Psychomotor agitation, seizures

Note: Suspect in patient with multiple recent ER visits for chest pain and palpitations who is young and otherwise healthy

## Complications

1. Acute myocardial ischemia
2. Aortic dissection
3. Intracranial hemorrhage (ICH)
   1. MCC of stroke in young patients
4. Seizures

## Management

### Chest pain

Note: All acute toxicity and myocardial ischemia patients given supplemental oxygen and IV benzos

1. Benzodiazepines — blood pressure and anxiety
   1. IV Diazepam
   2. Reduce sympathetic outflow, improve blood pressure and heart rate, alleviate cardio sx
2. ASA
   1. Retards thrombus formation and inhibits cocaine-induced platelet aggregation
3. Nitroglycerin and CCB — pain
   1. Vasodilators reduce coronary artery vasoconstriction
4. Immediate cath with reperfusion as needed (ex: STEMI)
5. BB — contraindicated due to increased risk of coronary vasoconstriction
6. Fibrinolytics — not preferred due to increased risk of intracranial hemorrhage

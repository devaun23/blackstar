# Acute Abdominal/Pelvic Pain

1. Mittelschmerz (hrs-days): recurrent mild and unilateral mid-cycle pain prior to ovulation
2. Ectopic Pregnancy: Amenorrhea, abdominal pain, vaginal bleeding, (+) B-hCG
3. Ovarian Torsion: Sudden, severe, unilateral, nausea, vomiting, tender adnexal mass
   1. US: Enlarged ovary with decreased or absent blood flow
   2. Associated inflammatory pelvic free fluid "free fluid in posterior cul-de-sac"
   3. Emergent laparoscopy for detorsion
4. Ruptured Ovarian Cyst: Sudden, severe, unilateral pain following strenuous or sexual activity
   1. US: Pelvic free fluid
   2. Ovarian cysts are common in reproductive age women
      1. Occur when fluid fills space of recently released follicle following ovulation
   3. Significant intraabdominal bleeding seen in patients on anticoagulants
   4. Phrenic nerve irritation may cause radiation to shoulder
   5. Peritoneal signs: rigidity, rebound, guarding
   6. Hemodynamic instability: hypotension, tachycardia
5. Pelvic Inflammatory Disease: Fever, chills, vaginal discharge, cervical motion tenderness
   1. US: +/- Tuboovarian abscess
   2. Pregnancy test
      1. Serum quantitative: 4 days of implantation
      2. Urine: 1 week
      3. Pelvic US required after positive test

# UTERUS

## Adenomyosis

1. Endometrial tissue (gland and stroma) in myometrium
2. RF: Multiparous >40yo
3. CF: New-onset dysmenorrhea
   1. Continued accumulation of endometrial tissue in myometrium causes an increase in the endometrial cavity surface area --> heavy menstrual bleeding and chronic pelvic pain
4. PE: Dense, uniformly enlarged, boggy, globular uterus due to entrapped endometrial tissue in myometrium
   1. Repeated menstrual cycles cause endometrial shedding within the myometrium --> progression of dysmenorrhea to chronic, dull pelvic pain
5. Evaluation: Pelvic US and or MRI
6. Definitive Dx: Histology post hysterectomy
7. Tx
   1. Medical: OCP, Progestin-releasing IUD
   2. Failed Medical tx: Hysterectomy

## Fibroids

1. Decrease in size after menopause, unlikely to cause postmenopausal bleeding
2. Smooth muscle and fibrous connective tissue tumor
3. CF
   1. Heavy, prolonged menses
   2. Dysmenorrhea differences in tissue contractility and vascularity
   3. Pelvic symptoms: Pelvic pain, Constipation, Urinary frequency
   4. Obstetric complications: Impaired fertility, Pregnancy loss, Preterm labor, Placental abruption
   5. Enlarged, irregular uterus --> Pregnancy size-date discrepancy
4. Evaluation: Pelvic US > CT Abdomen Pelvis
5. Treatment:
   1. Asymptomatic: Observation
   2. Symptomatic: Hormonal contraception (Combination OCP, Progestin-IUD), Desire fertility --> Tranexamic acid, Surgery
   3. Preoperative (short-term): GnRH agonist — Leuprolide
      1. Decrease pulsatile FSH and LH --> Decreased Estrogen --> Transient decrease in size *Long-term use increases risk of Osteoporosis
6. PP: Submucosal fibroids --> Infertility
   1. Benign smooth muscle (myometrial) tumor that can cause abnormal intrauterine anatomy by extending into endometrial cavity or impinging the endometrium --> compromises embryo implantation or growth
   2. Evaluation: Sonohysterography Pelvic US after filling uterine cavity with saline
   3. Management
      1. Preserve fertility: Hysteroscopic myomectomy to restore normal intrauterine anatomy
      2. Uterine artery embolization: reduces fibroid size by reducing blood flow to uterus
         1. Post-procedure physiological findings
            1. Pelvic pain due to fibroid necrosis and inflammation
            2. Watery, blood-tinged vaginal discharge due to on-going tissue sloughing
            3. Wet mount positive for leukocytes
            - *Note: excessive leukocytes and discharge warrant evaluation for infection
7. PP: Pedunculated/Prolapsed Submucosal fibroids
   1. Pressure on internal cervical os can cause dilation and effacement of the cervix with protrusion of the fibroid
   2. Labor-like pains due to cervical dilation
   3. "Beefy red tissue at the os" "firm and round spherical mass visible through the external os"
8. PP: Subserosal --> Bulk symptoms chronic pelvic discomfort, constipation
9. PP: Uterine size-greater-than-date discrepancy
   1. Enlarged myometrium (smooth muscle), could cause uterine size-greater-than-date discrepancy
10. PP: Intramural Fibroids
    1. Enlarge uterus by increasing surface area of endometrial cavity and decrease responsiveness to vasoactive factors (limiting spiral arteriole vasoconstriction)
    2. Tx:
       1. Desire fertility --> Tranexamic acid
          1. Reduces blood loss during menses by 50%
          2. Stabilizes thrombi/clots by preventing plasmin formation (impairs fibrin degradation), this allows for spiral arteriole vasoconstriction
          3. Safe in pregnancy
       2. Desire fertility and Contraception --> Progestin-releasing IUD
       3. Don't desire fertility --> Novasure: Endometrial ablation (thermal ablation)
11. PP: Pregnancy and fibroid ischemia
    1. Grow rapidly during pregnancy due to elevated estrogen and progesterone levels
    2. But myometrial blood flow shifts toward the developing fetus and placenta, fibroids can outgrow their blood supply, leading to fibroid infarction and necrosis
    3. Degenerating fibroids cause uterine contractions (prostaglandin release) and fundal tenderness, a tender mass, and leukocytosis (inflammatory cytokine release)
    4. Dx: Ultrasound
    5. Management: Indomethacin for patients <32 weeks gestation

## Atypical Endometrial Hyperplasia

1. RF:
   1. Unopposed Estrogen exposure due to obesity and Chronic Anovulation (PCOS) — counteracted with OCP
   2. Nulliparity, Early menarche, Late Menopause, Tamoxifen therapy

# CERVIX

## Pap smear

1. High false-positive rate for Actinomyces organisms
   1. Actinomyces can be part of normal vaginal flora or ascend into upper genital tract and cause severe PID especially in setting of long term IUD
   2. Asx: No further management (and keep an IUD if already in place)
   3. Sx (pelvic pain, abnormal vaginal discharge): further management
2. Endometrial cells (without cervical intraepithelial lesions)
   1. <45yo: Common, benign finding especially if Pap performed during first 10 days of menses
   2. >45yo: Requires follow up with Endometrial biopsy to rule out endometrial hyperplasia/cancer
3. Abundant clue cells
   1. Pap test has low sensitivity and specificity for identifying BV
   2. BV diagnosis requires further evaluation
      1. Symptomatic patient (malodorous vaginal discharge) --> wet mount, potassium hydroxide whiff test
      2. Asymptomatic patient — no further management
   - *Note: Treatment of asymptomatic BV avoided because it may resolve spontaneously and treatment may lead to symptomatic vaginal candidiasis
4. ASC-US: Atypical squamous cells of unknown significance
5. AGUS: Atypical glandular cells of unknown significance
   1. RF: HPV 16/18 (cervix), Unopposed Estrogen (endometrium)
   2. May indicate either cervical or endometrial adenocarcinoma
   3. Evaluation >35yo
      1. Colposcopy — Assess cervical adenocarcinoma
      2. Endocervical curettage — Assess cervical adenocarcinoma; completed if colposcopy is insufficient or transformation zone is not completely visualized
      3. Endometrial biopsy — Note: Hysteroscopy evaluates and treats endometrial or myometrial pathology, more invasive than endometrial biopsy
         1. Atypical endometrial hyperplasia presence would indicate precursor to endometrial cancer
         2. MCC of atypical endometrial hyperplasia is unopposed Estrogen — Obesity, Chronic Anovulation
6. LGIS
7. HGIS: CIN2, CIN3 or Invasive Cervical Cancer
   1. Evaluation:
      1. Immediate Colposcopy — Acetic acid used to identify abnormal cells (aceto-white changes) and abnormal vessels; suitable in pregnancy
      2. Endocervical curettage if unsatisfactory colposcopy (squamocolumnar junction not visualized) and high likelihood of cervical dysplasia
      3. Cervical Biopsy — Suitable in pregnancy
   2. Cervical Intraepithelial Neoplasia (CIN)
      1. CIN 1
      2. CIN 2
      3. CIN 3: High-grade, HPV 16/18
         1. Management: LEEP, Cold knife conization, Cryoablation
         - Note: All nonpregnant patients >25yo with CIN3 require transformation zone excision (Cone biopsy) due to high risk of progression to invasive squamous cell cervical carcinoma --> Pap test and HPV cotesting 1 and 2 years postprocedure
         2. Positive Surgical margins
            1. Fertility desired: Repeat conization (diagnostic and therapeutic)
            2. Fertility not-desired: Hysterectomy
         3. Negative Surgical margins
            1. High risk for recurrent cervical dysplasia due to persistent HPV infection --> Pap and HPV cotesting at 1 and 2 years

# MENSTRUAL DISORDERS

## Anovulation

1. Cause: Obesity
   1. Obesity causes increased insulin resistance and hyperglycemia --> Decreased sex hormone-binding globulin = Elevated Free Androgen — Androstenedione
   2. Free Androgens aromatized to Estrone in Adipose tissue = Persistently elevated Estrone levels
   3. High Estrone levels --> Imbalance in GnRH and LH/FSH imbalance = Lack of LH surge = Anovulation and Abnormal Uterine Bleeding
   4. Tx: Weight loss, OCP to regulate menstrual cycle and protect endometrium
2. Immature hypothalamic-pituitary axis
   1. Common in adolescents
   2. In Ovulatory cycle: Corpus luteum produces Progesterone after ovulation; Progesterone differentiates proliferative endometrium into secretory endometrium
      1. Corpus luteum degeneration leads to decreased Progesterone --> Decrease in endometrial blood supply and shedding of lining = Menses
   3. In immature cycle: No corpus luteum production = no Progesterone production = no differentiation into secretory endometrium
      1. Unopposed Estrogen --> uncontrolled endometrium proliferation
      2. Bleeding occurs when endometrium lining becomes too thickened and unstable = breakthrough bleeding
   4. Management: Progesterone to normalize menstruation by stabilizing unregulated endometrial proliferation; cyclic progesterone causes menstruation

## Primary Dysmenorrhea

1. Etiology: Excessive PG production causes uterine hypercontractility and hypertonicity resulting in ischemia
2. RF
   1. <30yo
   2. BMI <20
   3. Tobacco, Menarche <12yo, Heavy menstrual periods, Sexual abuse
3. CF
   1. Pain first 2-3days of menses radiating to lower back and thighs as uterine contractions initiate endometrial sloughing
   2. N/V/D — PG induced stimulation of GI tract
   3. Normal pelvic examination may be deferred in patients not sexually active
4. PE: Normal
5. Management
   1. SEXUALLY INACTIVE --> NSAIDS
   2. SEXUALLY ACTIVE --> COMBINED OCPS suppress ovulation (= contraception) and decrease PG release
6. Note: Endometriosis also presents with dysmenorrhea but pain exists throughout entire cycle often with chronic pelvic pain

## Secondary Dysmenorrhea

1. Suspect if following symptoms present
   1. Symptom onset >25yo
   2. Unilateral (nonmidline) pelvic pain "RLQ pain that radiates to right flank"
   3. No systemic symptoms during menses (fatigue, nausea)
   4. AUB (intramenstrual bleeding, postcoital spotting)
2. Causes
   1. PID
   2. Endometriosis
   3. Adnexal masses
   4. Uterine anomaly (rudimentary uterine horn)

# CONTRACEPTION

1. Combined OCP
2. Copper IUD
   1. Hx of coagulopathy/Antiphospholipid Antibody Syndrome --> Copper IUD
      1. Combined OCPs absolutely contraindicated
      2. Progesterone only (Medroxyprogesterone injection) not recommended also
   2. Best option for medication-controlled Hypertensive patients
   3. Avoid in: heavy bleeding
3. SERM

*Note: All estrogen agonist formulations (OCP, HRT, SERMs) inc risk of Venous thromboembolism (PE, DVT, Retinal vein thrombosis)

## Tamoxifen, Raloxifene

1. MOA:
   1. Competitive inhibitor of Estrogen binding
   2. Mixed agonist/antagonist action
2. Indications:
   1. Prevention of Breast cancer in high-risk patients
   2. Tamoxifen: Adjuvant treatment for Breast cancer
   3. Raloxifene: Postmenopausal Osteoporosis if Alendronate is not well tolerated or there is high risk of Breast cancer in Osteoporosis pt
3. Adverse effects:
   1. Hot flashes thermoregulatory dysfunction in the anterior hypothalamus similar to menopausal hot flashes
   2. Venous thromboembolism increased protein C resistance
   3. Endometrial Polyps in Premenopausal (Tamoxifen only)
   4. Endometrial hyperplasia & Carcinoma in Postmenopausal (Tamoxifen only)
   5. Uterine sarcoma (Tamoxifen only)

# MENOPAUSE

Absent Menses for 12 months

## HRT

1. Indication: Reduction of Vasomotor Symptoms in <60yo who has undergone menopause within past 10 years
   1. In patients with a uterus, HRT given with progestin component for endometrial protection
2. Note: No longer indicated in prevention of coronary heart disease or osteoporosis due to risk of Thromboembolism
3. Contraindications: Coronary heart disease, Thromboembolism, TIA, Stroke, Breast Cancer, Endometrial Cancer
4. Alternative therapy: SSRI

## Postmenopausal Bleeding (PMB)

1. Evaluate for Cervical Cancer and Endometrial Cancer
2. Cervical Cancer Evaluation
   1. Repeat Pap regardless of when last Pap was performed, cervical cancer peak incidence ~50yo
3. Endometrial Cancer Evaluation
   1. PMB is a common presenting sign for endometrial hyperplasia or cancer (especially in older obese women) --> PMB requires endometrial evaluation
   2. TVUS Endometrium: <4mm --> Observation; >4mm --> Endometrial Biopsy
   - or
   3. Endometrial Biopsy: Benign --> Observation; Atypia --> Progestins, Surgery (hysterectomy)
   - *Note: Endometrial ablation is not used in Postmenopausal bleeding because it can prevent future endometrial evaluation and obscure malignancy

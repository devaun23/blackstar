# Paradoxical Vocal Fold Motion

1. Vocal cord dysfunction
2. Mimics exercise-induced asthma — present in elite athletes
3. Episodic, inappropriate closing of the vocal cords during inspiration especially during exercise
4. Results in laryngeal obstruction and inspiratory stridor (vs. expiratory wheezing in asthma)
5. Dx: Laryngoscopy shows vocal cord adduction during inspiration
6. Management
   1. Acute: Sniff or pant
   2. Long term: Education, Therapy with speech-language pathologist

# Tracheomalacia

1. PP: Softening of trachea leading to expiratory stridor/end-expiratory collapse (laryngomalacia and croup/laryngotracheobronchitis leads to inspiratory stridor)
2. CF: Intubation longer than 7-10 days may damage trachea and surrounding tissue

# IMMUNODEFICIENCY

## Common Variable Immunodeficiency (CVID)

1. Suspect in patient 20-40s yo with recurrent sinus or pulmonary infections (pneumonia, bronchitis, sinusitis) and opportunistic infections (thrush)
2. PP: Abnormal differentiation of B cells into plasma cells --> decreased Ig production
3. CF
   1. Suspect in patient 20-40s yo with recurrent sinus or pulmonary infections (pneumonia, bronchitis, sinusitis) and opportunistic infections (thrush)
   2. Chronic disease
      1. Autoimmune: RA, Thyroid disease
      2. Pulmonary (bronchiectasis, fibrosis)
      3. GI (chronic diarrhea, IBD-like conditions)
4. Dx: Measure serum immunoglobulin concentration = Quantitative immunoglobulin assay
   1. Very low IgG, Low IgA/IgM
   2. No response to vaccination
5. Management: Ig replacement

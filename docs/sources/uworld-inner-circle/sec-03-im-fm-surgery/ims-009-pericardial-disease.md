# PERICARDIAL DISEASE

ACUTE PERICARDITIS → Fluid Exudation = PERICARDIAL EFFUSION → Fast fluid accumulation = CARDIAC TAMPONADE

MECHANISM: Endothelial contraction/disruption → Inc vascular permeability → leakage of protein-rich fluid (exudate) → Inc interstitial oncotic pressure
MEDIATORS: leukotrienes (C4, D4, E4), histamine, serotonin

## Pleuritic Chest Pain Differential

1. Pericarditis
2. Pleuritis
3. Pneumothorax
4. Pulmonary embolus

## Acute Pericarditis

MC Cardiac manifestation of SLE

### Etiology

1. Viral or idiopathic
2. Autoimmune — SLE (causes serositis)
3. Uremia — Acute or chronic renal failure
   1. Elevated BUN >60mg/dL → inflammation of visceral and parietal layers
   2. Note: Diffuse ST elevation or PR depression not present (myocardium not inflamed)
4. Post-MI
   1. Early — Peri-infarction pericarditis
   2. Late — Dressler syndrome (immune response to damaged mesothelial cells)

### Diagnosis

1. Pericardial friction rub — "high frequency grating or scratching sound" that is triphasic
   - Atrial systole, ventricle systole, ventricle early diastole
   - "Diastolic sound with a squeaking sound heard at left sternal border"
2. Diffuse PR depression — atrial myocardium inflammation (in aVR lead as well)
3. Diffuse ST elevation — ventricular myocardium inflammation (all leads except aVR)

### Treatment

1. NSAIDs and Colchicine for viral or idiopathic (Colchicine lowers rate of recurrent pericarditis)
   - Note: NSAIDs avoided in pericarditis post-MI (except ASA for Dressler syndrome)
2. 2nd: Corticosteroids (prednisone)
3. Uremia caused — Dialysis

Note: Chest pain that is Positional, Pleuritic, sharP and reproduced by Palpation reduces likelihood of cardiac ischemia

## Constrictive Pericarditis

*IMPORTANT CAUSE OF RIGHT HEART FAILURE*

1. PP: Impaired ventricular filling during diastole → symptoms of decreased CO (fatigue, dyspnea) and signs of venous overload (elevated JVP, ascites, pedal edema)
2. Cause
   1. Idiopathic or viral
   2. Cardiac surgery (CABG, valve surgery) or radiation therapy
      - Complication of mediastinal irradiation ("Survivors of Hodgkin lymphoma")
      - Scarring → loss of normal elasticity → Thickened and calcified pericardium → limits diastolic filling → signs of RHF
      - Tx: Pericardiectomy
   3. Tuberculous pericarditis — MCC in developing countries (Africa, India, China)
3. Presentation
   1. Elevated JVP with prominent x and y descents
   2. Hepatojugular reflux
   3. Kussmaul's sign (lack of decrease in JVP on inspiration)
   4. Pericardial knock (mid-diastolic sound after S2)
   5. Peripheral edema, Ascites
4. Diagnosis
   1. ECG: nonspecific or AFib or low-voltage QRS
   2. CXR: Pericardial thickening and calcifications "spotty calcifications along left heart border" "ring of calcification around the heart"
   3. Echo: Increased pericardial thickness, abnormal septal motion, BIATRIAL ENLARGEMENT
5. Treatment: Pericardiectomy

## Purulent Pericarditis

1. Hematogenous spread of S. aureus
2. RF: Chronic hemodialysis (fluid shifts and bacteremia)
3. Complication: Pericardial effusion (distant heart sounds, low-voltage QRS, enlarged cardiac silhouette with clear lungs)
4. Dx: Urgent Echocardiography-guided Pericardiocentesis

## Pericardial Effusion

1. RF: Malignancy, Infectious or inflammatory state (TB, viral pericarditis, SLE, trauma, aortic dissection)
2. S/S
   1. Progressive dyspnea, chest fullness, fatigue
   2. ECG: Low QRS voltage +/- electrical alternans
   3. CXR: Enlarged cardiac silhouette and clear lung
   4. Echo: Large effusion +/- signs of tamponade (RA collapse)
3. Dx: Echo to visualize effusion
4. Tx
   1. Mild: Conservative management
   2. Acute: Pericardiocentesis, Cytologic fluid analysis
   3. Recurrence prevention: Catheter drainage or Pericardial window
5. Complication: Tamponade
   - Large or rapidly accumulating fluid → compression of cardiac chambers → impairs filling → decreased CO
   - Beck's triad: Hypotension, distant heart sounds, JVD

## Cardiac Tamponade

1. Cause: Fluid accumulation increases intrapericardial pressure above diastolic ventricular pressure → restricts venous return → lowers R and L ventricular filling → decreased preload, SV, CO
2. Clear lungs — decreased ventricular filling (preload) rather than volume overload
3. Pulsus paradoxus — normal inspiration lowers intrathoracic pressure, increases venous return to RV; in tamponade, RV free wall cannot expand → shifts IVS towards LV → further reduces LV filling → reduces preload, SV, CO
   - "Intra-arterial catheter in radial artery shows significant variation of systolic BP related to respiratory cycle"
4. RF: Acute Aortic Dissection
   - Rupture → rapid accumulation of blood → rapidly raises pericardial pressure → compression → limits diastolic filling → decrease in preload and CO → hypotension and syncope
5. Presentation
   1. Beck's triad: hypotension, distended neck veins, muffled heart sounds
   2. Pulsus paradoxus (>10mmHg drop in systolic BP upon inspiration)
   3. Reduced LV preload, SV, CO
6. Imaging: ECG: Low-voltage QRS with electrical alternans
7. Diagnosis: CXR: Increased cardiac silhouette — enlarged and globular; Unable to palpate point of maximal apical impulse
8. Treatment: Pericardiocentesis

# TERATOGENS

## Major Teratogenic Drugs

1. Chemotherapy
2. Isotretinoin: Messes up HOX genes (where appendages are placed as body develops)
3. Statins: Prevent cholesterol synthesis in fetus
4. Ergotamine: Vasoconstriction
5. Warfarin
6. Benzodiazepines
7. Danazol (used for endometriosis)
8. Cyclophosphamide (Renal Agenesis, Cleft Palate, no digits at birth)
9. Methotrexate (inhibits Dihydrofolate reductase) --> Can cause abortion of fetus, neural tube defects
10. ACE-I (Renal Agenesis + Oligohydramnios --> Limb development issues, hypoplastic facies)
11. Valproic Acid (Neural Tube defects, Cardiac problems, Cleft lip/cleft palate, hepatotoxic)
12. DES (Adenocarcinoma (Clear Cell) Carcinoma of the Vagina, Anatomical uterine anatomy — T shaped uterus)
13. TMP-SMX (Trimethoprim-Sulfamethoxazole) Folate synthesis inhibitor
14. Pyrimethamine-Sulfadiazine (Used for toxoplasmosis) Folate synthesis inhibitor

## Clinical Scenarios

### Isotretinoin

1. Lady on acne medication for a few years, lost to follow up, gets pregnant — most likely defect?
2. Isotretinoin messes up HOX genes --> where appendages are placed on the body as it develops
3. Prior to using Isotretinoin, must be on 2 forms of contraception

### Statins

1. Diabetic patient on oral hypoglycemics, statins — which is most dangerous to fetus?
2. HMG-CoA reductase inhibitor prevents cholesterol synthesis in fetus — NEVER give to pregnant woman

### Ergotamine

1. Woman on medication for migraines — contraindicated in pregnancy
2. Severe vasoconstriction of placental arteries --> IUGR and possible fetal demise
3. May cause uterine contractions (premature labor)
4. Other migraine meds: Acute: Sumatriptan; Chronic (8+/month): Beta blocker, TCA, topiramate

### Cocaine

1. IV drug user, 3rd trimester, sharp abdominal pain, bright blood from vagina --> Abruptio Placentae
2. Cocaine: Powerful sympathomimetic; constricts all blood vessels --> ischemia to placenta --> possible fetal demise
3. Asymmetric IUGR (Head/torso small, arms and legs normal)
4. Symmetric IUGR (whole torso proportionally small) — Common in TORCHESZ infections, chromosomal abnormalities

### Warfarin

1. Pregnant with prosthetic valve — what drug CANNOT be given?
2. Can cause fetal hemorrhage, fetal bone damage, fetal brain bleeds
3. Can use heparin/LMWH safely in pregnancy: dalteparin, enoxaparin

### Benzodiazepines

1. Contraindicated in pregnancy
2. In Eclampsia/Preeclampsia --> Magnesium Sulfate used (benzodiazepines are 2nd line)

### Danazol

1. Patient who wants to get pregnant is on treatment for Endometriosis — must discontinue
2. Pro-androgenic drug
   1. Male fetus: precocious puberty
   2. Female fetus: virilization

### Cyclophosphamide

1. Patient with GPA (Wegener's) — must discontinue prior to pregnancy
2. Renal Agenesis, Cleft Palate, no digits at birth

### Methotrexate

1. Woman on therapy for RA wants to become pregnant — must stop
2. Can cause abortion of fetus, neural tube defects

### Alcohol (Fetal Alcohol Syndrome)

1. IV drug user delivers newborn with VSD (holosystolic murmur LSB) + hypoplastic/smooth/thin philtrum, microcephaly, intellectual disability
2. Most common cause of preventable intellectual disability
3. If VSD uncorrected --> may lead to Eisenmenger syndrome
4. Down Syndrome comparison: Thin upper lip, sharp Palpebral fissures, single palmar crease (also in FAS)

### ACE-Inhibitors/ARBs

1. Renal Agenesis --> Amniotic fluid depletion --> Limb development issues, hypoplastic facies
2. Oligohydramnios (fluid index less than 5 cm)

### Antiepileptic Drugs

1. Phenytoin, Valproic Acid — should NEVER be given during pregnancy
2. Valproic Acid: Neural Tube defects, Cardiac problems, Cleft lip/cleft palate, hepatotoxic
3. If woman on antiepileptic becomes pregnant and begins to have seizures:
   1. Put back on antiepileptic (unless it's Valproic Acid)
   2. If on Valproic acid --> find a different antiepileptic
4. Antiepileptic safe in pregnancy: Lamotrigine (Also approved for Bipolar Depression)

### DES (Diethylstilbestrol)

1. 35yo lady, multiple 1st and 2nd trimester abortions, TVUS shows T-shaped uterus
2. Side effects: Adenocarcinoma (Clear Cell) of the Vagina, Anatomical uterine abnormalities

### Tetracycline

1. Pregnant woman treated for Lyme disease (borrelia burgdorferi) — newborn with bone development issues, tooth discoloration
2. Tetracyclines bind divalent ions well (antacids chelate tetracyclines, rendering them ineffective)
3. Lyme Disease: <8yo patient: AMOXICILLIN/macrolide (NO tetracyclines)
4. Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever: EVERYONE gets a tetracycline (children and pregnant women included)

### Chloramphenicol (Gray Baby Syndrome)

1. Immigrant mother delivers child, baby has circulatory collapse with ashen-gray skin discoloration
2. Rarely used in US, used in developing world
3. Used for pregnant mom with RMSF (Otherwise: Doxycycline)

### Other Scenarios

1. Pregnant mother with toxoplasma infection --> Treat with Spiramycin
2. Thalidomide --> Phocomelia (hypoplastic limbs, distal limbs connected to shoulder)
3. PTU and Methimazole --> Aplasia Cutis (absence of hair in localized spot on head) in Grave's disease patient
4. Paroxetine --> Can lead to pulmonary Hypertension in the newborn (most other SSRIs safe in pregnancy)
5. Lithium --> Atrialization of the right ventricle (Ebstein's anomaly)
   1. If pregnant with mood disorder, can use antipsychotics instead of lithium
6. Aminoglycosides (Gentamicin) --> Ototoxicity (newborn cannot hear)
   1. If suspect hearing loss --> perform audiometry
   2. Classic clue: Language Delays

### Gestational Diabetes Effects on Fetus

1. VSDs, Transposition of the Great Vessels, Caudal regression syndrome (Sirenomelia)
2. Insulin inhibits synthesis of surfactant --> Neonatal Respiratory Distress Syndrome
3. Hyperinsulinemia in mother --> beta islet cell hyperplasia in fetus --> excess insulin production
4. Insulin excess can cause: hypocalcemic seizures, hypoglycemic seizures, underdevelopment of left colon
5. Failure to pass meconium (Small left colon syndrome) — pathophysiology: hypoglycemia-induced release of glucagon

### Vaccine Rules in Pregnancy/Newborns

1. Newborn (<1 year old) / Pregnant woman: Do NOT give Live-attenuated vaccine (MMR, Varicella, Intranasal influenza)
   1. Single exception: Rotavirus vaccine (can be given to infants)
2. Child born to mom with active Hepatitis B --> give newborn HepB surface antigen AND HepB vaccine
   1. If a child develops Hepatitis B, 90% likely to develop chronic Hepatitis B
3. Child born to mom with HIV --> give newborn Zidovudine (AZT)

# Splenic Infarct

1. Wedge shaped infarct on CT (non-contrast, hypoperfusion causes hypodensity)
2. RF
   1. Thrombosis due to hypercoagulable state: Cancer, SLE, Antiphospholipid antibody syndrome (APLAS)
      1. "MR murmur, joint pain, LUQ pain, low platelets", management — Antinuclear Antibodies
         1. Possible splenic artery thrombosis or emboli from nonbacterial thrombotic endocarditis (MR)
         2. Also should get Antiphospholipid antibody to evaluate for APLAS
   2. Embolism due to AF, Endocarditis, Atheroma
   3. Hemoglobinopathy: Sickle Cell Dz

# Splenectomy

1. Higher risk of infection with encapsulated: N. gonorrhoeae, H. flu, S. pneumo due to impaired antibody-facilitated phagocytosis

# Trauma — Splenic Laceration (Blunt Abdominal Trauma)

1. Intraabdominal hemorrhage, shock, FAST finding — intraperitoneal fluid
2. (-) FAST does not rule out injury, FAST does not detect injury <200mL free fluid
3. Signs
   1. Kehr sign: Pain in left shoulder
   2. Balance sign: Dull percussion on Left and shifting dullness on Right
4. Injuries
   1. Contusion
   2. Subcapsular hematoma
   3. Laceration
5. Management
   1. HD unstable (+ frank peritonitis): Xlap
   2. HD stable (+ or - FAST): CT Abdomen/Pelvis with contrast, observe with serial Hb and embolization

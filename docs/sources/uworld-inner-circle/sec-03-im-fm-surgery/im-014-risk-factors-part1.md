# Risk Factors

## Key Abbreviations

- A/w = Associated with
- MC = most common
- MCC = most common cause
- MCCOD = MC Cause of death
- MIPF = most important prognostic factor
- MSC = most serious complication
- ML = most likely
- MLM = Most likely mechanism
- NBSIM = Next best step in management

## General RF

1. #1 RF in development of pressure ulcers --> immobility

## Association of Infectious Agents with Neoplasms

1. Cervical/anal/head and neck/vocal cord cancers --> HPV (HIV increases risk of Cervical Cancer)
2. Kaposi Sarcoma --> HHV-8
3. Bacillary angiomatosis --> Bartonella henselae (similar presentation to Kaposi, also in HIV)
4. Hodgkin's Lymphoma --> EBV (Reed-Sternberg cells)
5. Burkitt's Lymphoma --> EBV (8;14 translocation, starry sky)
6. Lymphomas after transplant --> EBV
7. Opportunistic Infections post transplant --> CMV (Prophylactic Valganciclovir)
8. MALToma --> H pylori
9. HCC --> Hep B + C

## CVS

1. MI test needed in dx of IE --> Blood culture (prior to Echo)
2. MIPF in pt with CAD --> Left ventricular function
3. MC complication of left atrial thrombus --> embolization
4. #1 RF for AFib --> Mitral stenosis
5. #1 RF for Mitral stenosis --> Rheumatic fever
6. Most likely initial presentation in Rheumatic Fever --> migratory polyarthritis
7. MSC + MCCOD of Acute rheumatic fever --> myocarditis
8. #1 RF for MR --> mitral valve prolapse
9. MC valvular complication a/w Marfan syndrome --> Mitral valve prolapse
10. MCC of death in Ehlers Danlos syndrome --> aortic dissection
11. MI physical exam finding in dx of AS --> pulsus parvus et tardus (delayed upstroke in carotid pulse)
12. #1 RF for AAA development --> smoking
13. MC complication of AAA --> rupture
14. Greatest predisposing risk factor to rupture of AAA --> diameter of aneurysm
15. Most important modifiable RF for CAD --> smoking
16. Screen AAA --> men + 65-75yo + smoker. If >5.5 cm --> surgery. Or >0.5cm/6 mos or 1 cm/yr growth
17. #1 RF for ED --> CVD
18. Hyperlipidemia screen --> men: every 5 year >35yo; women: every 5y >45 yo
19. MI predictor of survival in aortic coarctation --> Age of defect repair
20. #1 RF for stroke and aortic dissection --> HTN
21. MC complication of Chronic HTN --> LVH
22. MCCOD in hx of chronic HTN --> Acute MI
23. MCC of HTN in young reproductive age female --> OCP use
24. MSC in low BMI pt --> ventricular arrhythmia from hypokalemia
25. MCC of arrhythmia in STEMI --> premature ventricular contraction
26. MI disease associated with central sleep apnea --> heart failure
27. Most common mutation (chromosome) that gives rise to HOCM --> Chromosome 11

## DERM

1. Most damaging UV radiation to skin --> UVB
2. #1 RF for SCC --> cumulative sun exposure
3. Most likely malignant complication in pt on chronic immunosuppressant --> SCC of skin
4. MC malignancy in kidney transplant recipient --> SCC of the skin
5. Most important prognostic factor in pt with melanoma --> depth of invasion "Breslow depth"
6. ML determination of Melanoma spread --> breslow depth
7. ML subtype of melanoma in a pt with a dark complexion --> acrolentiginous melanoma
8. MC complication of ganglion cyst resection --> recurrence
9. ML outcome of actinic keratosis --> resolution of disease
10. MCC of folliculitis --> S. aureus (hot tub — Pseudomonas)
11. MCC of skin abscess --> S. aureus
12. MCC of impetigo --> S. aureus
13. ML inciting factor in pt with erythema multiforme --> Recurrent herpes simplex virus infection
14. MSC of Perineal cellulitis --> necrotizing fasciitis — may progress to Fournier's gangrene
    1. In a person with necrotizing fasciitis include Clindamycin in the Abx regimen
15. ML inciting factor for TEN/SJS --> drugs
16. Most likely barrier to proper healing of wound --> persistent infection
17. #1 RF for Hidradenitis Suppurativa --> Smoking (not hygiene) + Obesity, Diabetes Mellitus

## PULM

1. Lung CA --> low dose CT
   1. Men and women, 55-80 who have >20pp smoking hx, who currently smoke + less than 15 yr since quitting
2. MCC cancer a/w asbestos exposure --> Bronchogenic carcinoma
3. Mesothelioma has no relation to smoking — psammoma bodies
4. MI goal in mgmt. of COPD --> smoking cessation
5. Which pneumoconiosis increases risk of TB --> Silicosis
6. #1 RF in development of lung disease related to asbestos --> cumulative exposure to asbestos fibers
7. MI factor in determining TB treatment success --> adhering to treatment regimen
8. 2 MC complications of influenza --> Primary influenza pneumonia, Secondary bacterial superinfection
9. MC infectious precipitant of an acute COPD exacerbation --> H. influenza
10. #1 RF for ARDS --> Sepsis
11. COVID19 vaccine is an mRNA vaccine
    1. Very low oxygenation --> PaO2/FiO2 very low --> Whiteout lungs
    2. Tx: ventilator, low tidal volumes and high PEEP (lung protective strategies)
    3. Make them prone — lie them on bellies in ICU
    4. Mortality rate ARDS 40%
12. Most important factor in preventing lung injury when using a ventilator --> mechanical ventilation with lung protective strategies (low TV)
13. 3 complications of O2 therapy --> Bronchopulmonary dysplasia, Retinopathy of prematurity, IVH
14. #1 RF OSA in Kids --> Adenotonsillar hypertrophy
15. #1 RF OSA in Adults --> Obesity
16. #1 RF Aspirin-exacerbated respiratory distress --> Nasal polyps
    1. Aspirin is irreversible COX 1&2 inhibitor so more flux via lipoxygenase pathway form more leukotrienes --> Bronchoconstriction
    2. Tx: Leukotriene Receptor inhibitor — Montelukast or Zafirlukast OR Lipoxygenase inhibitor — Zileuton (SE: Hepatotoxic)
    3. Another name for Leukotriene receptor = CYSLT1 receptor
17. MLM in patients with normal O2 sat and perioral cyanosis after TMP-SMX --> Methemoglobinemia
    1. Drugs: TMP-SMX, Nitrate (MI patient — chest pain, CAD), Dapsone (PCP prophylaxis, Leprosy Tx, Dermatitis herpetiformis)
    2. All iron is in ferrous form (Fe2+) but if a powerful oxidizing agent adds (becoming more positive) becomes ferric iron (Fe3+ cannot carry O2) --> cyanotic
    3. TX = Methylene blue, Vit C, Cimetidine (H2 blocker)

## ENDOCRINE

1. Most common arrhythmia in hyperthyroidism --> A fib
2. Controlling blood glucose only decreases microvascular risks (retinopathy, neuro, nephropathy), not macrovascular (CAD, stroke)
3. #1 RF for papillary thyroid cancer --> prior chest/neck radiation
4. MLCP in pt dx with primary hyperparathyroidism --> nephrolithiasis
5. MSC of acromegaly --> Death from dilated cardiomyopathy
6. MCCOD in acromegaly --> Heart failure
7. MLM of disease in pt presenting with signs of hypopituitarism --> pituitary adenoma
8. MC complication of DM (esp T1) --> Insulin-induced hypoglycemia
9. Most important mechanism underlying hyperglycemia in T1DM --> Gluconeogenesis
10. MC pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor in pt with Hx of MEN1 --> Gastrinoma
11. ML symptom that would trigger initial presentation in pt with MEN1 --> Sx of hypercalcemia
12. MLCP in MEN2 --> Medullary thyroid cancer

## Environmental/Poisoning

1. MCC of cyanide poisoning --> House fire
2. MCCOD in pt struck by lightning --> Cardiorespiratory arrest

## GI

1. MC infectious cause of odynophagia --> Candida albicans
2. Biggest RF in iron deficiency anemia pt with h/o H.Pylori --> PUD
3. ML vessel involved in bleeding gastric ulcer --> Left gastric A
4. MC artery involved in bleeding duodenal ulcer --> Gastroduodenal A (also involved in duodenal hematoma in children post trauma)
5. ML clinical complication of Barrett's esophagus --> Esophageal ulcers w/ strictures
6. #1 RF for esophageal adenoCa --> Barrett's esophagus which is caused by GERD
7. MC complication of Appendicitis --> Periappendiceal abscess
8. MC complication of cholelithiasis --> biliary colic
9. Diabetic with RUQ may have gangrenous cholecystitis --> Emergent cholecystectomy
10. NBSIM of Acalculous cholecystitis --> Percutaneous cholecystostomy (avoid cholecystectomy due to high mortality risk)
11. #1 RF for cholangiocarcinoma in US --> Primary sclerosing cholangitis
12. Ranson's criteria for pancreatitis (criteria for admission to ICU) — GA LAW:
    1. Glucose >200, AST >250, LDH >350, Age >55, WBC >16000
    2. 48 hours post admission — C and HOBBS:
       1. HypoCalcemia <8 mg/dL, Hematocrit dropping by >10%, O2 sat <60 mmHg, BUN increase on admission, Base deficit >4 mg/dL, Six liters of fluid needed in 48 hr period
13. MCC of acute pancreatitis in America --> Gallstone "Lithogenic bile"
14. MCC chronic pancreatitis --> Alcoholism
15. #1 RF for chronic pancreatitis in a child --> Cystic fibrosis
16. MI therapeutic step in mgmt of acute pancreatitis --> IV fluid resuscitation
17. MC complication seen within weeks of acute pancreatitis --> Pancreatic pseudocyst
18. #1 RF for pancreatic adenocarcinoma --> Smoking
19. MCC of esophageal varices --> Portal HTN
20. MCCOD in cirrhosis --> Rupture of esophageal varices
21. MCC of cirrhosis in kids --> Alpha 1 AT def
22. MCC of fatty change in liver --> chronic alcoholism
23. ML cause of abnormal LFTs on routine labs --> non-alcoholic fatty liver disease
24. #1 RF for hepatic adenoma --> OCP use
25. #1 prevention of Hepatocellular carcinoma --> Hep B vaccination (Hep B helps against Hep D)
26. 2 #1 RF in development of HCC --> Cirrhosis + Hep B infx
27. #1 RF for liver angiosarcoma --> Vinyl chloride exposure
28. #1 RF Budd Chiari --> Hypercoagulopathy = Polycythemia vera (then PNH, OCP + Smoking, Nephrotic syndrome)
29. MIPF in mesenteric ischemia --> bowel infarction
30. MI complication of peritoneal dialysis --> peritonitis
31. #1-2 RF for development of C. diff colitis --> hospitalization and Abx
32. MCC of osmotic diarrhea --> lactase deficiency
33. MLM of treatment failure in pt placed on gluten free diet for celiac's disease --> non-adherence
34. MI oncologic association in long term celiac disease --> small bowel lymphoma (enteric associated T-cell lymphoma)
35. MC complication of meckel's diverticulum --> bleeding
36. MSC of hirschsprung disease --> death due to enterocolitis from super dilated bowel
37. MC complication of diverticulosis --> diverticulitis
38. MSC of UC --> toxic megacolon
39. ML finding on further eval of 55 yo male presenting with Hb of 8, mcv 60 --> Polyps/CRC
40. MIPF in determining outcome of treatment in colon ca --> Stage at diagnosis
41. Colon CA Screening Guidelines --> Colonoscopy is gold standard
    1. Normally: Colonoscopy q 10 years from 50-75 yo or CT Colonography q5 years
    2. UC pt's: starting 8 years after diagnosis then every 1-2 years after
    3. If FHx colon cancer: Age 40 years or 10 year prior to family member's age at diagnosis
    4. FAP pt's: q 1 year starting at 10-12 yo

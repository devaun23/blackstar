# Hypoparathyroidism

1. Cause
   1. MC: Post-surgical (Primary)
      1. During sub-total parathyroidectomy (removal 3.5 parathyroid glands for parathyroid hyperplasia)
      2. Tx: Immediate Calcium gluconate
   2. Autoimmune Primary Hyperparathyroidism
      1. MC nonsurgical cause
      2. Hypocalcemia, Hyperphosphatemia
   3. Congenital absence: DiGeorge syndrome
   4. Defective Calcium-sensing receptor on the parathyroid gland
   5. Non-autoimmune destruction due to infiltrative disease — Hemochromatosis, Wilson disease, Neck irradiation
   6. Low Mag levels (malnutrition — Alcoholic)
      1. HypoMg --> Reduced PTH activity --> Hypocalcemia
2. CF
   1. PTH activates osteoclast activity and bone resorption to maintain calcium homeostasis
   2. inc PO4, dec Ca, dec Vit D (PTH stimulates renal conversion of 25-hydroxyvitamin D to active form)
   3. Perioral paresthesias, carpopedal spasm, laryngospasm, prolonged QT interval
   4. Chvostek sign (cheek), Trousseau sign (blood pressure cuff)
3. Tx
   1. Primary
      1. Calcium
      2. Calcitriol to maintain low-normal levels

# Hyperparathyroidism

## Primary HyperPTH

1. inc PTH --> inc Ca, dec PO4
2. inc PTH causes increased reabsorption of Ca from Distal Tubule, but net urinary Ca excretion is increased due to excess resorption of Ca from bones
3. Cause:
   1. Parathyroid Adenoma (#1)
   2. Hyperplasia
   3. Carcinoma — Squamous Cell Carcinoma
   4. Increased risk in MEN 1 and 2A
   5. Resembles Primary HyperPTH: Familial Hypocalciuric Hypercalcemia
      1. inc Ca, inc PTH, Low urinary Ca excretion (<100mg/24hr)
      2. Not curable by surgery
4. S/S: HyperCa symptoms (abdominal pain, renal stones, bone pain, neuropsychiatric symptoms)
5. Parathyroidectomy Indications
   1. <50yo, Symptomatic hypercalcemia
   2. Complications: Osteoporosis, Nephrolithiasis/Calcinosis, CKD (GFR <60ml/min)
   3. Elevated risk of Complications: Calcium >1 mg/dl above normal, Urinary Calcium excretion >400mg/day

## Secondary HyperPTH

1. dec Ca --> inc PTH; Normal or inc PO4
2. Causes
   1. Celiac Disease
      1. Malabsorption of Vitamin D due to loss of villi in the small intestine
         1. Activated Vitamin D required for optimal intestinal absorption of Ca and PO4
         2. Calcium absorption impaired = dec Ca --> (+) PTH
         3. PTH activates release of Ca from the bone, inc Renal Ca reabsorption, dec renal PO4 reabsorption
         4. Ca levels restored to normal or mildly low but PO4 levels significantly reduced
      2. Deficiency of Vitamin D leads to Osteomalacia (abnormal bone mineralization with bone pain) and Osteoporosis (dec bone density)
   2. ESRD: Renal Failure
      1. Normally kidneys respond to PTH secretion via increasing 1a-hydroxylase and converting inactive Vit D to active Vit D
      2. In renal failure, kidneys cannot respond to PTH
         1. Low Ca --> Excessive PTH elevation --> Excess bone resorption = Renal Osteodystrophy = bone pain
         2. dec Renal Phosphate excretion: inc PO4 leads to formation of Ca-PO4 complexes in the blood (binds free Calcium)
   3. Vitamin D Deficiency
   4. Xray: Subperiosteal resorption *highly specific for inc PTH

## Tertiary HyperPTH: worsening of Secondary

1. Autonomous secretion of PTH unresponsive to plasma calcium
2. RF: Longstanding CKD/ESRD, Chronic dec Ca, inc PO4
3. PP
   1. Parathyroid hyperplasia and loss of feedback inhibition of PTH by Calcium
   2. CKD leads to
      1. Decreased 1,25-dihydroxyvitamin D activation --> Low Ca
      2. Decreased GFR and inadequate clearance of PO4 --> High PO4
      3. Resulting PTH stimulation leads to High PTH levels = 2nd HPT
         1. Long-standing CKD = Chronic PTH stimulation causes Parathyroid Hyperplasia
         2. Leads to autonomous PTH secretion
            1. Not suppressed by rising Ca levels due to down regulation of Ca-sensing receptor and Vitamin D receptor in PT gland
            2. = Tertiary HyperPT
4. Labs
   1. Autonomous PTH — Very high PTH
   2. Bone mobilization — Mild/Moderate HyperCa
   3. Renal PO4 retention — inc PO4
5. Tx
   1. Refractory to meds; Parathyroidectomy

## Osteoporosis

1. Serum Ca, Vitamin D, Magnesium normal — However Low Vitamin D levels can cause Elevated PTH in the presence of Normal Serum Calcium

Note:
- PTH main goal is to increase Ca: increases Ca and decreases PO4 (less PO4 also decreases calcium-phosphate binding that further increases calcium)
- Vit D main goal is bone mineralization: increase Ca and PO4 because requires Ca-PO4 complex deposits

# PULMONARY SLEEP DISORDERS

## Obstructive Sleep Apnea

### Pediatric OSA

1. PP: Adenotonsillar hypertrophy — causes significant nasal airway obstruction leading to mouth breathing
2. Intermittent upper airway collapse
3. CF
   1. Night
      1. Loud snoring, pauses in breathing, gasping
      2. Enuresis, parasomnias (sleeping walking, sleep terrors)
   2. Day
      1. Inappropriate naps or falling asleep during school
      2. Irritability, inattention, learning problems, behavioral problems
      3. Mouth breathing, nasal speech
4. Complications
   1. Poor growth (failure to thrive)
   2. Poor school performance
   3. Cardiopulmonary: HTN, Structural heart disease
5. Management
   1. Tonsillectomy & Adenoidectomy

## Obesity Hypoventilation Syndrome

1. Chronic alveolar hypoventilation
   1. Hypercapnia --> Compensatory Metabolic Alkalosis with increased HCO3 concentration
   2. Hypoxemia --> Pulmonary HTN and Polycythemia
2. Obesity reduces chest wall and lung compliance
   1. Decrease Tidal Volume and TLC
   2. Increased Airway resistance
   3. "Can't breathe" due to excess weight and altered lung mechanics
   4. "Won't breathe" due to decreased chemosensitivity to hypercapnia from persistent nocturnal hypoventilation
5. Additional features
   1. Nocturia: increased ANP secretion
      1. Atrial distention due to increasingly negative intrathoracic pressure from occluded breathing during sleep or hypoxia-induced pulmonary vasoconstriction
   2. Daytime somnolence
   3. Headaches
6. Labs: Hypercarbia

# Risk Factors (Part 2)

## HEM/ONC

1. Most common gene mutation seen in malignancy --> p53 mutation
2. Most important prognostic factor in cancer --> stage of tumor (TNM staging). #1 Metastasis >> LN involvement
   1. Osteosarcoma staged by MRI
3. MC complication of terminal disseminated malignancy --> Cachexia
4. MSC of SCD --> acute chest syndrome
5. MCC of anemia in alcoholic --> anemia of chronic disease (be careful not to pick megaloblastic)
6. ML trigger of hemolytic ep in G6PD --> acute infection
7. MCC of thrombocytopenia in healthcare setting --> use of heparin
8. #1 RF Vit K def in hospitalized pt = broad-spectrum ABX (Warfarin leads to functional Vit K deficiency and Abx further exacerbate by killing GI flora)
9. Plt dysfxn most likely presenting clinical complaint --> nose bleeds
10. ML cause of perioral cyanosis and normal O2 saturation in pt on oxidizing agent/drugs --> Methemoglobinemia
    1. TX = methylene blue, vit C, Cimetidine (H2 blocker)
    2. #1 RF --> Nitrate (MI patient — chest pain, CAD)
    3. TMP-SMX, Dapsone: PCP prophylaxis (allergic to TMP-SMX), leprosy Tx, dermatitis herpetiformis
11. Favorable prognostic factor in an individual with ALL --> presence of t(12;21) translocation
12. Most important prognostic factor in a pt with Hodgkin's L --> clinical stage of disease not type
    1. Good prognostic factors for Hodgkin's: Young person > older person, More lymphocytes, Fewer Reed Sternberg cells
13. #1 RF DIC --> Sepsis
14. MC complication of BM transplant --> GVHD (CD8 mediated)
15. MCC of infx in BMT --> aspergillus (put on voriconazole). #2 is candida
16. MCC of infx in solid organ transplant (heart, kidney, lung, pancreas) --> candida
17. MCC of infx overall in transplant recipient --> CMV
18. Most likely cause of rejection in transplant pt --> acute rejection
19. MC malignancy in kidney transplant recipient --> SCC of the skin
20. ML infx due to blood transfusion --> CMV infection (don't choose HEP C)
21. MSC in pt receiving blood transfusion in the first 6-12 hours --> Transfusion associated circulatory overload (increased risk in CKD)
22. ML malignant complication of pt exposed to radiation --> acute leukemia (papillary thyroid only if radiation to head and neck)
23. ML reproductive complication of receiving systemic chemotherapy --> hypogonadism
24. #1 RF for uterine sarcoma --> radiation therapy to pelvis

## Mets

1. Most likely bone to have mets from cancer elsewhere in body --> vertebral column
2. Top 2 malignancies a/w mets to vertebrae: #1 Breast cancer, #2 Prostate ca (osteoblastic mets)
3. Most likely primary malignancy a/w mets to liver --> lung cancer
4. Most likely primary malignancy a/w mets to brain --> lung cancer
5. Most likely primary malignancy a/w mets to lung --> breast cancer
6. Most likely primary malignancy in child with multiple bone mets --> neuroblastoma

## ID

1. Diabetic pt with mucormycosis of sinuses --> give Ampho B + debride extensively
2. MCC CAP --> Strep. Pneumo
3. MCC UTI --> E.coli
4. MSC of tetanus --> pneumonia and cardiac arrest
5. MLCP of superficial dermatophytosis --> tinea pedis
   1. If pt has nail fungus or tinea capitis --> tx with oral agent, oral terbinafine/itraconazole
6. MC STI in US --> chlamydia
7. Pt from Hawaii with conjunctivitis --> Leptospirosis (Tx: Doxycycline)
8. MC HIV serotype in the U.S. --> HIV-1
9. MLM of transmission of HIV in US --> men having sex with men
10. Most likely cause of infx in HIV pt CD4 count of 25 --> PCP pneumonia or systemic candida infx
11. MCC of pneumothorax in HIV pt --> PCP infx
12. Most likely cause of HIV in Healthcare worker --> Needlestick injury
13. Most common neoplastic complication of HIV --> Kaposi sarcoma

## Immunology

1. MC complication a/w chronic granulomatous disease --> Pneumonia
2. MCC of infx in burn pts --> P. aeruginosa sepsis

## MSK

1. MLCP of relapsing polychondritis (inflammation of cartilage) --> red hot painful ear
2. MIPF in preserving neurologic function in spinal cord compression --> early diagnosis
3. #1 RF for OSA --> obesity
4. #1 modifiable RF for knee OA --> obesity
5. MI non-pharmacologic intervention in mgmt of osteoarthritis --> exercise
6. Common RF for osteoporosis --> Low BMI
7. MLCP in pt with MG --> ptosis
8. 2 biggest RF for carpal tunnel syndrome --> pregnancy and RA
9. MC extra-articular manifestation of ankylosing spondylitis --> anterior uveitis
10. ML cause of knee pain in pt <45 yrs --> Patellofemoral pain syndrome
    1. Young female + pain on walking up or down the stairs + chronic poorly localized anterior knee + atrophy of quadriceps + pain on isometric contraction of quadriceps (squatting, lunging)
11. ML cause of pain at inferior heel --> plantar fasciitis (+ calcaneal spurs)
12. MCCOD in SLE --> ischemic heart disease / Infx from immunosuppression
13. Most likely organ to dev complication in pt with lupus --> kidney
14. Lupus Ab that has the strongest association with kidney disease --> Anti ds-DNA Ab
15. MC cardiac finding in SLE --> fibrinous pericarditis
16. MCC of Drug induced lupus --> procainamide (used for WPW)
17. MC presenting complaint a/w dx of scleroderma --> Raynaud's phenomenon (skin most commonly involved)
18. MCCOD in Systemic sclerosis --> respiratory failure
19. MLCP in pt with Paget's disease --> bone pain
20. Malignancy that may develop in the future in retinoblastoma pt --> osteosarcoma

## RENAL

1. MCCOD in CKD --> CV Disease / arrhythmia followed by infection
2. MCCOD in ADPKD --> CV Disease (not SAH!)
3. MSC of extra-renal ADPKD --> rupture of intracranial aneurysm
4. MCCOD in pt with kidney transplant --> CV disease
5. #1 RF for ESRD in US --> Diabetes
6. Diabetic pt with pyelonephritis not responsive to Abx --> obtain a repeat CT to rule out pyelonephritis complications (perinephric abscess/emphysematous pyelonephritis)
7. MC complication of systemic amyloidosis --> renal failure
8. ML finding on ECG in pt with cardiac amyloidosis --> low voltage
9. MCC of nephrotic syndrome in African Americans --> FSGS
10. MCC of nephrotic syndrome in Caucasians --> Membranous nephropathy
11. MCC of nephrotic syndrome in kids --> Minimal change disease
12. ML Renal complication associated w/ SCD --> symptomatic hematuria (seen in sickle cell trait also)
13. #1 RF for hyperphosphatemia --> Chronic renal failure
14. ML renal complication associated with aminoglycoside --> ATN
15. Most likely clinical presentation in pt on HCTZ w/ EKG showing prolonged QT interval + U wave --> muscle weakness and fatigue due to hypokalemia
16. MCC of DI --> use of lithium
17. ML Mets of Wilms tumor --> Lungs
18. ML Mets of Neuroblastoma --> Bone *Neuroblastoma also calcify
19. #1 RF for General RCC --> Smoking
    1. Paraneoplastic syndrome — produces EPO
    2. Can cause L varicocele
    3. Histo — clear cells
    4. Most likely mets = lungs (Wilms tumor likes to metastasize to lungs as well)
    5. Most important predictor of prognosis = renal vein route to systemic circulation
20. Most important prognostic factor in pt with RCC --> invasion of renal vein
21. ML complication of VHL --> bilateral RCC
22. ML Mets of RCC --> Lungs
23. #1 RF for bladder cancer --> smoking
24. #1 RF for Egyptians with squamous cell carcinoma of the bladder --> Schistosoma haematobium
    1. Transitional cells under stress from S. haematobium undergo metaplasia squamous epithelium dysplasia SCC with keratin pearls +/- hyperCa 2/2 SCC production of PTHrP
25. RF of urinary incontinence:
    1. Age and multiple vaginal deliveries (stress incontinence)
    2. Diabetes --> overflow incontinence
    3. MS --> urge incontinence
26. MC complication of Upper UTI --> hydronephrosis
27. ML site of obstruction in nephrolithiasis --> ureter or UVJ
28. MC complication of BPH --> obstructive uropathy
29. MLM in Pt with hyponatremia and increased urine osmolality --> ectopic ADH production

## Pregnancy

1. All pregnant women 1st prenatal visit --> screen syphilis, asymptomatic bacteriuria, HIV
2. Pregnant + pyelonephritis = Treat + Nitrofurantoin prophylaxis for the rest of pregnancy
3. Rh immunoglobulin screen --> 28-32 wks + within 72 hours of delivery. Determine dose --> Kleihauer-Betke test
4. #1 RF for endometritis --> C-section
5. #1 RF for placenta previa --> prior C-section
6. #1 RF for preeclampsia --> prev hx of Preeclampsia / nulliparity
7. #1 RF for uterine inversion --> Prior inversion
8. #1 RF for ectopic pregnancy --> hx of ectopic pregnancy and smoking (affects cilia)
9. #1 RF for chorioamnionitis --> prolonged rupture of membranes
10. HY RF to preterm labor --> bacterial vaginosis
11. #1 RF for Cervical incompetence --> cervical conization procedure or LEEP
12. #1 RF for fetal macrosomia --> gestational diabetes or preexisting diabetes
13. #1 RF for shoulder dystocia --> fetal macrosomia
14. #1 RF for elevated maternal serum alpha-fetoprotein --> Incorrect pregnancy dating
15. MCC of placental abruption --> trauma/cocaine
16. Combined Contraception (Triphasic contain Estrogen) CI: Hepatic adenoma, Smoking, Factor 5 Leiden
17. Copper IUD CI: Wilson's disease
18. RF for Asherman's syndrome --> history of uterine curettage
19. MC pregnancy-related complication a/w Anti-phospholipid Ab's --> recurring spontaneous abortions (Elevated PTT)
    1. Prophylactic Heparin
20. MC complication a/w first 20 wks of pregnancy --> spontaneous abortion

### Mom

1. MC ovarian mass in pregnancy --> Corpus luteum cyst
2. MLM behind increased insulin resistance in pregnancy --> human placental lactogen

### Baby

1. MC complication in first few hours of life in baby of diabetic mom --> hypoglycemia (islet cell hyperplasia)
2. MCCOD 1mo-1 year --> SIDS
3. #1 RF for SIDS --> Smoking (passive) > Prematurity > Prone sleeping
4. #1 Complication in a kid with white reflex --> RB gene mutation
   1. Osteosarcoma — mostly in Knee aka Osteogenic sarcoma

## REPRODUCTIVE

### Female

1. Breast CA Screening --> every year from 40 yo. USPSTF --> every 2 years start at 50 until age 75
2. #1 RF for Breast Cancer --> Age (higher chance of spontaneous genetic mutations) #2: Increased Estrogen exposure
3. MIPF of Breast Ca --> 1. number of axillary LN "spread". 2. Size of tumor
4. #1 RF for Prostate and Breast Ca --> Age
5. #1 RF for Cervical cancer --> HPV
   1. Cervical CA screen --> every 3 years 21-29 regardless of sexual activity. >30-65 --> HPV + pap every 5 years
   2. ML initial clinical presentation of Cervical Ca --> Abnormal vaginal bleeding
6. MCCOD in Cervical cancer --> Renal failure esp ureters (cancer spreads there)
7. MIPF of cervical cancer --> 1. stage at which it's diagnosed. 2. Involvement of pelvic/paraaortic LN
8. #1 RF for Endometrial cancer --> unopposed estrogen > endometrial hyperplasia
9. #1 RF for Ovarian cancer --> FHx of OCa
10. #1 RF Uterine Sarcoma --> Prior radiation
11. MLM behind Endometriosis --> reverse menses thru fallopian tubes
12. #1 RF for female infertility/ectopic pregnancy --> PID + scarring from previous PID
13. Of the 3 main causes of vaginitis, the sexually transmitted one is --> Trichomonas

### Male

1. MCC of impotence >50 years --> Vascular insufficiency
   1. Cremasteric reflex present = Sacral input working

### Deformities/Congenital

1. MC complication of cleft lip/palate --> Chronic OM / speech problems
2. Boy with a genetic mutation most likely pathogenesis --> Enzyme defect
3. Mechanism of disease in 25 yo 6ft male, infertile, micropenis --> Klinefelter's (genetic nondisjunction — seminiferous tubule dysgenesis)

## PSYCH

1. #1 RF for suicide is prior suicide attempt

## NEURO

1. MIPF in retinal detachment --> time to surgical intervention to fix
2. MSC of ICP --> Brain herniation
3. #1 RF for intracerebral hmg --> HTN
4. MCC of rapidly progressive dementia --> Creutzfeldt-Jakob Disease
5. #1 RF for peripheral neuropathy in US --> diabetes
6. MCC of noncommunicating hydrocephalus in neonate --> stricture/obstruction in cerebral aqueduct of Sylvius
7. Any pt with CNS infx MLM of disease --> hematogenous spread
8. #1 RF for optic neuritis --> MS
9. MCC of blindness in HIV --> CMV retinitis
10. Pt with NF2 requires regular hearing screening + annual brain MRIs from ~10 yrs — 4th decade of life
11. Pt with NF1 --> screen for optic nerve gliomas
12. Common complication of Sturge-Weber syndrome --> seizures, developmental delay
13. MLM of disease in status epilepticus --> low level of antiepileptic drug

## ALCOHOLICS

1. #1 RF for fasting hypoglycemia in US --> Alcoholism

## Random

1. MC anterior mediastinal mass --> thymoma
2. MC middle mediastinal mass --> Lymphadenopathy
3. MC posterior mediastinal mass --> neurogenic tumor / schwannoma
4. MCCOD 1-44 years --> Motor vehicle accidents
5. MCCOD in ICU --> septic shock
6. MLC of sepsis in hospitalized pt --> indwelling catheter. MC bug is E. coli
7. A pt that is nauseous/vomiting --> don't place on BiPAP or CPAP
8. MLM behind weight increase in hospitalized pts --> increased total body sodium --> due to increased blood volume and hydrostatic pressure
9. MCC of Vit K deficiency in hospitalized pt --> use of Abx
10. #1 RF for development of pressure ulcers --> increased pressure on capillaries
11. Pt admitted to hospital in US over 65 yrs; most likely dx on admission --> CHF
12. In a hospitalized pt with "bones, groans, psychiatric overtones" (hypercalcemia) --> malignancy
13. A pt about to die, family comes in — don't ask close ended questions, ask how much they know, how much they want to know. Pick an answer that encourages conversation. Respond with a question.

## ELDERLY RF

1. 50yo: Zoster (Shingles) vaccine
2. 65yo: Pneumococcal 23V vaccine
3. Check vision, hearing, fall risk, urinary incontinence
4. MC Valvular abnormality in elderly --> AS
5. MCC of blindness in elderly --> macular degeneration
6. Polypharmacy — review meds for adverse effects and reduce falls (BEERS criteria)
7. Look out for Elderly abuse
8. Assess functional ability — assess ADLs
   1. Basic: Things needed to live independently
   2. Instrumental: Things needed to interact w/ society — make own food, manage meds, manage finances
9. MMSE to evaluate cognitive functioning (abN <24)
10. Mini-Cog --> tell pt a series of words then draw a clock then recall the words
11. Check TSH, B12, meds, S. Na, BNP if they have cognitive dysfunction
12. MCC of hearing loss in elderly --> presbycusis (high freq hearing loss first)
13. Best screening test to determine hearing loss --> take history then whispered voice test then audiometry
14. Access for Presbyopia, macular degeneration, cataracts
15. Rule out depression in the elderly esp in CKD, stroke, CAD pts; older pts have a greater risk of suicide
    1. Don't always use SIGECAPS for elderly; instead ask "have you felt depressed/sad/low" and "have you lost interest" in the past 2 wks
    2. SSRI is DOC — do NOT pick TCA (S/E profile)
16. Fall risk assessment --> ask for h/o problems w/ gait or balance in past year, do a get up and go test (normal is 10-11sec, abN is >20 sec)
    1. Interventions that dec fall risk --> exercise, physiotherapy, Vit D supplementation
17. Urge incont. / overactive bladder (hypertonic detrusor) --> bladder training is the best Tx, biofeedback, oxybutynin, tolterodine (best avoided in elderly; Tolterodine can cause close angle glaucoma)
18. Elderly women, G6P6 (multiple deliveries) --> Kegel's exercise, sling procedure
19. Overflow incont. (hypotonic detrusor) --> self catheterization, timed voiding
20. Functional incont. (cognitive problems or arthritis) --> need caregiver
21. Living will aka Advanced Directive
22. Surrogate decision maker --> use next of kin (spouse then adult children then parents then siblings then other relatives)
23. Health care proxy (DPOA durable power of attorney) — make only medical decisions when patient is incapacitated
24. Full power of attorney — make decisions beyond medical decisions
25. POLST (physician orders for life sustaining) --> what pt requires in emergency situation
26. Palliative care <6 mos prognosis --> initiate Hospice care (can be at home)
27. Elderly pt driving h/o mild dementia or taking benzo, TCA or h/o vision/falls/ambulation issues --> report to DMV
28. Pressure ulcer stages:
    1. Stage 1: erythema on skin, non blanchable
    2. Stage 2: lose partial thickness of skin epi and dermis
    3. Stage 3: subcut tissue exposed but no involvement of fascia/bone/musc/tendon — debridement
    4. Stage 4: involvement of fascia, muscle, tendon or bone — max nutrition, consider debridement, apply occlusive dressings

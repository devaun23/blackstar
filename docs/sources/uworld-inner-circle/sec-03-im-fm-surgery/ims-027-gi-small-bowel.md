# Absorption tests

1. Urine test for reducing substances: Glucosuria, Galactosuria
2. Stool test for reducing substances: Lactose intolerance
3. Hydrogen breath test: Carb malabsorption (Lactose intolerance)
4. Acid steatocrit test: Fat malabsorption

# Ascariasis

1. Small bowel obstruction; may cause Jaundice via Bile duct obstruction
2. Peripheral Eosinophilia
3. Endemic regions: Asia, Africa, South America
4. Tx: Albendazole or Mebendazole

# Groin hernia

1. Direct Inguinal
2. Indirect Inguinal
3. Femoral
   1. Can lead to small bowel obstruction (SBO) due to small bowel herniation
      1. High-pitched bowel sounds, distended loops of bowel with air-fluid levels
      2. Incarceration (entrapment) may lead to strangulation (ischemia, necrosis)
   2. Elective surgical repair due to risk of incarceration and strangulation

# Celiac Disease

1. PP: Immune mediated hypersensitivity to gluten
   1. Immune response to gliadin causes inflammation in the SI --> villi atrophy --> malabsorption of nutrients, fat soluble vitamins — Vitamin A, D, E, K and Fe
2. CF:
   1. GI
      1. Diarrhea +/- steatorrhea, weight loss
      2. Abdominal pain
      3. Flatulence/bloating
      4. dec D-xylose absorption: D-xylose absorption depends on viable mucosa (not enzymatic breakdown)
      5. Late: Ulcerative jejunitis, Enteropathy-associated TC lymphoma
   2. Mucocutaneous
      1. Dermatitis herpetiformis
      2. *Atrophic glossitis "red and smooth tongue"
   3. Endocrine
      1. Vitamin D deficiency
      2. Secondary hyperparathyroidism
   4. Bone
      1. Growth delay is often initial sign in pediatrics
      2. Adults: Osteomalacia/Osteoporosis bone pain
      3. Kids: Rickets
   5. Heme
      1. Iron-deficiency microcytic anemia
   6. *Neuropsych
      1. Peripheral Neuropathy
         1. Sensory loss in distal, symmetric, stocking-glove distribution (burning sensation in hands and feet) — raises concern for systemic disease
      2. Depression/anxiety
   7. *These features precede malabsorption symptoms, likely due autoantibodies (vs. nutritional deficiencies)
3. Assoc:
   1. T1DM (close association due to shared genetic loci and autoimmune disease)
      1. Hints: Patient with recurrent hypoglycemic episodes and low height/weight percentile despite normal nutrition
   2. Autoimmune thyroiditis
   3. Down Syndrome
4. Dx
   1. Serology — Antitissue Transglutaminase antibody, Anti-endomysial antibody
   2. Duodenal biopsy — Intraepithelial lymphocytosis, Villous blunting
5. Tx: Gluten free diet

# Long term PPI

1. Increased risk of B12 deficiency due to achlorhydria
2. Vitamin B12 bound to animal protein and liberated in stomach by Pepsin
3. Gastric acid activates Pepsinogen --> Pepsin

# IBS

1. PP: Smooth muscle hypersensitivity
2. Labs and physical exam normal (absence of alarm symptoms) --> No further evaluation required
3. Further evaluation required with alarm symptoms: >50yo at onset, GI bleeding, nocturnal diarrhea, unintended weight loss, FH of IBD or early colon cancer, abnormal labs

# Lactose Intolerance

1. Acquired deficiency seen in Asian (90%) and African populations
2. Normal labs and abdominal exam (helps differentiate from other causes of postprandial abdominal distress)
3. RF:
   1. Inflammatory disorders affecting the brush border — Infectious gastroenteritis, Celiac, Crohn
4. CF: Osmotic diarrhea, abdominal cramps, bloating and flatulence after ingestion of milk and dairy products
   1. inc stool osmotic gap >125 mOsm/kg — unmetabolized lactose and organic acids (Stool osmotic gap = 290-[2(stool Na + stool K)] and is >75 mOsm/kg in forms of osmotic diarrhea)
   2. Acidic stool pH — fermentation products
   3. (+) Stool test for reducing substances
5. Dx: Lactose hydrogen breath test
   1. (+) test indicates rise in measured breath hydrogen levels after lactose ingestion (= bacterial carb metabolism and impaired malabsorption)

# Small Intestinal Bacterial Overgrowth

1. Bloating, diarrhea, mild abd pain "unrelated to eating, does not improve after defecation"
2. RF: bowel motility changes — uncontrolled diabetes, chronic opiate use
3. Dx: Carb breath testing "carb test using glucose is abnormal"
4. Tx: Abx — Rifaximin, Neomycin

# Small Bowel Obstruction

1. MCC: Adhesions (multiple bowel procedures)

# Intussusception

1. RF: HUS
2. Colicky RUQ pain
3. Xray to rule out small bowel obstruction
4. Tx: Air enema
5. Contraindication: Rotavirus vaccine

# Volvulus: Midgut Malrotation

1. Constant pain
   1. Common cause of bilious emesis in infants, especially with other congenital anomalies (omphalocele)
   2. Episodic, bilious, vomiting, abdominal pain
   3. Rapid clinical deterioration due to intestinal ischemia and necrosis — GI bleeding, hypovolemic or septic shock, peritonitis due to intestinal perforation
2. Dx:
   1. Hemodynamically unstable: Exploratory laparotomy
   2. Hemodynamically stable: Upper GI series (X-rays with contrast material for visualization to rule out perforation)
3. Tx: Surgical (Ladd procedure) to divide peritoneal bands and bowel reposition

# Trauma

## Appendicitis

1. Severe symptoms due to peritonitis
2. Rovsing: LLQ, Psoas: Hip extension, Obturator: IR thigh
3. Tx: Laparoscopic surgery
4. Complication: Intra-abdominal abscess formation — Subphrenic abscess
   1. Higher incidence with laparoscopic appendectomy than laparotomy
   2. Recurrent fever and abdominal pain + fever, leukocytosis + pulmonary sx (SOB, R-sided effusion)
   3. Several days post-operation
5. Complication: Gangrenous perforation

## Duodenal tear

1. Rapid compression of bowel causes perforated viscus
2. Retroperitoneal viscus (i.e. duodenal, ascend/descend colon) has delayed symptoms of fever & diffuse abdominal pain
   1. Gastric spillage initially sequestered away from intraperitoneal space
   2. Back or flank pain due to retroperitoneal inflammation
   3. Retroperitoneal free air on imaging

## Laparoscopic Surgery

1. Risk: Bowel Injury
   1. Persistent bowel pain, guarding, ileus (diminished bowel sounds, SI/LI distention), leukocytosis
   2. Frank peritonitis --> Surgical exploration
   3. Without frank peritonitis --> Abdominal CT with contrast to identify leak

## Small Bowel Obstruction

1. Obstipation, hyperactive bowel sounds, diffuse unlocalized pain, Nausea, Vomiting, Abdominal bloating, Dilated loops of bowel
2. MCC: PMH of surgery can hint at adhesions (i.e. hysterectomy in elderly F)
   1. Hernias, Crohn disease, Intussusception, Volvulus, Intestinal atresia, Carcinoid, Foreign body, Neoplasms
3. "Tinkling" sound: intestinal fluid and air under high pressure in bowel
4. Hypovolemia due to 3rd spacing
5. Dx
   1. Hallmark: Elevated lactate with marked acidosis
   2. Initial test: Abdominal x-ray shows multiple air-fluid levels with dilated loops of small bowel
   3. Accurate: CT Abdomen shows transition zone from dilated loops of bowel with contrast to bowel with no contrast
6. Tx
   1. NPO: prevent further bowel pressure increase
   2. NG tube with suction: lower bowel pressure proximal to obstruction
   3. Medical management: IV fluids to replace loss from 3rd spacing
   4. Surgical: complete obstruction or lack of improvement with medical management
7. Type
   1. Partial (small GI contents can pass)
   2. Complete
8. Complication: Strangulated obstruction presents with peritoneal signs (rigidity, rebound tenderness) + shock
9. Complication: Incisional hernia
   1. Palpable mass at the level of the incision due to breakdown of fascia (obesity is important factor)

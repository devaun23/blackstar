# DISEASES OF HEART MUSCLE

## Dilated Cardiomyopathy

1. Alcohol
2. Coxsackie virus
3. **Peripartum CM**
   1. Presentation
      1. Last month of pregnancy
      2. Decompensated HF — progressive dyspnea on exertion, LE edema, S3
      3. MR — "holosystolic murmur best heard at apex"
      4. Absence of other causes of HF prior to final month of pregnancy
   2. Diagnosis: Echo — Dilated LV cavity with global systolic dysfunction and EF <45%
   - Note: Urgent delivery only for patients with advanced heart failure

## Brugada Syndrome

1. Autosomal Dominant
2. Mutations affecting cardiac sodium channels
3. ECG: ST elevation in V1 and V2
4. Increased risk of sudden cardiac death due to ventricular arrhythmia
5. Lacks structural cardiac abnormalities

## Fabry Disease

1. X-linked
2. Deficiency of lysosomal hydrolase alpha-galactosidase A → lysosomal accumulation of glycosphingolipids
3. CF: Neuro — paresthesias in adolescence; Concentric LV hypertrophy; Myocardial fibrosis; Heart failure at >30yo

## Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy

1. Cause: AD, Mutation in cardiac sarcomere protein genes, Variable expressivity/penetrance
2. Presentation
   1. Asx or identified by screening
   2. Murmur: "3/6 mid systolic murmur best heard at left lower sternal border without carotid or neck radiation"
      - Systolic ejection murmur exacerbated by dehydration/impaired LV filling
      - ↑: Standing, Valsalva | ↓: Squatting, Supine leg raise, Handgrip
   3. Diastolic dysfunction with audible S4 at cardiac apex
3. Phenotypic Features
   1. Asymmetric LV hypertrophy (primarily interventricular septum) causes LVOT obstruction
      1. Slight obstruction: exertional symptoms (dyspnea, angina) due to O2 supply-demand mismatch
      2. Severe obstruction: crescendo-decrescendo murmur at left sternal border without carotid radiation
   2. Syncope — Multifactorial
      1. Reduced CO due to LVOT obstruction (exertion + dehydration exacerbate)
      2. Ischemia-induced VTach
         - Increased muscle mass → increased O2 demand + Myocardial disarray → reduced O2 supply
         - Intense exertion amplifies mismatch → ventricular arrhythmia
         - Ventricular arrhythmia does not terminate → sudden cardiac death
   3. Myocardial disarray
   4. Coronary microvascular dysfunction with poorly developed capillary network
4. Diagnosis
   1. ECG: LVH, Left axis deviation, repolarization abnormalities
   2. TTE: LA enlargement, LVH, Inc LVOT obstruction, Systolic anterior motion of mitral valve
5. Management
   1. Avoid volume depletion (↓ LV blood volume)
   2. Negative inotropic agents (↑ LV blood volume, ↑ diastolic filling time)
      1. BB — Metoprolol, Atenolol: Prolong diastole, decrease contractility, decrease LVOT obstruction
      2. Nondihydro CCB — Verapamil, disopyramide: if symptoms persist despite BB
   3. ICD placement for increased risk of SCD
   4. Septal ablation, cardiac transplantation
6. Elective Surgery Optimization
   1. High LV EDV: High Preload (adequate IV fluids)
   2. Low HR: BB to increase diastolic filling time
   3. Avoid venous dilators: Nitroglycerin
   4. Low contractility or High Afterload to maximize CO
7. Complications: Increased risk of AFib and VTach, SCD, Heart failure, Stroke

## Athlete's Heart

1. Sinus bradycardia at rest with low-grade AV block (increased vagal tone)
2. Increased QRS voltage and J point elevation
3. Isolated T wave inversion in V1

## Concentric Cardiomyopathy

1. Abnormal increase in LV myocardial mass from chronically increased workload — most commonly pressure overload from chronic HTN or aortic stenosis

## Stress-induced (Takotsubo) Cardiomyopathy

1. Transient systolic dysfunction of apical and/or mid segments with hyperkinesis of basal segments "balloon-like"
2. RF: Postmenopausal women, Recent physical or emotional stressor
3. Presentation
   1. Chest pain mimicking MI
   2. Decompensated HF due to catecholamine surge → microvascular spasm → ischemia and myocardial stunning
      - Mid-to-apical hypokinesis, basilar hyperkinesis "octopus trap/takotsubo"
4. Diagnosis: ECG: ischemic changes in precordial leads | Echo: LV apical hypokinesis, basilar hyperkinesis
5. Treatment: Supportive

## Tachycardia-mediated Cardiomyopathy

1. Persistent or recurrent tachyarrhythmia with prolonged periods of rapid ventricular rate
2. Causes: AF, A flutter, VTach, Incessant atrial/junctional tachycardia, AVNRT
3. Chronic tachycardia → LV dilatation and myocardial dysfunction
4. Presentation: Palpitations and/or signs of CHF, LV systolic dysfunction (EF 35%)
5. Treatment: Aggressive rate control and restoration of normal sinus rhythm
   - Risk of reversibility of CM and LV systolic function normalization

## Myocarditis — Viral

1. Cause: Initial inadequate immune response → virus invades cardiac myocytes → Cytotoxicity → Ventricular dilation and eccentric hypertrophy
2. Presentation: Young adult <55yo with dilated CM and decompensated HF
3. Diagnosis
   1. Echo: 4-chamber dilation
   2. Cardiac MRI: late enhancement of epicardium
   3. ECG: nonspecific
   4. Biopsy: lymphocytic infiltrate, viral DNA or RNA

# RESTRICTIVE LUNG DISEASE — LOSS OF COMPLIANCE

1. Graphite — pneumoconiosis
2. Radon — household basement, bronchogenic cancer

## Interstitial Lung Disease

1. CF: Inspiratory "Velcro" (fine, dry) crackles are sensitive for interstitial fibrosis — audible long before CXR or PFT abnormalities develop
2. Dx: Initial disease may not be seen on CXR
   1. High-resolution CT (HRCT) in prone position provides better visualization of subtle reticulation or honeycombing in dorsal dependent lung
   2. PFT
   3. Definitive Dx: Lung biopsy and histological examination often not completed, doesn't change management

### Exposure Related

1. Asbestosis
   1. Ship building or shipyard work
   2. Pleural effusion, calcified pleural plaques (ie. mesothelioma)
   3. Malignancy effusion --> low glucose
2. Silicosis
3. Berylliosis
4. Coal Worker's pneumoconiosis
5. Meds: Amiodarone, Bleomycin
6. Hypersensitivity pneumonitis
   1. RF
      1. Birds, Hot tubs, Hay ("Farmers lungs")
   2. Acute
      1. Recurrent, short-lived episodes of sudden-onset fever, nonproductive cough, and dyspnea
      2. CXR: Scattered micronodular interstitial opacities, crackles
   3. Chronic
      1. Progressive cough, dyspnea, fatigue, weight loss
      2. Hypoxemia that worsens with exertion
      3. CXR: Diffuse reticular interstitial opacities
   4. Dx
      1. PFT: restrictive pattern
      2. BAL: high relative lymphocyte count
      3. Lung biopsy: lymphocytic infiltrate, poorly formed noncaseating granulomas, interstitial inflammation or fibrosis (chronic)
   5. Tx
      1. Remove antigen exposure
      2. Glucocorticoids +/- lung transplantation (chronic)

Note: Crackles on lungs are either fluid (CHF) or interstitial lung disease on NBME

7. Radiation-induced injury

### Idiopathic

1. Idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis
   1. Tx: Antifibrotic agents — pirfenidone
2. Cryptogenic organizing pneumonia
3. Acute interstitial pneumonia

### Systemic Disease or Connective Tissue Disorder

1. Polymyositis/Dermatomyositis
2. Sarcoidosis — Restrictive lung PFTs pattern: Normal FEV1, Decreased TLC, Decreased DLCO
   1. Chronic noncaseating granulomatous inflammation
   2. Imaging: Hilar adenopathy, Peribronchovascular nodules
   3. Dx: Bronchoscopy with transbronchial biopsy
3. Amyloidosis
4. Vasculitis
5. Systemic Sclerosis: ILD
6. Methotrexate-induced lung injury
   1. PP: Idiosyncratic (dose independent) hypersensitivity pneumonitis
   2. RF: Rheumatoid Arthritis, Parenchymal lung disease
   3. CF:
      1. Onset 1-12 months: Pneumonitis --> Fibrosis (Restrictive PFTs)
      2. CT scan with variable mix of patterns: inflammation (Ground glass opacities, consolidation) and fibrosis (reticulation)
      3. Bronchoalveolar lavage: lymphocytosis; peripheral blood: eosinophilia **rules out infection
      4. Trial of MTX cessation is diagnostic and therapeutic

### Eosinophilic Pulmonary Syndromes

1. Eosinophilic pulmonary infiltrates and abnormal peripheral blood eosinophilia
   1. ABPA
   2. Loffler's syndrome
   3. Acute Eosinophilic Pneumonia
   4. Drug-induced: NSAIDs, Nitrofurantoin, Sulfonamides

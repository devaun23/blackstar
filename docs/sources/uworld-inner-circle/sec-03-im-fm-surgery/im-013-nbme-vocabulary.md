# Alternative NBME Vocabulary

## Imaging

1. Virtual Colonoscopy = CT Colonography
2. Laparoscopy = minimally invasive surgical procedure (keyhole surgery), uses a small incision
3. Laparotomy = large incision in the abdomen to facilitate a procedure (i.e. exploratory laparotomy)

## General

1. Psammoma bodies = laminated calcifications
2. Never event --> Sentinel event
3. Near miss --> close call
4. Proteinase-3-ANCA --> cANCA
5. Myeloperoxidase-ANCA --> pANCA
6. Convex bilateral soles --> Rocker-bottom feet
7. Childhood-onset fluency disorder --> Stuttering
8. Synthetic cathinones --> Bath Salts
9. Bruton agammaglobulinemia = X-linked agammaglobulinemia
10. Reflex sympathetic dystrophy = Complex regional pain syndrome
11. Leukotriene receptor = CYSLT1 receptor

## CVS

1. Kawasaki = Mucocutaneous LN syndrome or Lymphocytic Myocarditis
   1. Rash on palms/soles OR edema of hands & feet OR desquamation of skin
2. Defibrillation --> unsynchronized cardioversion
3. Direct current cardioversion or direct current countershock --> synchronized cardioversion
4. Unstable angina, NSTEMI, STEMI --> acute coronary syndrome

## Pulmonary

1. Polysomnography = Sleep study
2. CT Angiography = Spiral CT Scan (if question mentions Pulmonary angiography — that does not mean CT angiography)

## GI

1. Exploratory laparotomy --> exploratory celiotomy
2. Palpable cord --> thrombophlebitis (Migratory thrombophlebitis associated with visceral cancers — Pancreatic)
3. Colonic dilation due to inflammatory cells --> Toxic Megacolon
4. Congenital Megacolon --> Hirschsprung's Disease
5. Pseudomembranous colitis --> C difficile colitis
6. Lithogenic bile --> Gallstones

## Infectious Disease

1. Tinea capitis --> Trichophyton tonsurans, Microsporum canis, Microsporum audouinii

## Reproductive

1. Intrauterine synechiae --> Asherman Syndrome
2. Mullerian agenesis = Mayer-Rokitansky-Kuster-Hauser syndrome
3. PCOS --> Stein-Leventhal
4. MSA --> Shy-Drager
5. Testicular insensitivity to Testosterone --> Androgen Insensitivity Syndrome
6. Submucosal myoma = leiomyoma uteri = uterine fibroids

## Neurology

1. Embolic Stroke of Undetermined Source = Cryptogenic Stroke (formerly named)
2. Neural tube defects = spinal dysraphism — includes Spina bifida occulta
3. Simple partial seizure --> focal seizure without loss of awareness
4. Acute inflammatory demyelinating polyneuropathy --> Guillain-Barre
5. Werdnig Hoffman disease --> Spinal muscle atrophy type 1
6. Multi system atrophy --> Shy-Drager Syndrome
   1. Loss of sweating is a characteristic feature
   2. Postural hypotension, with dizziness and weakness on standing or walking
   3. In the male impotence is an early symptom and sphincter disturbances are also common
   4. The condition is slowly progressive and has a poor prognosis
7. Hereditary motor and sensory neuropathy --> Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease
8. Hereditary hemorrhagic telangiectasias --> Osler-Weber-Rendu Syndrome
9. Multi-infarct dementia = Vascular Dementia
10. Encephalotrigeminal Angiomatosis = Sturge-Weber Syndrome
11. Von Recklinghausen's disease = Neurofibromatosis 1

## Psych

1. Hysterical amnesia = Dissociative amnesia
2. Hepatolenticular degeneration = Wilson's Disease
3. Frontotemporal dementia = Pick Disease

## MSK

1. Osteosarcoma = Osteogenic sarcoma
2. Osgood-Schlatter disease --> traction apophysitis, tibial osteochondritis
3. Heterotopic changes in muscle --> Myositis ossificans
4. Pyogenic arthritis --> septic arthritis
5. Pain improves when raising arm above head --> cervical radiculopathy
6. Meralgia Paresthetica --> lateral femoral cutaneous nerve compression
7. Axillary-subclavian venous thrombosis = upper extremity DVT due to repetitive microtrauma (jackhammer operator)

## Renal

1. Nephrotic syndrome = lipoid nephrosis fatty casts on UA
2. HIV-associated nephropathy --> Focal segmental glomerulosclerosis
3. IgA nephropathy — 2-6 days after URI = Synpharyngitic nephropathy
4. Thiazide diuretics --> Metolazone, Chlorthalidone, -iazide
5. Struvite stones --> triple phosphate stones, magnesium ammonium phosphate stones
6. Wilson disease --> hepatolenticular degeneration

## Hem/Onc

1. Von Willebrand Factor Disease --> Impaired Hemostasis

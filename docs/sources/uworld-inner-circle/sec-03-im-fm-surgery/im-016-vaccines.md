# VACCINES

*Source: Ep 250*

## Two Big Types of Vaccines

### Live Attenuated

1. Bug that has been severely weakened, but retains ability to infect cells
2. Generates a T-Cell response
3. No real need to give boosters
4. Examples:
   1. MMR
   2. Varicella
   3. Zoster (Adults >60 yo)
   4. Intranasal influenza vaccine
   5. Oral polio
   6. Zostavax Herpes zoster
   7. Yellow fever
   8. Rotavirus
5. Live attenuated are not usually given to those <1yo
   1. The rule does NOT apply to rotavirus — kids receive at 2, 4, 6 months
   2. Most dangerous complication of rotavirus vaccine --> Intussusception
   3. Contraindications: if pt has had intussusception, Hx of Meckel's, IgA nephropathy (or HSP)
6. Contraindicated in:
   1. Pregnant women
   2. <1 y/o (exception: rotavirus)
   3. CD4 <200 (immunodeficiency)

### Killed/Inactivated Vaccine

1. Destroyed bug but you keep antigenic parts, which generates a response from immune system
2. Generates Humoral response
3. Need to give boosters
4. Examples:
   1. Hep A
   2. Hep B — given to Healthcare workers
      1. Pt is a newborn with a mom that has HepB surface antigens + --> Baby gets HepB vaccine + HepB immunoglobulin (given in opposite extremities to prevent vaccines binding with Ig)
   3. Tdap --> toxoid
      1. 1 dose Tdap + Td booster every 10 years
      2. Tdap in every pregnancy at 27-36 weeks
   4. Pneumococcal-13
   5. Meningococcal — Teens going to college, Military recruits, Eculizumab (monoclonal Ab against C5), Terminal complement deficiency
   6. Influenza (injection)
   7. Polio (injection)
   8. Shingrix Herpes zoster

### Toxoid Vaccine

1. Toxin from bug that can act on receptors in the body to generate antibody response

## Two Types of Influenza Vaccine

1. Inactivated --> Intramuscular
2. Live-attenuated --> Intranasal (inhaled as mist)

## PCV-13 and PPSV-23

### PCV-13 (Conjugated)

1. B + T cell response
2. Always taken FIRST before 23 (13 comes before 23)
3. PCV13 is taken by everybody

### PPSV-23 (Polysaccharide)

1. Humoral response (No T cells)
2. Given for those >65 y/o
3. <65 yo with certain conditions --> PCV13 then after 8 weeks PPSV23:
   1. Asplenic, Sickle cell (functionally asplenic), Immunocompromised, HIV, CKD, Nephrotic syndrome, Transplant, Hematologic malignancy, Bruton/SCID/CVID, Cochlear implant, Alport syndrome, Chronic CSF leak
   2. *Get second dose of PPSV23 5 years later (eventually have a PPSV23 after 65yo)
4. <65 yo with chronic disease --> just PPSV23:
   1. DM, Smoking, Heart disease, Lung disease, Liver disease, Alcoholism
5. >65 yo --> just PPSV23 OR PCV13 then after 1 year PPSV23
6. Asplenic or functionally asplenic --> vaccinate against encapsulated organisms:
   1. Strep pneumo, H. flu, Neisseria meningitidis
7. Healthcare Workers --> Hep B vaccine

## Vaccines in Kids

### General Rule

1. Most pediatric patients get at least 3 doses
2. Most are given at 2, 4, 6 months of age
3. Those that don't follow this rule: Live attenuated vaccines

### Hep B Vaccine

1. Three doses
   1. First dose at birth (before leaving hospital)
   2. Second dose: within 2 months of birth
   3. Third dose: between 6-18 months of age

### Rotavirus Vaccine

1. 3 doses at 2, 4, and 6 months

### DTaP Vaccine

1. 5 doses:
   1. 3 doses at 2, 4, and 6 months
   2. 4th dose: ~15-18 months of age
   3. 5th dose: 4-6 y/o
2. When older: TDap (Adolescent), TD booster q10 years

### H. Flu Type B Vaccine

1. 3 doses:
   1. 1st: 2 months, 2nd: 4 months, 3rd: 12-15 months

### PCV-13 Vaccine

1. 4 doses:
   1. 1st: 2 months, 2nd: 4 months, 3rd: 6 months, 4th: 12-15 months

### Polio Vaccine

1. 4 doses:
   1. 1st: 2 months, 2nd: 4 months, 3rd: 6 months, 4th: 4-6 y/o

### Influenza Vaccine

1. Every year
2. Less than a year old do NOT give intranasal

### MMR and Varicella

1. Two doses:
   1. 1st: 12-15 months, 2nd: 4-6 y/o

### Hep A Vaccine

1. Two doses:
   1. 1st: within 2 y/o, 2nd: 6 months after 1st dose
   2. Later given in adolescence

## 10-18 y/o

### Meningococcal Vaccine

1. Two doses:
   1. 1st: 11-12 y/o, 2nd: 16 y/o
2. Teens going to college, Military recruits

### HPV Vaccine

1. Can give starting from 9-26 y/o
2. Girls age 9-26, Boys 11-21, Men-sex-Men 11-26

## Zoster Vaccine

1. Not the same as varicella
2. Get zoster at age of 50
3. Two doses (6 months apart)

## Special Populations

### Pregnancy

1. Women get TDAP vaccine at every pregnancy within 27-36 weeks

### Splenectomy / Sickle Cell

1. PCV-13 and PPSV-23, strep pneumo, H flu, and neisseria meningitidis
2. Wait 2 months between PCV-13 and PPSV-23
3. Amoxicillin or penicillin until they reach the age of 5

### Egg Allergy

1. Avoid Yellow fever (Everything else is fine, yes — even MMR!)
2. Influenza vaccine can have allergic reaction too (NBME)

# CONGENITAL HEART DISEASE

## ASD

1. Systolic murmur heard over Left 2nd ICS (increased flow across pulmonic valve)
2. Fixed Split S2

## VSD

1. Left sternal border holosystolic murmur: L-R intracardiac shunting
2. Increased LA preload: L-R shunting → RV volume overload → increased LA preload → RV and Left chamber enlargement → Enlarged Left heart contour

## Coarctation of the Aorta

1. Narrowing of descending aorta distal to left subclavian artery
2. Presentation
   1. UE Hypertension
   2. Diminished femoral pulses
   3. Brachial-femoral delay
   4. S4 — HTN induced LV hypertrophy
   5. Continuous murmur throughout thorax
   6. Less common: epistaxis and headaches
3. Diagnosis
   1. CXR: Inferior notching of 3rd-8th ribs, Erosions of inferior costal surfaces, Classical "3" sign
   2. Echo: Diagnostic confirmation
4. Treatment: Balloon angioplasty +/- stent or surgery

# MSC

## Costochondritis

1. Musculoskeletal pain with tenderness of ≥1 anterior chondral joints
2. Joint aggravation during physical activity
3. Presentation: Sharp, localized pain that is reproducible with palpation (anterior chest that worsens with movement, deep inspiration or coughing)
4. Self-limited
5. Tx: Reassurance and topical or systemic analgesics (Acetaminophen, NSAIDs)

# Diabetes Mellitus T1

## Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)

1. PP: Deficient insulin signaling --> relative state of starvation
   1. Dysregulated FA degradation and ketone synthesis of B-hydroxybutyric acid (causes acidosis), Acetoacetic acid and Acetone
2. CF
   1. Initial: Polydipsia/polyuria, blurred vision, weight loss rapid onset weight loss, polydipsia, abdominal pain
   2. Later: Altered mentation, hyperventilation, abdominal pain (due to delayed gastric emptying and ileum from electrolyte abnormality)
      1. Kussmaul respirations, rapid and deep due to respiratory compensation for metabolic acidosis
   3. Note: DKA is common initial presentation of T1DM, DKA may be precipitated by recent illness
3. Labs
   1. 1st step: Fingerstick glucose typically 300-800mg/dL
   2. Measure anion gap [Na-(HCO3 + Cl)] to determine High-anion gap
      1. Normalization of serum anion gap and beta-hydroxybutyrate levels most accurately indicates DKA resolution
   3. inc Glucose, inc Ketones
   4. Normal or serum inc K
      1. Hyperosmolarity — draws fluid and K passively out of cells into extracellular space
      2. Insulin deficiency — impairs cellular entry of K by cells, further increasing K concentration
      3. Total body potassium deficit
         1. Urinary potassium loss from osmotic diuresis
         2. Elimination of ketoacid anions as potassium salts (seen only in DKA)
         3. Secondary hyperaldosteronism due to volume contraction — which leads to K excretion and reabsorption of Na in the distal renal tubule
4. Tx:
   1. IV fluids (normal saline)
   2. IV Insulin
   3. K replacement esp when <5.3 mEq/L

# Diabetes Mellitus T2

1. Can be cause of elevated serum glucose in older, obese and sedentary persons and may be asx until complications arise
2. Elevated HbA1c despite normal fasting glucose possibly due to --> Postprandial Hyperglycemia
   1. Improve glycemic control with long-acting basal insulin (control fasting glucose) and rapid-acting premeal insulin (control postprandial glucose)

## Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State

1. Inciting factors
   1. Acute illness, trauma, infection
   2. Insulin non adherence
   3. Meds: Glucocorticoids, Diuretics, Atypical Antipsychotics
2. CF
   1. Gradual hyperglycemia symptoms (polyuria, polydipsia)
   2. Altered mentation (coma, focal signs, seizures)
3. Labs
   1. Glucose >600mg/dL (frequently >1000 mg/dL)
   2. Normal pH and bicarbonate, anion gap (10-14 mEq/L)
   3. Negative or small serum ketones
   4. Serum osmolality >320 mOsm/kg *Na levels decrease in hyperosmolar state, correct using formula
4. Evaluation
   1. 1st: Urgent chemistry profile and UA (evaluate glucose, anion gap, serum osmolality)
5. Initial Management
   1. Aggressive hydration with normal saline in 1st hour regardless of sodium levels
   2. IV Insulin
   3. K replacement if <5.3 mEq/L *total K depleted due to osmotic diuresis and insulin deficiency despite normal serum K values

## Step-up therapy for diabetes management

1. 1st: Lifestyle changes
2. 2nd: Metformin
3. 3rd: Metformin + Oral antidiabetic agents (glipizide) or GLP-1 analogue (exenatide)
4. 4th: Metformin + basal insulin
5. 5th: Basal-bolus insulin therapy

## Hypoglycemia Symptoms

1. Neurogenic: sweating, tremor, palpitations due to autonomic activation
2. Neuroglycemic: confusion, impaired consciousness due to inadequate CNS glucose supply

## Diabetic Foot Ulcer

1. RF:
   1. 80% Diabetic neuropathy
      1. Decreases pain sensation and pressure perception --> muscle imbalance --> foot deformities and impairs microcirculation and integrity of the skin
      2. Peripheral sensory neuropathy tested by recording pressure sensation with a 10-g monofilament (positive test indicated loss of monofilament sensation)
   2. Previous foot ulceration, vascular disease, foot deformity

## Diabetic Nephropathy (DN)

1. Common in Type 1 and Type 2
   1. Diabetes causes preferential atherosclerosis of the efferent arteriole --> inc glomerular pressure --> Chronic glomerular damage --> Proteinuria (initially GFR is elevated)
   2. ACEi essentially reverse the above mechanism by blocking efferent arteriole vasoconstriction
2. Dx:
   1. Persistent albuminuria (>30mg/g) and/or dec GFR + 1 of the following
      1. Prolonged history of Diabetes: Type 1 clinically obvious at onset (DN assumed if >=5yr duration), Type 2 might be diagnosed later
      2. Proliferative Diabetic Retinopathy (PDR) Retinal Neovascularization
         1. PDR and DN are chronic microvascular complications of Diabetes due to persistent Hyperglycemia
         2. Presence of PDR correlates with Diabetic kidney disease
   2. *Note: Renal biopsy reserved for unclear presentation
3. Tx: ACE-I and ARB equally effective
   1. Avoid using ACE-I and ARB together — inc Creatinine and inc dialysis risk

## Insulin and Exercise

1. Nondiabetic Patient: Insulin levels decrease as blood glucose levels fall during activity or fasting
   1. Counterregulatory hormones — Glucagon stimulate glycogenolysis and gluconeogenesis in the liver
2. Diabetic Patient: Insulin levels remain elevated due to release from injection site --> High risk of hypoglycemia
   1. Risk increased in patients Chronic Kidney Disease due to delayed clearance of Insulin
   2. Risk remains despite optimal glycemic control

## Stress Hyperglycemia (physiologic response to illness or injury)

1. Severe stress triggers Cortisol and Catecholamine release
   1. Act on liver to increase Glycogenolysis and Gluconeogenesis, this can be enhanced by Glucagon
2. Tx
   1. Mild elevations (140-180): No treatment
   2. Marked elevation (>180): Short-acting insulin to target 140-180mg/dL
      1. High glucose levels increase risk of mortality
      2. Goal kept at 140-180mg/dL to minimize risk of insulin-induced hypoglycemia

# Diabetes Insipidus

## Central DI

1. Labs
   1. Urine concentration increases by at least 50% after ADH administration
   2. Hypernatremia
2. Causes:
   1. Head trauma or mass

## Nephrogenic DI

1. Labs
   1. Urine concentration increases by <50% after ADH administration
   2. Hypernatremia
2. Causes:
   1. Hypercalcemia (RF: Hyperparathyroidism)
   2. Lithium toxicity

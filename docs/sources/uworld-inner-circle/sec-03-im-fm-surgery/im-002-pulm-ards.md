# ACUTE RESPIRATORY FAILURE

## ARDS

1. PP: Due to widespread inflammatory response — Sepsis (UTI, etc)
   1. Neutrophilic lung inflammation with increased vascular permeability --> pulmonary edema in the absence of cardiac failure or volume overload
2. PP: Near drowning
   1. Aspirated liquid during near-drowning event can wash away surfactant --> insidious onset of ARDS within 72 hours
3. Evaluation
   1. Defined by PaO2/FiO2 <300 Ex: PaO2 60 on 100% FiO2 = 60/1 = 60
   2. Normal: PaO2/FiO2 is ~500 (100/.21 because normally 21% of air we breathe is oxygen)
4. CF
   1. Improve mortality on ventilator — "Preventing alveolar over-distention"
   2. Recognize CXR — "wash out CXR" respiratory distress, diffuse crackles, severe hypoxemia, b/l alveolar infiltrates
5. Management
   1. Mechanical Ventilation
      1. Oxygenation determined by FiO2 and PEEP
      2. FiO2: ideally <60% or <0.6 to avoid oxygen toxicity (high FiO2 leads to reactive oxygen species formation and cellular toxicity)
      3. PEEP: usually >=10cm H2O
      4. PaO2: 60-90mmHg which correlates with SpO2 92-96%
   2. Avoid complications of mechanical ventilation by using
      1. Lung protection: Limit alveolar distending volume (VT 6mL/kg) and pressure
         1. Low VT = low pulmonary pressure — less likely to over distend alveolar and improves mortality
      2. Ventilation: Tolerate permissive Hypercapnia/Respiratory acidosis to avoid excessive VT
         1. Increased PaCO2 and decreased pH
      3. Oxygenation: Set lowest feasible FiO2 to avoid O2 toxicity
         1. Low VT, high PEEP, high FiO2 (<60%) **PEEP levels are elevated if FiO2 is already at 60%
            1. Low VT protects lungs by limiting alveolar over distention and PEEP opens collapsed alveoli to prevent atelectasis
         2. Target FiO2 = SpO2 92-96%/PaO2 60-90mmHg Avoid SpO2<88%
         3. FiO2 adjusted on results of first arterial blood gas analysis
            1. If target SpO2 achieved (ie SpO2 is 92-96% or higher) — reduce FiO2 but possible compensatory increase PEEP
         4. Avoid oxygen toxicity
            1. Hyperoxia = high PaO2 >90mmHg
            2. Leads to reactive oxygen species formation and cellular toxicity
            3. Worsens atelectasis by displacing nitrogen gas from the alveoli (nitrogen helps keep alveoli open)
   3. Supportive care
      1. Treat underlying etiology: source control (sepsis)
      2. Prevent iatrogenic harm:
         1. Negative fluid balance
            1. Initially patients have positive fluid balance (volume resuscitation for sepsis, blood transfusion for trauma)
            2. During hospitalization, conservative fluid strategy adopted to achieve neutral or negative fluid balance to accelerate recovery and decrease pulmonary edema
            3. "Dry lungs = happy lungs"
               1. Minimize intake: avoid unnecessary fluid boluses, concentrate IV drips
               2. Promote removal: diuretics, renal replacement therapy
         2. Timely extubation (minimize sedation)
            1. Pass spontaneous breathing trial
            2. Cause of respiratory failure has improved
         3. +/- CST (for moderate to severe ARDS)
6. Prognosis
   1. Mortality: 40% in hospital, death due to multi organ failure
   2. Morbidity: 50% with chronic cognitive impairment and physical debility, 25% with chronic pulmonary dysfunction (restriction and decreased DLCO)

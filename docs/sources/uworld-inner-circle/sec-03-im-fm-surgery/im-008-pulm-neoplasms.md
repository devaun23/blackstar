# LUNG NEOPLASMS

## Lung Abscess

1. COUGHING DURING EATING, malaise, night sweats, wt loss, yellow sputum, RLL cavitation — Oropharyngeal aspiration
   1. Pneumonitis --> Pneumonia --> Abscess/empyema
   2. Parkinson's: lose dopa neurons in oral cavity, pharynx, esophagus striated muscles — swallowing dysfunction
2. "Cavitary lesion with air-fluid level in superior segment of RLL" in alcoholic
   1. Higher risk with alcoholics, stroke, MG, depressed mental status, cyclic vomiting syndrome, TE fistula
   2. K. pneumoniae

## Lung Cancer

1. Small Cell
   1. Hilar mass with lymphadenopathy
   2. Widespread metastases at presentation due to rapid growth rate
      1. May be initially asymptomatic
      2. Vasculature invasion --> Hemoptysis
      3. Bronchus obstruction --> Atelectasis
   3. Complications
      1. SIADH: HYPOTONIC (SERUM OSM <275) HYPONATREMIA AND EUVOLEMIA (NOT DEHYDRATED)
         1. Causes
            1. CNS: Stroke, hemorrhage, trauma
            2. Medications: Carbamazepine, SSRI/Fluoxetine, NSAIDs
            3. Lung dz: pneumonia
            4. Ectopic: Small cell lung cancer
            5. Tx: Fluid restriction (initially), Hypertonic saline 3% (severe symptomatic hyponatremia)
         2. CF:
            1. Mild/Moderate: Nausea, forgetfulness
            2. Severe: Seizures, Coma
            3. Euvolemic: Moist mucous membranes, (-) edema or JVD
         3. Lab: Hyponatremia, Serum Osm <275mOsm (N: 280), Urine Osm >100mOsm/kg H2O in euvolemic patient
         4. Management
            1. Initial: Fluid restriction +/- salt tablets
            2. Severe: 3% hypertonic saline
      2. ACTH
         1. Hypercortisol: Weight loss, thinning of extremities, hyperpigmentation
         2. Cortisol stimulates Mineralocorticoid (Aldosterone): Hypokalemia, Metabolic alkalosis
      3. Lambert-Eaton Syndrome
2. Squamous Cell
   1. PTHrP
3. Large Cell
   1. Gynecomastia
4. Adenocarcinoma
   1. Migratory thrombophlebitis
5. Bronchogenic carcinoma
   1. Bilateral pleural plaques, asbestos exposure, smoking synergistic with asbestos exposure in worsening cancer
   2. Vs. mesothelioma (unilateral)
6. Post-radiation Secondary malignancy
   1. Peripheral lung lesion in patient with Hodgkin lymphoma 15yrs prior — lung, breast, GI (colorectal, esophageal, gastric)
   2. Smokers at greatest risk
7. Digital Clubbing: indicates occult malignancy
   1. Secondary clubbing due to lung malignancy, cystic fibrosis, R-L cardiac shunt
   2. Megakaryocytes skip normal fragmentation in lungs and enter systemic circulation — become entrapped in distal fingertips due to large size, release PDGF and VEGF — increase connective tissue hypertrophy, capillary permeability and vascularity
   3. COPD does not cause nail clubbing
   4. Hypertrophic osteodystrophy/osteoarthropathy: clubbing with painful joint enlargement, periostosis of long bones and synovial effusions --> follow up with CXR
8. Superior pulmonary sulcus tumor
   1. Malignant, arise in superior portion of lung; associated with smoking
   2. Presentation
      1. Invasion brachial plexus — Shoulder pain
      2. Invasion sympathetic chain/cervical ganglion — Horner syndrome (Ipsi ptosis, miosis, exophthalmos & anhidrosis)
      3. Invasion C8/T1 nerve roots — Arm pain (Weakness/atrophy intrinsic hand muscles, pain/paresthesia 4/5th digits)
      4. Supraclavicular lymphadenopathy
      5. Weight loss
   3. Note: Superior sulcus in lung periphery, pulmonary symptoms not seen until later in disease
   4. Dx: CXR
9. Screening (50-80yo): Low-dose CT scan of chest yearly
   1. Eligibility: Smoking >20yr history and current smoker or Quit <15yrs
   2. Termination: 80yo or >15yr quit
10. Hospice care
    1. Without treatment, life expectancy for Stage 4 non-small cell lung carcinoma is <6 months
11. Lung Resection Surgery
    1. Best predictors of postop outcome: FEV1 and DLCO

## Lung Transplant

1. Infectious
   1. Nosocomial
   2. Infectious: CMV, PCP, Aspergillus
      1. CMV: Acute, febrile, diffuse interstitial pneumonia
2. Immunological
   1. Hyperacute: ABO mismatch
   2. Acute (6mo): Cell/Ab mediated
      1. Low-fever, hypoxemia, CXR — perihilar opacities + interstitial edema, small bilateral pleural effusion
         1. Bronchoalveolar lavage & biopsy to rule out infection prior to high-dose glucocorticoids for ALTR
      2. Biopsy: submucosal lymphocytic infiltrate, perivascular inflammation
      3. Tx: Glucocorticoids
   3. Chronic (mo-yrs): Graft structure fibrosis
      1. Obstructive pattern on PFT
      2. BAL to rule out infection
      3. Biopsy: submucosal lymphocytic infiltrate, bronchiolitis obliterans
      4. Tx: Supportive or Repeat transplant
3. Postoperative
   1. Surgical site: anastomotic dehiscence
   2. Primary graft dysfunction: ARDS, ATN

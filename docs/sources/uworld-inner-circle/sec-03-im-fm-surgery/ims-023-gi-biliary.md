Cholestatic LFT pattern = Alkaline phosphatase elevation, Direct hyperbilirubinemia (impaired excretion of bile due to gallstones, pancreatic cancer, cholangiocarcinoma)

# Gallstones Management

1. No symptoms: No treatment
2. Typical biliary colic: Elective laparoscopic cholecystectomy or Ursodeoxycholic acid if surgery not possible
   1. Postprandial RUQ or epigastric pain, nausea, vomiting
   2. Normal Vitals, WBC and Liver function studies
   3. Imaging: Ultrasound
3. Complicated (Acute cholecystitis, Choledocholithiasis, Gallstone pancreatitis): Cholecystectomy 72hrs
4. Complication: Gallstone Ileus
   1. Pneumobilia "air in intrahepatic bile duct", hyperactive bowel sounds, dilated loops
   2. Stone may cause intermittent "tumbling" obstruction as it advances causing mechanical bowel obstruction
   3. Dx: Abdominal CT — GB wall thickening, pneumobilia, obstructing stone

# Acute Cholecystitis

1. Complete obstruction of cystic duct with subsequent inflammation and infection
   1. Fatty food ingestion causes GB contraction against obstructed cystic duct
   2. Tissue behind duct obstruction becomes inflamed from stasis leading to bacterial overgrowth
   3. Subsequent ischemic changes lead to gangrene and perforation, generalized peritonitis or well-circumscribed abscess
2. S/S
   1. Radiates to R shoulder
   2. Peritoneal signs
   3. Murphy sign
3. Complication: Perforation of Gallbladder
4. Dx: US, HIDA scan (if US ambiguous)
5. Note: Uncomplicated cholecystitis can cause mild elevations in Transaminases, Total Serum Bilirubin from 1-4 and serum Amylase due to sludge or pus passage through CBD (without bile duct or pancreatic disease). Alkaline Phosphatase is usually not elevated without associated Cholangitis or Choledocholithiasis

# Emphysematous Cholecystitis

1. RF
   1. DM, Vascular compromise, Immunosuppression
2. Presentation
   1. Fever, RUQ pain, Gas in GB wall/surrounding tissue/hepatobiliary system
   2. Gas forming Clostridium or E.coli infect damaged or ischemic tissue
3. Surgical emergency

# Postcholecystectomy Syndrome

1. Persistent abdominal pain or dyspepsia after cholecystectomy
2. Causes: Biliary (retained CBD, Cystic duct stone) or Extra-biliary (Pancreatitis, Peptic Ulcer Disease)
3. Dx: US, ERCP

# Biliary Cyst

1. Presentation
   1. Acute Cholangitis (Fever, RUQ pain, Obstructive jaundice, Cystic bile duct mass)
   2. Asymptomatic, "Abdominal mass in RUQ, normal enzymes"
2. Complications
   1. Cholangiocarcinoma, Acute Cholangitis, Pancreatitis, Stone formation
3. Tx
   1. Urgent biliary drainage via Endoscopic Sphincterotomy and drainage (ERCP)
      1. Before surgical excision of cyst to reduce Cholangiocarcinoma risk
   2. Cyst resection (to reduce malignancy risk)
   3. +/- Roux-en-Y hepaticojejunostomy (biliary drainage)

# Porcelain gallbladder

1. Calcified rim in GB wall with central bile-filled dark area
2. Chronic cholecystitis --> Porcelain GB --> GB Adenocarcinoma therefore cholecystectomy considered

# Choledocholithiasis

1. Block of CBD: inc Direct Bilirubin
2. Block Pancreatic duct: inc Amylase

# Ascending Cholangitis

1. Charcot triad: fever, jaundice, RUQ pain
2. Vs. Cholecystitis (does not have markedly elevated bilirubin or alkaline phosphatase)
3. Lab: Biliary stasis, Elevated Direct Bilirubin, Elevated Alk Phos, Elevated Aminotransferases
   1. Trauma related: Anion-gap metabolic acidosis due to lactic acidosis with severe sepsis
4. Imaging: Dilation of intrahepatic and common bile duct
5. Tx: Broad Abx, Drainage, ERCP

# Sphincter of Oddi Dysfunction

1. Recurrent, episodic pain in RUQ or epigastric region with corresponding increase in Aminotransferases and Alk phos
2. Precipitated by Opioid analgesics "worsened by pain medication" — Opioid analgesics also associated with Constipation
3. CBD dilation in absence of stones
4. Dx: Sphincter of Oddi manometry
5. Tx: Sphincterotomy

# Primary Biliary Cholangitis (Antimitochondrial antibody)

1. Autoimmune destruction of intralobular (small) bile ducts --> Cholestasis
2. Profound Alkaline Phosphatase and Bilirubin elevation
3. Near normal Aminotransferase
4. CF:
   1. Cholestasis (impaired biliary flow)
      1. Intense itching (bile salt deposits in skin)
      2. Fatigue
      3. inc Alkaline phosphatase
   2. Jaundice
   3. Hepatomegaly
   4. Chronic liver disease --> Portal Hypertension
   5. Skin excoriations
5. Complications: Hyperlipidemia (with xanthelasma), Metabolic bone disease
6. Dx:
   1. 1st: RUQ Ultrasound
      1. Distinguishes intrahepatic (no biliary tract dilation) from extrahepatic (biliary tract dilation)
   2. 2nd: Anti-mitochondrial antibody titers
      1. If Ultrasound reveals intrahepatic cholestasis --> Next step is Anti-mitochondrial antibody titers to assess for PBC (high sensitivity and specificity)
7. Tx:
   1. Ursodeoxycholic acid — prevents accumulation of toxic bile acids in liver, reverses liver inflammation
   2. Liver transplant — severe disease

# Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis

1. CF
   1. Asymptomatic
   2. Fatigue & Pruritus
   3. Alternating strictures of common bile duct "beads on a string"
      1. Fibrosis and stricturing of the medium and large intra-and extra hepatic bile ducts
      2. Promotes Cholestasis and Acute Cholangitis
   4. 90% associated with UC --> Perform Colonoscopy to rule out IBD
      1. IBD and PSC patients have annual Colonoscopies due to Colon Cancer risk
      2. p-ANCA = MPO-ANCA or perinuclear anti-neutrophil cytoplasmic antibodies
2. Labs: Initially may only see Elevated Alkaline Phosphatase and Gamma-Glutamyl Transpeptidase
3. Dx: MR Cholangiopancreatography (MRCP)
   1. Multifocal strictures/dilation of intrahepatic +/- extra hepatic bile ducts
4. Complications
   1. Biliary stricture
   2. Cholangitis or Cholelithiasis
   3. Cholangiocarcinoma, Colon Cancer, Biliary Cancer
   4. Cholestasis (Dec fat-soluble vitamins, Osteoporosis)

# Cholangiocarcinoma

1. Bile Duct Cancer
2. Occurs in pts with fibropolycystic liver disease or PSC from underlying UC
3. Subacute RUQ pain, Wt loss, Biliary obstruction, Cholestatic liver enzyme pattern, Dilation of intrahepatic or CBD
   1. Late clinical attention once tumor has obstructed biliary drainage
   2. Elevated Alk phos, normal transaminases
4. Vs. Hepatocellular carcinoma
   1. Cholangio: Elevated CEA/CA 19-9, normal AFP
   2. HCC: Normal CEA and CA-19, Elevated AFP
5. Dx: Endoscopic Ultrasound or ERCP

Note: Painless jaundice and weight loss is concerning for malignancy (Cholangiocarcinoma, Pancreatic CA, Hepatocellular CA) and should be evaluated with a CT Abdomen

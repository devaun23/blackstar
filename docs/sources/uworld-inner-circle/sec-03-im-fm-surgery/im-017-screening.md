# SCREENING

*Source: Ep 325*

## 1. Pregnancy

### First Appointment Screening

1. Gestational Diabetes
   1. Start screening at 24 weeks with 1 hour glucose test
   2. If positive follow with 3-hour glucose tolerance test
2. HIV
   1. At first prenatal visit
   2. Retest for HIV in the Third Trimester (tested twice)
3. HBV
   1. At first prenatal visit — Hep B Surface Antigen
4. Syphilis
   1. At first visit
5. Asymptomatic bacteriuria — Increase risk of preterm labor and delivery (Nonpregnant asymptomatic not treated)
   1. Treated with nitrofurantoin or 1st/2nd gen cephalosporin or Amoxicillin
   2. Test of cure
6. Group B STREP
   1. 35-37 weeks anal and vaginal
7. Women's RH status and antibody status (BY INDIRECT COOMBS TEST)
   1. Rhogam at 28 weeks and second dose within 3 days postpartum

## 2. Smokers

1. Screen for AAA (Abdominal aortic aneurysm)
   1. Between ages 65-75
   2. Only men
   3. If you have smoked as a male or have a family history of AAA
   4. With an ultrasound
   5. If more than 5.5 cm you need intervention (endovascular repair)
2. Screen for Osteoporosis
   1. Normally start screening with DEXA scan at age 65 for general population
   2. In smokers you can start screening at younger age
   3. Also screen for osteoporosis at less than 65 if: Very thin (Anorexia Nervosa), Premature ovarian failure
3. Lung Cancer
   1. Between ages 50-80
   2. Have a 20 or more pack-year history
   3. Currently smoking or have quit less than 15 years ago
   4. Screen with a low dose CT scan every year
   5. If you screen 3 times year after year and it is negative you can stop

## 3. Special Genetic Diseases

### Familial Hypercholesterolemia

1. People get MI at age 25
2. People have LDL receptor mutation generally
3. Rule of 3 --> Start checking LDL cholesterol at age of 3, 9 and 18

### Inflammatory Bowel Disease (Both)

1. Start screening for colon cancer 8 years after making the initial diagnosis
   1. Continue screening every 1-3 years after that
2. Exception: If diagnosed with primary sclerosing cholangitis --> screen for colon cancer at the time of diagnosis
   1. Then continue screening every 1-2 years

### Lynch Syndrome

1. Pts tend to have microsatellite instability problems
2. Start COLON cancer screening at age of 20 ("C before E in alphabet")
   1. Continue every 1-2 years
   2. Go straight to COLONOSCOPY
   3. You can give aspirin as a prophylaxis
3. Start ENDOMETRIAL cancer screening at age 30
   1. Continue every 1-2 years
   2. Endometrial biopsy or sampling
   3. Offer a prophylactic THBSO (total hysterectomy with bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy) after child bearing

### Patients with MEN 2A or 2B

1. It's not a matter of if but when will you get medullary thyroid cancer
2. Offer a prophylactic thyroidectomy

### Familial Adenomatous Polyposis

1. APC gene mutation --> Polyps --> Kras mutation --> p53 mutation --> Adenocarcinoma
2. Start colon cancer screening at age 10 years
3. Continue every year

### BRCA Mutations

1. Between ages 25-29 --> annual breast mammogram
2. Over the age of 30 --> Annual breast MRI and annual mammography

## 4. Risky Sexual Group

1. Individuals with HIV, Men that have sex with men, Sex workers, IV drug users
2. If between ages 15-64:
   1. Annual HIV
   2. Screen for syphilis every three months
   3. Screen for Chlamydia and gonorrhea
      1. Only screen women, DO NOT SCREEN MEN
      2. Screen higher risk patients annually no matter the age
      3. ALL patients (not only high risk) younger than 24 need annual testing
      4. Non risky patients don't need to be screened after age of 24
   4. Screen for Hep B surface antigen every year
      1. Also screen the antivaxxers every year for hep B

## 5. Metabolic Diseases

### Hypertension

1. Screening started at age 18 through 40 every 3-5 years
2. After age 40 screen every 1-2 years

### Diabetes

1. Tests: Fasting blood glucose, Oral glucose tolerance test, HbA1c
2. Obese individuals between age 40-70 years old --> Screen every 6 months
3. Hypertension over 135/80 and over age 45 --> Needs screening every 6 months

### Osteoporosis

1. Screening at age 65 for Women only
2. Also screen at less than 65 if: Smoker, Very thin (Anorexia Nervosa), Premature ovarian failure
3. DEXA scan looking at T score
   1. -2.5 or less --> Start on Bisphosphonates

### Hyperlipidemia

1. Everyone gets initial screening at age 20
2. Men:
   1. Ages 20-45: Every 5 years
   2. Ages 45-65: Every 1-2 years
   3. Over age 65: Every year
3. Women:
   1. Ages 20-55: Every 5 years
   2. Ages 55-65: Every 1-2 years
   3. Over age 65: Every year
4. Diabetics: Screen every year
   1. If LDL >=70mg/dL --> high intensity statin (Rosuvastatin or Atorvastatin)

## 6. Cancer Screening

### Colon Cancer

1. Start screening at age 45
2. Family hx --> age 40 OR 10 years before dx of relative (whichever is earlier)
3. Methods:
   1. Colonoscopy every 10 years
   2. Flexible sigmoidoscopy every 5 years
   3. Flexible sigmoidoscopy every 10 years + FIT every year
   4. FIT or FOBT every year
   5. CT colonography (Virtual colonoscopy) every 5 years
4. If any test other than colonoscopy shows an alteration --> DO A COLONOSCOPY WITH BIOPSY

### Breast Cancer

1. ACS: Mammogram start at age 40, every year
2. USPSTF: Mammogram ages 50-75, every 2 years

### Cervical Cancer

1. Age <21 --> NO Pap
2. Age 21-29 --> Pap q3y
3. Age 30-65 -->
   1. Pap every 3 years
   2. Co-testing Pap q5y
4. HIV or immunodeficiency --> yearly Pap (especially T cell deficiencies like DiGeorge)
5. Hysterectomy for benign reasons --> no screening
6. Hysterectomy for endometrial hyperplasia/cancer --> Pap of vaginal cuff
7. Hysterectomy for cervical cancer --> continue pap smears until 20 years post surgery
8. Stop screening at 65:
   1. No history of cervical cancer
   2. No history of severe Pap smear HSIL OR CIN 3
   3. If they did then need 3 negatives in a row or 2 pap smears plus HPV co-testing in a row

### Children

1. Patients with Language difficulties --> screening hearing with audiometry
2. All kids between ages 3-5 years old --> screen for amblyopia (Lazy eye)

## 7. Miscellaneous

1. Women Less than 24 years old: Screen annually for gonorrhea and chlamydia ONLY IN women
2. Who needs to be screened for Hep B every year:
   1. Patients who receive multiple blood transfusions or are constantly on dialysis
   2. Long term chemotherapy or long-term immunosuppressive therapy
   3. With hepatitis B surface antigen
3. All people between ages 18-79: Screen for Hep C once
4. Screening for depression:
   1. PHQ-9
   2. Beck depression inventory

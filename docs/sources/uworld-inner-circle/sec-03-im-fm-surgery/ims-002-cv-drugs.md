# CARDIOVASCULAR DRUGS

1. Afterload—Decreased: Nitroglycerin
2. Preload—Decreased: Diuretics, Nitrates (therefore CI in Inferior wall MI)
3. Glucocorticoids
   1. Leukocytosis
      1. Mobilization of marginated neutrophils into bloodstream
      2. Mobilization of immature neutrophils from bone marrow
      3. Inhibition of neutrophil apoptosis
      4. (Lower numbers of circulating lymphocytes and eosinophils)
4. Hydralazine — SE: Drug-induced lupus
5. Nitrates
   1. Mechanism
      1. Vasodilator: dilate veins, arterioles and coronary arteries by relaxing vascular smooth muscle cells
      2. Anti-ischemic: systemic vasodilation (rather than coronary vasodilation)
         1. Systemic venodilation: Lowers preload and left ventricular end-diastolic volume, Lowers myocardial oxygen demand by decreasing wall stress
         2. Systemic arterial and arteriolar vasodilation: Lowers SVR and BP, Fall in BP reduces wall stress and causes further decrease in myocardial oxygen demand
6. Antianginal
   1. Ranolazine (reserved for refractory stable angina)
   2. Sacubitril

## Lipid-Lowering — Statins

1. Indication
   1. Secondary prevention (Established ASCVD): ACS, Stable Angina, Arterial revascularization—CABG, Stroke, TIA, PAD
   2. Primary prevention: LDL >190mg/dL (suggestive Familial Hypercholesterolemia), Age >40yo with DM, 10yr ASCVD risk >7.5%-10
   3. Routine screening for <40yo Every 4-6 years
2. Atorvastatin or Rosuvastatin preferred due to higher potency than simvastatin/pravastatin
3. SE: Muscle injury (myalgia, myonecrosis)
   1. Switch from high intensity to moderate intensity if mild muscle toxicity (myalgias) develops
   2. Subclinical hypothyroidism associated with increased risk of statin myopathy—measure serum TSH prior to statin therapy
   3. Discontinue if rhabdomyolysis develops

## Decrease Mortality

- In MI: ACEi, BB, ASA
- In HF: ACEi, BB, Spironolactone

## Digoxin

1. Toxicity
   - ELDERLY AT RISK FOR DEHYDRATION (DUE TO FUROSEMIDE) & ACUTE RENAL DYSFUNCTION HAVE HIGH RISK OF TOXICITY
   1. Acute: GI distress, Weakness, confusion
   2. Chronic: Neurologic distress (lethargy, fatigue, disorientation), Vision changes (color vision, scotomas, blindness)
   3. Heart
      1. Life-threatening arrhythmia
      2. Atrial Tachycardia—increased automaticity of conduction in atria, around 200/min
         1. Draw serum electrolytes and serum Digoxin levels (low K and Mg potentiate toxicity)
      3. AV Block—increased vagal tone, typically Mobitz type 1
      4. Note: Digoxin competes with K to bind myocardium Na-ATPase, hypokalemia increases digoxin binding and worsens toxicity or causes toxicity at lower-than-expected digoxin levels
      - Most specific arrhythmia for digoxin toxicity is due to increased automaticity and increased vagal tone
2. Interactions: Amiodarone: Decrease Digoxin dose by 25-50%, monitor weekly

## Antiarrhythmics

### Class 1: Na Channel Blockers

1. 1a: Quinidine, Procainamide (Use: AF rate control in patients with WPW), Disopyramide
2. 1b: Lidocaine, Mexiletine
3. 1c: Flecainide, Propafenone
   - Flecainide Use: Atrial fibrillation in structurally normal hearts
   - Mechanisms: "Use Dependence" Enhanced pharmacological effect in faster HR
     1. Slowest rate of drug binding and dissociation from Na channel
     2. In faster heart rate patients, less time to dissociate from channel—greater number of blocked channels—decrease in impulse—widening of QRS complex
     3. Thus highly effective against SVTs

### Class 2: Beta Blockers

1. Metoprolol: HCM to prolong diastole, decrease myocardial contractility, lower LVOT obstruction and improve angina sx
2. Propranolol: A fib rate control; Hyperthyroidism to reduce T4 to T3 conversion
3. Esmolol: Acute Aortic Dissection—Reduce HR, SBP and LV contractility
4. Atenolol, Timolol, Carvedilol
5. Caution: Worsen glucose tolerance and cause weight gain
   1. Alpha-1 causes vasoconstriction and reduces glucose uptake into skeletal muscle—BB allow unopposed alpha-1 effects
   2. B-1 mediated lipolysis opposed—Decrease in lipolysis in adipocytes = weight gain
   3. Exception: BB with combined vasodilatory alpha-1 blockade—Carvedilol, Labetalol
6. Overdose—BB toxicity
   1. Symptom onset: 2-6hrs after ingestion
   2. CF: Cardiogenic Shock (Hypotension, Bradycardia), Bronchospasm, AMS, Seizures
   3. Labs: Hypoglycemia (prevent catecholamine stimulation of hepatic glucose production and glycogen breakdown)
   4. ECG: Prolonged PR interval, Bradycardia
   5. Management: Secure airway, GI decontamination, IV Fluid boluses, IV Atropine, IV Glucagon

### Class 3: Potassium Channel Blockers — Amiodarone

1. Use: Ventricular arrhythmia in patients with CAD and Ischemic Cardiomyopathy, LV Systolic Dysfunction
2. Adverse effects (50% patients on LT therapy develop significant side effects, 20% discontinue)
   1. Cardiac: Sinus bradycardia, heart block, QT prolongation, risk of Torsades de pointes
   2. Pulmonary: Chronic interstitial pneumonitis
      1. "Successfully treated with anti arrhythmic therapy for ventricular tachy 6mo ago, still taking medication"
      2. Progressive dyspnea, nonproductive cough
      3. CXR: New reticular or ground-glass opacities "primarily in middle lung fields"
      4. PFT: Restrictive pattern with reduced diffusion capacity of DLCO
   3. Endocrine: MONITOR TSH
      1. Hypothyroidism—decreases T4 to T3 conversion or inhibits hormone synthesis
      2. Hyperthyroidism—increases synthesis in nodular thyroid disease or latent Graves & releases preformed T3
   4. GI/Hepatic: Elevated transaminases, hepatitis
   5. Ocular: Corneal micro deposits, Optic neuropathy
   6. Derm: Blue-gray skin discoloration
   7. Neurologic: Peripheral neuropathy
3. Interactions: Digoxin, Verapamil, Quinidine, Propafenone
4. Others: Ibutilide, Dofetilide, Sotalol

## Dobutamine

1. MOA: B1 agonist, decrease LV end-systolic volume
   1. Positive inotropy—Inc cAMP in cardiac myocytes, enhanced calcium-mediated binding of actin-myosin complex to troponin C, inc myocardial contractility
   2. Positive chronotropic—HR inc via calcium channel activation
   3. Increased myocardial contractility allows forward ejection of higher volume —> decrease in LV end-systolic volume —> increased CO to better perfuse organs and less backup in pulmonary circulation
2. Use: Severe heart failure associated with LV systolic dysfunction and cardiogenic shock; Amphetamine CM

## Calcium Channel Blockers

1. Dihydropyridine (vascular selective—potent vasodilator)
   1. Amlodipine — SE: Peripheral edema due to peripheral blood vessel vasodilation (diuretics not helpful; Tx: ACEi—dilate post capillary venule and decrease capillary hydrostatic pressure)
   2. Felodipine, Nicardipine, Nifedipine, Nimodipine
2. Nondihydropyridine (myocardial selective—negative inotrope): Diltiazem, Verapamil, Gallopamil
   1. 2nd line if BB contraindication in Stable Angina
   2. Prophylaxis of Cluster headaches
   3. SE: Constipation, Hyperprolactinoma, Gingival hyperplasia, Leg swelling
3. Verapamil Toxicity: CNS stimulation (headache, insomnia, seizures), GI disturbance, Cardiac toxicity (arrhythmia)

CV Contraindications to Pregnancy: Symptomatic MS, AS, HF with LVEF <30%, Pulmonary Artery HTN, Bicuspid AV with ascending aorta enlargement >50mm

## Cardiac Surgery Complications

### Acute Mediastinitis

1. Deep tissue infection post cardiovascular surgery
2. S/S: Fever, chest pain, leukocytosis, Intraoperative wound contamination
3. CXR: Mediastinal widening
4. Dx: Chest and sternal imaging (especially if copious drainage from sternal wound in absence of classical symptoms)
5. Tx: Surgical debridement, Prolonged Abx, drainage

### Sternal Dehiscence

1. Separation of bone edges of the sternum
2. S/S: Chest wall instability and 'clicking' with chest movement
3. Dx: Displaced sternal wire or palpable rocking/clicking of sternum
4. Management: Urgent surgical exploration and sternal fixation

## Acute Adrenal Insufficiency (Primary AI)

1. Acute stressors trigger adrenal crisis due to autoimmune destruction of all 3 layers
2. S/S
   1. Shock
   2. Severe hypotension refractory to initial volume resuscitation
      1. Mainly due to Mineralocorticoid deficiency
      2. Exacerbated by Glucocorticoid deficiency because cortisol needed to potentiate alpha-1 stimulation on peripheral vascular tone
   3. Hyponatremia (hypovolemia-induced ADH secretion)
   4. Hypoglycemia (cortisol deficiency)
   5. Peripheral eosinophilia (eosinophils normally inhibited by corticosteroids)
3. Tx: Rapid volume repletion, Hydrocortisone or Dexamethasone
   1. Glucocortic: Dexamethasone preferred cause does not interfere with plasma cortisol level needed to confirm dx
   2. Mineralocortic: Fludrocortisone requires several days to exert sodium-retaining effects, thus started after initial tx
4. Secondary PAI: Only Cortisol deficient
   1. Patients on chronic glucocorticoid therapy can develop Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal axis suppression
   2. Pituitary cannot secrete ACTH when needed
   3. Tx: Hydrocortisone

## Heart Sounds

1. S3
2. S4: Ventricular hypertrophy; Diastolic dysfunction
   1. Atria contracting forcefully in pre systole
   2. Often heard during acute phase of myocardial infection due to LV stiffening and impaired relaxation induced by myocardial ischemia
   3. HTN induced LV Concentric hypertrophy

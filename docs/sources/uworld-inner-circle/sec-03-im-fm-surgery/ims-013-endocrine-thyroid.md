# Endocrine and Metabolic Screening Tests

- Aldosterone-renin activity ratio — Hyperaldosteronism (secondary HTN, hypokalemia)
- Late-night salivary cortisol — Cushing syndrome
- 24-hour urinary free cortisol --> Cushing Syndrome
- Low-dose dexamethasone suppression test — Cushing syndrome
- ACTH — Adrenal insufficiency (Addison's disease)
- Plasma fractionated metanephrine assay — Pheochromocytoma
- Plasma insulin-like growth factor-1 — Growth hormone secreting pituitary tumor
- Serum Prolactin assay — Prolactin secreting pituitary tumor

# Hypothyroidism

## Cause

1. Hashimoto thyroiditis — Anti-thyroid peroxidase antibody or Anti-thyroglobulin antibody
   1. RF: (Iodine-replete regions) Family history of autoimmune disease (T1DM, Primary adrenal insufficiency)

## CF

1. Metabolic slowing
   1. Fatigue
   2. Delayed DTR
   3. Weight gain
   4. Constipation: decreased gut motility
   5. Cognitive dysfunction: Memory changes, difficulty concentrating
      1. Important to distinguish memory changes between hypothyroidism (lack depressed mood or anhedonia) vs pseudo dementia (lack constipation, hoarseness)
   6. Psychiatric: Depression, increased irritability
2. Matrix glycosaminoglycan accumulation in tissue interstitial spaces
   1. Hoarseness, coarse hair and skin
3. Disruption of hypothalamic-pituitary-ovarian axis
   1. Anovulation and abnormal uterine bleeding (ie. oligomenorrhea)
   2. Low thyroxine levels stimulate hypothalamus — TRH release stimulates TSH and Prolactin
   3. inc inc Prolactin (-) hypothalamus and anterior pituitary — dec FSH, LH
   4. Tx with thyroxine replacement hormone normalizes TSH and prolactin levels and resolves symptoms
4. Recurrent pregnancy loss

## Evaluation

1. Serum TSH, free T4

## Complication: Myopathy

1. Myalgias, proximal muscle weakness, inc serum creatinine kinase

# Hyperthyroidism

## Cause

### Increased radioactive iodine uptake

1. Graves Disease
   1. Diffusely enlarged gland (gland hyperstimulated by thyrotropin receptor antibodies on TSH Receptors)
2. Toxic goiter (single or multinodular)
   1. One or multiple foci of increased uptake with decreased uptake in remainder of thyroid --> autonomous T4 production

### Decreased radioactive iodine uptake

3. Painless Thyroiditis: release of preformed thyroid hormone, inc serum thyroglobulin
   1. Patient transiently hyperthyroid due to release of preformed thyroid hormone, but thyroid actually producing less thyroid hormone due to inflammation
4. Exogenous thyroid hormone intake: dec serum thyroglobulin
   1. Exogenous thyroid hormone leads to decreased TSH, which leads to decreased stimulation of thyroid gland and decreased thyroid hormone production by thyroid gland itself

## Presentation

1. Anxiety, insomnia, palpitations
2. Tachycardia: T3 and T4 increases beta receptors on myocardium
3. Hypercalcemia: T3 direct effect on bone, elevated ALP/ALK (GGT is used to sort out between bone and liver disease)
4. Heat intolerance, increased perspiration, wt loss without decreased appetite
5. Proximal muscle weakness — due to muscle atrophy hyperthyroidism treatment improves myopathy
6. inc Prolactin — Oligomenorrhea or Amenorrhea

## Physical Exam

1. Goiter
2. HTN
3. Atrial fibrillation
   1. In 3-15% of patients
   2. Thyroid hormones increases beta-adrenergic receptor expression — increased sympathetic activity
   3. Hyperadrenergic symptoms controlled by Propranolol
4. Tremor of fingers/hands, hyperreflexia, lid lag
5. Proximal muscle weakness

## Management

1. Beta Blockers reduce hyperadrenergic symptoms and control heart rate
   1. Propranolol
      1. Also decreases conversion of T4 to T3 in peripheral tissue
   2. Atenolol

Note: BB use continued until patient becomes euthyroid with thionamides, radioiodine and/or surgery

2. Antithyroid drugs (Thionamides) preferred in mild disease, small goiter, low TSH receptor antibody
   1. Both drugs
      1. Most common SE: Allergic reaction
      2. Most deadly SE: Agranulocytosis — neutropenia
   2. Methimazole
      1. SE: 1st TM teratogen, Cholestasis
   3. Propylthiouracil
      1. SE: Hepatic failure, ANCA-associated vasculitis
3. Radioiodine ablation
   1. SE
      1. Permanent hypothyroidism
      2. Worsening of ophthalmopathy, patients given glucocorticoids to counter this effect
4. Surgery: Thyroidectomy
   1. SE
      1. Permanent hypothyroidism
      2. Risk of recurrent laryngeal nerve damage
      3. Risk of hypoparathyroidism

# De Quervain's Thyroiditis = Subacute granulomatous thyroiditis

1. Biopsy: Granulomas with epithelioid macrophages
2. No increase in uptake due to inflammation/destruction of thyroid gland --> follicular cells do NOT work --> no iodine uptake

# Thyroid Storm

1. RF
   1. Surgery
   2. Acute illness
   3. Acute iodine load
2. S/S
   1. High fever 104-106, Tachycardia, Hypertension, CHF, Cardiac arrhythmia
   2. Agitation, Delirium, Seizure, Coma, Goiter, Lid lag, Tremor, Warm/moist skin
   3. N/V, Diarrhea, Jaundice
3. Tx
   1. BB — decrease adrenergic response
   2. PTU and iodine — decrease hormone synthesis and release
   3. Glucocorticoids (Hydrocortisone) — decrease T4-T3 peripheral conversion and improve vasomotor stability
   4. Supportive care

# Thyroid Nodules

1. 1st: Assess TSH levels
   1. Cold nodule more concerning for malignancy: Elevated-normal TSH --> FNA
      1. FNA indications:
         1. >1cm + high risk features (microcalcifications, irregular margins, internal vascularity)
         2. >2cm noncystic
   2. Hot nodule: Low TSH, hyperthyroidism symptoms --> Radioactive Iodine Scintigraphy
      1. Hypofunctional/Cold — FNA based on size and US findings (higher risk of cancer)
      2. Hyperfunctional/Hot — Treat Hyperthyroidism
2. Thyroid Ultrasound determine size and sonographic features
   1. Note: Ultrasound > CT scan (CT scan useful for retrosternal extension or lymph node involvement)
3. FNA biopsy indications
   1. >1cm with high-risk features (micro calcification, irregular margins, internal vascularity)
   2. >2cm noncystic
4. Hyperthyroid (low TSH)
   1. Radionuclide thyroid scan with iodine-123 (hot nodules vs cold nodules)

# Euthyroid Sick Syndrome

1. Decreased T4-T3 peripheral conversion "low T3 syndrome"
2. RF
   1. Occurs in setting of hospitalized patients
   2. High endogenous cortisol
   3. Inflammatory cytokines (acute illness — TNF)
   4. Starvation
   5. Glucocorticoid
   6. Amiodarone
3. Tx not required, repeat tests once patient has returned to baseline health

# Thyroid Cancer

1. Thyroglobulin (produced only by thyroid tissue), is used as a tumor marker post-thyroidectomy
   1. Elevated levels indicate recurrence of malignancy
2. MC: Papillary — Empty-nuclei, psammoma bodies
   1. Tx: Surgical resection
      1. Recurrence suppressed by
         1. Radioiodine ablation post resection
         2. Thyroid replacement to suppress TSH secretion (prevent growth of residual/metastatic disease)
3. 2nd MC: Follicular — Neoplastic cells invade capsule (vs. Follicular adenoma with no capsule invasion)
4. Family Hx: Medullary Thyroid Cancer (Parafollicular cells) Present with symptoms of Hypocalcemia
   1. Serum Calcitonin measured for risk of metastasis and recurrence
   2. Associations
      1. 25% RET Proto-oncogene — MEN2
         1. (MTC, Pheo, Parathyroid Hyperplasia 2A or Marfanoid habitus and Mucosal neuromas 2B)

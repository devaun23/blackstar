# SOCIAL SCIENCES

## Agitated/Nonviolent patients in ER
1. Maintain distance of 2 arm lengths; avoid direct eye contact
2. Interview with door open, clinician closer to door
3. Verbal deescalation including offering food or drink
4. Chemical and physical restraints when deescalation fails and violence imminent

## Complementary and alternative medicine
1. Explain benefits and risks to patient
2. Schedule frequent follow ups if patient persistent on option with adverse effects

## Conflict of interest
- Most important factor in gift acceptance: patient's motivation

## Confidentiality
1. Adolescent nonsuicidal self-injury: Involve patient in plan to inform parents
2. Exceptions: Suicidal/homicidal ideation, Dangerous self-harm, Abuse, Neglect

## Driving
- Driving restrictions require evaluation of functional capacity (history, PE, on-road evaluation)

## Ethical principles
- Ethical and legal duty to treat life-threatening conditions regardless of patient behavior

## Healthcare quality
1. High value care: Quality/Total cost; Quality = outcome + safety + patient satisfaction
2. Fall risk in elderly: #1 cause injury/morbidity/mortality; Prevention: strength training; Canes/walkers may increase risk
3. Pay-for-performance: Links financial incentives to evidence-based care delivery

## End-of-life
1. Elderly dialysis: Minimal life expectancy improvement vs conservative care
2. Patient refuses diagnosis: Ask if another family member can know; Do not discharge without followup plan
3. Patient denies cancer therapy: Comfort care + IV morphine

## Patient Safety
1. Adverse drug events: Teach-back method; Multiple independent med reviews for new patients
2. Elderly prescribing: Limit number of prescribing physicians (PCP manages most meds)
3. Error by colleague: Discuss with responsible physician first before answering patient
4. Hand hygiene: Individual accountability + organizational culture when guidelines fail
5. High-risk deterioration: Rapid response team for clinical assessment
6. Human error: Design processes that anticipate and correct human error
7. Surgery complications: Full disclosure of preventable and non-preventable complications

## Informed Consent
1. Must include: Risk of refusing, Diagnosis, Risks/Benefits, Alternatives
2. Exceptions: Life-saving emergency (intubation), Related procedure during surgery
3. Adolescent drug testing without knowledge: Not recommended

## Intimate partner violence
1. Normalizing statements for screening
2. Risk increases postpartum; Routine screening required

## Medical Futility
- NEOT orders: Predetermined treatment limits; Useful when family reluctant to withdraw

## Professional Conduct
- Gifts: Not accepted during mood episodes; Cost, type, timing, motivation considered

## Common cognitive errors
1. Anchoring = premature closure
2. Availability (recency effect): Recent miss causes excessive workup
3. Confirmation bias: Reduce with electronic decision support tools
4. Most effective method to decrease diagnostic error: Metacognition

## Surgery Time-out
1. Preoperative Verification: Operative site + patient, 2 providers
2. Site Marking: Surgeon marks
3. Final Time-out: Prior to incision, entire team

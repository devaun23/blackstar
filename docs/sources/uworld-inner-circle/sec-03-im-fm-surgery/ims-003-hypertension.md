# HYPERTENSION

## New Diagnosis

1. Evaluate and confirm HTN diagnosis
   1. Lack end organ damage and mild HTN—> Ambulatory BP monitoring or home measurement
2. Laboratory evaluation
   1. Rule out secondary causes, end-organ complications
   2. Assess CKD via Serum Cr, Electrolytes, UA, U albumin:creatinine
      1. HTN is a major RF for CKD and CKD can cause secondary HTN
   3. Assess Thyroid dysfunction as cause of asymptomatic HTN via TSH
   4. Endocrine: Fasting glucose/HbA1c, Lipid profile, TSH
   5. Cardiac: ECG, Echo
   6. Other: CBC, Uric acid
3. Lipid panel (increased risk of ischemic heart disease — Atherosclerotic RF)
4. Fasting blood glucose or HbA1c (increased risk of DM — Esp for overweight/obese patients)

## Isolated Systolic Hypertension

*IMPORTANT CAUSE OF HYPERTENSION IN ELDERLY*

1. Presentation: Increased stiffness or decreased compliance of aortic and arterial walls
2. Diagnosis: Systolic BP ≥ 140mmHg with diastolic <90mmHg
   1. ECG: LVH with ST and T wave changes
   2. Echo: LVH without significant flow abnormalities
3. Management: Similar to primary hypertension

## Secondary Causes

*Suspect in young patients (<40yo) with end-organ damage (LVH)*

### Substance Abuse

1. Cocaine — Evaluate: Urine drug screen
2. Liquorice
   1. 11-beta hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase converts cortisol to cortisone (cortisone cannot activate mineralocorticoid receptors)
   2. Liquorice is a very powerful inhibitor of 11-beta hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase → persistence of cortisol → HTN
   3. Mutation of 11-beta hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase can cause a similar scenario
3. Ephedra, St. John's Wort → catecholamine like effects → HTN

### Drugs

1. Rebound HTN from antihypertensives: Clonidine/Methyldopa withdrawal (pregnant patient)
   - Clonidine is alpha2 agonist; Both can cause issues if a dose is skipped
2. NSAIDs: Inhibit COX → reduced prostaglandin secretion (vasodilator) → vasoconstriction → HTN
3. SNRI for depression (Venlafaxine, desvenlafaxine, milnacipram, duloxetine) → HTN
4. Tyramine crisis due to MAOi (isocarboxazide, tranylcypromine, phenelzine)
5. Cyclosporin toxicity: Potent afferent arteriole vasoconstriction → less perfusion of JG cells → Renin secretion → HTN

### Cardiac: Coarctation of the Aorta

1. Narrowing of descending aorta distal to left subclavian artery
2. Etiology: Congenital, Acquired—Takayasu arteritis
3. Presentation
   1. Upper body: Well developed, UE Hypertension—Headaches, Epistaxis
   2. Lower body: Underdeveloped, Claudication, Diminished femoral pulses
   3. Brachial-femoral delay
   4. S4—HTN induced LV hypertrophy
   5. Left inter scapular systolic or continuous murmur throughout thorax
4. Initial Evaluation: Simultaneous palpation of brachial and femoral pulses, Bilateral UE and LE BP measurements
5. Diagnosis
   1. ECG: LVH (High-voltage QRS complexes, lateral ST segment V5-V6 depression, lateral T wave inversion)
   2. CXR: Inferior notching of 3rd-8th ribs, Erosions of inferior costal surfaces, Classical "3" sign
   3. Echo: Diagnostic confirmation
6. Tx: Balloon angioplasty +/- stent or surgery

### Adrenal

1. **Congenital Adrenal Hyperplasia**
   1. Is 2nd number a "1" = virilization; Is 1st number a "1" = HTN
   2. 21-hydroxylase deficiency = virilization in females, peripheral precocious puberty in males
   3. 11b-hydroxylase deficiency = virilization + HTN (11-deoxycorticosterone has partial mineralocorticoid receptor activity)
   4. 17a-hydroxylase deficiency = HTN

2. **Cushing Disease**
   1. Hypercortisolism —> mild mineralocorticoid receptor activity
      1. Acts on aldosterone receptors → HTN, hypokalemia, metabolic alkalosis
   2. CF: Buffalo hump, hyperglycemia, fracture of the hip with AVN, recurrent fractures, Peptic ulcer disease

3. **Growth Hormone excess** —> Acromegaly —> HTN

4. **Hyperaldosteronism: Conn Syndrome**
   1. Adrenal adenoma/hyperplasia → ↑↑aldosterone → reabsorb sodium and water → ↑BP
      1. ↑↑BP distal to afferent arteriole — JG cells hyperperfused = ↓Renin
      2. ↑Aldosterone and ↓renin — PAC:PRA ratio >20
   2. Evaluation
      1. Screening: PAC:PRA ratio >30
      2. Diagnostic: Salt Suppression Test (Normal: salt infusion leads to ↓aldosterone; Conn's: aldosterone does not drop)
   3. Treatment
      1. Adrenal vein sampling guides Adrenalectomy vs. aldosterone-antagonists
         1. Unilateral: Adrenalectomy
         2. Bilateral: Aldosterone Antagonist (spironolactone or eplerenone)

5. **Hyperparathyroidism** — Hypercalcemia → smooth muscle contraction → vasoconstriction → ↑BP

6. **Hyperthyroidism** — Causes insertion of more beta1 receptors on myocardial cells → stimulate heart → ↑CO → ↑BP

7. **MEN 2: Pheochromocytoma**
   1. Family history of thyroid disorders and patient with HTN
   2. MEN 2a: RET gene mutation. Pheochromocytoma, MTC, primary hyperparathyroidism
   3. MEN 2b: RET gene mutation. Pheochromocytoma, MTC, marfanoid habitus, mucosal neuromas
   4. Pheochromocytoma: Tumor produces ↑↑catecholamine → alpha1 agonist → vasoconstriction → ↑SVR → HTN

### Heme

1. Anemia: poor O2 carrying capacity → tissue hypoxia → ↑CO → HTN
2. ↑EPO: ↑Blood viscosity → ↑SVR → HTN
3. Polycythemia vera: ↑Blood viscosity → ↑SVR → HTN

### Renal: Refractory HTN

1. **Fibromuscular Dysplasia** — Dysplasia of smooth muscle cells (arterial media) → compression of renal artery → RAAS activation → PAC:PRA <20
2. **Renal Artery Stenosis — Renovascular HTN**
   1. #1 RF: Smoking (Atherosclerosis in arterial intima layer)
   2. Evaluated if signs of renovascular disease (AKI after ACE-I, recurrent flash pulmonary edema)
   3. May present in CHILDREN AS WELL → Ultrasound to evaluate for stenosis
   4. PP: Renal artery stenosis → AA constriction → Less blood to AA → Stimulate JG cells → ↑↑renin → RAAS activation → PAC:PRA <20
   5. Dx: Renal angiography or renal arteriography
   6. Caution: Avoid ACEi due to AKI and ↑Renin
      - Stenosed afferent arteriole — ↓blood in glomerular capillaries — ↑Creatinine
      - ACEi dilate efferent arteriole — ↓hydrostatic pressure — ↓GFR — ↓Cr clearance
      - **Killing on both sides: Afferent stenosed and Efferent dilated → florid Renal Failure**
3. **ADPKD** — Family history of SAH, Cyst in liver/pancreas/kidneys, Mitral valve prolapse
4. **Glomerulonephritis** — PSGN, IgA nephropathy, any nephritic syndrome → damage to kidneys → renin → RAAS → HTN
5. **Tumors**
   1. Children: Wilms tumor (not calcified, do not cross midline), Neuroblastoma (calcified, cross midline) — can make renin
   2. Adults: Renal Cell Carcinoma — Secretes Renin

### MSK

1. Scleroderma: Fibrosis of blood vessels → ↑SVR → HTN

### Neuro: Cushing Reflex

1. Trauma patient with brain herniation symptoms
2. Bradycardia, hypertension, irregular respirations due to increased ICP

### Gynecology

1. OCP use *Biggest RF for HTN in reproductive age females*
2. Pre-eclampsia, eclampsia, HELLP syndrome

### Obstructive Sleep Apnea

1. Pulmonary HTN in adults
2. Systemic HTN in kids

## Comorbidities — Atrial Fibrillation

1. PP: Underlying substrate created by atrial remodeling — LA enlargement
   1. Age-related myocardial changes (Paroxysmal AF → persistent/permanent over time; 70% age ≥65)
   2. MS: Opening snap with mid diastolic rumble at apex; LA dilation → AFib
   3. Chronic HTN → LV Concentric hypertrophy → Decreased LV diastolic filling → LA stretching → Atrial fibrosis
   4. CAD causes ischemic reduction in LV function and LA dilation
2. PP: Foci of rapid electrical activity originating in pulmonary veins
   1. COPD → hypoxic pulmonary vasoconstriction → Pulmonary HTN and RA dilation
   2. B agonist used for COPD can trigger A fib
   3. Alcohol intake and increase sympathetic drive may contribute
3. Dx: ECG (variable R-R intervals, no P waves); Continuous Ambulatory ECG for paroxysmal disease

## Lifestyle Interventions

1. DASH diet
2. *Note: Smoking cessation in a light smoker not likely to produce significant reduction in BP*

## Caution: Contraception

1. OCP use discouraged in patients with HTN → discuss alternative contraception

## Treatment with Comorbid Conditions

1. ACE-I: Avoid in African Americans due to risk of angioedema
2. ARB: Greatest reduction of LVH (vs CCB, ACE-I, Diuretics); Losartan has mild uricosuric effect—preferred in Gout
3. Aldosterone Antagonist
4. BB
5. CCB
   1. Dihydropyridine: Amlodipine (SE: Peripheral edema)
   2. Nondihydropyridine: Diltiazem, Verapamil, Gallopamil
6. Diuretic: Thiazide—Chlorthalidone
   1. Avoid in Gout (increased uric acid levels)
   2. Preferred in Osteoporosis (increase Ca reabsorption in distal tubule)
7. Persistence despite treatment: Excessive alcohol intake (≥2 drinks/day, ≥5 in 1 setting)

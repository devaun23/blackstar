# SKIN INFECTIONS

## IMPETIGO CONTAGIOSUM

### Nonbullous—S. Aureus (MC), Group A Strep
- Contagious bacterial infection of superficial epidermis
- Erythematous papule and pustules coalesce and rupture → honey colored crusting
- Possibly itchy or painful
- Common occurs secondarily at site of prior trauma (infection, inflammation, open wound)
- Self-limiting
- Linear pattern due to spread via scratching
- Spares palms and soles
- Tx:
  - Localized—Topical Abx—Mupirocin
  - Extensive—Oral Abx—Cephalexin
- Complication: Poststreptococcal Glomerulonephritis

### Bullous Impetigo
- Staph aureus exfoliative toxin A
- Rapidly enlarging vesicles and bullae rupture → exudates and crusts
- Children or immunocompromised adults
- Flaccid bullae with yellow fluid, (+) Nikolsky sign

### Ecthyma (Ulcerative Impetigo)
- Penetrates Dermis

## HERPETIC WHITLOW

- Tingling and pain with small clustered vesicles on erythematous base
- Often with concurrent herpetic labialis

## CELLULITIS

### S. pyogenes (GAS)
- Unilateral lesions
- Spreading erythema, warmth and tenderness at site of breakdown
- Purulent lesions uncommon
- Tx: TMP-SMX

### Vibrio vulnificus
- Marine environment
- Cellulitis post inoculation into wound
- Spreading cellulitis similar to S pyogenes

## CANDIDAL INTERTRIGO (INTERTRIGINOUS CANDIDIASIS)

- Moist environments
- Beefy red patches with satellite red papules or macules
- Mild: Topical azole—Clotrimazole, Ketoconazole
- Severe: Oral azole—Fluconazole, Itraconazole

## ECTHYMA GANGRENOSUM

- Pseudomonas aeruginosa
- Setting of profound neutropenia and bacteremia

## HIDRADENITIS SUPPURATIVA

- Intertriginous areas
- Tender, inflammatory nodules with sinus tract formation and multiple open comedones
- Tx: Doxycycline

## INTERTRIGO

- Moist skin fold inflammation

## SHINGLES (HERPES ZOSTER)

- Neuritic symptoms—pain, itching, burning, allodynia
- Tx: Acyclovir, Famciclovir, Valacyclovir

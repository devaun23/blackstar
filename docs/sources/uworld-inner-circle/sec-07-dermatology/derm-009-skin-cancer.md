# SKIN CANCER

## BASAL CELL CARCINOMA (BCC)

### Risk Factors
- Severe sunburns, Family history, Fair skin, Numerous (>100) nevi

### Presentations
1. Slow-growing, ulcerated, pearly nodule with rolled border
2. Chronic ulcer that bleeds, oozes or crusts
3. Reddish patch or irritated area = superficial BCC
4. Pearly or translucent nodule that is pink, red, white or skin toned = nodular BCC
5. Pale, starlike with poorly defined borders

### Diagnosis
- Shave, punch, or excisional biopsy

### Treatment
- 1st line—Surgical excision with 4 mm margins
- Mohs micrographic surgery (face/high risk)
- 2nd line—Topical Fluorouracil, Topical Imiquimod
- Curettage & electrodissection (low risk)

## SQUAMOUS CELL CARCINOMA (SCC)

- Slow-growing and non-resolving lesion in sun-exposed area
- Keratinize or ulcerate with crusting and bleeding
- Early perineurial invasion → Numbness, paresthesias, burning pain
- Tonsil ulcer in smoker → Tumor invasion: Sore throat, Odynophagia, Referred Otalgia or isolated neck mass due to regional lymph node spread

## KERATOACANTHOMA

- Rapidly growing, dome-shaped nodule with central keratin plug
- Often self-resolves

## MELANOMA

### Risk Factors
- Severe sunburns, Family history, Fair skin, Numerous (>100) nevi
- Color variegation strongly suggests malignancy

### Types
- Superficial spreading (MC)
- Nodular
- Lentigo maligna
- Acral lentiginous

# SKIN CONDITIONS AND ASSOCIATED DISEASES

## ACANTHOSIS NIGRICANS

1. Benign AN: younger, insulin-resistant states (DM, Obesity, PCOS). Increased Insulin or ILGF stimulate epidermal and dermal proliferation. Skin tags common.
2. Malignant AN: middle-aged/elderly with sudden appearance. GI and GU neoplasm. Lesions in uncommon areas (mucous membranes, palms, soles)

## ACROCHORDONS (SKIN TAGS)

- Unexplained migratory superficial thrombophlebitis → Pancreatic Adenocarcinoma

## ACUTE FEBRILE NEUTROPHILIC DERMATOSIS (SWEET SYNDROME)

- Abrupt onset of painful edematous papule, plaques or nodules on the head, neck and upper extremities
- Associated: Hematologic malignancy, Upper respiratory and GI infections, IBD

## NECROBIOSIS LIPOIDICA

- Associated with DM
- Confluent annular lesions with yellowish-brown hue, dilated blood vessels and epidermal atrophy

## MEDICATION-SKIN DISEASE ASSOCIATIONS

| Medication | Associated Skin Condition |
|---|---|
| ACE-I, Thiazide | Lichen Planus |
| Antibiotics, OCP | Erythema Nodosum |
| Antibiotics, Phenytoin, Allopurinol | Leukocytoclastic vasculitis |
| Antimalarials, Indomethacin, Propanol | Psoriasis |

## COMMON TERMS QUICK REFERENCE

- Dermatitis herpetiformis: Celiac disease (Tx: Dapsone)
- Erythema nodosum: IBD, Behcet, Sarcoid, Coccidio, Histo, TB, Strep, Leprosy
- Erythema infectiosum: Parvovirus B19 (slapped cheek)
- Erythema marginatum: Lyme disease
- Erythema multiforme: Mycoplasma pneumonia, HSV, Drugs (Sulfa, B-lactam)
- Pyoderma gangrenosum: IBD, Rheumatoid Arthritis
- Pyogenic granuloma: vascular tumor

## COMMON TREATMENTS QUICK REFERENCE

- Topical Antifungal: Miconazole cream, Clotrimazole, Terbinafine, Tolnaftate—Tinea Pedis
- Nystatin powder—Candida
- Permethrin cream—Scabies
- Topical CST: Triamcinolone ointment—Acute Contact Dermatitis
- High potency Topical CST: Clobetasol—Bullous pemphigoid

# BURN CLASSIFICATION

## SUPERFICIAL (1ST DEGREE)

- Protective barrier/epidermis intact
- Tx: Wound care only

## PARTIAL THICKNESS (2ND DEGREE)

- Tx: Debridement to remove devitalized tissue

## FULL THICKNESS (3RD DEGREE)

- Tx: Complete surgical excision with skin grafting

## IMPORTANT BURN PRINCIPLES

- *Topical CST not recommended in burns—decrease in local inflammatory response may lead to impaired healing and increased risk of infection
- *Prophylactic Abx not used—do not prevent burn sepsis

## BURN INFECTIONS

- Immediately post-burn: Staph aureus (from hair follicles and sweat glands)
- Post 5 days: Gram-negative—Pseudomonas aeruginosa or Candida
- Wound infection: Large surface area burn (>20%), change in appearance of wound

## BURN SEPSIS (GRAM NEGATIVE)

- *Burn sepsis criteria differs slightly from SIRS
- Dx: Quantitative wound culture, Biopsy for histopathology to determine invasion depth
- Tx: Broad spectrum IV Abx—Piperacillin/Tazobactam, Carbapenem +
  - MRSA—Vancomycin
  - Multi drug resistant Pseudomonas aeruginosa—Aminoglycoside

# AUTOIMMUNE BLISTERING SKIN DISORDERS

## BULLOUS PEMPHIGOID

- Histology: Subepidermal cleavage
- IF: Linear IgG/C3 deposits along BM
- Serology: Autoantibodies to hemidesmosomes (bullous pemphigoid antigens)
- Associated neurological disorders: Dementia, Parkinson, Depression, Bipolar disorder
- Dx: Skin biopsy, Serology
- Tx: High-potency topical CST—Clobetasol
- Rupture of tense bullae → Erosions

# OTHER BLISTERING SKIN DISORDERS

## PORPHYRIA CUTANEA TARDA

- Sun-exposed areas
- Painless blistering cutaneous photosensitivity and hyperpigmentation
- Chronic scarring
- Exacerbated with alcohol consumption
- Causes: familial, hepatitis C
- Treatment: phlebotomy, sun avoidance, antimalarials

## DERMATITIS HERPETIFORMIS

- Subepidermal microabscess (blisters)
- Anti-epidermal transglutaminase IgA deposits in dermis
- Associated with Celiac disease
- Tx: Dapsone

## ERYTHEMA MULTIFORME

- Target-shaped lesions
- Associated with HSV, Mycoplasma pneumonia, Drugs (Sulfa, B-lactam)

## SCABIES

- Diffuse: Crusted scabies (Immunocompromised: HIV)
- Tx: Permethrin cream

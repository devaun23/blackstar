# PIGMENTED SKIN DISORDERS

## SEBORRHEIC DERMATITIS

1. Mild cases: "Dandruff"
2. RF: Parkinson Disease, HIV
3. Tx: Topical Antifungal—Selenium sulfide, Ketoconazole
4. Topical glucocorticoids **High potency CSTs not recommended on face due to dermal atrophy risk
5. Topical calcineurin inhibitors (Pimecrolimus)

# COMMON SKIN DISORDERS

## ATOPIC DERMATITIS (ECZEMA)

1. RF: Family history of atopy, Mutation in filaggrin gene
   - Filaggrin gene mutation also involved in Ichthyosis vulgaris
2. CF
   1. Acute: Pruritic, erythematous patches and papules on face, extremities, butt
   2. Chronic: Lichenified plaques—chronic scratching causes skin to be dry, scaly and excoriated
3. Associations: Eosinophilic Esophagitis, Langerhan cell histiocytosis
4. Tx:
   1. Topical emollients
   2. Topical CSTs
   3. Topical Calcineurin inhibitors (Pimecrolimus)
5. Infectious Complications
   1. Impetigo—S. Aureus, S. Pyogenes
      - Papules and pustules with honey crusted adherent coating +/- pain or pruritis
   2. Eczema Herpeticum—HSV 1
      - PAINFUL vesicular lesion, "punched out" erosions and hemorrhagic crusting "painful vesicle on erythematous base"
      - Life-threatening
   3. Molluscum Contagiosum—Poxvirus
      - Skin-colored papule with central umbilication
   4. Tinea Corporis—T. rubrum → Pruritic circular patch with central clearing, raised/scaly border

## NUMMULAR ECZEMA

- "Nummum" is latin for coin—lesions fit under a coin
- Chronic, relapsing/remitting
- Erythematous, pruritic, coin-shaped patches on the extremities
- Intermittent exudate (yellow fluid seeps out)

## CONTACT DERMATITIS

- Activation of memory T cells
- Acute: Pruritic red, indurated plaques with vesicles/bullae
- Chronic: Lichenification, fissuring
- Rash: 4-96hrs in sensitized, 3 weeks in unexposed
- Linear pattern where skin brushed against plant leaves
- Diffuse pattern if brushed against clothes, pets, smoke from burning plants
- Tx: Avoidance; Topical or Systemic Corticosteroids

## ACNE MECHANICA

- Occurs anywhere pressure or friction is applied (crutches, bra straps, turtlenecks, helmets)
- Mechanical pressure damages pilosebaceous follicles

## PSORIASIS

- Hand presentation presents with nail psoriasis
- Triggers: Local skin trauma (Koebner phenomenon), Systemic Glucocorticoid Withdrawal, Meds (Antimalarials, Indomethacin, Propanol)
- Sudden-onset severe Psoriasis → HIV testing required

## MISCELLANEOUS LESIONS (Pages 3-4)

### Callus
- Hyperkeratotic lesion next to bony prominence on feet or hands
- Pain or discomfort due to repeated shear or frictional forces

### Condyloma Lata
- Secondary syphilis
- Flattened pink or gray velvety plaques
- Mucous membranes and moist skin of genital organs, perineum, mouth

### Dermatofibroma
- Dome-shaped, firm, brown-pink, nontender nodule, < 1 cm in diameter resulting from fibroblast proliferation
- Dimples when pinched (dimple or buttonhole sign)
- Benign. Treatment with shave biopsy or excision for cosmetic reasons

### Irritant Contact Dermatitis
- Exposure to irritating chemicals (soaps, detergents, solvents)
- Marked erythema, burning sensation or pruritis in localized area
- Chronic: Dry scales, lichenification and fissuring
- Tx: Topical emollients (restore skin barrier), Topical CST (decrease inflammation)

### Lichen Simplex Chronicus (Neurodermatitis)
- Thickened, excoriated plaques due to persistent scratching and rubbing in areas easy to reach
- Associated with anxiety disorders

### Lipoma
- Slowly enlarging, mobile subcutaneous mass
- Biopsy required to differentiate from Melanoma

### Sporotrichosis
- Direct inoculation of the skin by a plant containing the fungus
- Ulcerating, pustular nodules at inoculation site and lymphatic channels

### Xerosis
- Dry skin. Tx: Topical emollients only

### Seborrheic Keratosis
- Keratin plugs
- Flat seborrheic keratosis variant exists

### Cutaneous Warts (HPV)
- Can occur on plantar, palmar or genital areas
- Enter through tiny cut; hyperkeratotic papule can be painful with standing
- HPV new diagnosis requires HIV screening
- HPV 16, 18: Squamous cell carcinoma of the anus, genital organs, throat
- Tx: Chemical (trichloroacetic acid, podophyllin), Immune (Imiquimod), Surgery

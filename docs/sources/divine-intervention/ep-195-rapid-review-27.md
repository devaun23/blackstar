# Biostatistics
NNH = 5 is more important RF than NNH = 10 or 20 (low NNH = significant RF, low NNT = effective drug)

## Power
Two things to increase power: increase study population, increase effect size
If you increase sensitivity: PPV decreases, NPV increases

# Surgical Prophylaxis
Pre-op abx? Cefazolin ("Ancef") — 30-60 mins BEFORE incision
Covers: Staph aureus, Staph epidermidis, Strep, some gram-negatives
If beta-lactam allergy: Clinda or Vanc
Infection after shoulder surgery → Cutibacterium acnes

# Endocarditis Prophylaxis
## Who gets ppx?
Hx prosthetic heart valve, Hx IE, Unrepaired cyanotic CHD, CHD fixed <6 months ago, Valve disorder after heart transplant, Any plastic in heart
## What procedures?
Invasive dental, Incision in respiratory mucosa, Ongoing GI/GU tract infection, Infected skin/muscle/bone, Surgery for prosthetic valve/stent
## What abx?
Oral amoxicillin — If PCN-allergic: cephalexin, macrolide, or clinda

# Urinary Incontinence

| | Stress | Urge | Overflow |
|---|---|---|---|
| Presentation | With increased intraabdominal pressure | Can't make it to bathroom | Small amounts throughout day |
| Pathophys | Pelvic floor weakness | Detrusor overactivity | Detrusor doesn't sense fullness |
| PVR | Normal | Normal or low | High |
| Associations | Many pregnancies/deliveries | MS, post-menopausal, UTI | MS, DM |
| Tx | Kegels → urethral sling | Anticholinergics or mirabegron | Catheterization → bethanechol or neostigmine |

Anticholinergics for urge: "On The Darn Toilet" = oxybutynin, tolterodine, darifenacin/solifenacin, trospium

# Hyperemesis Gravidarum
Pathophys: High beta-hCG
Associations: Multiples, Molar pregnancy
Criteria: Loss of >5% prepregnancy weight
Labs: Elevated ketones
Tx inpatient: IVF + IV thiamine/B1 + IV antiemetic
Tx outpatient: Vit B6 + doxylamine + small frequent meals

# Behcet's Disease
Painful sores on buccal mucosa + genitals, Mediterranean descent
HLA? HLA-B51 — Dx? Pathergy test
Other HLAs: HLA-B27 → seronegative spondyloarthropathies, HLA-B57 → HSR to abacavir

# Microcytic Anemias

| | IDA | ACD | Lead Poisoning |
|---|---|---|---|
| Serum iron | Low | Low | High |
| Ferritin | Low | High | High |
| TIBC | High | Low | Low |
| Transferrin sat | Low | Low | High |
| RDW | Increased | Normal | Normal |
| Pathophys | — | Hepcidin → sequesters iron | Inhibits ALAD + ferrochelatase |

Other microcytic causes: Thalassemia, Sideroblastic anemia (can be part of MDS → hyposegmented neutrophils → progression to acute leukemia)

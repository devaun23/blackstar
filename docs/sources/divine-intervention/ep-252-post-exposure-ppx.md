# HEPATITIS B PEP
Newborn to HBsAg+ mom → HepB vaccine + HepB Ig (separate arms)
HCW needlestick + unvaccinated → HepB vaccine + HepB Ig
HCW needlestick + vaccinated (adequate Ab response) → do NOTHING

# HEPATITIS C PEP
Not a lot you can do; Tx available: Sofosbuvir, NS-B inhibitors

# HEPATITIS A PEP
Never vaccinated → HepA vaccine
Immunocompromised → HepA Ig AND vaccine

# HIV PEP
Two regimens for ~4 weeks: Tenofovir + Emtricitabine (TE) OR Lamivudine + Zidovudine (LZ)

# VARICELLA ZOSTER PEP
Newborn to mom with active shingles/varicella, pregnant, HIV CD4 <200 → VZV Ig (CANNOT give vaccine — live)
Healthy person → Varicella vaccine

# RABIES PEP
Rabies Ig (injected AROUND the wound) + Rabies vaccine (contralateral extremity — don't give in same spot)

# N. MENINGITIDIS PEP
Ciprofloxacin, Rifampin (preferred), Ceftriaxone, Azithromycin
Pregnant → Ceftriaxone or Azithromycin

# PERTUSSIS PEP
Macrolide (clarithromycin, erythromycin)
Long QT or macrolides c/I → TMP-SMX

# TUBERCULOSIS PEP
Test with TST or IGRA; induration cutoff for exposure: ≥5mm
Latent TB regimen: INH 9 months + Vitamin B6

# TETANUS PEP
## Group A (unsure of vaccines or last tetanus >10 yrs)
Nasty wound → TDaP + Tetanus Ig; Small wound → TDaP only
## Group B (vaccine within last 5 yrs)
Do nothing (regardless of wound type)

# NECROTIZING FASCIITIS PEP
Azithromycin, Clindamycin, or Rifampin + Penicillin

# TRANSMISSION NOTES
Rotavirus/C. diff (spores) → handwashing (can't use hand sanitizer)
Staph aureus gastroenteritis → improper food refrigeration

# NEEDLESTICK SEROCONVERSION RISK
HepB: ~1 in 3 (25%) — highest risk
HepC: ~1 in 30
HIV: ~1 in 300

Most effective way to reduce HepB transmission in HCW → HepB vaccination

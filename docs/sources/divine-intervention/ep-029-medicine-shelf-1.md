# Tricuspid Stenosis

Opening snap + diastolic rumble at left 4th interspace + tall jugular venous A-waves. Murmur? Tricuspid stenosis
Note: "Tall Jugular Venous A-waves" = JVD
A-waves = atrial contraction
Contraction against increased resistance = taller A waves

What increases the intensity of tricuspid stenosis? Leg raise, IVF, Inspiration (increased preload)

What decreases the intensity of tricuspid stenosis? (increased afterload; less blood flow across valve)


# Porphyria Cutanea Tarda

Elevated AST/ALT + blistering lesions on dorsum of hands + hirsutism -> porphyria cutanea tarda
Deficiency enzyme? UROD (uroporphyrinogen decarboxylase)
Tx? Phlebotomy


# Hypoglycemia

Hypoglycemia + hypoglycemia sxs + resolution w/ glucose administration -> Insulinoma
These 3 symptoms together = Whipple's triad

DDx 3 major etiologies of hypoglycemia:
- Insulinoma = increased Insulin + increased C-peptide + negative secretagogue screen
- Exogenous insulin = increased Insulin + decreased C-peptide + negative secretagogue screen
- Sulfonylureas = increased Insulin + increased C-peptide + positive secretagogue screen

Tx of acute hypoglycemia? Juice/sugary drink (outpatient) or glucagon injection (inpatient; quickest way)

Skin finding a/w glucagonoma -> necrolytic migratory erythema


# Diarrhea

Pork consumption or new puppy + diarrhea = Yersinia enterocolitica
Undercooked shellfish + diarrhea = Vibrio parahaemolyticus or Vibrio vulnificus
- Which has worse prognosis in pt's with liver dz? Vibrio vulnificus
Rice-water stools in developing country = Vibrio cholera
- Tx? Oral rehydration + doxycycline
Very small inoculum + bloody diarrhea = Shigella
Eggs/poultry consumption + bloody diarrhea = Salmonella enteritidis
Potato-salad consumption + watery diarrhea just hours later = Staph aureus
Bloody diarrhea w/ low PLTs + unconjugated hyperbilirubinemia + elevated SCr = E. coli O157:H7 or Shigella
Diarrhea on return from Mexico -> ETEC
Foul-smelling watery diarrhea after recent abx treatment -> C. diff
- 1st Prevention? Hand washing
- Tx: 1st line: Oral vancomycin or fidaxomicin. Last resort: fecal transplant

Crampy abdominal pain after eating home-canned veggies = Botulism (Clostridium botulinum)
Bloody diarrhea w/ ascending paralysis in puppy owner = GBS 2/2 Campylobacter jejuni
Watery diarrhea after eating rice at Chinese restaurant = Bacillus cereus (Cereulide toxin)
Empiric ABX Tx of diarrhea? Fluoroquinolones (1st-line) or macrolides


# Lead Poisoning

75 yo M + lives in home built in 1930s + Prussian blue stain shows basophilic inclusions around nucleus. Toxicity? Lead poisoning
Histo? Ringed sideroblasts
Labs? Increased ferritin + decreased TIBC
Tx? Succimer aka DMSA, EDTA, Dimercaprol aka BAL
What vitamin supplement to give in sideroblastic anemia? B6 (ALAS rate-limiting enzyme uses B6 cofactor)


# PCP Pneumonia

Febrile patient + taking high-dose immunosuppressants + CXR shows diffuse bilateral ground-glass infiltrates -> PCP pneumonia
Dx? BAL (bronchoalveolar lavage)
Relevant stain? Silver stain
Elevated marker from pulmonary fluid? LDH
PPx? TMP-SMX for CD4 < 200
Alternatives? Aerosolized pentamidine, dapsone, or atovaquone (clindamycin)
Tx? TMP-SMX
When to add steroids? O2 Sat < 92% aka PaO2 < 70, or A-a gradient > 35


# Nephrolithiasis

Flank pain + gross hematuria -> nephrolithiasis
- Envelope-shaped = calcium oxalate (X in oXalate for X-shape of folds of letter envelope)
  - Etiologies: Ethylene glycol, Crohn's Disease (increased reabsorption of oxalate)
- Coffin-shaped = struvite (magnesium-ammonium-phosphate)
  - A/w which bug? Proteus mirabilis (Urease-positive -> makes urine alkaline -> precipitates stones)
- Hexagonal-shaped = cysteine ("SIXtine stones")
  - Etiology? Cystinuria
  - Pathophys? Defective COLA transporter -> cannot reabsorb cysteine, ornithine, lysine, and arginine at PCT
  - Tx? Acetazolamide (alkalinizes urine to solubilize the stones; cystine stones are acidic)
- Radiolucent stones = uric acid stones (U in radiolUcent for U in Uric acid)

Imaging to diagnose kidney stones? CT (1st line). U/S if pregnant.
Tx? Fluids + tamsulosin (alpha-1 blocker). Large stone: ESWL. Giant struvite stone: nephrostomy tube + surgery.


# Bone Tumors

24 yo M with painless, palpable bony mass on left knee. Knee XR reveals contiguous mushroom shaped mass -> osteochondroma
DDx:
- Osteosarcoma = kids
- Osteoid osteoma = pain in legs at night + relieved with NSAIDs


# Carbon Monoxide Poisoning

66 yo F found unconscious at home in December. PEx shows cherry-red appearance of skin -> CO poisoning
Classic presentation? HA + AMS + cherry-red lips
A/w? Winter, space heaters, garage suicide
Pathophys? CO occupies binding sites on Hgb with 200x affinity than O2 -> leftward shift of oxyhemoglobin dissociation curve -> prevents Hgb from releasing O2
NBS? Carboxyhemoglobin level
Tx? Hyperbaric O2 (reduces the half-life of CO binding to Hgb)
Neuroimaging finding? Hyperintense lesions in globus pallidus


# Contrast Nephropathy & NAC

Elevated creatinine 24 hrs after getting a CT scan -> Contrast nephropathy
Prevention? Hydration + N-acetylcysteine

Other uses of N-acetylcysteine:
- APAP overdose
- Inhalation tx for CF patients (breaks disulfide bonds)
- Hemorrhagic cystitis 2/2 cyclophosphamide (however, mesna is first-line)
  - What virus causes hemorrhagic cystitis? Adenovirus
  - What microbe causes hemorrhagic cystitis in pt from Egypt? Schistosoma haematobium

Skin fibrosis after getting a brain MRI -> nephrogenic systemic fibrosis (exposure: gadolinium)

What DM medication should be held before a CT scan? Metformin (risk of lactic acidosis with renal dysfunction)


# Peripheral Edema from Vasodilators

What common AE is a/w hydralazine and Ca2+ channel blockers? Peripheral edema
Pathophys? Dilation of precapillary arterioles
Tx? ACE-I (dilation of post-capillary venule -> decrease capillary hydrostatic pressure)


# SJS / TEN

HIV patient with 3-day h/o fever + targetoid skin lesions + lip/mouth ulcers + visual impairment. PEx shows skin sloughing (8% BSA). Positive Nikolsky sign. Started allopurinol 10 days ago for chronic gout -> SJS
NBS? Stop the drug
What Dx if there is > 30% BSA involvement? TEN


# HHS vs DKA

30 yo F with 3-day hx of polydipsia and polyuria. Blood glucose is 650 mg/dl, Bicarb is 21, pH is 7.35 -> HHS (Hyperglycemic Hyperosmolar Non-ketotic Syndrome)
- A/w T2DM; normal pH; no ketosis
- Why no ketosis in HHS? T2DM pt's still make insulin, which inhibits glucagon and thus prevents DKA
- In contrast: DKA is a/w T1DM and causes low pH & ketosis
- Tx? Fluids + insulin
- Na+ balance? Hyperosmolar hyponatremia. Na correction: add 1.6 for every extra 100 of glucose
- K+ balance? Serum K+ high, total body K+ low
- Patient with HHS receives rapid tx and becomes altered/comatose -> cerebral edema 2/2 rapid lowering of glucose


# Carcinoid Syndrome

49 yo F with wheezing and flushing. PE +ve for tricuspid regurgitation and pulmonic stenosis -> carcinoid syndrome
"Be FDR" = bronchospasm + flushing + diarrhea + R-sided heart lesions
R-sided heart lesions = "TIPS" = tricuspid insufficiency + pulmonary stenosis
Why R-sided heart lesions? Lungs metabolize serotonin
Dx? Urine 5-HIAA (metabolite of serotonin)
NBSIM if positive 5-HIAA? Abdominal CT
NBSIM if no tumor found on CT? Octreotide scintigraphy
Tx? Octreotide to reduce sxs
Note: Only have symptoms if tumor metastasizes to liver (liver metabolizes serotonin). No symptoms if carcinoid tumor is localized to GI tract (e.g. appendix)

Vit deficiency a/w carcinoid syndrome? Niacin (B3) deficiency -> pellagra (diarrhea, dermatitis + dementia + death)
Pathophys? Tryptophan = precursor to serotonin and niacin. Carcinoid tumors use up tryptophan for serotonin -> niacin deficiency

Thyroid tumor that causes diarrhea? Medullary thyroid cancer (calcitonin causes diarrhea)


# Iron Deficiency Anemia & Plummer-Vinson

61 yo M + exertional dyspnea + Hct of 27%. NBSIM? Colonoscopy to r/o colon cancer
Iron studies? Decreased ferritin + increased TIBC + decreased transferrin saturation
When is transfusion indicated? Hgb < 7

61 yo M + similar presentation + difficulty swallowing -> Plummer-Vinson syndrome
Triad = IDA + esophageal webs + atrophic glossitis (smooth red tongue)

# Heart Blocks

Uniformly prolonged PR interval without dropped QRS = 1st degree AV block. Tx? Nothing!

Progressive PR prolongation + dropped QRS = Wenckebach aka Type I 2nd degree AV Block
"Longer longer longer drop -> Wenckebach". Tx? Nothing!

Fixed PR prolongation + dropped QRS -> Type II 2nd degree AV Block aka Mobitz II

Constant P-P interval + constant R-R interval + no relation b/w P waves & QRS -> 3rd degree AV Block

Heart blocks that require a pacemaker: Type II 2nd degree AV Block, 3rd degree AV Block

Acute mgmt for decompensated pt w/ heart block? Transcutaneous or transvenous pacing + atropine

Contraindicated medications in heart block: Beta blockers, CCB (diltiazem/verapamil), Digoxin (d/t muscarinic agonist activity)


# Acromegaly

Reduced EF in patient with coarse facial features and enlarging fingers -> acromegaly
Diagnostic algorithm:
1. Check IGF-1
2. Glucose suppression test (normal: glucose suppresses GH release; failure to suppress -> acromegaly)
3. MRI
Tx? Transsphenoidal surgery (differs from prolactinoma which is usually managed medically)
Tx prior to surgery? Octreotide, dopamine agonist (bromocriptine/cabergoline), Pegvisomant (GH receptor antagonist)
MCC of death in acromegaly? HF


# Achalasia & Chagas

Dysphagia to solids and liquids in patient with thick/thin blood smears (Giemsa stain) revealing motile parasites -> Achalasia 2/2 Chagas disease
Microbe? Trypanosoma cruzi
Dx? "BME": 1st Barium swallow, 2nd Manometry, 3rd Endoscopy
Imaging finding? +Bird's beak sign
Tx? Dihydropyridine CCB, Botox injection, Balloon dilation (AE: esophageal rupture), Heller myotomy (cut LES muscles)
Manifestations of T. cruzi: Achalasia (megaesophagus), Dilated cardiomyopathy, Destruction of myenteric plexus (megacolon)


# Anemia of Chronic Disease

Reduced MCV in a patient with a long history of untreated rheumatoid arthritis -> anemia of chronic disease
Iron studies: Ferritin high, TIBC low, Transferrin sat low (iron locked away in macrophages)
Pathophys? Hepcidin -> iron sequestration by bone marrow macrophages & reduced iron export by enterocytes


# Toxic Shock Syndrome

25 yo M with nasal packing presents with BP 65/40, elevated Cr, respiratory distress, T of 104, and marginally elevated troponins -> Toxic shock syndrome
Pathophys? Superantigen production
Bugs? Staph aureus + Strep pyogenes
Tx? Fluids (+/- pressors) + abx


# CKD Sequelae

Acid-base anomalies: Metabolic acidosis 2/2 H+ retention (H+ pump in alpha-intercalated cell)

Electrolyte anomalies:
- Hyperkalemia: kidneys not excreting K+ (normally via ROMK channel)
- Hypocalcemia: Low 1-alpha hydroxylase -> cannot make active Vit D -> can't absorb Ca+ and phos from gut
- Hyperphosphatemia: Hypocalcemia -> 2ary hyperPTH -> BUT can't excrete phos because kidneys aren't working
- Contrast with liver disease: Can't make calcidiol -> 2ary hyperparathyroidism -> hypercalcemia & hypophosphatemia (kidneys functional)

Hematologic anomalies: Hgb low (kidneys not producing erythropoietin)

Manifestations of uremia: Pericarditis, Platelet dysfunction (Tx: Desmopressin; note: ADH analog so can cause hyponatremia 2/2 SIADH), AMS

Tx:
- Hyperphosphatemia -> phosphate binders (sevelamer)
- Hypocalcemia -> calcitriol (NOT calcidiol, because they can't convert calcidiol to calcitriol)
- Preserve kidney function in diabetic nephropathy? ACE-I

Indications for dialysis: AEIOU
- A = acidosis, E = electrolyte problems, I = ingestion, O = overload (fluid), U = uremia


# Osteosarcoma Risk Factors

Rb gene mutations, Paget's disease, and Teriparatide (PTH analog) increase risk of osteosarcoma
Associated radiological features: Codman's triangle, Sunburst pattern


# Asthma Step-Up Treatment

1. Albuterol
2. Albuterol + SABA
3. Albuterol + SABA + ICS
4. Albuterol + SABA + ICS + LABA (e.g. salmeterol)
5. Albuterol + SABA + ICS + LABA + oral steroids

Asthma + nasal polyps -> Aspirin Exacerbated Respiratory Disease
Pathophys? Arachidonic acid -> prostaglandins (COX) + leukotrienes (LOX). Aspirin blocks COX -> increased LOX pathway activity
Tx? Leukotriene receptor (CYSLTR1) blockers e.g. montelukast, LOX enzyme inhibitors e.g. zileuton (AE: hepatotoxic)

DDx for nasal polyps: AERD, Wegener's (GPA), CF


# SVT

Regular narrow-complex tachycardia -> SVT
Tx asymptomatic pt:
- Acute SVT: Vagal maneuvers, Adenosine
- Chronic SVT: Beta blocker, Verapamil/diltiazem
Tx HDUS pt? Synchronized cardioversion

Indications for defibrillation (unsynchronized cardioversion): Pulseless VTach, VFib

MC EKG finding in pulmonary embolism? Sinus tachycardia


# Atopic Dermatitis

18 yo F with scaly, erythematous UE lesions. Long history of allergic rhinitis -> Atopic dermatitis/eczema
CBC finding? Elevated eosinophils
Tx? Topical steroids
What if these lesions also had umbilicated vesicles -> eczema herpeticum (HSV superinfection). Tx? Acyclovir


# Hypercalcemia & Bisphosphonates

"Stones, bones, groans, and psychic overtones". PE notable for skin tenting. First step? Fluids!

Bisphosphonates indications: Osteoporosis (T-score cutoff -2.5), Hypercalcemia of malignancy, Paget's disease, Ppx if on oral steroids >3 months
AE? Esophageal irritation (prevent by sitting up 30 min after taking pill), Osteonecrosis of the jaw


# H. Pylori

Dx? Urea breath test or stool Ag or EGD with bx
Tx? Triple therapy = clarithromycin + amox + PPI. Quad therapy = metronidazole + bismuth + tetracycline + PPI ("MBTP")
RF for PUD: H. pylori infection, Chronic NSAID use


# Thalassemias

Alpha Thalassemia:
- 1 alpha gene mutation = clinically silent
- 2 alpha gene mutations = alpha-thalassemia trait (asymptomatic)
- 3 alpha gene mutations = Hb H disease (tetramers of beta). Age at presentation: Birth (alpha chains also required for HbF)
- 4 alpha gene mutations = Hb Barts (tetramers of gamma)

Beta Thalassemia:
- 1 beta gene mutation = beta-thalassemia minor. Electrophoresis: Elevated HbA2 (alpha2-delta2)
- 2 beta gene mutations = beta thalassemia major. Electrophoresis: Elevated HbA2 + elevated HbF. Age at presentation: 6 months (HbF persists until 6 months)

Blood smear for thalassemias? Target cells
Classic CBC findings in hemolytic anemias? Low haptoglobin + indirect hyperbilirubinemia
Classic "gallbladder" pathology for hemolytic anemia? Brown pigmented gallstones
What microbe causes aplastic crisis in pt's with hemolytic anemia? Parvovirus B19
Thalassemia: microcytic anemia with normal RDW (in contrast: IDA = microcytic anemia with high RDW)


# Coccidiomycosis

23 yo M with recent trip to Tucson + 2-week history of fever, ankle/knee pain, chest pain, and painful erythematous pretibial lesion -> Coccidiomycosis
Geographic association? Southwest U.S.
Derm finding? Erythema nodosum
Microscopy finding? Spherules

# Post-Infectious GN

Periorbital edema, hematuria, HTN in patient with recent cellulitis. BUN and Cr elevated -> Post-infectious GN
Pathophys? Nephritic syndrome
Ab? Anti-streptolysin-O and anti-DNase B
Type of HSR? Type III HSR
Complications of strep pharyngitis: Rheumatic fever (abx decrease risk), PSGN (abx have NO EFFECT on risk)
Complications of strep skin infection: Only PSGN


# Ewing Sarcoma

11;22 translocation + XR shows "onion-skin-like" periosteal reaction + bone bx histo shows small, round, blue cells -> Ewing's sarcoma
Tx? Actinomycin D


# Parapneumonic Effusions

Uncomplicated PNE: pH > 7.2, Glucose > 60, LDH < 2/3 ULN, NO bugs. Tx = ABX
Complicated PNE: pH < 7.2, Glucose < 60, LDH > 2/3 ULN, NO bugs. Tx = ABX + chest tube
Empyema: pH < 7.2, Glucose < 60, LDH > 2/3 ULN, YES bugs isolated. Tx = ABX + chest tube. VATS surgery if refractory


# VTach & VFib

VTach: Regular, wide-complex (constant RR interval)
Tx algorithm:
- Pulseless VTach = defibrillate (unsynchronized cardioversion)
- VTach + pulse + HDUS = synchronized cardioversion
- VTach + pulse + HDS = amiodarone

MCC of death in first 24h after MI? VFib. Tx = defibrillation (unsynchronized cardioversion)


# Pemphigus Vulgaris

45y M + oral mucosal ulcerations + positive Nikolsky sign -> Pemphigus vulgaris
"Pemphigus vulgaris is vulgar b/c it exposes what's under your skin - hence it's +Nikolsky sign"
Pathophys? Autoantibodies to desmosomes (anti-desmoglein-1/-3 Ab's)
Type HSR? Type II HSR
Diagnostic skin bx? Immunofluorescence -> intraepidermal (net-like pattern)
In contrast: IF of bullous pemphigoid shows a linear pattern
Tx? Oral steroids


# Hyperaldosteronism

1st Hyperaldosteronism (Conn Syndrome): Increased aldosterone + decreased renin + plasma aldosterone:renin ratio >20
Dx? Plasma aldosterone:renin ratio >20
Tx? Aldosterone antagonists (spironolactone, eplerenone)

2nd Hyperaldosteronism: Increased aldosterone + increased renin
Clinical scenarios:
- Young F with abdominal bruits OR resistant HTN + AV nicking -> fibromuscular dysplasia
- Elderly man with abdominal bruits -> renal artery stenosis


# IBS

Young F with abdominal pain relieved on defecation -> IBS
Rome Criteria: Recurrent abdominal pain associated with 2 of 3: related to defecation, change in stool form, change in BM frequency
Lab abnormalities? NO
IBS Classification: IBS-C (constipation) -> stool softener, IBS-D (diarrhea) -> antidiarrheal (e.g. loperamide)


# Cold vs Warm Agglutinins

Cold agglutinins = IgM. A/w Mycoplasma. Tx? Cytotoxic drug (cyclosporine, azathioprine etc)
Warm agglutinins = IgG. Tx? Splenectomy (IgG opsonizes things and splenic macrophages clear antibody-bound RBCs)

Labs in hemolytic anemia: Increased LDH + increased indirect bilirubin + decreased haptoglobin (haptoglobin binds free hemoglobin)


# Schistosomiasis

32 yo Egyptian immigrant with 2-week history of abdominal pain, hepatosplenomegaly, and hematuria. Swam in The Nile 2 months ago -> Schistosomiasis
Bug? Schistosoma haematobium
Transmission? Direct contact with water
CBC finding? Elevated eosinophils
Associated malignancy? Squamous cell carcinoma of bladder
Tx? Praziquantel


# IgA Nephropathy

Synpharyngitic glomerulonephritis aka IgA nephropathy
Presentation? 2-6 days after URI (in contrast: PIGN occurs 2-6 weeks after URI)
Complement levels in PIGN? Low complement
Tx? Steroids + ACEI
Urine casts in nephritic syndromes? RBC casts
Urine protein in nephritic syndrome? < 3.5 g/24 hrs

# Diverticulitis

69 yo M with fever + leukocytosis + LLQ pain -> Diverticulitis
Pathophys? Inflammation of diverticula
Dx? CT abdomen
Contraindicated imaging? Colonoscopy is contraindicated!

2 Tx cocktails for GI infections:
- Cipro (GNRs) + metronidazole (anaerobes)
- Metronidazole + amoxicillin + gentamicin ("MAG")
- Note: Never choose Pip-Tazo for GI infections (too broad)

Pt treated for diverticulitis presents with recurrent UTIs weeks later + UA shows air & fecal material -> Colovesical fistula
Dx? CT scan with oral and rectal contrast

Cross checked: Yes

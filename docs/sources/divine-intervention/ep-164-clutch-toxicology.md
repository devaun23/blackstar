# HYPOGLYCEMIA

Pt with hx of DM found unresponsive → likely hypoglycemia
NBSM: Administer dextrose solution OR give glucagon injection
Oral agent with highest risk of hypoglycemia: Glyburide (sulfonylureas)
Tx: Glucose (best choice), Glucagon, Octreotide

# OPIOID OVERDOSE

Pt found unresponsive with RR = 4 and bilateral pupillary miosis. ABG with pH 7.21, PaO2 55, CO2 70 → opioid overdose
NBSM: Naloxone (NOT naltrexone)

# WERNICKE'S ENCEPHALOPATHY

Pt with hx of multiple episodes of acute pancreatitis or calcified pancreas. Presents with nystagmus and trouble walking → Wernicke's encephalopathy
NBSM: IV thiamine (B1)
Triad: Confusion + ophthalmoplegia + ataxia
With amnesia + confabulation → Korsakoff's psychosis
Neuroanatomical association: Hemorrhagic infarction of mamillary bodies
Pathophys: Lack of B1 cofactor → dysfunction of transketolase enzyme (rate-limiting enzyme of nonoxidative phase of pentose phosphate pathway)
Patient populations: Alcoholics, Anorexic, Hyperemesis gravidarum, Chronic malnutrition

# CYANIDE POISONING

Pt on nitroprusside drip with metabolic acidosis (pH 7.19, bicarb 10) → cyanide poisoning
Pathophys: Lactic acidosis 2/2 anaerobic metabolism
NBSM: Hydroxocobalamin OR amyl nitrate + sodium thiosulfate
Mechanism: CN- binds to Fe3+ (oxidized state). Amyl nitrate oxidizes hemoglobin so CN- binds. Sodium thiosulfate converts CN- to thiocyanate, which can be excreted.

# TOXIC ALCOHOL POISONING

## Ethylene Glycol
Pt that consumes antifreeze + flank pain radiating to groin → oxalate nephrolithiasis 2/2 ethylene glycol poisoning
Other population with oxalate nephrolithiasis: Crohn's
NBSM: Fomepizole (alcohol dehydrogenase inhibitor)
Indications: Methanol, ethanol, or ethylene glycol poisoning

## Methanol
Lactic acidosis + visual difficulty → methanol poisoning

# BENZODIAZEPINE OVERDOSE

Respiratory depression + no pupillary findings +/- psych disorder → benzodiazepine overdose
NBSM: Flumazenil (GABA receptor antagonist)
Indications: Benzo overdose, Z drug overdose (zolpidem, zaleplon, eszopiclone)

# ANTICHOLINERGIC TOXICITY

Body temp 103F + dilated pupils + dry red skin + suprapubic fullness → anticholinergic toxicity
Causes: Atropine, TCAs (also look for wide QRS on EKG → give sodium bicarb), 1st gen antihistamines (diphenhydramine), 1st gen low potency antipsychotics, Clozapine, Belladonna plants/Jimson weed
Tx: Physostigmine (AChE inhibitor)

# CHOLINERGIC TOXIDROME

Diarrhea + miosis + sweating + lacrimation + bronchospasm → cholinergic toxidrome
Causes: Child that takes AChE pills, Organophosphate poisoning (e.g. terrorist attack with nerve gas)
Tx: Atropine (1st — block muscarinic receptors) + pralidoxime (2nd — helps regenerate AChE)

# NEUROLEPTIC MALIGNANT SYNDROME

Hx of DM with gastroparesis. Muscle rigidity + temp 105F → NMS 2/2 metoclopramide
Tx: Benzodiazepine, Dopamine agonist (e.g. cabergoline, bromocriptine)

# SEROTONIN SYNDROME

Pt on SSRI switched to MAOI. Fever + myoclonus + diarrhea → serotonin syndrome 2/2 inadequate washout time
Causes: SSRI/SNRI/TCA/MAOI, Trazodone, St. John's wort, Linezolid, Ondansetron, Triptans, Ergots, Tramadol, Ecstasy/MDMA
Which SSRI has longest half life: Fluoxetine
Tx: Benzo + Cyproheptadine (antihistamine with antiserotonergic properties)
Why diarrhea: Oversecretion of serotonin causes diarrhea
Recall carcinoid syndrome mnemonic "Be FDR": Bronchospasm, Flushing, Diarrhea, Right-sided heart lesions

# HYPERTENSIVE CRISIS (TYRAMINE + MAOI)

Pt recently started on psych drug at dinner with HA and vision changes. BP 240/120 → hypertensive crisis 2/2 tyramine consumption while on MAOI
Tx: Phentolamine (reversible alpha-1 antagonist)
General tx for hypertensive urgency/emergency: Nitroprusside, Nicardipine, Clevidipine, Labetalol, Fenoldopam
Do NOT use hydralazine for hypertensive emergency

# EXTRAPYRAMIDAL SIDE EFFECTS

## Acute Dystonia
Pt with diabetic gastroparesis has rigid UE → acute dystonia (dysfunction of nigrostriatal pathway)
Tx: Diphenhydramine, Benztropine (anticholinergic)

## Akathisia
Pt on antipsychotic feels restless → akathisia
Tx: Beta blocker

## Drug-Induced Parkinsonism
Pt on antipsychotic with cogwheel rigidity, trouble walking
Tx: Dopamine agonist (bromocriptine, cabergoline), Benztropine

## Tardive Dyskinesia
Pt on psych drug for a while with repetitive tongue movements → tardive dyskinesia
NBS: STOP the drug, switch to atypical antipsychotic

## Levodopa-Induced Psychosis
Parkinson's pt with increased carbidopa/levodopa dose, now with psychosis
NBS: Reduce dose of carbidopa/levodopa
If can't tolerate dose reduction → quetiapine (weaker antidopaminergic activity; AE: cataracts)

# SYMPATHOMIMETIC TOXIDROME

HTN + tachycardia + mydriasis → sympathomimetic toxidrome
If nasal septum perforation → cocaine toxicity
Tx for HTN: Alpha-1 blocker (phentolamine), Alpha-beta blocker (carvedilol), Benzodiazepine
Causes: Cocaine, Amphetamines, Benzo/alcohol withdrawal, Hyperthyroidism

# CO POISONING

Pt found down at home in winter with bad lactic acidosis → CO poisoning
Causes: Space heater use
Tx: Hyperbaric oxygen

# METHEMOGLOBINEMIA

Pt going to developing country on malaria ppx, now hypoxic with cyanosis → methemoglobinemia
Pathophys: Fe2+ in Hgb converted to Fe3+. O2 can't bind to Fe3+
Drug causes: Dapsone, Primaquine, TMP-SMX, Lidocaine/benzocaine, Nitrates & nitrites
Tx: Methylene blue
When do we induce methemoglobinemia: To treat cyanide poisoning

# WARFARIN TOXICITY

Pt with mitral stenosis and AFib + bloody BMs → warfarin toxicity
Manifestations: GI bleeds (duodenal hematomas/rectus sheath hematomas)
NBSM: Give four-factor PCC (prothrombin complex concentrate)
High INR but no sxs → stop warfarin + give oral Vit K

# ACETAMINOPHEN POISONING

Pt with psych disorder tried to overdose. LFTs high and Cr rising → acetaminophen poisoning
Pathophys: Production of NAPQI, toxic to hepatocytes
Tx: N-acetylcysteine to prevent hepatic damage
If within minutes → give activated charcoal
Other presentations: Hypoglycemia, Metabolic acidosis (mitochondrial poisoning → lactic acidosis), Encephalopathy

# ASPIRIN TOXICITY

Pt tried to overdose. RR = 30, PaCO2 = 20, pH 7.51, bicarb 13 → aspirin toxicity
Pathophys: Respiratory alkalosis + metabolic acidosis
Tx: Sodium bicarb (alkalinize urine → increases excretion of aspirin)
If very severe: Dialysis

# BETA BLOCKER OVERDOSE

Profound bradycardia + hypotension on antihypertensive drug → beta blocker overdose
Tx: Glucagon

# CCB OVERDOSE

Pt overdoses on CCB
NBS: Calcium-containing agent (calcium chloride, calcium gluconate)

# MAGNESIUM TOXICITY

Hyporeflexia + slow RR → Mg toxicity
NBS: Calcium gluconate

# DIGOXIN TOXICITY

Pt admitted for CHF on loop diuretic with ventricular arrhythmias → digoxin toxicity
Pathophys: Hypokalemia predisposes to digoxin toxicity
Tx: Anti-digoxin Fab fragments
Other AEs of digoxin: Yellow vision, Hyperkalemia

# REVERSAL AGENTS

Dabigatran → idarucizumab
Unfractionated heparin → protamine sulfate (does NOT treat LMWH toxicity)
Warfarin → Vit K or PCC

# MDMA (ECSTASY)

Pt partying at club took a drug. Hyperkalemia + hypocalcemia + rising Cr → MDMA & rhabdomyolysis
Other problems: Serotonin syndrome, Heat exhaustion, Psychogenic polydipsia

# MALIGNANT HYPERTHERMIA

Pt undergoing surgery gets rigid and febrile → malignant hyperthermia
NBS: Dantrolene (ryanodine receptor antagonist)
Inheritance: AD
Pathophys: Ryanodine receptor mutation → excessive Ca++ from sarcoplasmic reticulum

# CHEMOTHERAPY TOXICITY

## Propofol
Pt on ventilator gets pancreatitis → propofol (contains lipid → hypertriglyceridemia)
Contraindications: Familial hypertriglyceridemia

## MAO-B Inhibitors
Pt with Parkinson's presents with myoclonus + hyperthermia → MAO-B inhibitors

## Cyclophosphamide
Blood clots in urine → hemorrhagic cystitis 2/2 cyclophosphamide (acrolein metabolites)
Prevention: Mesna

## Cisplatin
Rising Cr and hearing difficulties → cisplatin
Prevention of renal toxicity: Amifostine
Renal + ototoxicity drugs: Aminoglycosides, Vancomycin, Ethacrynic acid

## Anthracyclines (Doxorubicin, Daunorubicin)
Breast cancer pt with SOB or PND → irreversible dilated cardiomyopathy
Prevention: Dexrazoxane (iron chelator)
HER2-positive breast cancer → reversible dilated cardiomyopathy 2/2 trastuzumab

# FOLATE DEFICIENCY IN ALCOHOLISM

Megaloblastic anemia in pt with alcoholism → folate deficiency (NOT B12 deficiency)
Folate stores don't last as long as B12 stores

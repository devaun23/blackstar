# 4 PRINCIPLES
Beneficence (act in best interest), Nonmaleficence (no harm), Autonomy, Justice (equitable care regardless of SES)

# INFORMED CONSENT
Risks + benefits + alternatives; Patient makes choice → respect it

# CLINICAL SCENARIOS

## PPE and duty to care
PPE available → cannot refuse care; No PPE → can ethically refuse

## Family/friends as patients
Cannot treat close family/friends (may not be impartial)

## Sexual relationships
Never with current patient; Break therapeutic relationship + wait 6 months
Never for psychiatrists; Never with patient's relatives

## Disability claims
Only fill what is medically appropriate; never assist with fraud

## Disclosure preferences
"How much would you like to know?" → do as patient says
Authorized delegation: patient wants only family to know

## Difficult patients
Serious illness or unstable → cannot sever relationship
Medically stable → try to repair first → if ending: mutual + 60 days written notice + provide other physician info
Transfer records only WITH patient approval

## Impaired colleague
Immediately remove from patient care → report to authority (chief → dept head → medical board)
Only report what you yourself observed (never hearsay)

## Medical errors
Always disclose + apologize + describe prevention plan; Even if no harm occurred

## Patient records & payment
Must tell results regardless of payment status

# CONFIDENTIALITY EXCEPTIONS
Transmissible disease + HCW/food prep → disclose to employer
Communicable illness (TB, meningitis) → hold patient if needed until not infectious
STI (HIV) → encourage patient to tell partner + notify health department regardless
Seizures/arrhythmias → report to DMV
Minors (<18): generally disclose to parents; EXCEPT sexual health, substance abuse, mental health

# OTHER RULES
No self-referral to facilities with financial interest
No accepting gifts from pharma/insurance to recommend products
No selling non-medically-proven products in office (exception: health equipment in rural areas)
Can be expert witness but should not accept payment for testimony
Research studies → cannot deny access based on SES
Living will always holds up over family wishes
Lucid patient refusing life-saving treatment → AMA form; can only override for danger to others
Patient wanting ventilator removed while lucid → gather more info → if persistent after counseling, then can proceed
Genetic disease + refuses to tell spouse → encourage disclosure; if child at risk, inform spouse

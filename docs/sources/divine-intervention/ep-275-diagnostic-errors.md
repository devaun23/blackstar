# DIAGNOSTIC ERRORS
MCC of malpractice claims (esp in internal medicine)
Most are due to cognitive issues

## Cognitive Error Types
Premature closure: ends workup prematurely before correct dx
Diagnostic momentum: keeps pursuing initial dx despite contradicting results
Confirmation bias: overlooks evidence pointing to other diagnoses (present with premature closure AND diagnostic momentum)
Faulty application of knowledge: knows correct approach but applies it incorrectly

## Heuristics
Availability heuristic: current diagnostics affected by memorable previous experiences (rare disease)
Anchoring heuristic: settling on dx early through initial impressions
Representativeness heuristic: classic presentation → classic disease (similar to availability but with common conditions)

## Prevention Strategies
Diagnostic timeout: re-consider diagnosis periodically (especially if pt not responding to tx)
Admit mistakes
Worst case scenario medicine: consider most life-threatening dx first (especially in EM)
Systematic checklists
Pathophysiology-based thinking ("ask why")
Bayes theorem: always consider pre-test probability before ordering tests
Low-risk patient → D-dimer for PE; High-risk patient → skip to CTPA
Ask what features DON'T fit the diagnosis

## Systems factors
High volume/turnover → diagnostic errors
High volume of tests ≠ high value care → increases cost

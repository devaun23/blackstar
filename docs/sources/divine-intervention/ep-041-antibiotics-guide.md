# MENINGITIS

## CSF Findings Summary

| Type | Opening Pressure | Cell Type | Protein | Glucose |
|------|-----------------|-----------|---------|---------|
| Bacterial | Elevated | PMNs | Elevated | Low |
| Fungal/TB | Elevated | Lymphocytes | Elevated | Low |
| Viral | Normal or elevated | Lymphocytes | Normal or elevated | Normal |

## Neonatal Meningitis
Neonate with nuchal rigidity → GBS meningitis
Microbe: Group B Strep (Strep agalactiae) — #1 cause bacterial meningitis in first 28 days of life
Tx: "CVA" = Cefotaxime + Vancomycin + Ampicillin
Cefotaxime covers GBS/Strep pneumo/Neisseria
Vancomycin covers MRSA
Ampicillin covers Listeria (Listeria is ~100% fatal if untreated)
Note: Do NOT use ceftriaxone in neonates (causes biliary sludge)
Alternate Tx: Ampicillin + gentamicin (not as common anymore)

## Elderly Meningitis (>50 yo)
Tx: "CVA" = Ceftriaxone + Vancomycin + Ampicillin
Elderly pts need standard meningitis tx plus Listeria coverage

## Adult Meningitis (18-65 yo)
MCC meningitis in adults 18-65 yo: Strep pneumo
33 yo F with no relevant PMH + nuchal rigidity + headache + fever → Strep pneumo meningitis
Tx: Ceftriaxone + Vancomycin + Steroids ("CVS")
LP shows gram-positive diplococci + neutrophil predominance → Strep pneumo
IV dexamethasone reduces morbidity & mortality of Strep pneumo meningitis — give before abx

## Hospital-Acquired/Post-Neurosurgical Meningitis
Microbes: Pseudomonas and MRSA
Tx: Ceftazidime or cefepime + vancomycin

## Cryptococcal Meningitis
HIV pt with severe headache + nuchal rigidity + papilledema → Cryptococcal meningitis
LP findings: Elevated WBC with lymphocyte predominance + low glucose + high protein
LP opening pressure: Significantly elevated (Cryptococcus occludes CSF flow)
Dx: CSF antigen test (highly sensitive and specific), Latex agglutination assay or India ink stain
Tx: (Liposomal) Amphotericin B + flucytosine
MOA amphotericin B: Binds ergosterol → makes holes in fungal membrane
MOA flucytosine: Inhibits thymidylate synthase
Maintenance therapy: Fluconazole until symptoms resolve + CD4 >100 for >1yr

## Meningitis in HIV+ Patient
Tx: "CVA" = Ceftriaxone + Vanc + Ampicillin (same tx as elderly pts)

# HIV OPPORTUNISTIC INFECTIONS

| Condition | CD4 Threshold | PPx | Tx |
|-----------|--------------|-----|-----|
| Coccidioidomycosis | < 250 (lives in AZ) | Itraconazole | — |
| P. jirovecii | < 200 | TMP-SMX or pentamidine or dapsone or atovaquone | TMP-SMX + dapsone |
| Histoplasmosis | < 150 (lives in KY) | Itraconazole | IV liposomal amphotericin B |
| Toxoplasmosis | < 100 | TMP-SMX | Sulfadiazine + pyrimethamine |
| MAC Complex | < 50 | None (per UW: no longer given d/t low risk, resistance, cost) | Azithromycin + ethambutol |

MOA of TMP: Inhibits dihydrofolate reductase
MOA of SMX: Inhibits dihydrofolate synthetase
MAC maintenance therapy: Continue tx for >1yr and CD4 >100 for >6mo

## Neutropenic Fever
Pt on chemo + fever + WBC 2000 → neutropenic fever
Microbes: Pseudomonas + MRSA
Tx: Ceftazidime (or Cefepime) + Vancomycin

## Toxoplasmosis
HIV + seizures + MRI shows multiple ring-enhancing lesions → toxoplasmosis
Tx: Sulfadiazine + pyrimethamine (+steroids if pt has papilledema or AMS)
"It's TOXic to STeal and Pyrate"
NBSIM for toxo + increased ICP: Add steroids!

## Neurocysticercosis
Tx for seizures due to neurocysticercosis: Phenytoin (anti-epileptics)
Do NOT give anti-helminth drugs (will cause fatal inflammatory response)

## HSV Encephalitis/Meningitis
HIV + headache + nuchal rigidity. LP positive for CSF with 500 RBCs. MRI: hyperintense lesions in temporal lobe → HSV encephalitis
Tx: IV Acyclovir or foscarnet

## CSF RBC Differentiation
HSV encephalitis/meningitis: RBCs in hundreds but < 1000
Subarachnoid hemorrhage: RBCs in thousands (4000-5000)

## TB Meningitis
HIV + headache + nuchal rigidity. LP shows lymphocytic pleocytosis. Imaging shows enhancement at base of brain (or basilar cisterns) → TB meningitis
Tx: "RIPE"
Rifampin — AE: red urine; MOA: RNA polymerase inhibitor
Isoniazid — AE: drug-induced lupus; supplement B6 to prevent neuropathy
Pyrazinamide
Ethambutol — AE: ocular/color blindness

# SKIN AND SOFT TISSUE INFECTIONS

## Cellulitis
Pt with tender lesion on lower extremity. PEx reveals blanching erythematous lesion on calf → cellulitis
MCC: Staph aureus
Tx: Clindamycin or TMP-SMX or Cephalexin

## DDx: Cellulitis vs Erysipelas vs Folliculitis
Cellulitis: blanching borders + lower extremities + normal vitals
Erysipelas: well-circumscribed + elevated + face + abnormal vitals (MCC: Strep pyogenes)
Folliculitis: infection of hair follicles (MCC: Staph aureus; recent hot-tub → Pseudomonas)

## Animal/Human Bites
Empiric Tx: Amox-Clav

# PNEUMONIA TREATMENT

## Community-Acquired PNA
Empiric Tx: "M or D (medical doctor)" = Macrolide (Azithromycin) or Doxycycline

## Hospital-Acquired PNA
Empiric Tx: "M or D-c (certified medical doctor)" = Macrolide or Doxycycline + Ceftriaxone
If pt is PCN allergic: FQ monotherapy (moxifloxacin or levofloxacin)
HAP + CXR with cavitary infiltrate: MRSA → Clindamycin or Vanc or Linezolid

# LYME DISEASE

7 yo M with bullseye rash → Tx: Amoxicillin (always give amoxicillin in children <8 yo — NO doxycycline!)
30 yo F with bullseye rash → Tx: Doxycycline
35 yo pregnant female with bullseye rash → Tx: Amoxicillin (same as kids!)
Dx: ELISA then confirm with Western Blot (needed for early disseminated or late Lyme)
Western blot without ELISA results in high false-positive rates
Meningitis or chest pain or myocarditis + history of bullseye rash → Tx: IV ceftriaxone (needed for advanced Lyme)

# ROCKY MOUNTAIN SPOTTED FEVER

Pt from North Carolina + severe headache + T of 104 + rash on palms and soles → RMSF
Microbe: Rickettsia rickettsii (+ve Weil-Felix test)
Tx: Doxycycline (even < 8 yo, even non-pregnant — d/t high mortality if untreated)
Pregnant female: Chloramphenicol (essentially only time it will be the right answer; AE: Gray-baby syndrome)

# URINARY TRACT INFECTIONS

## Cystitis
23 yo non-pregnant woman + suprapubic pain + urinary frequency + urgency + burning → cystitis
MCC: E. coli
Tx: Nitrofurantoin or ciprofloxacin or TMP-SMX or Fosfomycin
Pregnant woman: Nitrofurantoin or Fosfomycin (do not give cipro or TMP-SMX — teratogenic)

## Pyelonephritis
Pt with CVA tenderness + dysuria → pyelonephritis
Tx: Ceftriaxone or fluoroquinolone (do not give FQ to pregnant patient!)

# OBSTETRIC INFECTIONS

## Endometritis
31 yo F + fever + uterine tenderness 2 days after C-section → endometritis
Tx: Clindamycin + Gentamicin ("ECG" = Endometritis Clindamycin Gentamicin)

## Chorioamnionitis
33 yo F in active labor + T 102 + fetal HR 190 bpm → chorioamnionitis (maternal fever + fetal tachycardia)
Tx: Ampicillin + Gentamicin ("CAG" = Chorioamnionitis Ampicillin Gentamicin)

# TUBERCULOSIS

## Latent TB
Isoniazid for 9 months + B6; or Rifampin for 4 months

## Active TB
RIPE + B6

# PELVIC INFLAMMATORY DISEASE

21 yo sexually active F + vaginal discharge OR cervical motion + adnexal tenderness → PID
Microbes: Chlamydia + Gonorrhea
Tx: Azithromycin (or Doxy) + Ceftriaxone
If only Chlamydia: Azithromycin or Doxy
If only Gonorrhea: Azithromycin (or Doxy) + Ceftriaxone (cover both even if only Gonorrhea detected)
RUQ pain + history of PID → Perihepatitis aka Fitz-Hugh-Curtis syndrome

# VAGINITIS

## Gardnerella Vaginosis (BV)
Off-white/gray discharge with fishy odor
Labs: pH > 4.5, Clue cells
Tx: Metronidazole or clindamycin

## Trichomonas Vaginosis
Frothy yellow-green malodorous discharge
Labs: pH > 4.5, Motile trichomonads
Tx: Metronidazole for patient + partner

## Candida Vaginosis
Thick cottage-cheese discharge
Labs: pH < 4.5, Pseudohyphae (grows as germ tubes at 37C)
Tx: "-azoles" (fluconazole)

## Systemic Candidemia
Tx: Echinocandins ("-fungin") or Ampho B (e.g. caspofungin, micafungin, anidulafungin)
Oral candidiasis Tx: Nystatin swish-and-swallow

# FUNGAL INFECTIONS

## Aspergillus
Pt on CTx + neutropenia + eosinophilia + cough + hemoptysis → Aspergillus
Pt on CTx + non-resolving PNA despite standard tx → Aspergillus
Histo: Acute angles of 45 degrees
Tx: Voriconazole

## Mucor/Rhizopus
Pt with uncontrolled DM + glucose in hundreds + facial pain → Mucor or Rhizopus
Histo: Budding at 90 degree angles
Tx: Surgical debridement + liposomal Amp B

## Allergic Bronchopulmonary Aspergillosis
Tx: Oral steroids (+ itraconazole if chronic/recurrent)

## Endemic Fungi
Coccidioidomycosis → Itraconazole
Histoplasmosis → Itraconazole
Blastomycosis → Itraconazole
Sporotrichosis (Sporothrix schenckii) → Itraconazole (classic: rose gardener with lymphadenopathy along lymph node chain)

## Tinea
Tinea corporis → topical antifungal ("-azole")
Tinea capitis and tinea unguium → terbinafine or griseofulvin (oral antifungals)
Microbes: Trichophyton, Microsporum, Epidermophyton
All invasive/systemic fungal infections → Amphotericin B

# SYPHILIS

General treatment for all stages: Penicillin G IM
PCN allergic: Macrolide (AZT) or doxycycline
PCN allergic + pregnant or neurosyphilis: Desensitization then penicillin!

| Stage | Tx | PCN-allergic |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| Primary | PCN G IM x1 dose | Doxycycline x14d |
| Secondary | PCN G IM x1 dose | Doxycycline x14d |
| Latent (asx) | PCN G IM x3 doses | Doxycycline x28d |
| Tertiary | PCN G IM x14 days | Ceftriaxone x14d |

# GI INFECTIONS

Tx for diverticulitis, appendicitis, cholecystitis:
"MAG" = Metronidazole + Amoxicillin + Gentamicin
"MC" = Metronidazole + Cipro
"MAG-MC" ("Mad EmCee")

Tx of gastroenteritis (Salmonella, Shigella): Fluoroquinolone or macrolide

## Metronidazole Mnemonic: "GET GAP"
Giardia — foul-smelling diarrhea after drinking from a stream
Entamoeba — bloody diarrhea + liver abscess
Trichomonas — malodorous yellow-green discharge + strawberry cervix
Gardnerella — fishy-smelling gray discharge + clue cells
Anaerobes — why metronidazole is used in GI infections!
Protozoa

# MALARIA

Chloroquine — tons of resistance
Mefloquine — most common PPx
Atovaquone + Proguanil
Artemether + lumefantrine
Primaquine — kills hypnozoites (latent malaria)
NBSIM before prescribing primaquine: Test for G6PD deficiency
Microbes a/w hypnozoites: Plasmodium vivax and Plasmodium ovale

# HIV DRUG SIDE EFFECTS

AE pancreatitis → Didanosine or Zidovudine
AE fat lipodystrophy → Ritonavir/Protease inhibitors (redistribution from extremities to central trunk)
Bactrim with hemolytic anemia → G6PD
AE vivid dreams and teratogens → Efavirenz and Delavirdine
AE fatal hypersensitivity reaction → Abacavir
NBSIM before starting abacavir: Check for HLA*B5701 mutation

## Bartonella
HIV pt with bacillary angiomatosis (Bartonella henselae) → Tx: Macrolide or doxycycline
Add rifampin for severe infections

# EMPIRIC TREATMENT QUICK REFERENCE

Post-antibiotic diarrhea → C. difficile
Prevention: Hand washing
Tx: Metronidazole or vancomycin or fidaxomicin (Vancomycin now first-line)

Bloody diarrhea + liver abscess → Entamoeba histolytica (only HY protozoa a/w bloody diarrhea)
Tx: Metronidazole

Watery diarrhea in AIDS pt → Cryptosporidium parvum (acid-fast positive)
Tx: Nitazoxanide

Diarrhea in "post-transplant" pt → CMV colitis
Tx: Ganciclovir

Kidney infection in post-kidney transplant patient → BK virus (polyomavirus)

# PCP PNEUMONIA

HIV + bilateral interstitial infiltrates on CXR → PCP
Tx: TMP-SMX or IV pentamidine

With A-a gradient >35 or PaO2 <70 or SaO2 <92%:
Tx: TMP-SMX + steroids (or IV pentamidine + steroids)
Note: pt will have hypoxia + respiratory alkalosis

3 populations at risk for PCP: HIV pts, chronic steroids (non-HIV), immunosuppressants s/p solid organ transplant

# INFLUENZA

Sxs > 48h: Supportive care
Sxs < 48h: Oseltamivir or zanamivir

# MASTITIS vs BREAST ENGORGEMENT

Post-partum + fever + unilateral breast tenderness → mastitis (S. aureus → nafcillin or dicloxacillin)
Post-partum + fever + bilateral breast tenderness → breast engorgement (continue breastfeeding)

# HIV REGIMEN

General: 2 NRTIs + 1 other drug (integrase inhibitor "-tegra-", NNRTI, or protease inhibitor "-navir")

# MISCELLANEOUS

Conjunctival injection after returning from Hawaii → Leptospirosis (Tx: doxycycline; Transmission: animal urine in water)
RMSF: rash on palms and soles with thrombocytopenia → think Anaplasmosis, Ehrlichiosis, Rickettsia
Tx for Legionella: Macrolide or fluoroquinolone
H. pylori: Triple therapy "CAP" = Clarithromycin + Amoxicillin + PPI; Quad therapy "MBTP" = Metronidazole + Bismuth + Tetracycline + PPI
Anthrax: Spore-forming rods in chains, "medusa head" colonies; Cutaneous: black eschar; Pulmonary: widened mediastinum; Tx: doxycycline or FQ

# MRSA COVERAGE

Vancomycin, Daptomycin, Ceftaroline (5th gen), TMP-SMX, Clindamycin (a/w C. diff), Linezolid (50S inhibitor, weak MAOI → serotonin syndrome risk), Streptogramins (dalfopristin/quinupristin)
MC microbe in osteomyelitis: MRSA

# PSEUDOMONAS COVERAGE

Ceftazidime (3rd gen), Cefepime (4th gen), Cipro, Aminoglycosides, Carbapenems (exception: ertapenem does NOT cover Pseudomonas), Aztreonam (monobactam), Piperacillin-Tazobactam
CF pts > 20 yo: inhaled tobramycin for chronic Pseudomonas
CF pts < 20 yo: Staph aureus

# MISCELLANEOUS INFECTIONS

Pertussis (violent cough + subconjunctival hemorrhage or post-tussive vomiting):
Tx: Macrolides (erythromycin also used for gastroparesis)
PPx for close contacts: Macrolide

PPx for close contacts of meningitis: Rifampin or Ceftriaxone or Cipro

Acute otitis media: Amoxicillin +/- Clavulanic acid
Acute otitis externa: FQ ear drop (Pseudomonas)
Contact lens wearer + conjunctivitis or corneal ulcer: Pseudomonas → FQ drops

Measles: Vitamin A supplementation
Croup: Racemic epinephrine (Parainfluenza/paramyxovirus)

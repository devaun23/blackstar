# EATING DISORDERS

## Anorexia Nervosa
Pt BMI = 16 + vomiting + hypotensive & HDUS → anorexia
NBS: Admit (even involuntarily)
MUST be underweight (BMI < 18.5) to make dx
Electrolyte abnormalities:
Hypochloremic (vomiting)
Hyponatremic (loss of electrolyte-rich fluid & hypovolemia → nonosmotic ADH release → free water reabsorption)
Hypokalemia (RAAS activation)
Alkalotic (aldosterone effect on alpha-intercalated cell)
If comorbid depression: Consider mirtazapine

## Bulimia Nervosa
Binge + compensatory behavior (vomit, work out) + BMI normal → bulimia nervosa

## Binge Eating Disorder
Binge but no compensatory behavior → binge eating disorder

# COCAINE INTOXICATION

BP 210/120 + pupillary mydriasis → cocaine intoxication
Pathophys: Sympathomimetic (alpha-1 agonist → increased SVR → increased BP)
Tx: Benzodiazepine, Alpha + beta blocker (carvedilol), Alpha-1 antagonist (phentolamine)
What drug should NOT be given: Beta blockers (will lead to unopposed alpha)

## Cocaine vs Methamphetamine
Both sympathomimetics with pupillary mydriasis
Meth → milder BP elevation, poor dentition, + prominent hallucinations

# TYRAMINE + MAO-B CRISIS

Pt with hx Parkinson's consumes lots of cheese, wine, smoked meats → hypertensive crisis
Combination of tyramine in food & MAO-B inhibitors (selegiline)

# SEROTONIN SYNDROME

Pt with depression has MRSA infection + fever + myoclonus + hyperreflexia → serotonin syndrome
Antidepressant + MAO-I activity of linezolid → serotonin syndrome
If suspected, diagnostic metabolite: Urine 5-HIAA

# MEDICATION-INDUCED PSYCHOSIS IN PARKINSON'S

70 yo M with Parkinson's, meds increased, now acting weird → medication-induced psychosis
Pathophys: Pro-dopaminergic effects of Parkinson's meds
NBS:
1st: Try to lower dose of drug
If Parkinson's sxs come back → put back on controlling dose and add quetiapine

# ADJUSTMENT DISORDER

Sxs of MDD/GAD but doesn't meet criteria (# of sxs or time) + recent stressor → adjustment disorder

# ACUTE STRESS DISORDER vs PTSD

Veteran with nightmares, flashbacks for 2 weeks → acute stress disorder
Sxs for < 1 month = acute stress disorder
Sxs for > 1 month = PTSD
Tx for general sxs: SSRI
Tx for nightmares: Prazosin

# PANIC DISORDER

22 yo M with episodes of tremulousness, sweating, dry mouth, feeling of loss of control → panic disorder
Panic attack = one episode
Panic disorder = multiple episodes + fear of having another one
Tx: SSRI

# BIPOLAR DISORDER

Acute manic episode NBSM: Lithium + antipsychotic
If poorly controlled after 6-7 weeks on lithium → add atypical antipsychotic (e.g. lithium + quetiapine)
Lithium adverse effects: Tremors, Hypothyroidism, Nephrogenic DI (Tx: amiloride or triamterene)

# ALZHEIMER'S DISEASE

41 yo M with Trisomy 21 + forgetting way home + 15/30 MMSE → early-onset Alzheimer's
NT: Low ACh
Neuroanatomical association: Basal nucleus of Meynert
Deficiency of what enzyme: Choline acetyltransferase (rate-limiting enzyme in ACh synthesis)

## Congenital Myasthenic Syndrome
Similar to MG but doesn't improve with AChE inhibitor (edrophonium/"Tensilon test")
Pathophys: Choline acetyltransferase deficiency

# LEWY BODY DEMENTIA

Parkinsonism + visual hallucinations + syncopal episodes → Lewy Body dementia
Dementia BEFORE Parkinsonian sxs (contrast with Parkinson's disease dementia)

# PARKINSON'S DISEASE

Cogwheel rigidity + wide gait + bradykinesia → Parkinson's
NT: Low dopamine

# INH-INDUCED SEIZURES

Pt treated with 9 months INH for latent TB starts having seizures
Pathophys: INH w/o B6 (pyridoxine) → decreased activity of glutamate decarboxylase (converts glutamate to GABA) → high glutamate, low GABA → seizures

# NEUROTRANSMITTER ASSOCIATIONS

MDD: Low serotonin, Low norepinephrine, Low dopamine
Pheochromocytoma: High catecholamines, High urine metanephrines
Huntington's: Low GABA, Low ACh, High dopamine → Tx: Tetrabenazine

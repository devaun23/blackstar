# LOCALIZE THE LESION

Trouble forming new memories (anterograde amnesia) → hippocampus (temporal lobe)
Non-fluent aphasia → Broca's area (inferior frontal gyrus) of dominant hemisphere (supplied by MCA)
Fluent aphasia → Wernicke's area of dominant hemisphere (supplied by MCA)
Neglecting one side of the world → non-dominant parietal lobe lesion (in most people: L sided neglect)
Agraphia, acalculia, finger agnosia, left-right disorientation → Gerstmann syndrome → dominant parietal lobe lesion (+ fluent aphasia)
Cortical blindness → primary visual cortex in occipital lobe → contralateral homonymous hemianopia
Bitemporal hemianopsia → optic chiasm compression (e.g. prolactinoma, craniopharyngioma)

## Eye deviation
Toward side of lesion → frontal eye field
Away from side of lesion → PPRF lesion

## Frontal lobe
Executive dysfunction + disinhibition + contralateral UMN signs (Babinski, pronator drift, hyperreflexia)
Can have reemergence of primitive reflexes
Brain tumor — calcified w/ fried egg appearance → oligodendroglioma

## Basal ganglia & related
Tremors → basal ganglia problem
Loss of all motor function on one side → posterior limb of internal capsule (likely rupture of Charcot-Bouchard microaneurysm of lenticulostriate artery — HTN is #1 RF)
Parkinsonian sxs → substantia nigra
Causes in young person? MPTP contamination, Wilson's disease (movement sxs + psych sxs + liver problems — copper deposits in basal ganglia)
Huntington's disease → atrophy of caudate
Flailing movements of one arm (hemiballismus) → contralateral subthalamic nucleus

## Brainstem
Person cannot be aroused → reticular activating system (midbrain)
Decorticate posturing → problems above level of red nucleus (think CORTEX with deCORTicate)
Decerebrate posturing → problems below level of red nucleus
Loss of all motor function, can only wink eyes (locked-in syndrome) → pons (basilar artery stroke)

## Osmotic demyelination syndrome
2/2 rapid correction of hyponatremia → pons
"From low to high, the pons will die. From high to low, the brain will blow"

## Other
Vertical diplopia → Parinaud's syndrome → compression of superior colliculus (often due to pinealoma)
Truncal ataxia → cerebellar vermis lesion (medulloblastoma, pilocytic astrocytoma)
Limb ataxia → IPSILATERAL cerebellar hemisphere lesion

## Hydrocephalus
Obstructive/non-communicating: MC location of obstruction? Cerebral aqueduct of Sylvius
Communicating: arachnoid granulations (where CSF is reabsorbed) — causes: meningitis, malignancy
Schizophrenia → enlarged lateral ventricles
Alzheimer's → basal nucleus of Meynert (or CHAT enzyme dysfunction)

# ABSENCE SEIZURES

Child not doing well in school, teacher observes staring into space with no awareness → absence seizures
EEG pattern: 3 Hz spike and wave
Tx: Ethosuximide (T-type Ca++ channel blocker)

# VESTIBULAR DISORDERS

## Meniere's Disease
55 yo F with 6 mo history of room spinning (3 days at a time) + difficulty hearing + "ringing sound" in ear → Meniere's disease
Triad: vertigo + tinnitus + sensorineural hearing loss
Pathophys: Endolymphatic hydrops, poor reabsorption of endolymph → high pressures
Tx:
Dietary Na+ reduction
For vertiginous sxs: scopolamine (anticholinergic), meclizine (antihistamine/anticholinergic), diphenhydramine (antihistamine/anticholinergic)
Diuretics
Definitive tx: Gentamicin injection to ablate CN8
Causes of permanent sensorineural hearing loss

## BPPV
Vertigo triggered by changes in position, NO hearing loss → BPPV
Pathophys: Otoconia/otoliths in semicircular canals
Dx: Dix-Hallpike maneuver
Tx: Epley maneuver

## Vestibular Neuritis
Recent viral URI + constant non-positional vertigo, lasts days to weeks before spontaneous resolution, NO hearing loss → vestibular neuritis

## Labyrinthitis
Recent viral URI + constant vertigo + hearing loss → labyrinthitis

# TRAUMATIC BRAIN INJURY

## Epidural Hematoma
Hit in the head with a bat, passed out for < 30s, finished the game, now severe headache and somnolence → epidural hematoma
Pathophys: Fracture of frontal bone → tearing of middle meningeal artery
Dx: Non-con head CT → lens-shaped hematoma
Do NOT perform LP (increased ICP will cause herniation)
Tx: Neurosurgical evacuation, reduce ICP
Elevate head of bed
Hyperventilate pt (CO2 down → cerebral vasoconstriction) — quickest means of reducing ICP acutely
Mannitol

## Subdural Hematoma
Old person on warfarin with multiple falls OR alcoholic → subdural hematoma
Pathophys: Brains shrink with aging/alcoholism → stretches bridging veins → greater susceptibility to shearing
Dx: Non-con head CT → crescent-shaped
Brain bleed in shaken baby → subdural hematoma

# HYPERTHERMIA SYNDROMES

## Malignant Hyperthermia
Super tense muscles and T of 105 after intubation → malignant hyperthermia
Pathophys: Mutation in ryanodine receptor → increased Ca++ release from sarcoplasmic reticulum → hypercontractile state generating heat
Inheritance: AD
Tx: Dantrolene (ryanodine receptor antagonist)
Electrolyte disturbance: Hyperkalemia → peaked T waves → wide QRS → sinusoidal pattern
Hyperkalemia Tx: 1st calcium gluconate, then insulin/glucose, albuterol, or sodium bicarb, Kayexalate, Furosemide
Why elevated Cr: Rhabdomyolysis → myoglobin release → damages kidney → Tx: IVF

## Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome
Same presentation after starting fluphenazine (1st gen high-potency antipsychotic) → NMS
Tx: Dantrolene + Dopamine agonist (bromocriptine/cabergoline, amantadine)

## Serotonin Syndrome
Hx of depression + tx for S. aureus bacteremia (or migraines) + high fevers + diarrhea + clonus → serotonin syndrome
Triggering drugs: Any antidepressant, Linezolid, Ondansetron, Triptans, MAO-B, MDMA/ecstasy
Tx: Cyproheptadine (antihistamine with powerful serotonin receptor blocking activity) + benzo

# WITHDRAWAL AND TOXICITY

## Alcohol Withdrawal
Tremors 6 hrs after successful completion of AAA repair in a business executive → alcohol withdrawal
Ppx: Long-acting benzo (chlordiazepoxide, diazepam)

## Lithium Toxicity
Tremors after starting indapamide (thiazide-like diuretic) in patient with bipolar disorder → lithium toxicity
Pathophys: Diuretics can raise lithium levels → lithium toxicity (manifests as tremors)
Causes: Anything that causes RAAS activation (aldosterone acts on principal cell to increase Na+ reabsorption → Li+ can also be reabsorbed through this channel)

# HYDROCEPHALUS

## Obstructive Hydrocephalus in Children
Child with bulging fontanelles and somnolence → most likely region of stenosis: Cerebral aqueduct of Sylvius

## Normal Pressure Hydrocephalus
70 yo F that is forgetful, staggers into exam room, history of recurrent perianal sores from urinary incontinence → "wet wobbly wacky" → NPH
Dx: Non-con head CT with enlarged ventricles, LP → normal opening pressure
Tx: VP shunt

# TUBEROUS SCLEROSIS

Inheritance: AD
Mutated genes: TSC1 & TSC2 (tumor suppressor genes)
Mutated proteins: Hamartin & tuberin
Brain findings: Subependymal tumors (usually calcified)
Renal findings: Angiomyolipoma
Cardiac findings: Rhabdomyoma
Skin findings: Hypopigmented = ash leaf spots, Hyperpigmented = Shagreen patch
Kind of infantile seizure: West syndrome = infantile spasms
EEG findings: Hypsarrhythmia
Tx: ACTH or vigabatrin

# NEURAL TUBE DEFECTS

Mom has inconsistent condom use → delivers stillborn fetus with no brain → anencephaly 2/2 Zika
Pathophys: Anterior neuropore fails to close
Polyhydramnios because no swallowing center

## Spina Bifida Occulta
Dimple or tuft of hair in lumbosacral area → spina bifida occulta
Pathophys: Failure of spinous processes to fuse around spinal cord
Usually causes no problems

## Meningocele
Pathophys: Failure of fusion of posterior neural arch → herniation of meningeal tissue and CSF
Associated deficiency in mom: Folate
Tx: Surgery (good prognosis after surgery)

## Myelomeningocele
Pathophys: Failure of neural tube to close → herniation of meninges and spinal cord tissue through defect
Associated deficiency in mom: Folate
Tx: Surgery (worse prognosis)
Associated dz: Chiari 2 malformation

## Chiari Malformations
Type 1: Cerebellar tonsils herniate through foramen magnum → associated with syringomyelia ("Syr1ngomyelia")
Type 2: Cerebellar tonsils herniate through foramen magnum → associated with lumbosacral myelomeningocele

## Serum Markers
Elevated maternal serum AFP → neural tube defect/spinal dysraphism (also elevated AChE)
Decreased msAFP → Down syndrome

# SUBACUTE COMBINED DEGENERATION

Vitamin deficiency: B12
Loss of dorsal columns → loss of vibration, touch, proprioception
Loss of lateral corticospinal tract → UMN signs
Differentiate folate & B12 deficiency:
Megaloblastic anemia in both
Homocysteine high in both
MMA high in ONLY B12 deficiency

# NEUROCUTANEOUS DISORDERS — NF-1 AND NF-2

## NF-1
Cafe au lait spots + brown "pigments" in axilla + "tuber-like" skin growths + episodic headache and severe HTN → NF-1
Eye malignancy: Optic nerve gliomas
Iris finding: Lisch nodules
Brain malignancy: Meningioma
Neuroendocrine malignancy: Pheochromocytoma
Inheritance: AD
Gene: Neurofibromin
Chromosome: 17

## NF-2
Bilateral acoustic neuromas/vestibular schwannomas
Location: Cerebellopontine angle
Inheritance: AD
Gene: Merlin
Chromosome: 22

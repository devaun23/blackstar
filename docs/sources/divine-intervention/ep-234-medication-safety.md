# MEDICATION ERRORS & ADVERSE DRUG EVENTS

Medication error = any error between clinician prescribing and medication arriving to pt
Adverse drug event = any harm experienced as a result of taking a drug

## RF for adverse drug events
#1 = polypharmacy, Elderly, Pediatric (weight-based dosing), Low health literacy, Look-alike drugs

## Screening tools for inappropriate Rx in elderly
BEERS criteria, STOPP criteria

## 4 drugs responsible for 50% adverse drug events
Insulin, Warfarin, Antiplatelets, Opioids

## Drugs NOT to prescribe
Benzos in elderly, Opioids for dental/small surgical procedures

# MEDICATION SAFETY PATHWAY

## Clinician orders
Prescribe conservatively, Computerized order entry w/ clinical decision support, Medication reconciliation

## Pharmacist dispenses
Tall man lettering, Automated dispensing cabinets

## Nurse administers
5 Rights Rule: Right med, dose, time, route, patient
Barcode scanners, Minimize interruptions, Smart infusion pumps
IV meds more prone to error than oral
MC type of medication error: administering at wrong time

# OPIOID SAFETY
Biggest issue: overprescribing
Appropriate for: acute traumatic pain, cancer pain, SOB in cancer (morphine)
Use prescription monitoring program, short course, only when clinically warranted

# TRANSITION OF CARE
Two most likely complications of poor transition: Readmission, Adverse drug events
Measures: discharge instructions, teach back, detailed records, medication reconciliation, definitive f/u appointment, discharge checklist

## I-PASS (for handoffs)
I = Illness severity, P = Patient summary, A = Action list, S = Situation awareness (if-then), S = Synthesis by receiver (teach back)

## SBARR (for communication)
S = Situation, B = Background, A = Assessment, R = Recommendation, R = Response

# MENINGITIS

## CSF Findings Summary

| Type | Opening Pressure | Cell Type | Protein | Glucose |
|------|-----------------|-----------|---------|---------|
| Bacterial | Elevated | PMNs | Elevated | Low |
| Fungal/TB | Elevated | Lymphocytes | Elevated | Low |
| Viral | Normal or elevated | Lymphocytes | Normal or elevated | Normal |

## HSV Meningitis/Encephalitis
CSF findings: Elevated WBCs (lymphocytic predominance), lots of RBCs (but < 1000), elevated protein, normal glucose
Note: If 4000-5000 RBCs → think SAH instead
Location: Temporal lobe
Dx: HSV PCR of CSF
Tx: IV acyclovir

## Brain Abscess vs Meningitis
Young adult + fever + headache + neurologic deficits + NO nuchal rigidity → brain abscess
Headache + high fever + sudden-onset nuchal rigidity → bacterial meningitis

## Bacterial Meningitis
CSF: Elevated WBCs (neutrophilic predominance) — exception: TB meningitis is only bacterial meningitis with lymphocytic pleocytosis
HIGH protein, LOW glucose

#1 cause in children through middle-aged adults: Strep pneumo
Tx: Ceftriaxone + vancomycin + steroids

#1 cause in neonates: Group B Strep (Strep agalactiae)
Tx: Cefotaxime + vancomycin + ampicillin
Cefotaxime covers GBS, Vancomycin covers Staph aureus & resistant Strep pneumo, Ampicillin covers Listeria

Special cause in neonates & elderly only: Listeria monocytogenes → add ampicillin

## Neisseria Meningitidis
Young adult with skin petechiae → Neisseria meningitidis
Tx: Ceftriaxone
PPx for close contacts: Rifampin, ciprofloxacin, or ceftriaxone ("RCC")
Prevention: Neisseria meningitidis vaccine

## Waterhouse-Friderichsen Syndrome
Pt with Neisseria meningitidis becomes profoundly hypotensive + hypoglycemic + hyperkalemic + bleeding from mucosal sites → Waterhouse-Friderichsen syndrome
Pathophys: Adrenal hemorrhage → primary adrenal insufficiency
Cortisol impaired → hypoglycemia & hypotension
Aldosterone impaired → hyperkalemia & non-AG metabolic acidosis (can't excrete H+) → Type 4 RTA

## Populations at Risk for Neisseria Meningitis
Asplenic patients (e.g. sickle cell disease)
Terminal complement deficiency or eculizumab use (tx for PNH)
Terminal complement = C5-C9
Eculizumab = C5 inhibitor

# ELECTROLYTE EMERGENCIES

## Hyponatremic Seizures
25 yo M having seizures, began intranasal desmopressin 5 days ago → hyponatremic seizures
Pathophys: Desmopressin = ADH analog, acts on V2 receptors of principal cells → increased water reabsorption → dilutes serum Na+
Tx: Hypertonic saline (to raise serum Na+ levels)
Cannot increase Na+ levels > 12 mEq over 24 hrs

## Complications of Rapid Correction
Hyponatremia corrected too quickly → Osmotic demyelination syndrome aka central pontine myelinolysis aka "locked in" syndrome
Hypernatremia corrected too quickly → Cerebral edema → transtentorial herniation
General rule: SLOW management strategy to correct electrolyte imbalances

# SLEEP DISORDERS

## Narcolepsy
Bizarre dreams on awakening (hypnopompic hallucination) or on falling asleep (hypnagogic hallucination) + history of accidents from falling asleep while driving + sudden falls from LE weakness (cataplexy) → narcolepsy
Dx: Polysomnography (decreased sleep latency — pt goes straight into REM sleep)
Tx: Frequent naps, Modafinil (stimulant), Sodium oxybate (for cataplexy)
CSF findings: Decreased hypocretin/orexin levels

## Insomnia
Tx: Suvorexant (hypocretin receptor antagonist)

# CAVERNOUS SINUS THROMBOSIS

Severe headache + multiple extraocular muscle deficits + recent otitis media → cavernous sinus thrombosis
Pathophys: CN III, IV, VI, V1, V2 go through cavernous sinus
Note: Loss of CN VI function occurs first (lateral gaze palsy)
Ophthalmic veins anastomose with facial veins & veins that drain sinuses
MC organism: Staph aureus
Tx: IV abx + heparin

# MUCORMYCOSIS IN DKA

Severe headache + necrotic sinusitis in patient with blood glucose of 300 + pH 7.13 + HCO3- of 15 → mucormycosis in context of DKA
Tx: Debridement + amphotericin B

# PARKINSON'S DISEASE

Asymmetric resting tremor + cogwheel rigidity + bradykinesia/festinating gait + postural instability → Parkinson's disease
Associated findings: Micrographia, Orthostatic hypotension
Pathophys: Destruction of dopamine-producing cells in substantia nigra pars compacta (midbrain)
Gross pathological finding: Loss of pigmentation of substantia nigra
Histology: Lewy bodies (eosinophilic inclusions made of alpha-synuclein)

## Treatment
Start with:
NMDA receptor antagonists (e.g. amantadine — increases dopamine release in CNS)
OR Dopamine agonists (e.g. bromocriptine, ropinirole, cabergoline, pramipexole)
OR MAO-B inhibitors (e.g. rasagiline, selegiline)
OR COMT inhibitors (e.g. entacapone — acts peripherally, tolcapone — acts centrally/crosses BBB)
Last line: Levodopa-carbidopa (only works a few years then less effective due to on-off phenomenon)

Drug-induced Parkinsonism: Dopamine receptor antagonists, Metoclopramide (2 indications: antiemetic, gastroparesis)
Anticholinergic for tremors: Benztropine (muscarinic receptor antagonist)
Parkinsonism in young patient from "drug" at party: MPTP-contaminated heroin

## DDx Parkinson's vs Essential Tremor
Parkinson's: asymmetric resting tremor + improves with activity
Essential tremor: symmetric + worsens with activity + responds to propranolol

## Acute Dystonias
Caused by dopamine antagonists → Tx: Benztropine, diphenhydramine
Pathophys: Dopamine antagonists cause ACh-dopamine imbalance → unopposed ACh overactivity
Focal dystonia → Tx: Botox injection

## Psychosis in Parkinson's
Reduce dose of carbidopa-levodopa
Quetiapine (atypical antipsychotic with weak dopamine receptor antagonist activity)
MAO-B inhibitor → can cause hypertensive crisis → Tx: Phentolamine (alpha-1 blocker)

# IDIOPATHIC INTRACRANIAL HYPERTENSION

35 yo F + BMI 35 + visual difficulty + intermittent severe headaches + treated for acne → IIH
RFs: Obese female, Vit A derivatives, Tetracyclines
Ocular finding: Papilledema
Dx: CT scan (normal) + LP (elevated opening pressure >250)
Tx: Acetazolamide, Serial LPs

# HUNTINGTON'S DISEASE

41 yo M with 1 year of forgetfulness + behavioral changes. Father died at 47 with similar presentation → Huntington's disease
Pathophys: Atrophy of caudate (part of striatum)
Trinucleotide repeats: CAG
Chromosome: 4
Inheritance: AD with anticipation
Tx: Anti-dopaminergic drugs (Haloperidol, Tetrabenazine — VMAT inhibitors)

# SUBARACHNOID HEMORRHAGE

Worst headache of patient's life + bilateral flank masses → SAH in pt with ADPKD
Pathophys: Rupture of berry aneurysm in circle of Willis
MC aneurysm location: ACom aneurysm (anterior communicating artery)
Associated heart murmur: MVP
NBSIM: CT head non-contrast (blood shows up as hyperdense)
If CT negative → LP (looking for xanthochromia)
Tx: Lower pt's BP to 140/90
Drug to prevent superimposed ischemia: Nimodipine

# HEADACHES

## Cluster Headache
Sxs: Unilateral tearing/conjunctival injection/rhinorrhea, occurs for several days
Tx: 100% O2 and triptans
35 yo smoker + R-sided headache + occurs everyday at same time for past month + rhinorrhea & pupillary miosis → cluster headache

## Tension Headache
Sxs: Bilateral + high-stress environment + worse at end of day
Tx: NSAIDs

## Migraine Headache
Sxs: Unilateral + pulsatile + photophobia & phonophobia +/- aura
Abortive tx: Sumatriptan (5-HT receptor agonist) — contraindications: variant angina/Prinzmetal, CAD, CREST syndrome; can trigger serotonin syndrome
Ergotamine
PPx: Propranolol, Topiramate, TCA (contraindicated in elderly, glaucoma pts — anticholinergic mydriasis), CCBs

## Analgesic Rebound Headache
Use of analgesics at least 8-10x in last month; headache returns once analgesic wears off
Tx: Wean off analgesics

## CO Poisoning
Headache + mental status changes in patient using fireplace in winter → CO poisoning
Dx: Carboxyhemoglobin levels (pulse ox cannot distinguish oxyhemoglobin from carboxyhemoglobin)
Tx: High-flow O2 / hyperbaric O2
Imaging: Hyperintense lesions in globus pallidus on MRI

# LEISHMANIASIS
31 yo M active military returned from Afghanistan — painless purplish ulcerating papule on arm
Transmission: sandfly; Dx: skin biopsy; Tx: Amphotericin B or Paromomycin

# HERPES ZOSTER

## Zoster vaccine
Recombinant (NOT live attenuated): start at age 50
Live attenuated: given to those over 60 (immunocompetent only — NOT for HIV, CLL, immunodeficiency)

## Zoster "explosion" → screen for HIV
Sudden outbreak of molluscum contagiosum → screen for HIV
Porphyria cutanea tarda → screen for Hep C

## Zoster ophthalmicus
Vesicular rash on V1 (ophthalmic branch) + tip of nose + eye → refer to ophthalmologist

## Ramsay Hunt Syndrome (herpes zoster oticus)
Vesicles in ear + anterior taste of 2/3 tongue gone + CN7 facial paralysis
Tx: Acyclovir

## Post-herpetic neuralgia
Tx: gabapentin, nortriptyline, amitriptyline (careful in elderly)

Do NOT give steroids in herpes zoster!

# SCABIES
Young kid/homeless + itchy rash between finger webs, penis, scrotum
Sarcoptes scabiei — mites burrow in upper skin layer
Disseminated scabies: HIV/immunocompromised
Dx: KOH prep (mites and eggs)
Tx: permethrin (also family members), ivermectin; wash everything in hot water
Do NOT pick Lindane lotion (neurotoxic, induces seizures in children)

# BED BUG BITES
Grouped papules in close approximation — "Breakfast lunch and dinner lesions"
Usually in the morning; Tx: antihistamines, topical steroid

# SEBORRHEIC KERATOSIS
Brown well-demarcated plaques with "stuck-on" appearance
Tx: excision or liquid nitrogen
Sudden onset of many → screen for GI malignancy (Leser-Trelat sign)

# CONDYLOMA ACUMINATUM
Flesh-colored papules/genital warts (HPV 6,11)
Do not confuse with condyloma latum (syphilis)
Tx: topical salicylic acid, cryotherapy, podophyllin
Most likely outcome: spontaneous resolution
HPV 1 → plantar warts (verruca plantaris); distinct from genital HPV 6,11

# ACTINIC KERATOSIS
Red lesion on sun-exposed spots with rough sandpaper texture
Precursor to SCC
Tx: topical 5-FU, imiquimod
Biggest RF for skin cancer: sun exposure
UVB > UVA (thymidine-thymidine dimers)
Primary prevention: sun-protective clothing (pick sunscreen if clothing not an option)
Do NOT apply sunscreen to baby <6 months old

# SQUAMOUS CELL CARCINOMA
Usually bottom lip, scalp, ear, neck
Burns/scars that develop evolving lesions on scalp → SCC

# KERATOACANTHOMA
Red nodule rapidly growing like a volcano + keratin debris at center
Keratin = squamous malignancy; Tx: excision

# BASAL CELL CARCINOMA
Pink pearly translucent lesions with telangiectasias on upper lip
Spreads horizontally; very locally destructive
Tx: resection or Mohs (micrographic) surgery

# MELANOMA
Lesion with many colors, irregular borders

## ABCDE Criteria
A = Asymmetry, B = Borders (irregular), C = Color variation, D = Diameter >6mm, E = Evolution

## Dysplastic nevus
RF for melanoma; looks like melanoma
Family hx + many dysplastic nevi → familial melanoma dysplastic syndrome (AD)

## Melanoma subtypes
Nodular: worst prognosis
Acral lentiginous: African American, under nail bed (not as bad as nodular)
Lentigo maligna: face/upper trunk, prominently sun-exposed
Superficial spreading: best prognosis (back in men, legs in women)

## Management
Complete excision; >1mm thick → sentinel lymph node biopsy
Prognosis: Breslow depth/thickness

# URTICARIA & ANGIOEDEMA
Whealing of skin → Tx: antihistamine (cetirizine, H1 blocker)
Angioedema (C1 esterase inhibitor deficiency) → angioedema without hives or pruritus

# DRUG ALLERGY
MC reported drug allergy: penicillin
Testing: skin testing (not RAST/ELISA)
Anaphylaxis to penicillin → avoid anti-staph penicillins and all cephalosporins; aztreonam is safe alternative

# JARISCH-HERXHEIMER REACTION
Treating spirochetes (Treponema/Borrelia) with cell wall inhibitors → organisms lyse → endotoxin release
Fever, headache, myalgia, malaise, sweating, hypotension
Classic vignette: 6 yo with Lyme given doxycycline → fever, headache, myalgia, hypotension
Resolves quickly; continue antibiotic; supportive care
NOT an allergic reaction; may use same abx in future

# DRESS SYNDROME (Drug Reaction with Eosinophilia and Systemic Symptoms)
Recent drug (e.g. TMP-SMX) + edematous face + generalized rash + elevated AST/ALT + eosinophilia + elevated lymphocytes + lymphadenopathy
Type IV hypersensitivity; Tx: IVIG

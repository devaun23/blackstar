# Vitamin Deficiencies & Neurological Manifestations

## INH Toxicity
Paresthesias in pt being treated for TB -> INH toxicity
Prevention? Vit B6 (pyridoxine)

## B12 Deficiency
Pt with hx of poorly treated Crohn's disease now with paresthesias + loss of proprioception + lower extremity hyperreflexia -> B12 deficiency
Pathophys:
- Crohn's destroys the terminal ileum, where B12-IF is absorbed
- B12 deficiency -> subacute combined degeneration of spinal cord
  - Destroy dorsal columns -> loss of proprioception, vibration, and fine touch
  - Destroy lateral corticospinal tract -> UMN signs (spasticity, hyperreflexia)
Dx? Macrocytic anemia on CBC + high MMA + high homocysteine
Schilling's test: inject B12 then give radiolabeled oral B12, everything is saturated so oral B12 should go to urine -> if not, it indicates pernicious anemia or terminal ileum disease. Then give radiolabeled oral B12 + IF. If you see B12 in urine now, the problem is pernicious anemia. If not, it's probably a terminal ileum absorption issue.
D-xylose test will be abnormal
Tx? Supplementation

## Vitamin E Deficiency
Ataxia and hemolytic anemia in pt with hx abetalipoproteinemia. MRI brain shows cerebellar atrophy -> Vit E deficiency
Pathophys? Deficiency in microsomal triglyceride transfer protein necessary for creating beta-lipoproteins (ApoB48 & ApoB100), which transport fats, cholesterol, and fat-soluble vitamins from intestines to bloodstream
Peripheral blood smear finding? Acanthocytosis

## Wernicke's Syndrome
Mamillary body infarcts in a chronic alcoholic -> Wernicke's syndrome
Pathophys? Thiamine (B1) deficiency

## Niacin Deficiency / Pellagra
Dermatitis and dementia in a patient with long history of carcinoid syndrome -> pellagra -> 2/2 niacin deficiency
Pathophys? All tryptophan shunted towards serotonin production, so niacin cannot be produced
Dx of carcinoid? Urine 5-HIAA (serotonin metabolite)
Other causes of pellagra: Hartnup disease (defect in neutral amino acid transporter)


# Toxicology - Neurological

## Lead Poisoning
Child with chronic abdominal pain and foot drop who over past 6 mo has been performing poorly in school -> lead poisoning
Peripheral blood smear findings? Ringed sideroblasts
Dx? Blood lead levels. Confirmatory test: Venous lead level
Tx? Lead chelator (EDTA, succimer)

## Arsenic Poisoning
Weird lines on the fingers + garlic breath -> arsenic poisoning
Derm findings? Mees lines (horizontal white lines) on nails


# Seizures

## Absence Seizures
Child with declining grades and many staring episodes -> absence seizures
Seizure type? Generalized seizure
EEG findings? 3 Hz spike-and-wave
Tx? Ethosuximide (T-type CCB)

## Temporal Lobe Epilepsy
Seizures heralded by olfactory hallucinations and a rising sensation in the abdomen -> temporal lobe epilepsy
Seizure type? Auras = simple partial seizure

## Parietal/Frontal Lobe Seizures
Seizures associated with sensory/motor problems -> parietal/frontal lobe seizures

## Todd's Paralysis
Focal post-seizure paralysis and weakness -> Todd's paralysis (will go away on its own in hours)

## Seizure Classification
Focal seizures = partial seizures (activity localized to 1 hemisphere)
- Focal seizure w/o loss of awareness = simple partial seizures
  - Examples: Focal muscle rigidity, bizarre sensations (e.g. hearing hissing sound), autonomic sxs
- Focal seizure with loss of awareness = complex partial seizures
  - Can be a person who appears awake but is not aware of surroundings
  - Presentation? Automatisms (e.g. chewing, smacking lips)
Generalized seizures (activity in both hemispheres)
- Absence
- Generalized tonic-clonic
- Atonic seizures (loss of muscle tone -> pt drops to ground). Also consider cataplexy in narcolepsy
- Myoclonic seizures (jerking movements)

## Brain Death
Loss of all brain function, including the brainstem
- Loss of vestibulo-ocular reflex (perform caloric testing)
- Loss of pupillary light reflex
- Apnea test -> no spontaneous respiration after CO2 is allowed to rise (hypercarbia normally stimulates respiratory center in medulla)

## Reflex Syncope
- Syncope with sight of blood, emotional event, pain -> vasovagal syncope. Dx? Tilt table test
- Syncope after tightening tie -> carotid sinus hypersensitivity
- Pt trying to pee and they pass out -> micturition syncope
- Syncope w/o prodrome in pt with ASCVD risk factors -> cardiogenic syncope

## Status Epilepticus Management
1st step: benzo (e.g. lorazepam)
2nd step: IV phenytoin/fosphenytoin
3rd step: sedate with phenobarbital

## AED Teratogenicity
AED with the strongest risk of neural tube defects -> valproic acid
What if patient is already pregnant and couldn't be controlled on other agents but is now well-controlled on valproic acid? Continue valproic acid (seizures are very dangerous to the fetus)


# Movement Disorders

## Hemiballismus
Involuntary flailing movement of 1 arm -> hemiballismus
Pathophys? Lesion in contralateral subthalamic nucleus

## Focal Dystonia
Involuntary movements of the jaw with no other neuro deficits relieved with "gentle stroking" (geste antagoniste) -> focal dystonia
Tx? Botulinum toxin injection (cleaves SNARE proteins -> no vesicle fusion -> no ACh release)


# CNS Infections

Fever, headache, stiff neck -> meningitis. NBS? Head CT, especially if signs of increased ICP on exam
Sudden onset severe headache and neck stiffness -> SAH. NBS? Non-con head CT
Fever, headache, and FNDs -> brain abscess. Dx? Brain MRI. Tx? Broad-spectrum abx + drainage by neurosurg
Recent history of otitis media with FNDs and papilledema on fundoscopic exam -> brain abscess
IVDU with back pain + FNDs -> spinal epidural abscess. Dx? MRI spine. Tx? Broad-spectrum abx + drainage by neurosurg


# Dementia

## Normal Pressure Hydrocephalus
Ataxia, urinary incontinence, and dementia -> normal pressure hydrocephalus
"Wacky, wet, and wobbly"
Tx? Decrease ICP with VP shunt

## Delirium
Elderly patient with MMSE of 20/30 with no past neurological history who took Benadryl last night -> delirium
Pathophys? Diphenhydramine has powerful anticholinergic activity
Anticholinergic drugs: drugs for urge incontinence (e.g. oxybutynin, trospium, darifenacin/solifenacin, tolterodine)

## Alzheimer's Disease
MMSE 19/30 + difficulty speaking + inability to perform ADLs -> Alzheimer's disease
What do you need before formal AD dx? Brain MRI
Apo association? ApoE4 -> higher risk, ApoE2 -> protective
Associated genetic dz? Down syndrome (amyloid precursor protein is on chromosome 21, presentation: AD in 40s)
Pathology? Senile plaques (extracellular amyloid deposits), Neurofibrillary tangles (intracellular hyperphosphorylated tau protein)
Neuroanatomical association? Basal nucleus of Meynert (produces ACh)
Enzyme association? ChAT = choline acetyltransferase
Tx? AChE-inhibitors (Galantamine, Donepezil, Rivastigmine), Memantine (NMDA receptor antagonist)

## Lewy Body Dementia
Parkinsonian features + visual hallucinations + syncopal episodes -> Lewy body dementia

## Huntington's Disease
Dementia + choreiform movements -> Huntington's disease
Inheritance? AD. Repeat? CAG. Imaging? Atrophy of caudate
Pathophys? Think of it as a high-dopamine disorder
Tx? Dopamine antagonist (Haloperidol, Tetrabenazine - VMAT inhibitor)

## Pick's Disease / Frontotemporal Dementia
Mild dementia + difficulty speaking + inappropriate behavior + Knife's edge appearance on brain imaging -> Pick's disease = frontotemporal dementia

## Vascular Dementia
"Stepwise" deterioration in cognitive function w/ neuro deficits -> vascular dementia
Presentation? 5 years ago pt started to be forgetful. 2 years ago this pt xyz.
RF? HLD, CAD, Hx stroke
Tx? Cholinesterase inhibitors used in Alzheimer's

## Creutzfeldt-Jakob Disease
Rapidly progressive dementia in a patient that got a corneal transplant a year ago with myoclonus -> Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease
CSF findings? Elevated 14-3-3 protein

## Parietal Lobe Lesions
Inability to calculate + left to right disorientation -> dominant parietal lobe lesion (Gerstmann's syndrome). Which side in most people? Left
Neglect of one side of the body -> nondominant parietal lobe lesion. Which side in most people? Right


# HY AED Side Effects

- Valproate: Highest risk of NTD, hepatotoxic
- Carbamazepine: Agranulocytosis, SIADH, Teratogen
- Phenytoin: Gingival hyperplasia (other drug: cyclosporin), Arrhythmias (class 1b antiarrhythmic), Diplopia/visual issues, SJS, Drug-induced lupus (Ab: anti-histone), Fetal hydantoin syndrome (microcephaly + flat nasal bridge + cleft lip/palate)


# Juvenile Myoclonic Epilepsy

15 yo with jerky hand movements in the morning and occasional generalized tonic clonic seizures -> juvenile myoclonic epilepsy


# Hereditary Hemorrhagic Telangiectasia (Osler-Weber-Rendu)

Recurrent seizures + contralateral homonymous hemianopia + problems understanding speech + recurrent nosebleeds + positive FOBT -> Hereditary hemorrhagic telangiectasia (Osler-Weber-Rendu disease)
Presentation? Lip telangiectasias
Pathophys? AVMs in multiple organs (e.g. brain, GI tract). In this example, AVM in the temporal lobe
Imaging? Can have dystrophic calcifications
Inheritance? AD
Contrast with Peutz-Jeghers: Hyperpigmented macules on lips, multiple nonmalignant hamartomatous polyps in GI tract, increased risk of colon, pancreatic, breast, gyn cancers


# Carotid Cavernous Fistula

Sudden onset of redness in the eye + "bulging eyes" + "humming sound" on auscultation of the skull + visual acuity 20/100 in affected eye -> carotid cavernous fistula
Pathophys? Essentially an AVM
PE finding? Bruit on auscultation of the skull
Dx? MRA
Tx? Surgery


# Muscular Dystrophies

## Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy
12 yo M that stands from seated position by moving his hands over his legs -> Duchenne muscular dystrophy
Inheritance? X-linked recessive. Mutated protein? Dystrophin
Pathophys? Defective cytoskeleton proteins
Prognosis? Death in teens to early 20s

## Becker Muscular Dystrophy
Inheritance? X-linked recessive
Pathophys? Mutated dystrophin still has some function
Prognosis? Live to 50s+

## Myotonic Dystrophy
Kid that is hypotonic at birth + pediatrician has trouble releasing grip from his mom during 3 mo well child check OR 25 yo M with "severe balding" -> myotonic dystrophy
Inheritance? AD. Repeat? CTG. Mutated gene? DMPK

## Facioscapulohumeral Dystrophy
18 yo M with facial weakness and UE weakness progressively worsening over past 18 months -> facioscapulohumeral dystrophy
Presentation? Usually in teens. Inheritance? AD. Affects boys and girls!


# Inflammatory Myopathies

## Polymyositis / Dermatomyositis
Presentation? Symmetric proximal muscle weakness
Pathophys? Inflammatory myopathies
Ab? Anti-Jo1, anti-Mi2, anti-SRP
Derm findings in dermatomyositis: Gottron's papules, Heliotrope rash, Shawl sign
Associated malignancy? Usually lung cancer or visceral malignancy
Dx? CK level -> if elevated -> MRI of muscle (no longer muscle bx)
Tx? Steroids
Which one can show up in kids? Dermatomyositis

## Inclusion Body Myositis
Presentation? Asymmetric distal arm + proximal leg muscle weakness


# Toxidromes

Diarrhea + rhinorrhea + sweating + miosis -> cholinergic
- Causes? Nerve gases (e.g. sarin), Organophosphate poisoning
- Tx? Atropine + pralidoxime

Mydriasis + constipation + flushing + hot skin + tachycardia -> anticholinergic
- Causes? Jimson weed

Pt with psych hx with arrhythmia or wide QRS -> TCAs
- Tx? Sodium bicarb

Hypertension (possibly MI) + mydriasis + nasal septum perforation -> cocaine
- Tx? Benzos + alpha-1 blocker (e.g. phentolamine)
- What drug is contraindicated? Beta blocker

AMS + rash near nose/mouth + nothing on Utox + type 1 RTA -> Glue/Toluene
- Pathophys? Glue sniffing causes hyperadrenergic response (the high), but can also cause arrhythmias (often V-fib)
- Electrolyte imbalance? Hypokalemia due to type 1 RTA (no H+ excretion and K+ reabsorption in alpha-intercalated cells)

Miosis + respiratory depression (RR = 6) -> opioid overdose
- Tx? Naloxone
- No tolerance to which effects of opioids? Miosis, Constipation

Normal pupils + respiratory depression -> benzodiazepines (could also be barbiturate)
- Tx? Flumazenil for benzos or Z-drugs

Flashbacks + visual hallucinations + synesthesias -> LSD

Pt acting like the hulk + nystagmus -> PCP

Bad oral hygiene + sympathetic activation -> Methamphetamine

Hyperthermia + hyponatremia + affectionate behavior -> Ecstasy/MDMA

Someone who took drugs now with parkinsonism -> MPTP-mediated destruction of substantia nigra


# Paralysis Patterns

Symmetric descending flaccid paralysis w/o sensory deficits -> botulism
Ascending paralysis w/o sensory deficits -> GBS. CSF findings? Albuminocytologic dissociation


# Electrolyte Emergencies

## Hyponatremia & Seizures
Seizing patient with small cell lung cancer with Na of 115 -> hyponatremia 2/2 SIADH
NBS? Hypertonic saline (only indication on NBMEs is seizing pt with Na+ < 120)

## Infant of Diabetic Mother
2 HY electrolyte imbalances that could cause seizures:
- Hypoglycemia: hyperplasia of pancreatic islet cells -> hypersecretion of insulin -> hypoglycemia after delivery
- Hypocalcemia: associated syndrome? DiGeorge

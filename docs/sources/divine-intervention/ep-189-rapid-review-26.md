# Hepatic Masses

## Focal Nodular Hyperplasia vs Hepatic Adenoma
| | FNH | Hepatic Adenoma |
|---|---|---|
| Central scar | Has central stellate scar | No central scar |
| CT arterial | Centrifugal (peripheral) | Centripetal (edges → center) |
| HIDA | Enhanced (contains biliary epithelium) | Non-enhancing (no biliary tree) |
| Association | — | Women on COCs |

# Strictures from Chronic Inflammation
GERD → esophageal strictures → dysphagia
Crohn's → small bowel strictures → SBO
Recurrent PID → fallopian tube strictures → infertility (Dx? Hysterosalpingogram)

# Dermatology

## Psoriasis
Silver scale on extensor surfaces
Tx: 1st topical steroids/coal tar/Vit D analog (calcipotriene), 2nd methotrexate or cyclosporine, 3rd TNF-alpha inhibitors or UV-B/PUVA
Mental health association? Depression

## Acne
Tx: 1st topical benzoyl peroxide/salicylic acid/retinoid, 2nd topical abx (clinda), 3rd oral abx (doxy), 4th isotretinoin (2 forms birth control, check LFTs)

## Ectopic vs Blighted Ovum
Ectopic: fertilized egg implants OUTSIDE uterus
Blighted ovum: fertilized egg but embryo does NOT develop, implants INSIDE uterus (+gestational sac w/o embryo)

## Hyperemesis Gravidarum
1st trimester, admit if dehydrated
Tx: IVF + IV B1 + IV B6 + IV antiemetics
Labs: Ketones in blood/urine

## Cervical Incompetence
RF: Hx LEEP, connective tissue disorder, DES exposure, hx preterm delivery
Presentation: No contractions, but bag/fetal parts emerging from cervix
Tx: Cerclage — Prevention: Vaginal progestin

## Cervical Ectopy/Ectropion
Columnar epithelium of endocervix more susceptible to infection
More common in young females & OCP users → more susceptible to STIs

## Amnioinfusion Indications
Variable decels (cord compression), Severe oligohydramnios, Decrease risk of meconium aspiration in post-term fetus

## Ureaplasma urealyticum
Potential cause of UTIs, chorioamnionitis, PID, miscarriages
High urine pH (urease-positive)
Tx: Doxycycline, Azithromycin — No cell wall agents!

## Chorioamnionitis
NOT an indication for C-section — Tx: Induce to deliver baby soon

## Amniotic Fluid Embolism
Perinatal period + unresponsive + low platelets + elevated fibrin split products + elevated PT/PTT

# PERITONSILLAR VS RETROPHARYNGEAL ABSCESS

## Peritonsillar abscess
- Teens
- Overall not-sick kiddo
- "Hot potato" voice
- Uvular deviation

## Retropharyngeal abscess
- Kid < 5 yo
- Toxic-appearing kid
- Neck asymmetry
- Inability to extend neck
- Widened prevertebral tissue on lateral neck XR

# GENETIC SYNDROMES — RAPID ASSOCIATIONS

ID, low BW, microcephaly, high pitched cat like cry → cri-du-chat

Obesity, infantile hypotonia, ID, small extremities, Cr 15 deletion → Prader-Willi

Happy mood, ID, inappropriate laughter, ataxia, Cr 15 deletion (mom) → Angelman syndrome

"Elfin facies", short upward turned nose, long philtrum → Williams syndrome

X-linked, CGG repeat, anticipation, long face, large ears, ID, macroorchidism → Fragile X

Short palpebral fissures, thin upper lip, smooth philtrum, microcephaly, VSD → FAS

Tricuspid valve displaced downward, RV hypoplasia → Ebstein's anomaly (Li toxicity)

Lateral neck mass that does not move with swallowing, derived from ectoderm → branchial cleft cyst

Midline neck mass, moves with swallowing, may have ectopic thyroid tissue → thyroglossal duct cyst

Hypopigmented macules identified with Wood's lamp, shagreen patches, adenoma sebaceum (face), infantile spasms, subependymal nodules in brain, cardiac rhabdomyoma, renal angiomyolipoma → tuberous sclerosis

# GLYCOGEN STORAGE DISEASES

## Von Gierke disease (GSD type 1)
Severe fasting hypoglycemia, lactic acidosis, hepatomegaly, gout, may have kidney sxs (since this also does gluconeogenesis), no muscle sxs, give cornstarch
Deficiency of? Glucose-6-phosphatase
V for "very bad"
Also remember the two L's (liver only & lactic acidosis)

## Pompe disease (GSD type 2)
Glycogen storage disease associated with heart failure and death at < 2 yo
Deficiency of? Lysosomal alpha-glucosidase = acid maltase
P for "pump"

## Cori disease (GSD type 3)
Mild hypoglycemia, hepatomegaly, muscle sxs, no lactic acidosis
Deficiency of? Debranching enzyme
C for "combined" (muscle & liver sxs)

## McArdle's disease (GSD type 5)
No lactic acidosis, muscle weakness, no hepatomegaly
Deficiency? Glycogen phosphorylase (removes one glucose from the chain)
M for "muscle"

# HIGH-YIELD LYMPHADENOPATHY

Anterior cervical → strep
Posterior cervical → mono
Posterior auricular & suboccipital → rubella
Unilateral → Kawasaki

# CONGENITAL HEART DISEASE

## Ostium primum ASD
Ostium primum defects associated with Down's
Ostium primum is down by the endocardial cushions so this makes sense

## Transposition of the great vessels
Associated with (pre-existing) maternal diabetes
"Egg on a string" on CXR

## Truncus arteriosus
Associated with DiGeorge

## Tricuspid atresia
Need ASD + VSD OR ASD + PDA
LAD on EKG

## Tetralogy of Fallot
Squatting helps during Tet spells because it increases SVR (which decreases the R to L shunting)

# AORTIC STENOSIS

# BILIRUBIN — OBSTRUCTIVE VS HEMOLYTIC

## Biliary obstruction pattern
Increased direct bilirubin + Increased urine bilirubin + decreased urine urobilinogen + elevated alkaline phosphatase
- Increased urine bilirubin because D-bili is water soluble
- Urobilinogen is decreased because GI flora convert D-bili to urobilinogen

## Hemolysis pattern
Increased indirect bilirubin + No urine bilirubin + increased urine urobilinogen
- Urine bilirubin is NOT increased because I-bili is not water soluble
- Urine urobilinogen increased because you are sending more bilirubin to the GI tract

## Common causes of direct hyperbilirubinemia
Biliary atresia
Primary sclerosing cholangitis
- Pathophys? destruction of intrahepatic and extrahepatic bile ducts
- Association? Ulcerative colitis
- Marker? p-ANCA
- Imaging finding? Beads on a string
Primary biliary cholangitis
- Pathophys? Destruction of intrahepatic bile ducts
- Ab? Anti-mitochondrial

## Common causes of indirect hyperbilirubinemia
Gilbert syndrome
Crigler-Najjar syndrome
Hemolysis

# CUSHING'S SYNDROME

Pathophys? Overproduction of cortisol
Dx?
1st step: confirm hypercortisolism
- 24-hr urinary cortisol
- Late night salivary cortisol (normally is low)
- Low-dose dexamethasone suppression test
2nd step: check ACTH levels
- Low ACTH → adrenal adenoma (overproduction by adrenal cortex → ACTH suppression)
- High ACTH → Cushing's disease, ectopic ACTH
3rd step: high-dose dexamethasone test
- Suppression → pituitary lesion
- No suppression → ectopic ACTH (small cell lung cancer)

Interstitial infiltrates in a patient on long term steroids. → PCP pneumonia
Bone protection for pt on chronic steroids? Bisphosphonates

# SECRETORY VS OSMOTIC VS INFLAMMATORY DIARRHEA

## Secretory diarrhea
Pathophys? Secreting electrolytes into GI tract and water follows
Examples:
- Cholera
- ETEC
- VIPoma
- Zollinger-Ellison syndrome
- Medullary thyroid cancer (calcitonin increases electrolyte secretion in the GI tract)
- Microscopic colitis
Stool osmotic gap < 50
Stool osmotic gap = 290 - 2(Na + K)
Small gap means that electrolytes account for most of stool osmolality

## Osmotic diarrhea
Pathophys? Something that is osmotically active (non-electrolyte) is in the GI tract and drawing in water
Examples?
- Lactose intolerance
- Celiac dz
- Osmotic laxatives (e.g. lactulose, PEG)
- Sugar alcohols (e.g., mannitol, sorbitol, xylitol)
- Alpha-glucosidase inhibitors (acarbose, miglitol)
Stool osmotic gap > 100
Large gap means that unmeasured non-electrolytes account for most of stool osmolality

## Inflammatory diarrhea
Causes?
- UC
- Crohn's
- Shigella
- EHEC
- Salmonella
- Campylobacter
- Entamoeba histolytica

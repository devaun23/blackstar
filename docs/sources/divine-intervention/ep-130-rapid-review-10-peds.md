# CROUP

2 yo kid has rhinorrhea for 2 days, mild fever, barky cough
Dx: croup
Patho: parainfluenza virus
Tx: supportive care
Symptoms: subglottic problem, steeple sign on CXR, upper airway problem — stridor, less wheezing
If stridor → think croup or epiglottitis
If wheezing (lower airway) → think asthma and RSV pneumonia (most common in >28 days old)
Kid in 1st month of life with pneumonia = GBS

# EPIGLOTTITIS

Kid presents with SUDDEN onset high fevers, grunting, drooling, tripod position
Dx: epiglottitis
Patho: staph aureus, strep pneumo or H.flu
**not only kids can get epiglottitis, may see it in adults!

# PERTUSSIS

Kid with really bad bouts of cough with cyanosis and vomiting
Dx: bordetella pertussis
*bacterial infection (lymphocytosis instead of neutrophilia) and WBCs are very high!
Vaccine preventable
Tx: macrolide (erythromycin *may prolong QT interval)
PPX for close contact: give erythromycin

# NEONATAL BILIOUS VOMITING

New born that has bilious vomiting
Differentials (do I see a double or triple bubble on XR?)

## Malrotation w/volvulus
Dx: upper GI series

## Duodenal Atresia
Double bubble on XR
Pathophys: Failure of recanalization
Associated w/ Down syndrome

## Jejunal atresia
Triple bubble on XR
Pathophys: associated with vascular problem

# CELIAC DISEASE

Kid that is 10th percentile of weight, bone development problem, very tiny, FTT and floating stool
Dx: celiac disease
Pathophys: problem with microvilli
Check for anti-gliadin or anti-endomysial antibodies and anti-TGA
Tx: avoid gluten

## Complications
- Bone problems: can't absorb ADEK vitamins → secondary hyperparathyroidism (low calcium, high PTH and low PO4) → rickets or osteomalacia
- Vitamin K deficiency → recurrent bleeds
- Vitamin E deficiency → acanthocytosis on blood smear
- Vitamin A deficiency → night blindness
- Derm: dermatitis herpetiformis

## Malignant degeneration
Pt has a h/o celiac and has lost a ton of weight over the last few months, abdominal distension
Dx: malignant degeneration → causes an enteric associated lymphoma (EATL)

# INTUSSUSCEPTION

Kid has intermittent abdominal pain and between episodes they feel ok
Dx: Intussusception — telescoping of the bowel
Presentation: recent URI or GI infection, history of Meckel's diverticulum
→ no Rotavirus vaccine because can predispose intussusception
h/o IgA nephropathy → also avoid rotavirus
Tx: air or contrast enema — both diagnostic and therapeutic

# MINIMAL CHANGE DISEASE

Kid has edema everywhere and had URI 4 days ago and has 4+ proteinuria
Dx: Minimal change disease
Effacement of the foot processes
Pee more than 3.5g of protein
Oncotic pressure is very low → edema everywhere!

## Complications
- Ascites may become infected → SBP (paracentesis >250 neutrophils, treat with 3rd gen cephalosporin)
- DVT susceptible — pee out antithrombin III so can't inhibit factor X and II → hypercoagulable
- Budd Chiari: hepatic vein thrombosis (usually in setting of polycythemia vera)
- Renal vein thrombosis (usually in setting of membranous nephropathy)

MCC of nephropathy in kids!
Associations: URI, hematologic malignancies
Tx: glucocorticoids

# POST-STREP GLOMERULONEPHRITIS VS IGA NEPHROPATHY

URI 4-6 weeks ago with dysmorphic erythrocytes/RBC casts → nephritic syndrome

## PSGN
URI 2-6 weeks ago
Had a skin rash and then hematuria
Labs: Anti-ASO titers or Anti-DNAse B antibodies (skin infection present)

## IgA nephropathy
URI 2-6 days ago

# IMMUNODEFICIENCY SYNDROMES

## CGD
Kid has recurrent skin abscess and recurrent candidal or aspergillus infections
NADPH oxidase deficiency

## IgA deficiency
Kid has anaphylactic response to transfusions, recurrent URI and Giardial diarrhea

## Bruton's agammaglobulinemia
Kid has recurrent URI and giardial infections in a BOY after 6 mo of age
Problem with B cell maturation (BTK gene)
GI infection is almost always giardia!
Tx: Monthly IVIG

## DiGeorge syndrome
Kid has seizures, EKG = prolonged QT interval and PCP infection
3rd and 4th pouch don't form
Recurrent viral and fungal infections
Hypocalcemia → prolonged QT interval
Tracheomalacia

Seizure in infant of diabetic mother = hypoglycemia

## Wiskott-Aldrich syndrome
Boy has recurrent infections, CBC = platelet 40000 and augmented lesions on skin
Associated with thrombocytopenia and eczema

## Male-associated immunodeficiencies (X-linked recessive)
- Wiskott-Aldrich
- CGD
- Bruton's Agammaglobulinemia

# AORTIC STENOSIS
Old guy + syncopal episodes = AS
Heard best at R upper sternal border, radiates to carotids
Pulsus parvus et tardus
Pathophys: calcified valve leaflets; early onset: bicuspid aortic valve (Turner syndrome)
Intensity increases with more blood in LV (increased preload)
Tx: Replace valve → "ASH" threshold (Angina, Syncope, HF — HF = must replace)
Can't do valvotomy (valve too calcified)

# AORTIC REGURGITATION
Heard best at LSB; Diastolic murmur
Wide pulse pressure (e.g. 120/40) — systolic ↑ (increased CO from increased preload), diastolic ↓ (blood relaxes back)
Findings: head bobbing, hyperdynamic pulse, Waterhammer pulse
Wide pulse pressure in a kid → PDA
Tx: lower afterload (less regurgitation)

# MITRAL STENOSIS
Diastolic murmur with opening snap, heard best at apex
#1 RF: rheumatic fever; #1 RF for AFib: mitral stenosis
Left atrium dilates → AFib
If AFib from valvular cause (mitral stenosis) → only WARFARIN, NOT NOAC

# MITRAL VALVE PROLAPSE
Midsystolic murmur with click at apex
Caused by myxomatous degeneration; assoc: Marfan's, Ehlers Danlos, ADPKD
Increased preload/afterload (squatting, handgrip) → murmur LESS intense (leaflets overlap better)
Contrast with MR: increased preload/afterload makes MR LOUDER

# VSD
Holosystolic murmur at LLSB; most common congenital heart defect
Initially L→R shunt; eventually → Eisenmenger syndrome (R→L)

# ASD
Fixed split S2 (on both inspiration AND expiration)
Commonly caused by secundum defect; Down syndrome → primum defect

# PULMONARY HYPERTENSION
Loud P2

# PARADOXICAL SPLITTING
A2 occurring after P2 → splitting only in exhalation
Causes: LBBB, severe aortic stenosis

# HOCM
Athlete collapses on field; AD inheritance (myosin binding protein C mutations)
Systolic ejection murmur at LSB, does NOT radiate to carotids
Bifid pulse; systolic anterior motion of mitral valve leaflet → obstruction + regurgitation
Increased preload/afterload → murmur LESS intense (more blood pushes leaflet out of way)
Tx: Beta blockers (slow heart → more diastolic filling)

# AORTIC DISSECTION
Caused by cystic medial degeneration; assoc: tertiary syphilis, Marfan's, Ehlers Danlos

# CARCINOID SYNDROME
TIPS: Tricuspid Insufficiency, Pulmonic Stenosis
Also: Bronchospasm, Flushing, Diarrhea, Right-sided heart problems

# WHICH MURMURS TO INVESTIGATE
Systolic ≥ Grade III; ANY diastolic murmur; Any symptomatic murmur; Holosystolic
Peds: musical quality → benign, don't investigate

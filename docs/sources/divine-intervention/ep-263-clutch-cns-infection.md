# CRYPTOCOCCAL MENINGITIS
IVDU + HIV + severe headache + nuchal rigidity + high fevers
CSF: high protein, opening pressure 300, low glucose ~10, lymphocyte predominance
Dx: LP → latex agglutination test or India ink; MCC meningitis in HIV
Tx: Amphotericin B + Flucytosine → Fluconazole ppx 6-12 months

# TOXOPLASMOSIS
HIV + CD4 55 + generalized tonic-clonic seizures + calcifications in caudate/putamen
Ring-enhancing lesion on brain imaging
Tx: Pyrimethamine + Sulfadiazine + Leucovorin
Ppx (CD4 <100): TMP-SMX
Pregnant → Spiramycin (anti-folates c/I in pregnancy)
Congenital toxo: diffuse cortical/basal ganglia calcifications
Congenital CMV: periventricular calcifications

# NEUROCYSTICERCOSIS
Farmer + pig exposure + seizures + increasing head circumference
Taenia solium → cysticercosis → calcified brain cysts → seizures/hydrocephalus
Tx: Albendazole/Mebendazole + steroids

# MUCORMYCOSIS
Diabetic (DKA) + severe facial pain + high fever
Rhizopus; starts in frontal sinuses → spreads to frontal lobe
Tx: extensive debridement + Amphotericin B

# NAEGLERIA FOWLERI
Swim instructor + fever → death in 2 days; loves freshwater; destroys frontal lobes
Tx: Amphotericin B (usually fatal)

# AFRICAN SLEEPING SICKNESS
Recent immigrant from Africa + somnolence + refusing food + weight loss
Trypanosoma brucei; Vector: TseTse fly; initially bloodborne → enters brain → sleepiness → death from starvation
Dx: LP → elevated IgM in CSF
Tx: 1. Pentamidine (also for PCP); 2. Melarsoprol (2nd stage/CNS involvement)

# VIRAL MENINGITIS
July + AMS + CSF: normal glucose, normal opening pressure, slightly elevated WBC with lymphocyte predominance
Tx: Supportive (enterovirus = MCC viral meningitis)

# CMV MENINGITIS
HIV + CD4 <50 + somnolence + CSF: normal glucose, normal opening pressure, lymphocyte predominance, RBC 0-5
Tx: Ganciclovir/Valganciclovir; resistant → Foscarnet
DDx from herpes: RBC in CSF → herpes; Owl's eye intranuclear inclusions = CMV

# HSV ENCEPHALITIS
Temporal lobe involvement; CSF: RBCs
Tx: IV Acyclovir

# RABIES
Animal bite → virus to NMJ → binds AChR → retrograde transport to brain → 100% fatal
Spastic paralysis + hydrophobia (throat muscle spasms)
PEP: Rabies Ig (inject around wound) + vaccine (contralateral extremity)
Autopsy: Negri bodies (pink intracytoplasmic inclusions) — different from CMV intranuclear inclusions

# POLIO
Immigrant + infection of anterior horn of spinal cord → LMN problems → muscle paralysis

# BACTERIAL MENINGITIS BY AGE
## Neonatal (first 20 days)
#1 GBS (Group B Strep agalactiae); Tx: 3rd gen cephalosporin + Ampicillin
Screen pregnant women at 35-37 weeks for GBS
#2 E. coli; #3 Listeria (deli meat, soft cheese → granulomatosis infantisepticum)

## 28 days - 20 years
N. meningitidis: + skin findings (petechiae/purpura); Gram-negative diplococci
Tx: Ceftriaxone; Close contacts: rifampin (preferred), ceftriaxone, or cipro
Pregnant contact → ceftriaxone

## Adults
S. pneumoniae: Gram-positive diplococci; CSF: low glucose, high protein, neutrophils, high opening pressure
Tx: Ceftriaxone + Vancomycin + Steroids (decrease morbidity)

# TB MENINGITIS
CSF: high opening pressure, high protein, low glucose, lymphocyte predominance
Imaging: enhancement at base of brain
Tx: RIPE + B6 + Steroids

# SYPHILIS — CNS
Tabes dorsalis: dorsal column damage → abnormal fine touch/vibration, positive Romberg, Argyll Robertson pupil (accommodates but doesn't react)
Tx: Penicillin

# SPIROMETRY REVIEW
Indications? Suspected asthma or COPD
Obstructive: FEV1/FVC <0.7; increases by >12% after bronchodilator → asthma; increased lung volumes
Restrictive: FEV1/FVC >0.8; decreased lung volumes

## DLCO
Inversely proportional to thickness → Pulmonary fibrosis/ILD → decreased DLCO
Directly proportional to surface area → Emphysema → decreased DLCO
Increased DLCO? Polycythemia, Pulmonary hemorrhage
Restrictive + normal DLCO → mechanical ventilation problem (MG, ALS, GBS, obesity, scoliosis)

## CO Poisoning
PaO2 normal, SatO2 decreased (but pulse ox will be normal) → Order CO-oximetry

## Methacholine Challenge
Suspect asthma but spirometry normal → Methacholine is muscarinic agonist → bronchospasm in asthmatics

# ASTHMA VARIANTS
Asthma + nasal polyps → AERD (aspirin-exacerbated respiratory disease)
Pathophys: ASA/NSAIDs inhibit COX → increased LOX pathway → increased leukotrienes
Tx: montelukast, zileuton (hepatotoxic)

Asthma + renal problems → EGPA/Churg-Strauss
Asthma + really high IgE + upper lobe infiltrates → ABPA (Dx: skin-testing for Aspergillus)

# ASTHMA TREATMENT LADDER
Mild intermittent (sxs ≤2x/week, nighttime ≤2x/month): SABA
Mild persistent (>2x/week but not daily): SABA + low-dose ICS
Moderate persistent (daily, nighttime >1x/week): SABA + low-dose ICS + LABA (or medium-dose ICS or leukotriene antagonist)
Severe persistent (throughout the day, nightly): Oral corticosteroids

Triggers: non-selective beta-blocker (propranolol, timolol)
High IgE: Omalizumab (anti-IgE)
Vaccines: Influenza, Pneumococcal
ICS → thrush (prevention: wash mouth after ICS)
Chronic oral steroids → PPIs + calcium/vitamin D + bisphosphonates
LABA: never monotherapy (increases mortality)
Theophylline: avoid macrolides and fluoroquinolones (reduce clearance)

## Acute Exacerbation
DuoNeb (albuterol/ipratropium) + IV steroids; if severe: IV Mg
Discharge with 3-4 days oral corticosteroids
Decompensation sign: normalization of PaCO2 → NBS: intubate

# COPD
Age <45 + family history → Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency (Tx: IV pooled alpha-1 AT)

## COPD Treatment Ladder
1. SAMA or SABA (choose SAMA if must pick)
2. Add LAMA (tiotropium) or LABA (don't give SAMA + LAMA together)
3. LAMA + LABA OR LABA + ICS
4. Oral steroids

Home O2 indications: PaO2 <55, O2 sat <88, Polycythemia, Pulmonary HTN
Survival benefit: Smoking cessation, Home O2 therapy

## COPD Exacerbation
DuoNebs + BiPAP + IV corticosteroids + Azithromycin
Discharge on 3-5 days oral corticosteroids
SOB in terminal COPD → Opiates

# CYSTIC FIBROSIS
Low weight + nasal polyps + recurrent sinusitis + finger clubbing + fat malabsorption
MC cause of bronchiectasis in US
If sudden-onset severe abdominal pain → Intussusception
Tx: Pancreatic enzymes + fat soluble vitamins, Chest physiotherapy, Inhaled dornase alfa or N-acetylcysteine, Inhaled tobramycin ppx
Dx: Sweat chloride testing

## CF Pneumonia
<20 yo: Staph aureus (cover MRSA)
>20 yo: Pseudomonas or Burkholderia cepacia

# BRONCHIECTASIS
Chronic coughing + sputum production, linear streaks on CXR
Dx: High-resolution CT → tram-track pattern
MC cause in US: CF
Causes restrictive lung disease

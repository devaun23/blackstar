# GERIATRIC ASSESSMENT

Check vision, hearing, fall risk, urinary incontinence
Polypharmacy — review meds
BEERS criteria
Look out for elderly abuse
Assess functional ability — assess ADLs
Basic and instrumental ADLs (things needed to live independently vs things needed to interact w/ society)

## Cognitive Assessment
MMSE to evaluate cognitive functioning (abnormal <24)
Mini-Cog → tell pt a series of words then draw a clock then recall the words
Check TSH, B12, meds, serum Na, BNP if they have cognitive dysfunction

## Hearing
MCC of hearing loss in elderly → presbycusis (high freq hearing loss first)
Best screening test → take history then whispered voice test then audiometry

## Vision
Assess for presbyopia, macular degeneration, cataracts

## Depression
Rule out depression in the elderly esp in CKD, stroke, CAD pts; older pts have a greater risk of suicide than younger
We don't always use SIGECAPS for elderly; instead ask "have you felt depressed/sad/low" and "have you lost interest in the past 2 wks?"
SSRI is DOC — do NOT pick TCA (because of S/E profile)

## Fall Risk
Assessment → ask for h/o problems w/ gait or balance, do a get up and go test (normal is 10-11sec, abnormal is >20 sec)
Interventions that decrease fall risk → exercise, physiotherapy, Vit D supplementation

# URINARY INCONTINENCE

Urge incontinence/overactive bladder (hypertonic detrusor) → bladder training is the best Tx, biofeedback, oxybutynin, tolterodine (best avoided in elderly)
Elderly women, G6P6 (multiple deliveries) → Kegel's exercise, sling procedure
Overflow incontinence (hypotonic detrusor) → self catheterization, timed voiding
Functional incontinence (cognitive problems or arthritis where they can't move fast enough) → need caregiver

# ADVANCED DIRECTIVES

Living will aka Advanced Directive
Health care proxy (DPOA)
POLST forms → a health care provider has to be present when pt is signing this
Palliative care < 6 mos prognosis → initiate Hospice care

# DRIVING SAFETY

Elderly pt driving h/o mild dementia or is taking benzo, TCA or h/o vision problems or h/o falls, ambulation issues, degenerative dz who can have trouble moving neck side to side → report to DMV

# PRESSURE ULCERS

Old pt, sensory issues, chronic illness, stool or urinary incontinence, vascular dz all RF for developing pressure/decubitus ulcer
Prevention: reduce pressure points by using air beds, reduce friction, reduce shearing forces
4 stages:
- Stage 1: erythema on skin, non-blanchable
- Stage 2: lose partial thickness of skin — epidermis and dermis
- Stage 3: subcutaneous tissue is exposed but no involvement of fascia/bone/muscle/tendon
- Stage 4: involvement of fascia, muscle, tendon or bone
Give them as much nutrition as possible, consider debridement for stage 3 and 4 ulcer, apply occlusive dressings

# SURROGATE DECISION MAKER

Use next of kin: spouse → adult children → parents → siblings → other relatives

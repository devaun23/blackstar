# ISOLATION PRECAUTIONS
Contact: gown + gloves (C. diff, HUS from EHEC/Shigella)
Droplet: gown + gloves + surgical mask (N. meningitidis, Pertussis, Influenza, walking pneumonias)
Airborne: gown + gloves + N95 respirator (COVID-19, SARS, Smallpox, TB)
TB: negative pressure room when available

# C. DIFF
Contact isolation; HANDWASHING only (no hand sanitizer — spores!)

# VENTILATOR MANAGEMENT
ARDS survival strategies: low tidal volumes + high PEEP + PRONE position (not supine)
Weaning criteria: good O2 sat (FiO2 ≤30-35%) + very low PEEP (<5)
Remove plastic (catheters/lines) as soon as possible

# CENTRAL LINE CARE
Clean skin with chlorhexidine; full sterile technique (drape both you and patient)
Surgical mask, cap, gown, STERILE gloves
AVOID femoral central line (highest infection risk/CLABSI)
Prefer jugular or subclavian
TPN through central line; MCC complication of TPN: CLABSI

# SURGICAL PROPHYLAXIS
Cefazolin (Ancef) 30-60 min before incision; if time changes → re-dose

# NEEDLESTICK INJURY
Clean wound immediately → start PEP ASAP

## Hep B
Vaccinated (adequate Ab) → do nothing
Not immune → HepB vaccine + HepB Ig (separate arms)

## Hep C
Not much you can do

## HIV PEP
Tenofovir + emtricitabine + raltegravir for 4-6 weeks

## Seroconversion risk
HepB: ~1 in 3 (highest!); HepC: ~1 in 30; HIV: ~1 in 300
Most effective prevention of HepB in HCW → vaccination

# PREVENTION MEASURES
PPE, sharps containers, safe needles (blunt tips/capped)

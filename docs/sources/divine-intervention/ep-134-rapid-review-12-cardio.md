# Recent MI Complications

Pt with recent MI + 12h later becomes suddenly unresponsive then dies. Cause of death? VFib
VFib = MCC death in first 24h after MI
Tx: chest compressions, defibrillation, epinephrine + amiodarone
Epinephrine: used to tx arrhythmias, and anaphylactic shock
Note: Defibrillation (unsynchronized cardioversion) only used in 2 arrhythmias: VFib and Pulseless VTach

## Cardiogenic Shock
Pt with recent MI + bilateral crackles + profoundly hypotensive → Cardiogenic shock
MI = death of cardiac myocytes → inadequate contractility

Hemodynamic Parameters:
- LV End-Diastolic Volume: increased
- LV End-Diastolic Pressure: increased
- Stroke volume: decreased
- LV End-Systolic Volume: increased
- SBP: decreased
- CO: decreased
- LA pressure (surrogate for PCWP): increased
- SVR: increased
- CV pressure (surrogate for RA): increased

Note: cardiogenic shock leads to pulmonary edema
In contrast: ARDS (increased pulmonary permeability) has normal PCWP (< 18 mmHg)

## Papillary Muscle Rupture
Pt with recent MI + flash mitral regurgitation d/t papillary muscle rupture → florid backflow of blood

## Interventricular Septal Rupture
Pt with recent MI + holosystolic murmur @ LLSB → intraventricular septal rupture

## Acute Mesenteric Ischemia
Pt with recent MI + severe abdominal pain → acute mesenteric ischemia
"heart is quivering and not contracting appropriately, causing/flicking off a clot"
Vs chronic mesenteric ischemia: pain after eating, "angina of the bowel"

## Acute Limb Ischemia
Pt with recent MI + severe leg pain → acute limb ischemia
Vs chronic = peripheral arterial disease

## Ventricular Free Wall Rupture
Pt with recent MI a few days ago, BP 80/50, JVD, tall + tiny QRS (Beck's triad) → ventricular free wall rupture
Blood pools in pericardial space causing cardiac tamponade
Sx: distant heart sounds, electrical alternans (heart "dancing" in fluid), low-voltage EKG (also seen in amyloid deposition)

## Reinfarction
Pt with recent MI + revascularization, chest pain → reinfarction? Dx with CK-MB

## Cholesterol Embolization
Pt with recent MI + PCI, rising Cr, "blue-toe syndrome", livedo reticularis on LE → Cholesterol embolization

## Post-Infarction Pericarditis
Pt with recent MI + few days, chest pain (worse supine), scratchy sound in systole + diastole → Post-infarction pericarditis
Widespread ST elevation, PR depression

## Dressler's Syndrome
Pt with recent MI + weeks, chest pain, scratchy sound → Dressler's syndrome (autoimmune pericarditis)
DDx of pericarditis: recent viral syndrome (MCC), renal failure/uremia, radiation therapy to chest (also huge RF for thyroid CA!)

## RCA Infarct
Pt with recent MI + chest pain radiating to jaw, nitroglycerin leading to hypotension → RCA infarct (preload dependent!)
EKG: II, III, AVF
Tx: fluids
*steroids are not generally helpful in MI bc they prevent wound healing*

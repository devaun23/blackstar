# Pulmonary Embolism

Pt with CKD 2/2 FSGS with sudden severe CP and palpitations. HR 125. CP worse with deep breath -> pulmonary embolism
Pathophys? Pts with nephrotic syndrome lose AT-III in urine -> hypercoagulable

Risk factors: Virchow's triad (Stasis, Hypercoagulability, Endothelial injury)
Associations: Malignancy (especially mucin-secreting), Nephrotic syndrome, Factor V Leiden, Prothrombin 20210 Mutation, Antiphospholipid Ab syndrome

Classic presentation? Pleural CP + SOB
Lab findings: Respiratory alkalosis (2/2 hyperventilation), Hypocalcemia (negative charges bind Ca++), Increased A-a gradient
MC EKG finding? Sinus tachycardia. Other: New RBBB, S1Q3T3 (uncommon)

Risk stratification with Well's score:
- <=4 -> low-risk -> order D-dimer. If negative discharge; if positive -> CT angio
- >5 -> high-risk -> straight to CT angio

Dx? CT angio w/ contrast (avoid in CKD; acceptable in pregnancy if V/Q scan isn't an option). V/Q scan. Gold standard: Pulmonary angiogram (BUT NOT DONE!)

Classification:
- R heart strain -> submassive PE
- HDUS (SBP < 90, required pressors) -> massive PE

Tx? Heparin (UFH only in renal failure). Submassive + mild sxs -> heparin. Submassive + severe sxs -> tPA. Massive -> tPA. Embolectomy if contraindications to tPA or pt deteriorating after tPA
Long-term: chronic anticoagulation if unprovoked


# Other Emboli

Sudden-onset severe flank pain + nephrotic syndrome -> renal vein thrombosis (strongest assoc: membranous nephropathy)
Pt with hx DVT that gets stroke -> PFO or ASD
Woman that just delivered becoming comatose + bleeding from IV sites -> amniotic fluid embolism
Scuba diving with rapid ascent -> air embolism (nitrogen bubbles occlude vessels)
Recent IV placement + SOB/CP -> air embolism
Recent fracture or ortho surgery + SOB -> fat embolism (hypoxia + petechiae + AMS)

DVT: Unilateral leg pain & swelling. Dx? LE Doppler. Tx? Heparin


# Arrhythmias - AFib

35 yo M on methimazole, EKG with irregular narrow-complex tachyarrhythmia -> AFib
EKG? Irregularly irregular rhythm
Tx? AV nodal blocking agent (beta blockers, non-dihydropyridine CCBs)
HDUS? Synchronized cardioversion
Duration-based management:
- <48 hrs -> synchronized cardioversion
- >48 hrs or unknown -> anticoagulate, TEE, cardiovert if no clot

Long-term anticoagulation: CHA2DS2-VASc score. Options: Warfarin, Factor Xa inhibitors, Direct thrombin inhibitors

Reversal agents: Heparin -> protamine sulfate. Warfarin -> 4-factor PCC. Factor Xa inhibitors -> andexanet. Dabigatran -> idarucizumab
Rhythm control: Amiodarone, Propafenone


# Electrolyte Emergencies

## Hyperkalemia
Pt ran marathon, now with peaked T waves -> hyperkalemia 2/2 rhabdomyolysis
Other causes: Tumor lysis syndrome, Missing dialysis
EKG progression: Peaked T waves -> Wide QRS -> Sine wave -> V-fib
First step? Calcium gluconate
Tx: Insulin + glucose (drives K+ into cells), Albuterol (drives K+ into cells), Fluids + furosemide, Kayexalate

## TCA Toxicity
Pt on imipramine with wide QRS -> TCA toxicity. First step? Sodium bicarb

## EKG Findings by Electrolyte
- Hypokalemia: U waves, Prolonged QT
- Hypocalcemia: Prolonged QT
- Hypercalcemia: Short QT
- Hypomagnesemia: Prolonged QT

## Digoxin & Potassium
Digoxin causes hyperkalemia (inhibits Na+-K+ ATPase). BUT hypokalemia predisposes to dig toxicity (more K+ binding sites available for digoxin)

QT-prolonging drugs: Azithromycin, Haloperidol, many antipsychotics
Prolonged QT -> Torsades -> sudden cardiac death. Tx of Torsades? IV Mg

Hypocalcemia refractory to repletion -> replete Mg first
Corrected Ca+: Every 1 mg/dL drop in albumin reduces Ca+ by 0.8
K+ 8 with no sxs/EKG findings -> likely hemolyzed sample -> repeat
Hypercalcemic crisis first step? NS


# Pneumothorax

Severe sudden CP, profoundly hypoxic, BP 40/palp, +JVD, recent central line -> tension pneumothorax
NBS? Needle decompression (2nd ICS midclavicular or 5th ICS mid-axillary). Tx? Chest tube
PE: Unilateral decreased breath sounds, hyperresonant to percussion

Primary spontaneous: Tall thin male. If <20% hemithorax -> observe 6 hrs -> repeat CXR
Tx if large/worsening? Chest tube


# Esophageal Perforation

Recent EGD, now CP + subcutaneous emphysema + black line outlining mediastinum -> esophageal perforation
Dx? Gastrografin esophagram (not barium -> causes mediastinitis). Tx? Surgical repair
Populations: Alcoholics, Bulimics


# Hypertensive Emergencies

Hx scleroderma, very high BP + HA + vision changes -> hypertensive emergency (end organ damage)
BP > 180/110 without sxs -> hypertensive urgency
Tx: Labetalol, Esmolol, Nitroprusside (AE: cyanide toxicity -> tx: amyl nitrate + thiosulfate or hydroxocobalamin), Nicardipine, Clevidipine
"Observing LENT in North Carolina". Don't lower BP >25% in 1st hour

## SAH
BP 210/150 with WHOL -> SAH. NBS? NCCT. If negative -> LP (xanthochromia, thousands of RBCs)
Contrast: meningitis less sudden; HSV has hundreds of RBCs
Pathophys: Rupture of berry aneurysm (most often ACom)
Tx: Lower BP (labetalol/nicardipine), Nimodipine (prevent vasospasm), Phenytoin/fosphenytoin (seizure ppx)
If rapidly increasing ICP -> hyperventilate (fastest way to relieve increased ICP)


# Aortic Dissection

Long hx uncontrolled HTN + sudden-onset severe tearing CP radiating to back
Pathophys? Cystic medial necrosis/degeneration. Other causes: Marfan's, Ehlers-Danlos, Syphilis, Bicuspid aortic valve
First step? Beta blocker (alternative: nitroprusside)
Imaging: Widened mediastinum on CXR
Dx: Stable -> CT angio. HDUS -> TEE
Tx: Stanford A (ascending) -> surgery. Stanford B (descending) -> medical management
Complications: Extension -> occlusion of RCA -> inferior MI


# Thyroid Storm

Pt with thyroid problems gets CT contrast, now markedly tachycardic, T 103F, AMS -> thyroid storm
Causes: Hx hyperthyroidism (missed meds), Iodinated contrast, Amiodarone
Labs: TSH low, T3/T4 high
Tx: 1st Propranolol (blocks T4->T3 conversion) -> 2nd PTU -> Wait 1 hr -> 3rd Lugol's solution (Wolff-Chaikoff effect) -> 4th Dexamethasone/hydrocortisone
MC arrhythmia? AFib


# Pheochromocytoma

Neck mass + family hx thyroid cancer + episodic severe HTN -> pheo 2/2 MEN2
Inheritance? AD. Gene? RET
Tx: 1st alpha block (phenoxybenzamine, phentolamine, labetalol) -> 2nd beta block
Associations: VHL, MEN2A/B, NF-1


# Tyramine Crisis

Pt on MAO-I eats at restaurant, becomes altered, SBP 250 -> tyramine crisis
MAO-Is: Phenelzine, Tranylcypromine, Isocarboxazid
Tx: Phentolamine, Labetalol


# Endocarditis

IVDU with fevers + new murmur at LLSB -> tricuspid endocarditis
Embolic phenomena: Osler's nodes, Janeway lesions
First step? Blood cx. Dx? TEE. Empiric tx? Vancomycin (cover MRSA)


# ACS

## Stable Angina (NOT ACS)
CP with exertion, goes away with rest -> NBS? Stress test

## Unstable Angina
Worsening sxs, CP at rest. EKG: T-wave inversions, ST depression. Troponin: NORMAL

## NSTEMI
EKG: T-wave inversions, ST depression. Troponin: elevated

## STEMI
EKG: ST elevation. Troponin: elevated

Risk stratification: HEART score (6-week MACE risk), TIMI score (mortality UA/NSTEMI)

Lead/Vessel relationships:
- II, III, aVF -> RCA (inferior). Drug contraindicated: Nitrates (preload dependent)
- V1-V4 -> LAD (anterior septal)
- V5-V6 -> LCX (lateral)
- New heart block -> RCA (supplies AV node)
- Also avoid nitrates if pt takes PDE-inhibitor

ACS Management:
- First diagnostic step? EKG (hyperacute T waves -> ST changes -> Q waves)
- First therapeutic step? 324 mg aspirin within 10 min (mortality benefit)
- Dx: Troponins (repeat 6 hrs), CK-MB for reinfarction (troponin takes 7-10 days to fall)

Initial Tx: MONA BASH
- M = morphine, O = O2, N = nitrate, A = aspirin, B = beta-blocker, A = antiplatelets (P2Y12), S = statin, H = heparin
- If going for PCI -> GpIIbIIIa antagonists

Definitive Tx:
- PCI: Door-to-balloon <90 min
- If no PCI hospital: <2 hrs away -> transport. >2 hrs -> thrombolytics (tPA, up to 12 hrs)
- L main or triple-vessel disease -> CABG (internal mammary artery > saphenous vein)

Cardiogenic shock 2/2 MI: NBS? Intra-aortic balloon pump (inflates in diastole -> coronary perfusion)

Discharge meds: Beta blocker, ACE-I, High-intensity statin, Aspirin, If stent -> DAPT

## MI Complications
- 3 days post-MI + flash pulmonary edema + new holosystolic murmur LLSB -> interventricular septal rupture
- 3 days post-MI + flash pulmonary edema + MR on echo + normal ventricular size -> acute MR 2/2 papillary muscle rupture
- Recent MI + unresponsive + JVD + BP 60/palp + muffled heart sounds -> free wall rupture (tamponade, Beck's triad)
- 7 days post-PCI + blue toes + petechiae + elevated Cr -> cholesterol embolization syndrome (livedo reticularis)
- Hypotensive immediately post-PCI + back pain -> RP bleed (if femoral access)
- Progressive hypotension immediately post-PCI -> tamponade 2/2 puncture -> surgery
- 3 days post-MI + sudden severe abdominal pain -> acute mesenteric ischemia (dx: mesenteric angiography)
- Recent MI + severe LE pain -> acute limb ischemia (heparin -> arteriography -> vascular surgery)
- Pericarditis days after MI -> fibrinous pericarditis
- MI 6 weeks ago + CP better leaning forward -> Dressler's syndrome (EKG: diffuse ST elevation + PR depression). Tx: NSAID + colchicine
- MCC death within 24 hrs of MI? V-fib

## Prinzmetal Angina
Multiple episodes CP at night + ST elevations + troponin elevations + no occlusion on cath -> Variant angina
Tx? CCB (diltiazem or amlodipine). Contraindicated: Sumatriptan (vasoconstriction)


# Arrhythmias

## Long QT
QTc > 440ms men, > 460ms women
Congenital: Romano-Ward (AD, ventricular tachyarrhythmias), Jervell and Lange-Nielsen (AR, congenital hearing loss)
Tx: Beta-blocker for life + ICD
Common cause of inappropriate ICD discharge? Magnet exposure (e.g. MRI)

## WPW
Short PR, delta wave, wide QRS -> antidromic AVRT (signal down Bundle of Kent, up AV node)
Tx? Procainamide (AE: drug-induced lupus). Contraindicated: AV blocking agents (BB, CCB, digoxin)
Contrast orthodromic AVRT: narrow QRS (down AV node, up accessory pathway)

## Heart Blocks
1st degree: Prolonged PR, no dropped beats
Mobitz I (Wenckebach): Progressive PR prolongation, then dropped beat
Mobitz II: Fixed PR with dropped beats (high risk -> 3rd degree)
3rd degree: No relationship P waves to QRS, constant P-P and R-R intervals
Pacemaker indicated: Mobitz II, 3rd degree. Acute tx: Transcutaneous pacing, Atropine

## SVT
Regular narrow-complex tachyarrhythmia. Tx: Vagal maneuvers -> Adenosine (6->12->12mg) -> BB/CCB -> If HDUS: synchronized cardioversion
Unsynchronized cardioversion (defibrillation): V-fib, Pulseless VTach

## Atrial Flutter: Sawtooth (best in II, III, aVF)
## MAT: 3+ P wave morphologies. Population: COPD. Tx: Stop smoking, verapamil/diltiazem
## VTach: Regular wide-complex. No pulse -> ACLS. Pulse + HDUS -> synchronized cardioversion. Stable -> amiodarone (2nd line: lidocaine)
## PEA/Asystole: Chest compressions + epi


# Valvular Disorders

## HOCM
Athlete collapses during basketball -> HOCM. AD, sarcomere protein mutations (e.g. beta myosin heavy chain)
Murmur: LLSB, no radiation, pulsus bisferiens
Increase preload/afterload -> softer. Decrease preload/afterload -> louder
Tx: Beta-blocker (more diastolic filling). Arrhythmias -> ICD

## MVP: Marfan's + midsystolic click. Pathophys: myxomatous degeneration
## MR: Holosystolic at apex, radiates to axilla. Complication: volume overload. Acute: papillary muscle rupture
## AS: Systolic ejection murmur RUSB, radiates to carotids. Early cause: bicuspid aortic valve
## AR: "Blowing" diastolic murmur LSB + wide pulse pressure
## VSD: Holosystolic at LLSB. Adult cause: interventricular septal rupture post-MI


# Heart Failure

CHF exacerbation: Crackles, JVD, hepatomegaly, LE edema
Triggers: Nonadherence, Missing dialysis, Metabolic stress (infection)

R heart failure: COPD/cor pulmonale/CF. JVD + hepatomegaly + LE edema, no crackles. Elevated CVP, normal PCWP
L heart failure: All R heart failure signs + pulmonary edema. PCWP > 18 mmHg

Tx for dyspnea: Nitroglycerin (venodilator, decreases preload) -> Furosemide -> Add thiazide if inadequate -> NIPPV (BiPAP: decreases WOB, increases intrathoracic pressure -> decreases preload)
Cardiogenic shock: Dobutamine, Milrinone (PDE inhibitor -> increased cAMP -> contractility + vasodilation)

Young pt with recent URI + new S3 + JVD + global ventricular enlargement -> viral myocarditis (Coxsackie B)
Idiosyncratic finding: Tachycardia not proportionate to fever. Treat like CHF exacerbation


# Syncope

- Sweating + pale -> vasovagal
- No prodrome, quick recovery -> cardiogenic (dangerous arrhythmia)
- Neuro deficit or postictal -> neurogenic (seizure or stroke)

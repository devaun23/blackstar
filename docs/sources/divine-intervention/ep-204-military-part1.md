# TRAUMATIC BRAIN INJURY

## TBI Classification by GCS
GCS >= 13 → mild TBI
GCS 9-12 → moderate TBI
GCS <= 8 → severe TBI
Pathognomonic finding: Diffuse axonal injury
Most susceptible areas: Anterior temporal lobes + orbital frontal cortex

## TBI Management
N-Acetyl Cysteine → to TBI pts to prevent complications
Tranexamic acid within first 3 hrs
Hyperventilation: Fastest measure to lower ICP (short-term only)
Mannitol: Can lower ICP but contraindicated in CHF; Hypertonic saline is alternative (risks hypernatremia)
NEVER give steroids in TBI
Keep TBI patients normothermic

## TBI Complications
Central diabetes insipidus can occur after TBI
Most common cognitive impairment after TBI: Memory loss
After discharge from hospital → send pt to rehab

## Post-Concussive Syndrome
Neuropsych symptoms in post-TBI (mild) pt: Headache, dizziness, increased sensitivity to light and sound, anxiety, depression

## Second Impact Syndrome
A second episode of TBI after a mild TBI → persistently vegetative or death

# PTSD

## Presentation
Distress + autonomic hyperactivity on re-experiencing triggers
Usually arises from interpersonal trauma vs natural disaster (e.g. rape vs earthquake)
Small volume of hippocampus seen in PTSD
Exaggerated response to dexamethasone suppression test
Abnormal low cortisol and high catecholamines in urine (maladaptive hyperadrenergic response)

## Treatment
Rx: SSRIs (sertraline, fluoxetine, paroxetine), venlafaxine
CBT of choice: Exposure therapy (also for OCD, specific phobias)
For nightmares: Prazosin
Do NOT give benzodiazepines (especially in veterans — worsens it)
Do NOT pick debriefing as treatment
4 months pharm Rx does NOT apply to ASD (acute stress disorder — symptoms < 1 month)

## Acute Stress Disorder vs PTSD
Sxs < 1 month = acute stress disorder
Sxs > 1 month = PTSD

# MILITARY-SPECIFIC CONSIDERATIONS

Alcohol abuse: Very common post-PTSD
Military sexual trauma (W >> M): Biggest risk factor for PTSD among servicemen/women
MC comorbidity in veterans: Substance use disorder (commonly alcohol)
Highest risk: Can be decades after service
Homelessness: Very common in veterans
Family impact: Poor mental health in family, behavioral disorders in children, high risk of suicide/divorce
Better outcomes with pre-deployment planning (medical insurance, etc.) and communication with family while deployed

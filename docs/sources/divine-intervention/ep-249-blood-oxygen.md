# OXYGEN CONTENT FORMULA
O2 content = 1.34 x Hemoglobin x SaO2 + (0.0031 x PaO2)
SaO2: how much Hgb is saturated with O2
PaO2: O2 dissolved in plasma (does NOT involve Hgb-bound O2)
PAO2: O2 entering alveoli

# HIGH ELEVATION
Less atmospheric pressure → decreased O2 tension → low PAO2 → low PaO2 → low SaO2 (chain reaction)
Proportion of O2 in atmosphere stays same (21%)

# CARBON MONOXIDE POISONING
Primary site: hemoglobin; PAO2 and PaO2 normal but SaO2 decreased
Oxyhemoglobin curve: left-shift
Pulse ox will be NORMAL (misleading); blood appears cherry red
Tx: 100% hyperbaric oxygen
Chronic exposure → necrosis of globus pallidus (hyperintensity on imaging)

# METHEMOGLOBINEMIA
Caused by: nitrates (angina), sulfa drugs (TMP-SMX)
Fe2+ → Fe3+ (ferric); O2 can't bind; blood appears "chocolate color"
SaO2 decreased; no headache like CO
Tx: Methylene blue (methemoglobin reductase converts Fe3+ → Fe2+), Vitamin C

# INDUCED METHEMOGLOBINEMIA (for cyanide poisoning)
Cyanide inhibits Complex IV of ETC → lactic acidosis
Tx: Amyl nitrite (induces methemoglobin) → sodium thiosulfate (thiocyanate excretion) → hydroxocobalamin (B12)

# ANEMIA AND OXYGENATION
Anemic patient: PAO2, PaO2, SaO2 all FINE (lungs/vessels/saturation normal)
Problem: decreased hemoglobin → decreased O2 content → heart compensates with increased CO → HIGH OUTPUT HEART FAILURE

## Clinical applications
Hydrops fetalis: Rh incompatibility → profound anemia → heart failure → edema
Parvovirus: torches RBC precursors → anemia → high output failure
Pregnancy monitoring: increased MCA velocity on US = surrogate for anemia (less viscous blood)

# DLCO AND GAS DIFFUSION
Diffusibility ∝ area / thickness
Emphysema: decreased surface area → hypoxemia with increased A-a gradient
Pulmonary fibrosis: increased thickness → hypoxemia with increased A-a gradient

## Drug-induced pulmonary fibrosis → chronic hypoxia
Nitrofurantoin (UTI), Methotrexate (RA), Bleomycin/Busulfan (chemo), Amiodarone (AFib)

# A-a GRADIENT
= PAO2 - PaO2; PAO2 = 150 - (PaCO2 x 1.25); Normal ≤10
Elevated: PE, R-to-L shunt (PFO, VSD, Eisenmenger), HHT/AV malformations
Normal with hypoxemia: opioid overdose (decreased RR), high elevation

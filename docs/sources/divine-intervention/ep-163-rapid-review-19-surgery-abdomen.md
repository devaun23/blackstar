# Trauma Management

## Penetrating vs Blunt Trauma Algorithm

Penetrating trauma to abdomen → ex lap
Blunt trauma + rebound/guarding (peritonitis) → ex lap
Blunt trauma + stable + no rebound/guarding → CT abdomen
Blunt trauma + unstable → FAST scan (then DPL if FAST equivocal/negative)

## Retroperitoneal Hemorrhage

Cardiac cath → hours later back pain + hypotensive + unstable → retroperitoneal hematoma/hemorrhage
Dx/Tx? Emboli angiogram

## Urinary Retention / Spinal Cord Compression

MVC + minimal urine output + suprapubic tenderness/fullness → urinary retention or cauda equina syndrome
NBS? Urinary cath
Mets cancer w/ hx prostate or breast cancer + suprapubic fullness + spinal cord compression → IV dexamethasone

## Bladder Rupture

Intraperitoneal rupture (dome) + diffuse pain + peritonitis → straight to urology surgery
Extraperitoneal rupture (neck/trigone) → conservative treatment

## Spleen Rupture

MVC + left shoulder pain → spleen rupture (+ Kehr sign)
Post-splenectomy → vaccinate for SHiN organisms (Strep pneumo, H. influenzae, N. meningitidis)

## Liver Laceration / Diaphragmatic Rupture

MVC + blunt abdominal trauma + right shoulder pain → liver laceration
Diaphragmatic rupture typically on left side (abdominal contents in thoracic cavity)

## Subphrenic Abscess

Recent abdominal surgery + fever + abdominal pain for days → subphrenic abscess

## Diaphragmatic Hernia

Newborn with abdominal contents in thoracic cavity → diaphragmatic hernia
Embryo: arises from pleuroperitoneal membrane

## Traumatic Pancreatitis

Injury/MVC or child with handlebar injury + epigastric pain + elevated amylase/lipase → traumatic pancreatitis

## Duodenal Hematoma

Kid with handlebar injury + epigastric/back pain + pulsatile mass + bilious vomit + low Hgb → duodenal hematoma
Pt started on warfarin + epigastric pain + low Hgb → duodenal hematoma

## Kidney Injury

Blunt trauma to abdomen involving kidney → non-operative management (do NOT do nephrectomy)
Kidney transplant → new kidney in pelvic fossa, bad kidney is not removed


# Upper GI Surgical Emergencies

## Peptic Ulcer Perforation

PUD + sudden hypotension + low Hgb + severe abdominal pain → perforation 2/2 gastroduodenal artery laceration
NBS? EGD

## Massive GI Bleed Management

2 large-bore IV needles + IV fluids + blood products + consider surgery

## Cholecystitis

RUQ pain + shoulder pain + fever + leukocytosis → acute cholecystitis
Dx? RUQ US (then HIDA if equivocal)
Imaging: thickened GB wall + pericholecystic fluid
Tx? Lap chole + abx
Negative HIDA = GB fills with contrast → rules out acute cholecystitis

## Acalculous Cholecystitis

Elderly/critically ill + RUQ pain + fever + leukocytosis + no gallstones → acalculous cholecystitis
NBS? Percutaneous cholecystostomy (NOT cholecystectomy)
These pts are very sick (ventilators, ICU level care)

## Mirizzi Syndrome

RUQ pain + fever + leukocytosis + profound jaundice + ascending cholangitis signs + CBD NOT distended → Mirizzi syndrome
Pathophys: obstructing stone in cystic duct → cystic duct dilation → compresses common hepatic duct → direct hyperbilirubinemia
Complication: cholecystocholedochal fistula (fistula between cystic duct and common hepatic duct)
Dx? RUQ US or MRCP

## Ascending Cholangitis

RUQ pain + fever + leukocytosis + jaundice + hypotension → ascending cholangitis
NBS? ERCP (diagnostic and therapeutic)
This is an emergency!

## Choledocholithiasis

RUQ pain + no fever + dilation of CBD → choledocholithiasis
Dx? RUQ US (or MRCP if equivocal)
These pts are NOT as sick as ascending cholangitis pts


# Lower GI Surgical Emergencies

## Appendicitis

Acute-onset RLQ pain + fever + leukocytosis + positive psoas sign → acute appendicitis
NBS? Ex lap (or laparoscopic appendectomy)
Pathophys: fecolith obstructs appendiceal lumen
Perforated appendicitis → rapid appendectomy
Appendiceal abscess → abx + drain + appendectomy weeks later

PEx findings: psoas sign, obturator sign, Rovsing sign, McBurney point

Imaging (if NBME asks): CT for adults, US for children or pregnant females
Stable diabetic pt needing CT w/ contrast → discontinue metformin (risk of lactic acidosis w/ renal dysfunction)

## Psoas Abscess

7-9 days gradually worsening RLQ pain + fever + leukocytosis → psoas abscess
Dx? CT w/ contrast
Tx? Ciprofloxacin + metronidazole (or cefazolin)
Key difference: psoas abscess = DAYS of symptoms; appendicitis = ACUTE onset

## Ruptured Ectopic Pregnancy

Young adult female + RLQ pain + high fever + leukocytosis + b-HCG positive → ruptured ectopic pregnancy

## Mesenteric Ischemia

### Acute Mesenteric Ischemia

Hx recent MI + irregularly irregular rhythm → acute mesenteric ischemia (SMA)
Dx? Angiography
May need bowel resection

### Chronic Mesenteric Ischemia

LUQ pain with eating + weight loss + systemic hypotension → chronic mesenteric ischemia

## Toxic Megacolon

Hx UC + severe abdominal pain + rebound/guarding + abd distention + massive colon dilation → toxic megacolon
NBS? IV steroids (hydrocortisone, dexamethasone) → surgery if no response
Other etiologies: C. diff colitis (2/2 abx), Trypanosoma cruzi (pt from South America)

## PID / Fitz-Hugh-Curtis

Severe lower abdominal pain + bilateral adnexal tenderness → PID
Tx? Ceftriaxone + doxycycline/azithromycin
Hx PID + now RUQ pain → Fitz-Hugh-Curtis syndrome (peri-hepatitis, inflammation of hepatic capsule)

## Diverticulitis

Elderly + LLQ pain + fever + leukocytosis → diverticulitis
NBS? CT w/ contrast
Tx? Abx
Colonoscopy weeks later to r/o colon cancer
Hx diverticulitis + poop/air in urine → colovesical fistula

## Volvulus

Severe abdominal pain + coffee-bean sign head toward RUQ → sigmoid volvulus
Severe abdominal pain + coffee-bean sign head toward LUQ → cecal volvulus

## Ogilvie Syndrome (Colonic Pseudo-Obstruction)

Elderly + abdominal pain + severely distended abdomen + not passing BM/gas + distended colon but NO obstruction on CT + non-dilated small intestines → Ogilvie syndrome
Tx? NPO + NG tube decompression + rectal tube decompression
Refractory (> 48h) → neostigmine (AChE inhibitor)
Etiologies: electrolyte abnormalities (watery diarrhea + diuretic use), neurologic disorders

## Small Bowel Obstruction

Abdominal pain + bilious vomiting + not passing gas + hx appendectomy or C-section → SBO 2/2 adhesions
Etiologies: #1 adhesions (hx surgery), #2 hernias, #3 malignancy


# Vascular / Surgical Emergencies

## Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm

Smoker + severe mid-abdominal/back pain + profound hypotension + pulsatile abdominal mass → ruptured AAA
Calcifications anterior to vertebral body = ruptured or impending AAA
NBS? Immediate surgery (abdominal US if NBME asks for imaging)

### Surgery Options

Open surgical repair or EVAR

### Complications of AAA Repair

Aorto-enteric fistula: microcytic anemia weeks-months after repair (slow leak of blood into GI tract). Note: colon cancer will be incorrect answer choice.

Anterior spinal artery syndrome: multiple episodes of profound hypotension during surgery → paraplegic + cauda equina sxs/urinary retention post-op. Pathophys: ischemia to artery of Adamkiewicz → anterior 2/3 spinal cord damage.

Acute tubular necrosis / prerenal AKI: rising SCr after repair (renal ischemia during surgery)

Endoleak: post-AAA repair + slowly dropping Hgb + microcytic anemia + contrast beyond margins of excluded aneurysm on CTA

### AAA Screening

Screen: male smokers age 65-75; male smokers/non-smokers > 50 w/ first-degree relative who had AAA rupture

AAA > 5.5 cm or > 0.5 cm growth in 6 months → repair ASAP
3-4 cm → repeat US in 2 years
4-5 cm → repeat US in 1 year
5-5.5 cm → repeat US in 6 months

#1 RF for AAA? Smoking

## High-Output Heart Failure

Recent AV fistula for hemodialysis + chest pain + hypotension + crackles + EF 75% → high-output heart failure
Etiologies: AV fistula, anemia, Paget disease of bone (hypervascularization of bone marrow)


# Other Surgical Abdomen

## Pancreatitis

Tx for acute pancreatitis: NPO + IVF + pain control
#1 MCC: alcoholic pancreatitis
#2 MCC: gallstone pancreatitis (RUQ pain + elevated amylase/lipase + gallstones + ALT >= 150)

Abdominal pain + epigastric tenderness/fullness + hx pancreatitis weeks ago → pancreatic pseudocyst

Chronic epigastric pain + fat malabsorption + white dots in pancreatic walls on imaging → chronic pancreatitis
#1 RF: EtOH abuse

## Spontaneous Bacterial Peritonitis

Hx peritoneal dialysis + mild fever (100.7) + AMS + low-grade abdominal pain → SBP
Dx? Paracentesis (> 250 PMNs)
Tx? Ceftriaxone or cefotaxime
PPx for pts with ascites but no fever → fluoroquinolone

Peritoneal dialysis vs hemodialysis: no difference in outcome
Peritoneal dialysis: insert cath at least 1 month prior
Hemodialysis: insert AV fistula at least 2 months prior

## Ovarian Torsion

Young female + sudden-onset abdominal pain + cyst found weeks earlier → ovarian torsion
Tx? Urgent surgery
RF: PCOS, dermoid cyst, teratoma, prior ovarian cyst
If "free fluid in peritoneum" mentioned → pick ruptured ovarian cyst
If "free fluid" NOT mentioned → pick ovarian torsion

## Strangulated Hernia

Bulging abdominal mass for a long time + not reducible + severe abd pain → strangulated hernia
Tx? Surgery

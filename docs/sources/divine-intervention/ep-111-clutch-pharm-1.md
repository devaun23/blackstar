# Cardiovascular Pharmacology

## Nitrates
Indication? Angina. AE? Headache (due to vasodilation)

## Statins
Mechanism? Inhibit HMG CoA-reductase
AE? Hepatotoxic, Myotoxic
Interactions? Fibrates, Niacin

## Niacin
Raises HDL the best

## Cholestyramine
Mechanism? Prevent bile acid reabsorption

## Fibrates
Lower TGs. AE? Hepatotoxic, Myotoxic

## Digoxin
Mechanism? Inhibits Na/K-ATPase (positive inotrope + muscarinic agonist activity)
Indications? AFib, CHF (doesn't improve survival)
AE? Hyperkalemia. Predisposition to toxicity: Hypokalemia
Sign of dig toxicity? PVCs, vision problems, abdominal pain

## Procainamide
Class Ia antiarrhythmic. Indication? WPW. AE? Drug-induced lupus

## Amiodarone
Class III antiarrhythmic. Mechanism? K+ channel blocker
AE? Hypothyroidism/hyperthyroidism, Skin discoloration, Pulmonary fibrosis

## Beta Blockers
A-M -> selective, N-Z -> nonselective
Contraindication? Asthma
Indications? Performance anxiety, AFib

## Non-Dihydropyridine CCBs (Verapamil, Diltiazem)
Mechanism? Negative inotropes + slow conduction through AV node
Indication? AFib, Prinzmetal angina
CCB for post-SAH? Nimodipine (prevents vasospasm and resultant ischemic stroke)

## Dihydropyridine CCBs
AE? Peripheral edema (dilation of precapillary arterioles -> increased hydrostatic pressure)
How to decrease? Dilate post-cap venules with ACE-I

## Nitroprusside
Indication? Hypertensive emergency. AE? Cyanide poisoning

## Bisphosphonates
Indications? Osteoporosis, Hypercalcemia of malignancy
AE? Pill esophagitis (drink water, be upright for 30 mins), Osteonecrosis of jaw

## Teriparatide
Mechanism? PTH analog. Continuous PTH -> suppressed bone production. Pulsatile PTH -> increases bone formation
AE? Increased risk of osteosarcoma

## Uses of Magnesium
Prevent seizures in pre-eclampsia, Severe asthma attack


# Psychiatry Pharmacology

## Dopamine Agonists (Bromocriptine/Cabergoline)
Mechanism? Dopamine inhibits prolactin release (tuberoinfundibular pathway). Indication? Prolactinoma

## Typical Antipsychotics
High-potency: Haloperidol, fluphenazine, trifluoperazine
Low-potency: chlorpromazine, thioridazine
AE? QT prolongation, Hyperprolactinemia (dopamine = "prolactin-inhibiting factor"), EPS
- Acute dystonia -> Tx: Benztropine, diphenhydramine
- Akinesthesia -> Tx: Beta blocker
- Parkinsonian
- Tardive dyskinesia

## Metoclopramide
Mechanism? Dopamine antagonist. Indication? Diabetic gastroparesis. AE? EPS (e.g. drug-induced parkinsonism)

## Atypical Antipsychotics
- Risperidone -> hyperprolactinemia
- Ziprasidone -> QT prolongation
- Quetiapine -> cataracts. Special indication? Psychosis in setting of Parkinson's treatment
- Olanzapine -> metabolic syndrome. Indication aside from schizophrenia? OCD (O = olanzapine, C = clomipramine, D = antidepressants/SSRIs)
- Clozapine -> agranulocytosis, myocarditis. Decreases risk of suicide
- Aripiprazole -> partial dopamine receptor agonist

## Tricyclic Antidepressants
AE? Anti-HAM: Anti-histamine (sedation), Anti-alpha-1 (orthostatic hypotension), Antimuscarinic (urinary retention, constipation)
Tx for toxicity? Sodium bicarb
Special indications: OCD (clomipramine), Nocturnal enuresis (imipramine; other tx: desmopressin = ADH analog)

## MAO-Is
Isocarboxazid, phenelzine, tranylcypromine
MAO-B inhibitors: rasagiline, selegiline. Indication? Parkinson's
AE? Hypertensive crisis with tyramine consumption
Serotonin syndrome: High fevers + myoclonus. NBS? Supportive care + benzos. If not responding? Cyproheptadine

## SSRIs
Indications? 1st line for depression, GAD, PTSD. AE? Sexual dysfunction, Weight gain

## SNRIs (Venlafaxine, Duloxetine)
Indications? Neuropathic pain. AE? HTN

## NDRIs (Bupropion)
Benefits? No sexual side effects, no weight gain, helps with smoking cessation
Contraindications? Anorexia & bulimia (lowers seizure threshold)

## Lithium
Decreases risk of suicide
AE? Hypothyroidism, Nephrogenic DI, Tremors (sign of toxicity)

## Vaptans (Conivaptan, Tolvaptan)
Mechanism? ADH receptor antagonists. Indications? SIADH


# Renal Pharmacology

## Darbepoetin
Mechanism? EPO analog. Indications? Anemia of CKD

## Cinacalcet
Mechanism? CSR modulator -> decreased PTH release

## Loop Diuretics
Site of action? Na-K-2Cl transporter in thick ascending limb
AE? Hypokalemia, Hypocalcemia & hypercalciuria, Hypomagnesemia
Contraindication? Hx nephrolithiasis

## Thiazide Diuretics
Site of action? Na-Cl symporter in DCT
AE? Hyper-GLUC: Hyperglycemia, Hyperlipidemia, Hyperuricemia, Hypercalcemia

## Potassium-Sparing Diuretics
ENaC blockers (amiloride, triamterene). Special indication? Nephrogenic DI from lithium
Aldosterone antagonists:
- Spironolactone: AE gynecomastia (also blocks androgen receptors). Indications: Conn's syndrome, Hirsutism of PCOS (inhibits 5-alpha reductase), Improves survival in HF
- Eplerenone
Type of RTA? 4

## Drugs That Improve Survival in HF
ACE-I, Beta blockers (bisoprolol, carvedilol, ER metoprolol), Spironolactone, Isosorbide dinitrate-hydralazine in African Americans

## Acetazolamide
Mechanism? Carbonic anhydrase inhibitor
Electrolyte effects? Hypokalemia + metabolic acidosis
Indications: Central sleep apnea, Idiopathic intracranial HTN, Glaucoma, Altitude sickness
Type of RTA? 2

## Mannitol
Mechanism? Non-reabsorbable sugar. Indications? Elevated ICP, Diuretic


# Anti-Epileptic Drugs

## Ethosuximide
Mechanism? T-type CCB. Indication? Absence seizures

## Carbamazepine
Indications? Seizures (narrow spectrum), Trigeminal neuralgia. AE? Agranulocytosis

## Phenytoin
AE? NTD in fetus, Drug-induced lupus

## Valproic Acid
AE? Most teratogenic antiepileptic, Hepatotoxic


# Endocrine Pharmacology

## Hyperthyroidism Drugs
PTU: Better in pregnancy (1st trimester), Inhibits peripheral T4 -> T3 conversion, Useful in thyroid storm
Methimazole: AE agranulocytosis
Tx of thyroid storm: 1st propranolol -> 2nd PTU -> then steroids, Lugol's solution, etc.


# Antibiotic Pharmacology

## MRSA Coverage
- Vancomycin: AE: Red man syndrome (tx: slow infusion + antihistamine/NSAID), Nephrotoxic, Ototoxic
- Daptomycin: Can't be used in lungs (inhibited by surfactant). AE: Myotoxicity
- Linezolid: 50S inhibitor. AE: Serotonin syndrome
- Rifampin: AE: Orange bodily fluids
- Tigecycline

## Pseudomonas Coverage
Ceftazidime (only 3rd gen), Cefepime (4th gen), Aztreonam (little cross-reactivity with penicillins)
Aminoglycosides: AE: Nephrotoxicity (ATN), Ototoxicity
FQ: AE: QT prolongation, Achilles' tendon rupture. C/I: Pregnancy, Children
Pip-tazo
Colistin/Polymyxin E: Last resort for serious GNR infections. VERY nephrotoxic

## TMP-SMX
TMP -> inhibits dihydrofolate reductase. SMX -> inhibits dihydropteroate synthetase
Tx for bone marrow suppression? Leucovorin
PPx for PCP (CD4 < 200), PPx for toxo (CD4 < 100)

## Pyrimethamine-Sulfadiazine
Indication? Treatment of toxoplasmosis

## Macrolides
Indications: Diabetic gastroparesis (motilin receptor agonist), Atypical pneumonia (Mycoplasma, Chlamydia, Legionella), Triple therapy for H. pylori (azithromycin), PPx against MAC (CD4 < 50), Pertussis tx & ppx
AE? Diarrhea, QT prolongation, Erythromycin -> pyloric stenosis in NB

## Ceftriaxone
Indications? Meningitis, PPx of close contacts (rifampin preferred)
AE? Intrahepatic cholestasis in neonates (use cefotaxime = "baby ceftriaxone")

## Penicillins
Indications? Syphilis. AE: Jarisch-Herxheimer reaction
Aminopenicillins (PO amoxicillin, IV ampicillin): Ampicillin for Listeria (#3 cause of serious neonatal infections)

## Acyclovir
Indications? Herpes, VZV. AE? Crystalline nephropathy. If resistant? Foscarnet

## Ganciclovir
Indication? CMV. Mutation? UL97 kinase. If resistant? Foscarnet


# Autonomic Pharmacology

## Alpha-1 Blockers
Phenoxybenzamine (irreversible), Phentolamine (reversible)
Indication? Pheo (alpha block before beta block)

## Alpha-Beta Blockers
Carvedilol, Labetalol

## Alpha-1 Agonist (Phenylephrine)
Indications? Allergic rhinitis, Hypotension. AE? Nasal septum perforation

## Alpha-2 Agonists (Clonidine, Guanfacine)
Indications? Opioid withdrawal, Resistant HTN, Tourette's, ADHD

## Beta Blockers
Indications: Improve survival in HF (bisoprolol, carvedilol, metoprolol), Thyroid storm (propranolol), Glaucoma (timolol), Performance anxiety
C/I: Cocaine intoxication (unopposed alpha -> profound HTN)
Tx for toxicity? Glucagon
RAAS response? Decreased (beta-1 on afferent arteriole)


# GI Pharmacology

## PPIs
Mechanism? Irreversible inhibit Na-H antiporter on parietal cells
Indications? GERD, Barrett's, Triple/quad therapy for H. pylori (hold PPI before testing)
AE? CYP inhibition, Osteoporosis, Aspiration pneumonia

## H2 Blockers (Cimetidine)
Indications? GERD, PUD. AE? CYP inhibition, Gynecomastia (cimetidine)


# Hormonal Pharmacology

## GnRH Analogs (Leuprolide)
Indication? Shrink fibroid before surgery, Fertility tx (pulsatile), Metastatic testicular/prostate cancer (continuous)
Alternatives? Androgen-receptor blockers (flutamide, bicalutamide)

## Tamoxifen
Mechanism? SERM: Antagonist in breast, Agonist in bone & uterus
AE? Increased risk of endometrial cancer

## Raloxifene
Mechanism? SERM: Antagonist in breast, Agonist in bone, Doesn't act in uterus

## Pegvisomant
Mechanism? GH receptor antagonist. Indication? Acromegaly

## Steroids
Dexamethasone -> glucocorticoid
Hydrocortisone -> glucocorticoid & mineralocorticoid
Fludrocortisone -> mineralocorticoid


# Miscellaneous

## PDE Inhibitors (Sildenafil, Tadalafil)
Indications? ED, Pulmonary arterial HTN. C/I: Pts on other vasodilators

## Aspirin
Mechanism? Irreversible COX-1/COX-2 inhibitor. Indication? Chest pain 1st drug
AE? PUD (prevention: misoprostol)

## P2Y12 Blockers (Clopidogrel, Prasugrel, Ticagrelor)
Indications? Post-stroke, Post-MI

## Dipyridamole
Mechanism? Increases cAMP. Indication? Stress test (coronary steal principle)

## Cilostazol
Mechanism? Increases cAMP. Indication? Symptomatic relief of PAD

## GpIIbIIIa Antagonists (Abciximab, Tirofiban, Eptifibatide)
Mechanism? Prevents platelet aggregation

## Heparin
Mechanism? Activates AT-III. Reversal? Protamine sulfate (for UFH)
AE? HIT. NBS? Direct thrombin inhibitor (dabigatran, argatroban, bivalirudin)
Reversal of dabigatran? Idarucizumab

## Factor Xa Inhibitors (Apixaban, Rivaroxaban)

## Warfarin
Mechanism? Inhibits VKOR -> decreases gamma-carboxylation of factors 2, 7, 9, 10, protein C & S
Bridge with heparin (avoid transient hypercoagulability)
Reversal? Vit K, Four-factor PCC

## Nitrofurantoin
Indication? Cystitis in females. AE? Pulmonary fibrosis
Drugs associated with pulmonary fibrosis: Busulfan, Bleomycin (G2 phase), Amiodarone

## Methotrexate
Mechanism? Dihydrofolate reductase inhibitor. Rescue? Leucovorin. AE? Hepatotoxic
Indications? Autoimmune dz (RA), Choriocarcinoma, Ectopic pregnancy

## Cyclophosphamide
Mechanism? Alkylating agent. AE? Hemorrhagic cystitis (prevention: mesna), Bladder cancer

## Cisplatin
AE? Ototoxicity, Nephrotoxic (ATN). Prevention? Amifostine

## Vinca Alkaloids (Vincristine, Vinblastine)
Mechanism? Prevent polymerization of microtubules. AE? Peripheral neuropathy

## Taxanes
Mechanism? Prevent depolymerization of microtubules

## TNF Inhibitors (Adalimumab, Etanercept)
Check for latent TB before starting

## ATRA
Indication? APML (t(15,17), DIC risk)

## Imatinib
Indication? CML (t(9,22), BCR-ABL)

## Eculizumab
Mechanism? Monoclonal Ab against C5. Indication? PNH. Vaccine against? Neisseria meningitidis

## Adenosine
Indication? Break SVT, Regadenoson for cardiac stress tests
Antagonized by? Caffeine, Theophylline

## Octreotide
Indication? Variceal bleed. Give spironolactone + propranolol to reduce recurrence risk


# Diabetes Medications

## Insulin
Ultra rapid-acting: lispro, aspart, glulisine. Rapid-acting: regular. Intermediate-acting: NPH. Long-acting: detemir & glargine

## Metformin
Weight neutral. Stop before contrast (risk of lactic acidosis if renal injury)

## Sulfonylureas
Mechanism? K+ channel blockers -> close ATP-dependent K+ channel -> insulin release. Weight gain
2nd gen: glyburide, glipizide, glimepiride

## PPAR-gamma (Pioglitazone, Rosiglitazone)
C/I: CHF. Weight gain

## Alpha-glucosidase Inhibitors (Acarbose, Miglitol)
AE? Diarrhea

## GLP-1 Analogs (Exenatide, Liraglutide)
C/I: MTC. AE? Pancreatitis. Liraglutide also for weight loss

## DPP4 Inhibitors (Sitagliptin)
C/I: MTC

## SGLT-2 Inhibitors (Canagliflozin, Dapagliflozin)
C/I: Renal failure. AE? UTIs

Cross checked: No

# DYSPHAGIA

Difficulty swallowing, lost weight NBS: EGD
Alarm symptoms: lost weight, dysphagia, odynophagia, max medical therapy w/o improvement, >50Y
Difficulty initiating swallowing NBS: video fluoroscopy ~ barium swallow → Oropharyngeal dysphagia (assoc w neuromuscular dz)

## Zenker's diverticulum
Regurgitation of undigested food, halitosis
Et: cricopharyngeal muscle dysfunction; Dx: Barium swallow; c/I EGD (r/o perforation)

## Achalasia
Dysphagia to solids and liquids (decreased peristalsis)
RF: Chagas disease; Dx: barium swallow ("bird's beak sign") → esophageal manometry → EGD (r/o pseudo-achalasia)
c/b SCC of esophagus; c/I pneumatic dilation (perforation)
Tx: CCB, botox, surgical myotomy + PPI

# GERD
Heartburn, cough worsened in morning, hoarseness, asthma-like sx
NBS/Tx (if no alarm sx): PPI; GERD + ANY other sx → EGD
Dx: 1. EGD 2. Esophageal pH monitor
c/b Barrett's esophagus → Tx: endoscopic resection + ablation, PPI; F/u: routine EGD q6-12mo
→ Esophageal adenocarcinoma (RF: Barrett's, GERD)

# ESOPHAGITIS IN HIV
Candidal esophagitis (MCC) → Tx: azole lozenges, nystatin
CMV → Tx: ganciclovir, foscarnet
HSV → Tx: acyclovir/valacyclovir, foscarnet

## Pill-induced esophagitis
Bisphosphonates, tetracycline, ibuprofen, NSAIDs, potassium, iron tablets; Tx: lots of water

## Eosinophilic esophagitis
Young pt with dysphagia, GERD; Dx: EGD ("crap ton of eosinophils"); Tx: corticosteroid therapy

# PEPTIC ULCER DISEASE
Bloating, burning, gnawing in epigastrium, feel full early
Et: H. pylori, NSAIDs; Dx: urea breath test, stool antigen test
Tx: "CAP" or "MBTP"; c/b perforation, hemoptysis (esp if ulcer erodes gastroduodenal artery)
Chronic steroid therapy → requires bisphosphonates + PPI ppx (steroids → gastric acid + avascular necrosis)

# GASTROPARESIS
Diabetic, early satiety; Other causes: hypothyroidism, scleroderma, chronic opioids, daily diphenhydramine
Tx: erythromycin (motilin-receptor agonist), metoclopramide (dopamine antagonist, AE: parkinsonism)

# DUMPING SYNDROME
Weight loss surgery, cramping pain, nausea, sweating, light-headed
Tx: smaller meals more frequently, low-carb (prevent hyperinsulinemia)

# PANCREATITIS
Sudden-onset epigastric pain radiating to back
RF: EtOH, gallstones (4 F's), familial hypertriglyceridemia (>1000), stavudine, didanosine, valproic acid, CF, hypercalcemia, GLP-1 agonist
Dx: 2/3 severe abd pain, lipase 3x normal, CT; Tx: pain control, IVF; F/u: Ranson's criteria (CA HOBBES)
c/b: pancreatic pseudocyst, chronic pancreatitis (tx: fat-soluble vitamins + enzymes), peri-pancreatic abscess

## Autoimmune pancreatitis
Hypothyroidism, Graves, vitiligo, Addison's + recurrent epigastric pain
Et: IgG4 = pancreatitis, cholecystitis, retroperitoneal fibrosis
Dx: CT scan "sausage-shaped pancreas"; Tx: corticosteroids

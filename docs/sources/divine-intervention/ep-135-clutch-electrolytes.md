# Calcium

## Hypocalcemia
Sx: Chvostek sign @ jaw, Trousseau sign @ upper extremities

## Hypercalcemia
Tx: 1. fluids 2. calcitonin
Pathophys: high Ca messes with signaling cascade of ADH → ~nephrogenic DI~ → low volume
@hospital: think about malignancy!
@outpt: think about primary hyperparathyroid!

## ACEi Effects
Pt placed on ACEi → high renin, high K, metabolic acidosis
ACEi → decreased conversion AT1-AT2 → low aldosterone → volume down → positive feedback on RAAS
Aldosterone → H+ pump

## Hypercalcemia — Short QT interval

### Primary Hyperparathyroidism
Parathyroid adenoma: PTH secretion → osteoclasts → reabsorb bone
Vs parathyroid hyperplasia (MEN)
NBS: Check ionized Ca, PTH, Tch-99 sestamibi scan
Sx/dx: High Ca, low PO4 (PTH trashes phosphate), High urine Ca
Tx: parathyroidectomy — "CHIMPANZEES"

### Familial Hypocalciuric Hypercalcemia (FHH)
Pathophys: calcium-sensing receptor does not respond to right signals
Sx/dx: high PTH, high Ca, low PO4, low urine Ca
Receptor does not function well → reabsorb tons of Ca from urine
Tx: benign!

### CKD
Pathophys: no activity of 1-alpha-hydroxylase → impaired conversion of calcidiol to calcitriol → cannot reabsorb Ca, PO4 in gut
Sx: High PTH, High Ca, high PO4 (cannot excrete through kidneys)
Tx: sevelamer = binds PO4

### Tertiary Hyperparathyroidism (Post-Transplant)
Glands of parathyroid undergo hyperplasia due to CKD (they're "always on!") → no longer respond to normal signals
Sx/dx: high PTH, high Ca, low PO4 (new kidneys)
Tx: resect 3.5 parathyroid glands, re-implant 0.5 gland; cinacalcet (if poor surgical candidate)

### Chronic Liver Disease
Pathophys: no calcidiol (comes from liver) → decreased calcitriol → cannot reabsorb Ca, PO4
Sx/dx: High PTH, High Ca, low PO4 (kidneys still functioning!)

### Sarcoidosis
AA with bilateral hilar adenopathy
Pathophys: noncaseating granulomas → 1-alpha-hydroxylase → calcitriol → reabsorption in gut → high Ca → low PTH

### Lung SCC (Paraneoplastic)
Heavy smoker, cavitary lesion, hypercalcemia
Pathophys: paraneoplastic PTHrP → high Ca, low PO4, low PTH
Dx: "keratin pearls" on histology

### MEN Syndromes
MEN2B: medullary thyroid cancer, Marfanoid habitus, mucosal neuromas, pheochromocytoma
Dx: calcitonin → tones down calcium! Low Ca. Ppx: resect thyroids
MEN2A: primary hyperparathyroid, pheo, MTC
MEN1: primary hyperpara, pituitary adenoma, pancreatic neuroendocrine (VIPoma, insulinoma, glucagonoma, gastrinoma)
Tx insulinoma: diazoxide (open K channels to prevent insulin secretion)

### EDTA (Transfusion-Related)
Large volume blood transfusion, carpopedal spasms, seizures
Anticoag that chelates Ca → hypocalcemia
Sx: dilutional thrombocytopenia → bleeding
Tx: calcium gluconate (Also used for hypermagnesemia, hyperkalemia)

### Other Causes of Hypocalcemia
Rhabdomyolysis: necrosed skeletal muscle fibers sequester Ca
Pancreatitis: saponification reaction by fatty acids
Infants of diabetic mothers: low Ca, low glucose
DiGeorge syndrome: failure of 3rd/4th pharyngeal pouches → low Ca, recurrent infections, hypocalcemic seizures

### Hypervitaminosis D
Sx/dx: high Ca, variable PO4, low PTH — Increased reabsorption of Ca, PO4 in gut

### Hypercalcemia of Malignancy
Tx: bisphosphonates
Multiple myeloma: plasma cells secrete IL-1 (osteoclast-activating factor) → reabsorbs bone → high Ca

## Magnesium-Calcium Interaction
High Mg → inhibits PTH → low Ca
Low Mg → PTH → high Ca
Very low Mg → inhibits PTH → low Ca
Won't respond to Ca (or K) depletion w/o Mg repletion!

## Thiazide Diuretics
Sx/dx: high Ca, low urine Ca
Pathophys: inhibits Na/Ca exchanger @ DCT
Good for kidney stones, osteoporosis!

## Loop Diuretics
Sx/dx: low Ca, high urine Ca
Pathophys: inhibits NKCC transporter @loop of Henle → no extrusion of K → no reabsorption of Ca
C/I: kidney stones

## Low Albumin States
Nephrotic syndrome, Menetrier's disease (protein-losing gastropathy): low albumin → low Ca, normal ionized Ca

# Zinc

Low zinc Sx: impaired taste, impaired wound healing, loss of hair
DDx: Nutritional, Trientine (zinc chelator, used in Wilson's disease tx)

# Magnesium

## Low Mg
Sx/dx: prolonged QT interval ("hypo___" causes prolonged QT!)
DDx: Alcoholics (hypomagnesemia, folate deficiency, B1/thiamine deficiency), Refeeding syndrome
*Hypophosphatemia is the killer in refeeding syndrome!

## High Mg
Sx/dx: 1. decreased DTR 2. respiratory depression 3. CV collapse
DDx: Pre-eclampsia with Mg infusion
Tx: calcium gluconate
Also used for premature labor, preterm infant

# Potassium

## RAAS Pathway
Hypoperfusion @ JG cells → Renin → AT1 → AT2 (@ ACE in lung capillaries)
AT2: Vasoconstrictor @ type1 receptors, Stimulates aldosterone from zona glomerulosa, Stimulates ADH from supraoptic nucleus, Efferent arteriolar constriction to maintain GFR

## Low K
Sx: flat T waves, U waves, prolonged QT interval
DDx: anything that increases RAAS activity!

### Hypovolemic
Pathophys: decreased perfusion of afferent arteriole → JG cells → renin → aldosterone → principal cell → reabsorb Na, dump K

### Conn Syndrome
Sx: resistant hypertension, metabolic alkalosis
Pathophys: adrenal adenoma making aldosterone → reabsorb Na, waste H, waste K → volume expansion, metabolic alkalosis
Hyperperfusion inhibits renin
Dx: increased plasma aldosterone:renin ratio
Vs: renal artery stenosis/FMD/NSAID = hypoperfusion → RAAS → low K, high Na, metabolic alkalosis, normal aldosterone:renin ratio

### Diuretic-Induced
Diuretic → decreased volume → hypoperfusion → RAAS
Sx/dx: low K, metabolic alkalosis (proton pumps @ alpha-intercalated cells)
*low K does not always mean alkalosis*

### Acetazolamide/Dorzolamide
Pathophys: inhibits carbonic anhydrase → inhibits reabsorption of HCO3 → dumping HCO3 in urine → NAGMA (Type 2 RTA) with volume depletion → RAAS
Sx/dx: low K, metabolic acidosis, volume depletion
Uses: Volume overload, Central sleep apnea, IIH/pseudotumor cerebri, Glaucoma, Altitude sickness

### Hyperventilation
Pathophys: respiratory alkalosis → pulls H+ from cells → K enters cells
Also binds up positive charges (Ca) → low Ca

## High K
Sx: peaked T waves → widened QRS (also caused by TCA!) → sinusoidal EKG → flat line/asystole → death

### Tx Hyperkalemia
Calcium gluconate
Insulin + glucose (activate Na/K pump)
Albuterol/beta agonist (activate Na/K pump)
Sodium bicarb (induce alkalosis)
Kayexalate, loop diuretic

### Addison's Disease
Sx: orthostatic hypotension, low Na, high K, NAGMA (Type IV RTA, positive urine AG), eosinophilia
Vs. diarrhea NAGMA: urine AG negative/"neGUTive"
Pathophys: autoimmune destruction of adrenal glands → cannot produce aldosterone

### Rhabdomyolysis
Muscle cells dying → release K
Ppx: telemetry to track fatal arrhythmia
Tx: hydration, monitor EKG

### Tumor Lysis Syndrome
Lymphoma/leukemia cells dying → release K

### Potassium-Sparing Diuretics
Spironolactone/eplerenone (inhibit aldosterone receptor)
Amiloride/triamterene (inhibit ENaC)
Can treat nephrogenic DI 2/2 lithium

# Sodium

Serum osmolarity = 2(Na) + glucose/18 + BUN/2.8 — Na is the greatest contributor

## Low Na + Low Serum Osmolarity

### Low Volume
DDx: Diuretics (inhibit NKCC, losing more Na than water → high urine Na), Vomiting (low volume → RAAS → reabsorb Na → low urine Na)
Tx: fluids

### Normal Volume
DDx:
SIADH (also seen with early gen sulfonylurea, clozapine, SSRI) → ADH → reabsorb water → low serum osm, high urine osm
Tx: fluid restriction, ADH antagonist (-vaptan)
Hypothyroidism
Psychogenic polydipsia (also seen in MDMA) → excess water → dilution → decreased ADH → low serum osm, low urine osm
Tea and toast diet

### High Volume
DDx: Cirrhosis, nephrotic syndrome, renal failure (low oncotic pressure → not perfusing vasculature → RAAS)
CHF: effective blood volume low → not perfusing kidneys → RAAS
High aldosterone: increase Na + H2O, High ADH: increase H2O
Dx: low urine Na (*high urine Na in renal failure)
Tx: restrict fluid

## High Na
DDx: Diabetes insipidus — Not enough ADH activity → peeing out a ton → high serum osm, low urine osm

### DDx DI
Central: closed head injury
Nephrogenic: lithium, demeclocycline, hypocalcemia

### Dx DI
Water deprivation test: Urine osm goes up = normal; Barely increases = DI
Desmopressin: Urine osm goes up = Central DI; Barely increases = Nephrogenic DI

### Desmopressin Uses
Central DI, vWD (increased release of vWF from WP bodies), mild hemophilia A, nocturnal enuresis, kidney-associated coagulopathy (uremia prevents degranulation of platelets)
Vs. coagulopathy of liver disease (tx: FFP)
AE: hyponatremic seizure

### Tx Hypernatremia
Normal saline, D5W
High → low: cerebral edema!
*Think about this in overly rapid correction of glucose in DKA, HHNK
Low → high: pons will die! (central pontine myelinolysis)

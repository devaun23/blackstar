# EMBRYOLOGY
Thyroid from base of tongue (foramen cecum, endoderm) → thyroglossal duct
Midline neck mass + moves with swallowing → thyroglossal duct cyst
Lateral neck mass + doesn't move with swallowing → branchial cleft cyst (ectoderm)
No thyroid on CT but normal labs → lingual thyroid
Newborn hypothyroidism MCC → thyroid dysgenesis (large tongue, umbilical hernia)

# THYROID NODULE WORKUP
Neck lump → NBS: TSH
Low TSH (hot nodule) → RAIU scan
- Global uptake → Graves disease (#1 cause hyperthyroid; Tx: methimazole/PTU or radioactive iodine, NOT thyroidectomy)
- One hot spot → toxic adenoma
- Multiple hot spots → toxic multinodular goiter
Normal/high TSH (cold nodule) → US then FNA (75% colloid cysts, 20% could be cancer)

# HYPERTHYROID DIFFERENTIALS
Cold RAIU + recent URI + tender thyroid → Subacute (De Quervain's) thyroiditis
Cold RAIU + exogenous thyroid → factitious
DDx: Thyroglobulin (De Quervain's = high, factitious = low) — "C-peptide of thyroid"
Cold RAIU + adnexal mass → Struma ovarii (ectopic thyroid tissue)

# THYROID CANCER
## Papillary (most common)
#1 RF: prior head/neck radiation; Histology: psammoma bodies, Orphan Annie nuclei
Spreads via lymphatics; great prognosis

## Follicular
Spreads hematogenously (can metastasize to brain)
FNA can't distinguish follicular adenoma from carcinoma → lobectomy needed

## Medullary
Neck mass + seizures + low Ca + prolonged QT + family hx → MEN 2A/2B
Tumor marker: calcitonin; Congo red staining positive
MEN 2A/2B → prophylactic thyroidectomy (100% risk of MTC)

## Anaplastic
Elderly + widespread firm neck mass; awful prognosis

# EUTHYROID SICK SYNDROME
Very sick ICU patient: normal TSH, normal T4, decreased T3, increased reverse T3
Body conserving energy by decreasing metabolic rate

# HASHIMOTO'S
MCC hypothyroidism in adults; T3/T4 low, TSH high; often hx of other autoimmune disease
Rapidly expanding neck mass + germinal follicles on biopsy → Thyroid lymphoma

# THYROID STORM
Decompensating Graves' pt: very hypertensive, tachycardic, AMS
Tx: 1. Beta blocker (propranolol) → 2. PTU → 3. SSKI (Wolff-Chaikoff effect) → 4. Steroids

# HYPOTHYROID CONSEQUENCES
Low HR (fibrosis of conducting system), decreased fertility (↑TRH → ↑prolactin → ↓GnRH)

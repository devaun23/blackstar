# PREGNANCY SCREENING
Gestational diabetes: screen at 24 weeks (1-hr glucose → if + → 3-hr GTT)
HIV: first prenatal visit + retest 3rd trimester
HBV: first visit (HBsAg)
Syphilis: first visit
Asymptomatic bacteriuria: treat with nitrofurantoin or 1st/2nd gen cephalosporin + test of cure
GBS: 35-37 weeks (anal + vaginal)
Rh status + antibody (indirect Coombs): Rhogam at 28 weeks + within 3 days postpartum

# SMOKERS
AAA: men only, ages 65-75, US (>5.5cm → intervention)
Osteoporosis: can start screening earlier than 65
Lung cancer: ages 50-80, ≥20 pack-year hx, currently smoking or quit <15 yrs ago → low-dose CT x3 years

# GENETIC CONDITIONS
Familial hypercholesterolemia: check LDL at ages 3, 9, 18 (rule of 3)
IBD: screen for colon cancer 8 years after dx, then every 1-3 years; PSC → screen at diagnosis then q1-2y
Lynch syndrome: colon cancer screening at age 20, q1-2y with colonoscopy + aspirin ppx; endometrial cancer screening at 30 with biopsy q1-2y; offer prophylactic THBSO after childbearing
MEN 2A/2B: prophylactic thyroidectomy (100% MTC risk)
FAP: colon cancer screening at age 10, every year (APC → Kras → p53 → adenocarcinoma)
BRCA: breast mammogram annually ages 25-29; age 30+ → annual breast MRI + mammography

# RISKY SEXUAL GROUP (HIV, MSM, sex workers, IVDU)
Ages 15-64: annual HIV
Syphilis: q3 months
Chlamydia/Gonorrhea: screen WOMEN only; high-risk annually; ALL women <24 annually
HepB surface antigen: annually (also antivaxxers)

# METABOLIC DISEASES
HTN: ages 18-40 every 3-5y; >40 every 1-2y
Diabetes: obese ages 40-70 q6mo (FBG, OGTT, or HbA1c); HTN >135/80 + >45 → q6mo
Osteoporosis: women at age 65 with DEXA scan (T-score ≤-2.5 → treat)
Hyperlipidemia: initial at 20; Men: 20-45 q5y → 45-65 q1-2y → >65 annually; Women: 20-55 q5y → 55-65 q1-2y → >65 annually; Diabetics: annually (LDL ≥70 → high-intensity statin)

# CANCER SCREENING
Colon cancer: age 45-75; colonoscopy q10y OR flex sig q5y OR FIT/FOBT annually; if abnormal → colonoscopy with biopsy
Breast cancer: ACS → mammogram at 40, annually; USPSTF → mammogram 50-75, q2y
Cervical cancer: 21-29 Pap q3y; 30-65 preferred Pap + HPV q5y; HIV/immunodeficient → Pap annually; hysterectomy for benign → stop; for malignant → vaginal cuff Paps; stop at 65 if normal history

# CHILDREN
Language difficulties → audiometry
Ages 3-5 → screen for amblyopia

# MISCELLANEOUS
Women <24 → annual chlamydia/gonorrhea
HepB screening annually: dialysis, chronic transfusions, chronic immunosuppression
HepC: all ages 18-79 once
Depression: PHQ-9 or Beck Depression Inventory

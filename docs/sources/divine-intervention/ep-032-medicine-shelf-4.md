# Hypokalemia

What 2 "big-picture" mechanisms result in hypokalemia?
Anything that makes you volume-down → which causes RAAS activation
Anything that causes afferent arteriole hypoperfusion (e.g. NSAIDs which cause afferent a. vasoconstriction)

## RTA and Hypokalemia

Which 2 RTA's cause hypokalemia?
Type I RTA = defect in acid/H+ secretion at DCT
DCT unable to secrete H+ → therefore, H+/K+ antiporter at DCT cannot run → so, H+ builds up inside DCT cells while K+ remains in lumen → hypokalemia
Type II RTA = defect in bicarb reabsorption at PCT
2 mechanisms
1. PCT unable to reabsorb HCO3- → so, HCO3-/Na+ co-transporter at PCT cannot run → cannot take up Na+ or water → excess loss of Na+ and water from PCT to the tubular lumen → activation of RAAS → ↑ALDO → Na+ and water are reabsorbed more distally in nephron while K+ is secreted
2. Excess HCO3- in lumen combines with K+ in lumen → so, even less K+ is available to be taken up by nephron!

Which RTA causes hyperkalemia? Type IV RTA (hypoaldosteronism → K+ builds up → hyperkalemia)

## Primary Hyperaldosteronism (Conn Syndrome)

Hypokalemia + metabolic alkalosis + unrelenting HTN (refractory to tx) → Primary Hyperaldosteronism aka Conn syndrome
Pathophys? Aldosterone-secreting adrenal adenoma
HTN is 2/2 reabsorption of Na+ and water
Metabolic alkalosis is 2/2 aldosterone causing upregulation of H+-ATPase
What candy mimics Primary Hyperaldosteronism? Licorice (i.e. glycyrrhizic acid)
Mechanism? Inhibits 11-betaHSD-2 → so, cortisol cannot be inactivated into cortisone → excess cortisol acts like aldosterone → hypokalemia + metabolic acidosis

## Diuretics and Potassium

Most diuretics cause hypokalemia + metabolic alkalosis
However, which diuretic causes hypokalemia + metabolic acidosis? Acetazolamide
MOA? CAI (carbonic anhydrase inhibitor) → results in bicarb being dumped into urine

## Hypoxic Respiratory Drive
Why is mechanical ventilation with SpO2 ~99% contraindicated in COPD? B/c COPD pt's depend on hypoxic respiratory drive
COPD pt's chronically retain high CO2 → respiratory drive switches from hypercapnia-driven to hypoxia-driven → if supplemental O2 causes high SpO2, hypoxic respiratory drive is shut off → no more respiration

## Hypocalcemia

Which causes hypocalcemia: metabolic acidosis or metabolic alkalosis? Metabolic alkalosis
"Metabolic alkaLOWsis" = LOW calcium
Mechanism? Metabolic alkalosis causes excess negative charges on albumin → albumin binds up more serum Ca2+

# Hyponatremia

## Hyper-Osmolar Hyponatremia
MC Etiology? Hyperglycemia (e.g. DKA or HHNS)
Corrected Serum Na+ = [Na+] + [1.6 x [(glucose - 100)/100]]

## Iso-Osmolar Hyponatremia
Etiologies? Hyperlipidemia and hyperproteinemia (Lab artifact)

## Hypo-Osmolar Hyponatremia
Next step? Assess volume status

### Hypervolemic Hyponatremia
Mechanism? Activation of both RAAS and ADH → reabsorb way more water than Na+
Etiologies: Cirrhosis (UNa <10), Right-sided HF (UNa <10), Nephrotic syndrome (UNa <10), Kidney failure (UNa >10)
Tx? Restrict fluids

### Euvolemic Hyponatremia
Etiologies:
SIADH – ↓Serum OsM + ↑ Urine Osm (> 100)
Psychogenic polydipsia – ↓Serum OsM + ↓ Urine Osm (< 100)
Hypothyroidism
Beer potomania/tea-and-toast diet
Tx? Fluid restriction

### Hypovolemic Hyponatremia
Mechanism? Pt loses more Na+ than water
Etiologies: Loop diuretics (UNa >10), Vomiting/diarrhea (UNa <10), Addison disease
Tx? Normal Saline aka 0.9% NS

# Hypernatremia

## Hypervolemic Hypernatremia
Mechanism? Net gain of Na+ in excess of water
Etiologies: Infusion of Na+-containing abx, Excess sodium bicarb, Excess NaCl, Primary hyperaldosteronism
Tx? NS until euvolemic then D5W or 0.45% saline

## Hypovolemic Hypernatremia
Mechanism? Net loss of water in excess of loss of Na+
Etiologies: Osmotic diuresis, Sweating, Diarrhea, Vomiting
Tx? NS until euvolemic then D5W or 0.45% saline

## Euvolemic Hypernatremia
Mechanism? Net loss of only water (pure water loss)
Etiologies: Fever (insensible water loss), Diabetes insipidus
Tx? D5W

# Anion Gap and Acid-Base

## AG Metabolic Acidosis — "MUDPILES"
Methanol, Uremia, DKA, Propylene glycol/Paraldehyde, Iron/INH, Lactic acidosis, Ethanol, Salicylic acid

## Non-Anion-Gap Metabolic Acidosis
RTAs, Diarrhea

## NAGMA Workup Algorithm
Step 1: Confirmed NAGMA
Step 2: Check Urine AG
Negative urine AG = diarrhea ("NeGUTive")
Positive urine AG = RTA
Step 3: If positive, check K+
↑ K+ = Type IV RTA
↓ K+ = Type I or Type II RTA
Step 4: If low K+, check urine pH
Urine pH < 5.5 = Type II RTA
Urine pH > 5.5 = Type I RTA

## Winter's Formula
When? In metabolic acidosis to check respiratory compensation
Formula: Expected pCO2 = (1.5 x bicarb) + 8 ± 2
If pCO2 > expected → concurrent respiratory acidosis
If pCO2 < expected → concurrent respiratory alkalosis

## Etiologies of Metabolic Alkalosis
Drugs (Diuretics), Disease (Bartter's/Gitelman's, Conn syndrome), Volume depletion

# Urology

Hematuria + smoker OR Egyptian with schistosomiasis OR cyclophosphamide use → bladder cancer
Dx? cystoscopy
HY causes of hemorrhagic cystitis: Cyclophosphamide, Adenovirus, Schistosoma haematobium
Prevent hemorrhagic cystitis from cyclophosphamide? Mesna!
Type from schistosomiasis? Squamous cell carcinoma

# Nephrology

## Indications for Dialysis — "AEIOU"
A = Acidosis, E = Electrolyte imbalance, I = Ingestion, O = fluid Overload, U = Uremia

Complications of uremia? Uremic pericarditis and platelet dysfunction (Tx: Desmopressin)
First-use syndrome → anaphylactic reaction to dialysis membrane
Amyloidosis in chronic dialysis → β2 microglobulin
Peritoneal dialysis + abdominal pain + fever → SBP

## Pyelonephritis
Flank pain + CVA tenderness + high fevers + urgency/frequency/dysuria
Urine Casts? WBC casts
Tx? Ceftriaxone or cipro
MCC? E. coli

## FSGS
Classic demographic: African Americans, HIV+, Heroin use, Obesity
HIV variant? Collapsing variant FSGS
EM? Effacement of foot processes
Tx? Steroids + ACE-I +/- cyclophosphamide (poor response to steroids)

## Nephritic Syndromes / RPGN by IF
Linear IF = Goodpasture Syndrome (anti-GBM Ab, young male + hemoptysis + hematuria)
Granular IF = PSGN (cola-colored urine 2-6 wks after Strep, subepithelial humps on EM); Lupus nephritis/DPGN (wire-loops on EM)
Negative IF (pauci-immune) = GPA, MPA, Churg-Strauss

## IgA Nephropathy (Berger Disease)
Hematuria 2-6 days after URI + recurrent hematuria

## Alport Syndrome
Hematuria + hearing loss + ocular disturbances — Inherited Type IV collagen defect

# Vasculitides

## Henoch-Schonlein Purpura
Child + joint pain + abdominal pain + palpable purpura on butt/LE's + hematuria after URI — Associated with IgA nephropathy

## GPA (Wegener's)
Sinusitis + hemoptysis + hematuria + c-ANCA — Pauci-immune IF

## EGPA (Churg-Strauss)
Asthma + eosinophilia + granulomas (lungs) + p-ANCA

## Microscopic Polyangiitis
Hemoptysis + hematuria + p-ANCA
DDx EGPA vs MPA: EGPA has asthma + eosinophilia

## Polyarteritis Nodosa
HBV + asymmetric neuro deficits + abdominal pain + ANCA-negative + segmental transmural inflammation
Lungs unaffected! Dx? Sural nerve biopsy

## Takayasu Arteritis
Japanese female + abdominal pain + >10 mmHg SBP difference between arms + fevers + elevated ESR/CRP
Alternate presentation: "weak or absent pulse in UE"

# Cardiology — Arrhythmias and Hemodynamics

## Cardiac Tamponade
JVD + hypotension + muffled heart sounds → Tx? Pericardiocentesis

## Constrictive Pericarditis
Pericardial knock + JVP increase with inspiration (+Kussmaul sign) + reduced EDV
JVP: Blunted Y-descent = tamponade, Steep Y-descent = constrictive pericarditis
Normal JVP height? < 4 cm above sternal angle

## AVRT
Orthodromic AVRT — Down AV node, back up accessory pathway → Narrow QRS
Acute Tx: HDUS → Synchronized cardioversion; Stable → Adenosine
Chronic Tx: Beta-blocker or ND-CCB

Antidromic AVRT — Down accessory pathway, back up AV node → Wide QRS
Classic cause? WPW (Delta waves!)
Tx? Procainamide
Contraindicated: Beta-blocker or ND-CCB

## Prolonged QT
Drugs: Ondansetron, Haloperidol, Ziprasidone, Quinidine/disopyramide/procainamide, Amiodarone/Sotalol, FQ, Macrolides
Electrolytes: Hypokalemia, Hypocalcemia, Hypomagnesemia
Risk? Torsade de Pointes → Tx: IV Mag

## Cardiovascular Pharmacology
Milrinone ("inodilator"): ↑CO, ↑SBP, ↓SVR, ↓DBP, Widens pulse pressure

## AV Fistula
Knife wound + warm pulsating mass + EF 75% → high output HF 2/2 AV fistula

# Pneumothorax

Primary spontaneous: Tall skinny male, Emphysema
Tension: Trauma pt, Ventilated pt (barotrauma), Central line placement
Auscultation: Decreased breath sounds, Hyperresonant to percussion
Tx stable → O2 & observe
Tx unstable → needle thoracostomy then chest tube at 2nd mid-clavicular line at top of ICS

# GI / Hepatology

## Diarrhea Associations
Cruise ship → norovirus
AIDS → Cryptosporidium parvum
2 hrs after potato salad → Staph aureus
Daycare → rotavirus
Oysters → Vibrio parahaemolyticus (Tx: Doxycycline, Severe in liver disease)
Fried rice → Bacillus cereus
Pork → Yersinia enterocolitica
Poultry/eggs → Salmonella
Common cause in US → Campylobacter

## Colon Cancer
Screening: Start at 50 yo — Colonoscopy q10y, Flex sig q5y, FOBT q1y
1st-degree relative: Screen at 40 yo or 10 years before relative's dx age (whichever earlier)
Screen after UC diagnosis? 8 years after
Multiple polyps → FAP (APC gene)
Multiple polyps + brain tumor → Turcot syndrome
Multiple polyps + bone/soft-tissue tumors → Gardner syndrome
Early colon cancer from normal mucosa → HNPCC/Lynch (MSH/MLH)
Hyperpigmented lip macules + GI hamartomas → Peutz-Jeghers

## GERD
Tx? PPIs
AEs: Aspiration pneumonia, Osteoporosis, B12 deficiency
AE of cimetidine? Gynecomastia (men)
H. pylori Tx: Triple (PPI + amoxicillin + clarithromycin) or Quad (metro + bismuth + tetracycline + PPI)

## Hepatic Adenoma
35 yo F + 15-year OCP use + RUQ pain + well-circumscribed hepatic mass

## Whipple's Disease
60 yo M + bilateral knee pain + chronic diarrhea + PAS+ macrophages + villous atrophy + new murmur
Microbe? Tropheryma whipplei
Tx? Ceftriaxone 2 weeks then TMP-SMX 1 year
PAS+ in liver → alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency
PAS+ in GI → Whipple's disease

## Zenker Diverticulum
60 yo M + halitosis + difficulty swallowing + palpable neck mass on swallow
Dx? Barium swallow (Do NOT do upper endoscopy — can perforate)

## Diffuse Esophageal Spasm
Difficulty swallowing + chest pain radiating to jaw + relieved with nitrates
Dx? Manometry
Tx? CCBs or nitrates or TCAs

## Glucagonoma
47 yo F with refractory DM + necrotic erythematous skin lesion
Associated syndrome? MEN1
Tx? Surgery or octreotide

## Diabetic Gastroparesis
Tx? Metoclopramide or Erythromycin (motilin receptor agonist)
AE? Extrapyramidal symptoms (tardive dyskinesia)

## Ogilvie Syndrome
60 yo + sepsis + severe abdominal pain/distension + dilated colon without obstruction
Tx? Neostigmine (AE: bradycardia)

## Hepatorenal Syndrome
Elevated SCr + oliguria + ESLD
Pathophys: Cirrhosis → splanchnic vasodilation → steals blood from kidneys
Urine labs similar to prerenal AKI (FeNa <1%, BUN/Cr >20)
Tx? Liver transplant
DDx from prerenal: No improvement with fluids = hepatorenal; Improvement = prerenal

## Hepatopulmonary Syndrome
Hypoxia on standing + SpO2 improves lying down + ESLD → Platypnea-Orthodeoxia syndrome

## Zollinger-Ellison Syndrome
MEN1 + jejunal ulcers + refractory GERD
Dx? Gastrin level → Secretin stimulation test
Tx: Medical = PPIs, Surgical = resection

## Autoimmune Hepatitis
Type 1 = anti-SM Ab's (anti-smooth muscle)
Type 2 = anti-LKM Ab's (anti-liver kidney microsomal)

## UC vs Crohn's
UC: Bloody diarrhea + continuous lesions, a/w PSC (men)
Crohn's: Skip lesions + non-caseating granulomas, Oxalate kidney stones, Pyoderma gangrenosum, Uveitis

## Hemochromatosis
Joint disease? CPPD aka pseudogout
Dx? Rhomboid-shaped with apple-green birefringence on Congo stain

## Pancreatic Cancer
Dx? CT scan

## Budd-Chiari Syndrome
Thrombosis of hepatic vein
Classic populations: OCP users, Polycythemia vera, PNH

## APAP Overdose
Tx? N-acetylcysteine
Mechanism of toxicity? Elevated NAPQI levels

## Lactose Intolerance
Brush border enzyme deficiency in Asian immigrant + abdominal pain + bloating after cheese
Pathophys? Lactase deficiency
Dx? Hydrogen breath test (↑ H+ ions)

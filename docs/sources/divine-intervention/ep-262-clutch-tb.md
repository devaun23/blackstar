# SCREENING
TB skin test or IGRA
BCG-vaccinated or non-compliant patient → prefer IGRA
TB skin test results interpreted same way regardless of BCG status

## Induration cutoffs
5mm: immunocompromised (HIV, transplant, chronic prednisone, TB contact)
10mm: immigrants <5 yrs, IVDU, crowded settings (jail/nursing home), HCW
15mm: positive in everyone

TST/IGRA cannot differentiate latent from active TB

# LATENT TB (3 criteria)
Positive TST/IGRA + negative CXR + no systemic manifestations
Primary Tx: INH + B6 for 9 months
Alternatives: INH + B6 for 6 months, or Rifampin alone for 4 months

# ACTIVE TB
B symptoms: night sweats, fever, cough, hemoptysis, chest pain
Dx: Induced sputum or BAL → AFB smear (negative does not rule out!); Gold standard: sputum culture
If AFB negative and no culture option → PCR/NAAT testing
Tx: RIPE + B6 for 2 months → INH + B6 + Rifampin for 4 months

## Isolation criteria for release
≥2 weeks of therapy + clinically improving + ≥3 consecutive negative AFB smears

## Pre-treatment checks
Uric acid (before pyrazinamide — can trigger gout)
Ophthalmology (visual acuity + color vision before ethambutol)
LFTs

## CXR findings
Cavitary lesion, unilateral pleural effusion, miliary TB, unilateral hilar lymphadenopathy

# EXTRAPULMONARY TB
CNS: TB meningitis — CSF: high opening pressure, elevated lymphocytes, high protein, low glucose; enhancement at base of brain; Dx: AFB/PCR of CSF; Tx: RIPE + B6 + steroids
Bone: Pott's disease (vertebral osteomyelitis)

# MAC (Mycobacterium Avium Complex)
HIV + CD4 <50: MAC ppx no longer routinely recommended
Non-HIV MAC: older thin white female + connective tissue disorder + pectus excavatum/scoliosis + MVP
Tx: macrolides (check QT interval first)

# HCW PROTECTION
N95 respirators; negative pressure room when available

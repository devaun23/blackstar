# AORTIC STENOSIS

Elderly pt passes out while mowing lawn → aortic stenosis
NBSIM: Echocardiogram (for ANY suspected valve pathology)
S/Sx: Systolic ejection murmur (SEM) + radiates to carotids
Auscultation: RUSB at 2nd intercostal space
Pathophys: Calcification of valve
Note: AS in young pts = bicuspid aortic valve
Pulse: Pulsus parvus et tardus ("late") d/t trouble getting blood out of ventricle
LV Hypertrophy: Concentric (sarcomeres in parallel) → HFpEF
Mechanism: Pressure overload → concentric hypertrophy → decreased lumen of LV → diastolic dysfunction

## Maneuvers
Increase intensity: Rapid squatting (increases venous return, preload, afterload)
Decrease intensity: Valsalva phase II (decreases preload), Standing up (decreases preload)

## Treatment
Valve replacement

# HOCM

Age: Young adult
Auscultation: LLSB
Murmur: SEM
Pulse: Pulsus bisferiens d/t mitral valve moving towards LVOT mid-systole ("bifid carotid pulse" = 1. small 2. big)

## Maneuvers
Increase intensity: Valsalva phase II (decreases preload), Standing up (decreases preload)
Decrease intensity: Rapid squatting (increases venous return, preload, afterload), Handgrip (increases afterload)
Note: Both increased preload AND increased afterload decrease HOCM intensity

## Treatment
Defibrillator, Beta-blockers (slow HR → more time for LV to fill)

# MITRAL STENOSIS

Diastolic murmur at apex → mitral stenosis
RF: Rheumatic fever
MC complication: AFib (LA contracts against increased resistance → dilation of LA → disrupts electrical conduction)
Upper GI complication: Dysphagia (dilated LA compresses esophagus)

# MITRAL REGURGITATION

Holosystolic murmur at apex + radiates to axilla → mitral regurgitation

## Maneuvers
Increase intensity: Handgrip (increases afterload)
Decrease intensity: Valsalva phase II (decreases preload), Standing up (decreases preload)

# MITRAL VALVE PROLAPSE

Mid-systolic murmur at apex with opening snap/click → MVP
RF: ADPKD, Marfan syndrome, Ehlers-Danlos syndrome
Pathophys: Myxomatous degeneration

## Maneuvers
Increase intensity (earlier click): Valsalva phase II (decreases preload), Standing up (decreases preload)
Decrease intensity (later click): Rapid squatting (increases venous return, preload, afterload), Handgrip (increases afterload)

# AORTIC REGURGITATION

Diastolic blowing murmur at RUSB → aortic regurgitation
RF: Takayasu, Tertiary syphilis (vasa vasorum of aortic arch), Connective tissue disorder, Retrograde aortic dissection, Marfan, Ehlers-Danlos
Effect on Pulse Pressure: Widened
Wide pulse pressure in adults = aortic regurgitation
Wide pulse pressure in kids = PDA
LV Hypertrophy: Eccentric (sarcomeres in series) → HFrEF
Mechanism: Chronic volume overload → eccentric hypertrophy → dilation → systolic dysfunction

# AORTIC DISSECTION

Severe sudden onset chest pain with radiation to back → aortic dissection
RF: HTN
S/Sx: >10 mmHg BP difference between arms, mediastinal widening, unilateral pleural effusion + chest pain, elevated BP
Dx:
Stable pt → CTA (contraindicated in renal insufficiency)
Unstable pt → TTE
Tx:
Stanford Type A (ascending aorta) → surgery
Stanford Type B (does NOT involve ascending) → Propranolol
Do NOT use hydralazine (reflex tachycardia → vasodilation → baroreceptor activation)
Increased HR = increased shear stress on aortic valve = increased risk of worsening dissection
Pathophys: Cystic medial degeneration/necrosis

# ENDOCARDITIS

IVDU + fevers + 10lb weight loss + murmur at LLSB → tricuspid valve endocarditis
NBSIM: Blood culture
Dx: Echocardiogram
Tx: IV abx for 6 weeks
Etiology: Staph aureus (MCC IVDU endocarditis) → veins drain to R side (tricuspid valve)

## Endocarditis by Etiology
Recent dental procedure → Strep viridans (subacute endocarditis); PPx: oral amoxicillin
Culture-negative → Coxiella burnetii
Hemolytic anemia + malar rash → SLE Libman-Sacks endocarditis
Strep bovis → NBS: Colonoscopy (S. bovis bacteremia indicates colon cancer)
Weeks-months after valve replacement → Staph epidermidis (biofilm)
Recent immigration + untreated URT → GAS Rheumatic fever

# PERIPHERAL ARTERIAL DISEASE

70 yo heavy smoker with severe BLE pain when walking → PAD
NBSIM: ABI (ABI < 0.9 indicates PAD!)
ABI > 1.1 → Monckeberg arteriosclerosis 2/2 T2DM (calcified vessels) → NBS: toe brachial index
Tx: Supervised walking program, Cilostazol (PDE inhibitor → increased cAMP → smooth muscle relaxation), Arteriography → bypass

## Occlusion by Location
Femoral artery → pain at thigh
Popliteal artery → pain at leg
Leriche syndrome → occlusion of internal + external iliac branches of aorta + impotence + buttock pain
RF: Smoking

# ABDOMINAL AORTIC ANEURYSM

Long-term smoker + severe back/abdominal pain + pulsatile abdominal mass + hypotensive → ruptured AAA
RF: Smoking
Dx: Bedside ultrasound or CTA
Screening: NBSIM for 65-75 yo M with h/o smoking or FMHx AAA → abd US (one-time screening)
>5.5 cm, growing >0.5 cm/6mo or >1 cm/yr, symptomatic → surgery

## Complications of AAA Surgery
AKI d/t transient hypoperfusion of kidneys (AAA at infrarenal aorta near renal artery)
Anterior spinal artery syndrome d/t infarction of Artery of Adamkiewicz → paraplegic/quadriplegic; all spinal cord knocked out except dorsal columns (vibration/fine touch)
Endoleak → contrast going past contours of aneurysm stent
Aortoenteric fistula → dropping Hgb s/p weeks or months, heme-occult positive stools

# DVT

Bed-bound hospitalized patient + severe unilateral leg pain/tenderness → DVT
RF: Virchow's triad (stasis, hypercoagulability, endothelial dysfunction)
NBS & Dx: Duplex scan of LE
Tx: Heparin or IVC filter (prevent PE)

## Effort Thrombosis (Paget-Schroetter)
Young male athlete (pitcher or weight-lifter) + sudden-onset arm swelling + heaviness + pain → spontaneous upper extremity DVT
Tx: Thrombolysis and/or 3 months anticoagulation

# ACUTE CORONARY SYNDROME

| Type | Chest Pain | Troponin | ST Elevation |
|------|-----------|----------|--------------|
| STEMI | + | + | + |
| NSTEMI | + | + | - |
| Unstable Angina | + | - | - |

Note: Stable angina is NOT an "acute coronary syndrome"
MCC death s/p 48h MI: VFib

## Post-MI Complications
Recent MI + diffuse STE + chest pain worsened with inspiration/lying down + relieved sitting up → Pericarditis
Post-MI pericarditis = days after MI
Dressler's (autoimmune pericarditis) = weeks after MI
Tx: NSAIDs, Colchicine, Steroids (do NOT give NSAIDs or colchicine in renal failure)

## Workup
Unstable angina NBSIM: Coronary angiography
Stable angina NBSIM: Echo stress test (if known cardiac anomaly)
Exercise stress test preferred over pharmacological
Pharm stress test: Adenosine/dipyridamole/dobutamine (for obese pts)
Adenosine contraindicated in asthma/COPD
Dobutamine contraindicated in arrhythmias

# WPW

Short PR segment + wide QRS + palpitations → WPW
Pathophys: Direct communication from atria to ventricles via bundle of Kent
Tx: Procainamide

# CHRONIC VENOUS INSUFFICIENCY

Severe leg pain worse at end of day + painless ulcer above medial malleolus → chronic venous insufficiency
Dx: Duplex U/S of LE
Pathophys: Incompetence of venous valves
Sx: Painless ulcer, stasis dermatitis (d/t blood pooling)
Tx: Leg compression (Unna boots), leg elevation

# VENTRICULAR TACHYCARDIA

Wide-complex regular tachyarrhythmia → VTach
Tx:
Pulseless VTach → defibrillation (unsynchronized cardioversion)
VTach + HDUS → synchronized cardioversion
VTach + HDS → amiodarone

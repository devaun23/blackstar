# Parasites

Bloody dysentery and liver abscess -> Entamoeba histolytica
Foul-smelling diarrhea and recently drank contaminated water; presents in pts with CVID or IgA deficiency -> Giardia lamblia -> do stool ova and parasites
Diarrhea in AIDS patient with acid-fast oocysts in stool -> Cryptosporidium parvum
Vaginal infection with green discharge and pH >4.5 -> Trichomonas vaginalis. Give PO metronidazole to both partners
Recently came back from developing country with thrombocytopenia, anemia, fevers every 48hrs -> P. vivax and ovale. What if every 72 hours -> P. malariae
Which Plasmodium with dominant stage in liver -> P. vivax and ovale
Patient plays with cats at home with ring-enhancing brain lesions -> T. gondii
Indirect hyperbilirubinemia with Hgb of 8 and recently treated for malaria -> G6PD deficiency
Diarrhea after consuming pork -> T. solium
Cysts in brain from pork -> neurocysticercosis; consuming eggs associated with this
Child that itches a lot especially his buttocks -> Enterobius vermicularis. Remember scotch tape test
Microcytic anemia and colonoscopy shows worms attaching to intestines -> Necator americanus or Ancylostoma duodenale
Recently came back from Egypt and recent swimming expedition -> Schistosoma haematobium
Eggs with terminal spine and came back from Egypt -> Schistosoma haematobium
Liver abscess and consumes snails -> Schistosoma mansoni or japonicum


# Fungi

Pt immunocompromised and diffuse markings in CXR -> PCP and silver stain +; if A-a gradient > 35 or PaO2 <70 give steroids with TMP-SMX
White lesions in tongue of individual on chemo -> Thrush
Meningitis in immunocompromised -> Cryptococcus neoformans. Remember latex agglutination and India ink stain
Ohio/Kentucky with pulm infx -> H. capsulatum
What are the bugs that cause tinea infxns -> Trichophyton, Microsporum, Epidermophyton; pick Trichophyton as answer mainly
California with pulmonary infection and spherules on histology -> Coccidioides immitis; erythema nodosum associated with this
Immunocompromised pt and 10% eosinophils with RUL fungus ball -> Aspergillus fumigatus -> must bx to r/o cancer
- Causes of eosinophilia: Drugs, Addison's dz, Lymphoma, Collagen vascular dzs like scleroderma, parasites
- Order of CBC with decreasing frequency: neutrophils, lymphocytes, monocytes, eosinophils, basophils ("Never Let Monkeys Eat Bananas")
Lesions in lung, bone, skin and see broad-based bud -> Blastomycosis
Gardener with lesions on one extremity and associated LAD -> Sporothrix
T1DM in DKA with eschars in face -> Mucormycosis; histology shows aseptate hyphae branching at 90 degrees vs 45 degrees is Aspergillus; in Aspergillus "A" for acute angle

# Bacteria - GI

Mexico with watery diarrhea -> ETEC

# Meningitis

Febrile acute onset with nuchal rigidity -> Meningitis
- College student with skin lesions -> Neisseria
- Signs of increased ICP must do head CT first to r/o mass lesion b/c LP will worsen them
- If no signs of elevated ICP then LP -> will see gram-negative diplococci
- Ceftriaxone for N. meningitidis; may empirically treat with ceftriaxone and vancomycin
- Intubated a pt with meningitis you must take ppx like rifampin, ciprofloxacin, or ceftriaxone; rifampin 1st line but if pregnant then ceftriaxone
- Whooping cough ppx -> azithromycin
- Meningitis in >60 -> S. pneumo
- Meningitis in unimmunized infant -> S. pneumo as well
- Meningitis in neonate -> GBS
- Neonate with encephalitis and mom works in pet stores -> Toxoplasma


# STDs

Ataxic, loss sensation, sexually active female -> Tabes dorsalis (tertiary syphilis)
Pt with encephalitis weeks after undercooked pork -> T. solium
HIV pt CT scan shows ring-enhancing lesions -> T. gondii. PPx with TMP-SMX; seen in CD4 count <100. Treatment with pyrimethamine and sulfadiazine
Sexually active guy with arthritis and skin petechiae -> N. gonorrhoeae
- N. meningitidis with Waterhouse-Friderichsen syndrome -> hyperkalemia, hypotension, hyponatremia, metabolic acidosis
Painless chancre -> T. pallidum; indurated raised edges
Painless ulcers in genitals no inguinal LAD -> Klebsiella granulomatis; intracellular Donovan bodies; painless ulcer with beefy-red base
Histology shows epithelial cells with inclusion bodies -> HSV-2
Genital warts -> HPV 6/11
Urethral discharge gram stain is gram-negative diplococci -> N. gonorrhoeae
Painless ulcer and becomes massive painful LAD -> Lymphogranuloma venereum (Chlamydia trachomatis L1-L3)
STI with dark-field microscopy -> T. pallidum
Painful ulcer with inguinal LAD -> H. ducreyi
Painful vesicles -> HSV-2
Septic arthritis and urethral discharge -> N. gonorrhoeae
STI tx with macrolide or doxycycline -> Chlamydia
- Chlamydia A-C type causes trachoma (most common cause of blindness in developing world)
- D-K causes STIs
- L1-L3 for painless ulcer and inguinal LAD
Give ceftriaxone and doxy for N. gonorrhoeae infxn


# Viral Infections

Large testicles and large parotid gland -> Mumps (can also cause pancreatitis)
Sickle-cell pt with low Hgb -> Parvovirus causing aplastic crisis
Permanent flaccid paralysis in unvaccinated child -> Polio
Most common bacterial cause in STDs in U.S. -> Chlamydia D-K
Most common cause of UTI -> E. coli then S. saprophyticus
Elevated liver enzymes in pregnant women that then dies -> HEV
Influenza virus has segmented genome (reason for epidemics/pandemics)
Sore throat with viral conjunctivitis -> Adenovirus; assoc with hemorrhagic cystitis and gastroenteritis
Viral myocarditis -> Coxsackie B; A is assoc with hand-foot-mouth dz
Conjunctivitis, jaundice after visiting Hawaii -> Leptospirosis
Kid that goes to day-care and has rash -> Parvovirus B19
Cough, coryza, rash, conjunctivitis, Koplik spots -> Measles; cephalocaudal rash
Kid with gastroenteritis and immigrant and becomes quadraplegic -> Poliovirus (enterovirus)
Pt with cervical cancer and sexually active -> HPV 16, 18, 31, 33; most common cause of death in pt with cervical cancer is renal failure!
Triad of parotitis, orchitis, infertility -> Mumps
Cataracts, blindness in newborn -> Rubella. Assoc with PDA = wide pulse pressure
Painful skin lesion in dermatomal pattern -> VZV. Stays dormant in dorsal root ganglia. Tx with acyclovir; if resistant then foscarnet (pyrophosphate analog)
Genital warts -> HPV 6, 11 condyloma acuminata
Retinitis or colitis in AIDS pt or recent transplant -> CMV with intranuclear inclusion bodies. Tx with ganciclovir
Painful vesicles on erythematous base -> HSV-2; HSV-1 associated with oral ulcer
Pregnant women with high ALT/AST and high mortality -> Hep E
Watery diarrhea in cruise ship -> Norovirus ("Norwalk virus")
Young sexually active female feeling wiped and LUQ mass/splenomegaly, atypical lymphocytes, posterior LAD -> EBV; don't be hung up with posterior cervical LAD, they write questions with anterior as well
Kid with really bad watery diarrhea -> Rotavirus. Assoc with genetic reassortment. Vaccine is c/i in pts with hx of intussusception or Meckel's diverticulum
Two most common causes of common cold -> Rhinovirus and Coronavirus
Pneumonia with expiratory wheeze in <2yrs -> RSV; if high-risk ppx with Palivizumab; UWorld says if premature <29wks, chronic lung dz, or unstable CHD
Fevers, myalgia around December -> Influenza
Pt bitten by an animal and has encephalitis and hydrophobia -> Rabies (rhabdovirus)
Neonate with encephalitis and LP shows RBC or temporal enhancement on CT -> HSV with elevated lymphocytes on LP


# Rash Patterns

Erythema chronicum migrans -> Lyme dz
Erythema marginatum -> Group A Strep
Erythema infectiosum -> Slapped-cheek finding with Parvovirus
Ecthyma gangrenosum -> Pseudomonas
Kid with choreiform movement with arms and sore throat recently -> PANDAS (Pediatric Autoimmune Neuropsych Disorder Assoc with Group A Strep)
Rash on palms soles and trunk with recent painless chancre -> Syphilis
Sandpaper rash on trunk, strawberry tongue -> Scarlet fever (Group A Strep)
Rash starts on face and descends caudally -> Measles
Rash on palms and soles and migrates centrally -> RMSF. Give doxy even if <7yrs old but if pregnant give chloramphenicol
Rash palms soles lesions in mouth -> Coxsackie A
Rash lasting for 3 days -> Rubella. Posterior auricular lymphadenopathy


# Pneumonia By Demographics

Pt 2-4 age range with pneumonia -> RSV
Pneumonia in teens -> Mycoplasma
Elderly pneumonia -> S. pneumo
HIV with interstitial infiltrates -> PCP. But if lobar consolidation then S. pneumo
Pneumonia in alcoholic -> Klebsiella
Alcoholic with foul-smelling sputum -> Anaerobic pneumonia
Pneumonia with interstitial infiltrate and pet store owner/bird handler -> Chlamydia psittaci
Live in Kentucky/Ohio went into caves for spelunking -> Histoplasma
Pneumonia recently visited New Mexico -> Coccidioides
Pneumonia with rose-colored sputum -> S. pneumo
Red-currant jelly sputum -> Klebsiella
Pneumonia, hyponatremia, diarrhea -> Legionella
Pneumonia and handle cow poop -> Coxiella burnetii (Q fever); also culture-negative endocarditis
CF with pneumonia <20 -> S. aureus. If >20 -> Pseudomonas or Burkholderia cepacia
3 most common causes of atypical pneumonia -> Mycoplasma, Chlamydia, Legionella. Cover with macrolides (has M, C, L in it)
- Erythromycin is a motilin receptor agonist used for gastroparesis; macrolides prolong QT interval and inhibit 50S ribosome
Pneumonia in neonate and find gram+ cocci -> GBS
Sputum of gram-negative rods in neonate -> E. coli
Pneumonia and expiratory wheeze in neonate -> RSV (not stridor which is either croup or epiglottitis)
FBI worker or postal worker with widened mediastinum on CXR -> Bacillus anthracis (polyglutamate capsule)
Person with pneumonia then with lung abscess/cavitations -> S. aureus
Pneumonia in IV drug abuser and septic emboli -> S. aureus
Pneumonia with worsening of sxs -> S. aureus
Classic anaerobes: Peptostreptococcus, Fusobacterium, Bacteroides


# Diarrhea By Presentation

Pt with watery diarrhea and recent travel -> ETEC
Watery diarrhea that's rice-water -> Vibrio
Hiker or camper diarrhea -> Giardia
Cruise ship diarrhea -> Norovirus
Diarrhea in infant -> Rotavirus
Diarrhea in immunosuppressed -> Cryptosporidium. Tx with nitazoxanide
Bloody diarrhea after eating beef -> C. jejuni or EHEC/Shigella. Remember HUS and relation with schistocytes and hemolytic anemia
Bloody diarrhea after poultry or eggs -> Salmonella
Bloody diarrhea that's a lactose fermenter -> EHEC
Most common cause of bloody diarrhea in US -> C. jejuni (may get GBS: symmetric ascending paralysis)
Pt with diarrhea and recent tx of anaerobic infx -> C. diff
Diarrhea that feels like appendicitis in LLQ pain -> Yersinia enterocolitica
High yield protozoal cause of bloody diarrhea on NBMEs -> Entamoeba histolytica
Bloody diarrhea that needs just 10 or so bugs to cause it -> Shigella
Bloody diarrhea with fever, low Hgb, low platelets -> EHEC
Watery diarrhea after oysters or seafood -> V. parahaemolyticus
Diarrhea after oysters and elevated AST/ALT especially in those with liver dz -> V. vulnificus
Watery diarrhea after swimming in fresh water or aquarium -> Aeromonas ("A aquarium A Aeromonas")
Diarrhea with massive amounts of electrolyte abnormalities -> V. cholera
Korean BBQ with watery diarrhea especially fried rice -> B. cereus
Diarrhea after two hours having potato salad -> S. aureus (also vomiting)
C. perfringens also may cause diarrhea
Swims in freshwater then dies after -> Naegleria fowleri


# Endocarditis

Fever and new-onset murmur in IV drug abuser -> S. aureus
Fever and recent dental procedure -> S. viridans
Fever and heart murmur in prosthetic valve -> S. epidermidis
Fever and heart murmur after sore throat -> S. pyogenes
If it comes from UTI -> Enterococcus
S. pyogenes IE attacks left side of heart vs S. aureus on right side of the heart
You cannot prevent PSGN with penicillin
Sterile vegetations on each side of the valve -> Libman-Sacks endocarditis (assoc with SLE)

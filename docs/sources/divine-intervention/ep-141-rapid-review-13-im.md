# Surgical Abdomen

## Cholecystitis

40 yo F, BMI 40, upper abdominal pain + fever 102F + positive Murphy's sign → acute cholecystitis
Dx? RUQ US w/ GB wall thickening + pericholecystic fluid
If US negative → HIDA scan (don't see GB at 90 min → acute cholecystitis)
Tx? Lap chole

Pt on TPN or in ICU, RUQ US shows characteristic findings but no stones → acalculous cholecystitis
Tx? Percutaneous cholecystostomy
Do NOT perform cholecystectomy (increased mortality)

## Mirizzi Syndrome

Pt with stone in cystic duct + dilation of intrahepatic bile ducts → Mirizzi syndrome

## Ascending Cholangitis

RUQ pain + fever + AMS + scleral icterus + BP 80/48 → ascending cholangitis
Charcot triad = RUQ pain, fever, jaundice
Reynolds pentad = Charcot triad + AMS + hypotension
Dx? ERCP
Tx? ERCP + abx

## Subphrenic Abscess

Pt s/p spleen repair for traumatic injury, now with referred pain to L shoulder & fevers → subphrenic abscess
Tx? Drain abscess

## Splenic Venous Thrombosis

Pt recently treated for acute pancreatitis, isolated gastric varices on imaging/endoscopy → splenic venous thrombosis

## Weird Thromboses

Renal vein thrombosis → association: nephrotic syndromes (esp membranous nephropathy)
Hepatic vein thrombosis = Budd-Chiari syndrome → associations: OCPs, polycythemia vera

## Peptic Ulcer Disease

Pt with hx OA presenting with epigastric pain → peptic ulcer disease

## Pancreatitis

Alcoholic pt with pain radiating to back → acute pancreatitis
Drugs that cause pancreatitis: didanosine, stavudine, GLP-1 agonists (exenatide, liraglutide), DPP4 inhibitors (sitagliptin)
Associations: alcoholism, gallstones, sickle cell, IgG4-related dz (pancreas looks like sausage on imaging), retroperitoneal fibrosis

## Appendicitis

Periumbilical pain progressing to RLQ → acute appendicitis

## Nephrolithiasis

Flank pain radiating to groin + hematuria → nephrolithiasis

## Ectopic Pregnancy

23 yo F with severe RLQ pain + inconsistent condom use → ectopic pregnancy

## Ovarian Torsion

15 yo F with 5 cm simple ovarian cyst, now with sudden-onset severe abdominal pain → ovarian torsion
Contrast with ruptured cyst: will have free fluid in peritoneal cavity on USMLEs

## PID

Cervical motion tenderness + vaginal discharge → PID
Fitz-Hugh-Curtis syndrome = involvement of hepatic capsule
Bugs? CT & NG
Tx? Ceftriaxone + azithro/doxy
Don't give IUD recently after PID episode

## Diverticulitis

80 yo F with LLQ pain + fever → diverticulitis
Dx? CT w/ contrast
Colonoscopy weeks later to r/o CRC

80 yo F with LLQ pain + pneumaturia or fecal matter in urine → colovesical fistula 2/2 diverticulitis

## Typhlitis

Pt on chemo with thickened cecum → typhlitis
Pathophys: severe inflammation of cecum (similar to necrotizing enterocolitis)


# Hematology

## Hemolytic Uremic Syndrome (HUS)

MAHA + thrombocytopenia + renal failure → HUS
Bugs? O157:H7 E. coli or Shigella

## Coagulation Profiles

| Condition | Platelet Count | Bleeding Time | PT | PTT | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|-----|-----|-------|
| HUS | Low | — | — | — | MAHA + renal failure |
| Hemophilia A/B | Normal | Normal | Normal | High | X-linked recessive. A = factor VIII, B = factor IX |
| von Willebrand Disease | Normal | Prolonged | Normal | High | vWF binds/protects factor VIII. Ristocetin test assesses Gp1b-vWF interaction |
| DIC | Low (consumption) | Prolonged | High | High | Coagulation factors consumed |
| Warfarin | Normal | Normal | High | High | Affects factors 2, 7, 9, 10. Reversal: Vit K, four-factor PCC |
| Heparin | Normal | Normal | High | High | Affects factors 2 & 10 (common pathway). Reversal: protamine sulfate |
| Aspirin | Normal | Prolonged | Normal | Normal | Irreversibly inhibits COX → impairs platelet function |
| Liver disease | Low (sequestration) | — | High | High | Low factor production + splenomegaly |

Ristocetin test abnormal in: VWD & Bernard-Soulier syndrome


# Pulmonology

## Pleural Effusions

45 yo F with hx HIV, bilateral pedal & periorbital edema, decreased breath sounds + dullness to percussion + UA 4+ protein + CXR bilateral blunting of costophrenic angles → transudative effusion 2/2 FSGS
Pathophys: protein loss in urine → decreased oncotic pressure → transudative effusions

Causes of transudative effusions: CHF, cirrhosis (low albumin), nephrotic syndromes, Menetrier disease (protein-losing enteropathy), PE (can cause both)

Causes of exudative effusions: malignancy, PE

Light's criteria (transudative if ALL met):
- Pleural protein / serum protein < 0.5
- Pleural LDH / serum LDH < 0.6
- Effusion LDH < 2/3 ULN serum LDH

## ARDS

55 yo M s/p type A aortic dissection 2 days ago, progressive dyspnea + pH 7.47 / pCO2 44 / PaO2 58 + CVP 8 + PCWP 14 + BNP 97 + bilateral crackles + CXR pulmonary edema ("batwing appearance") → ARDS
Pathophys? Increased pulmonary vascular permeability
PCWP must be < 18 to make ARDS diagnosis (non-cardiogenic pulmonary edema)

ARDS diagnostic criteria:
- PaO2/FiO2 < 300
- CXR with bilateral infiltrates
- PCWP < 18

Tx? Ventilation with high PEEP & low tidal volumes


# Diarrhea Extravaganza

## Watery Diarrhea

Recent travel to Mexico → ETEC
Rice-water stools → Vibrio cholerae. Tx: rehydration with ORS (salt & sugar) + macrolide or tetracycline
Hiker/camper, IgA deficiency, or Bruton's agammaglobulinemia → Giardia
Cruise ship → norovirus
Infant → rotavirus. Vaccine contraindicated if hx intussusception, Meckel's, or HSP
AIDS pt → cryptosporidium. Special stain: acid-fast oocysts. Tx: paromomycin, nitazoxanide
Fried rice → Bacillus cereus
Potato salad, vomiting 2 hrs later → Staph aureus

## Bloody Diarrhea

After beef consumption → EHEC, Shigella, Campylobacter
Campylobacter = most common cause of bloody diarrhea in US
Campylobacter associated with GBS → Dx: albuminocytologic dissociation in CSF. Tx: plasmapheresis or IVIG
After abx → C. diff. Tx: oral vancomycin
RLQ pain + hx pork consumption → Yersinia enterocolitica ("pseudoappendicitis")
Bloody diarrhea + liver abscess → Entamoeba histolytica. Tx: metronidazole + inject iodoquinol into abscess
Blood diarrhea + low Hgb + low platelets + elevated Cr → HUS (Shigella or EHEC)
Shigella has very low microbe requirements for infection

## Other GI Infections

After oyster/seafood consumption → Vibrio parahaemolyticus
Pt with liver dz, consumes oysters, now severely ill → Vibrio vulnificus
Swam in freshwater then died soon after → Naegleria fowleri


# Poisoning & Overdose

## AChE Inhibitor / Organophosphate Poisoning

44 yo M brings 4 yo son to ED, 4 hr hx profuse sweating + drooling + watery diarrhea, BP 89/58 + HR 33 + T 99.1 + RR 8, diffuse wheezing + bilateral pupillary constriction, mother with MG → AChE inhibitor poisoning
Organophosphates inhibit AChE → similar presentation
Tx? Atropine (anticholinergic, blocks muscarinic receptors)

## Overdose Reversal Agents

| Toxicity | Antidote |
|----------|----------|
| Anticholinergic (jimson weed, atropine) | Physostigmine (cholinesterase inhibitor) |
| Benzodiazepines (+ zolpidem, zaleplon, eszopiclone) | Flumazenil |
| Opioids | Naloxone |


# IBS Treatment

IBS will not have any PE or lab abnormalities

IBS-D treatment: loperamide (synthetic opioid), TCA (anticholinergic activity)
IBS-C treatment: stool softener, lubiprostone (chloride channel activator → secretory diarrhea)

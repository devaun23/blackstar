# THYROID NODULE ALGORITHM

Palpable thyroid nodule on PE → NBS: Order TSH level

## Low TSH (Hot Nodule = Low Risk Cancer)
NBS: RAIU scan
Single hot spot → toxic adenoma
Multiple hot spots → toxic multinodular goiter
Diffusely increased uptake → Graves disease

## Normal/High TSH (Cold Nodule)
Usually benign (70-75% are colloid cysts) but some are malignant
NBS: Thyroid US w/ FNA to check for thyroid cancer

# THYROID CANCER

## Papillary Thyroid Carcinoma
Histology: Psammoma bodies, Orphan Annie eye nuclei
#1 RF: Hx of head/neck radiation
Spread: Lymphatic
Best prognosis

## Follicular Thyroid Carcinoma
Spread: Hematogenous

## Medullary Thyroid Carcinoma
Histology: Amyloid, apple green birefringence on Congo red stain
Poor prognosis
Paraneoplastic syndrome: Hypocalcemia 2/2 increased calcitonin
EKG finding: Prolonged QT interval
Clinical picture: Pt with neck mass + multiple relatives with thyroid cancer → medullary thyroid carcinoma 2/2 MEN2A or MEN2B

# THYROID DISEASES

## Hashimoto's Thyroiditis
Thyroid biopsy full of lymphoid follicles → Hashimoto's thyroiditis (aka lymphocytic thyroiditis)
#1 cause of hypothyroidism in US
Sxs: Bradycardia + fatigue + weight gain + hair loss + high cholesterol
Note: Initially causes increased T3/T4 but eventually decreased T3/T4
Progresses to: Thyroid lymphoma (pt with hx Hashimoto's + enlarging thyroid mass)

## Hypothyroidism vs Hyperthyroidism
Hypothyroidism: Bradycardia + fatigue + weight gain + hair loss + high cholesterol
Hyperthyroidism: Tachycardia + weight loss + heat intolerance + insomnia + irritability

## Factitious Hyperthyroidism
Hyperthyroid sxs + low TSH + low uptake on RAIU → factitious hyperthyroidism (exogenous thyroid hormone)
Pathophys: Excess exogenous T3/T4 suppresses endogenous TSH

## Subacute/De Quervain's Thyroiditis
Tender/painful thyroid + low TSH + low uptake on RAIU → subacute thyroiditis
Pathophys: Diffuse inflammatory process causes release of preformed thyroid hormone

## DDx Subacute Thyroiditis vs Factitious Hyperthyroidism
Subacute/De Quervain's: HIGH thyroglobulin levels
Exogenous thyroid hormone: LOW thyroglobulin levels
Thyroglobulin is the "c-peptide of the thyroid" (akin to C-peptide for exogenous insulin)

## Congenital Hypothyroidism
Newborn with umbilical hernia + macroglossia → congenital hypothyroidism
MC cause: Thyroid dysgenesis

# MEN SYNDROMES

## MEN 1 — "3 P's"
Pituitary adenoma + Pancreatic endocrine tumor + Primary hyperparathyroidism

Pituitary adenoma examples:
Prolactinoma → gynecomastia (men), galactorrhea (women), infertility
GH-secreting → acromegaly (enlarging head/hands/feet in adult)

Pancreatic neuroendocrine tumors:
Insulinoma
Glucagonoma → new-onset DM + rash (necrolytic migratory erythema)
Gastrinoma → Zollinger-Ellison syndrome → jejunal ulcers
VIPoma → WDHA syndrome (watery diarrhea + hypokalemia + achlorhydria)

## MEN 2A — "2 P's"
Primary hyperparathyroidism + Pheochromocytoma + Medullary thyroid carcinoma
Pheochromocytoma → episodic HA + HTN
Dx: Increased urine metanephrines
NBSIM: CT abdomen or MIBG scan

## MEN 2B — "1 P"
Pheochromocytoma + Medullary thyroid cancer + Mucosal neuromas + Marfanoid habitus

# STATIN INDICATIONS

4 indications for statin tx:
1. LDL >= 190
2. Hx of ASCVD (MI, CAD, stroke, PAD)
3. 40+ yo + DM + LDL > 70
4. 40+ yo + ASCVD risk > 7.5%

High-intensity statins (reduce LDL by >= 50%):
Atorvastatin (40 or 80 mg qday)
Rosuvastatin (20 or 40 mg qday)

# VITAMIN AND MINERAL DEFICIENCIES

## Vitamin B1 (Thiamine)
Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome or wet beriberi
Pathophys: B1 depletion → issues with transketolase in TCA cycle and pentose phosphate pathway
Triad: Confusion + ataxia + ophthalmoplegia → Wernicke encephalopathy (reversible)
+ Amnesia + confabulation → Korsakoff syndrome (irreversible, complication of untreated Wernicke)
Tx: IV thiamine THEN glucose
Neuroanatomical association: Hemorrhagic infarction of mammillary bodies
Populations at risk: Alcoholics, Pregnant woman with hyperemesis gravidarum (hypokalemic hypochloremic metabolic alkalosis — vomiting triggers RAAS → H+ & K+ excretion)
Wet beriberi = dilated cardiomyopathy

## Vitamin B2 (Riboflavin)
(mentioned but not elaborated)

## Vitamin B3 (Niacin)
Pellagra = Diarrhea + Dermatitis + Dementia + Death
Dermatitis on hands + around neck (necklace-like rash)
Etiologies:
Dietary deficiency
Carcinoid syndrome (excess serotonin → shunts tryptophan to serotonin instead of niacin; tryptophan = precursor for both)
Hartnup disease (defect in neutral amino acid transporter in PCT → cannot reabsorb tryptophan)

## Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine)
Drug cause of deficiency: Isoniazid (TB treatment)
3 pathologies from B6 deficiency:

1. Sideroblastic anemia: ALAS enzyme rxn cannot run → heme production halted (ALAS = rate-limiting enzyme for heme synthesis, requires B6 as cofactor)
2. Seizures: Glutamate decarboxylase requires B6 to convert glutamate to GABA → B6 deficiency → GABA deficiency → unopposed excitatory activity
3. Abnormal LFTs: Transaminases unable to function (use B6 as cofactor) → AST/ALT > 2 (same finding as alcoholics because alcoholics often have B6 deficiency)

## Vitamin B9 (Folate)
2 pathologies: Megaloblastic anemia, Neural tube defects
Megaloblastic anemia: High homocysteine, normal MMA
Neural tube defects: Folate in prenatal vitamins prevents NTDs
Populations at risk: Tea & toast diet/malnutrition, Alcoholics, Pts on anti-seizure drugs (esp. valproate)

## Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin)
2 pathologies: Megaloblastic anemia, Subacute combined degeneration
Megaloblastic anemia: Elevated homocysteine + elevated MMA
In odd-chain fatty acid pathway, methylmalonyl-CoA mutase converts methylmalonyl-CoA to succinyl-CoA using B12 as cofactor → MMA builds up in deficiency
Subacute combined degeneration:
Loss of dorsal columns → loss of vibration, proprioception, light touch
Loss of corticospinal tract → UMN signs (hyperreflexia)
Populations at risk: Vegans (B12 from animal products)

## Zinc Deficiency
Dysgeusia + alopecia → zinc deficiency
Etiologies: TPN use, Wilson's disease treated with trientine (chelates copper AND zinc)

## Vitamin C Deficiency (Scurvy)
Mucosal bleeds + fatigue/weakness + muscle aches → scurvy
Pathophys: Unable to do collagen synthesis (hydroxylation of proline & lysine)

## Vitamin A Deficiency
Night blindness → Vit A deficiency

## Vitamin D Deficiency
Osteoporosis + secondary hyperparathyroidism → Vit D deficiency
Pathophys: Decreased Vit D → decreased Ca2+ absorption from intestines → increased PTH in response to low Ca2+

## Vitamin E Deficiency
Acanthocytosis on blood smear + ataxia → Vit E deficiency
Pathophys: Demyelination of spinocerebellar tracts (myelination requires Vit E)

## Vitamin K Deficiency
Bleeding problems → Vit K deficiency

## Fat-Soluble Vitamin Malabsorption (Vitamins ADEK)
Etiologies: Crohn's (esp. if terminal ileum resected), Cystic fibrosis (pancreatic insufficiency), Celiac disease, Chronic pancreatitis

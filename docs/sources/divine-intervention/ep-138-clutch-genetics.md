# Chromosomal / Trisomy Disorders

## Down Syndrome (Trisomy 21)
Newborn w/ flat face, epicanthal folds, Brushfield spots on iris, single palmar crease
Pathophys: Maternal nondisjunction (MCC), Robertsonian translocation
Dx: elevated bHCG + inhibin, low AFP + estriol, karyotyping
Sx (mostly from neural crest issues):
Endocardial cushion defects → atria and ventricles not separated properly
Duodenal atresia (inhibits recanalization) vs jejunal atresia (vascular insult in utero)
Hirschsprung disease (inhibits Auerbach/Meissner plexi) → meconium ileus
C/b ALL, Alzheimers (amyloid-precursor protein on chr21)
RF: advanced maternal age

## Edwards Syndrome (Trisomy 18) — Second most common trisomy
Sx: prominent occiput, micrognathia, overlapping digits, rocker-bottom feet, death by age 1-2

## Patau Syndrome (Trisomy 13) — Least common trisomy
Sx: microcephaly, micropthalmia, holoprosencephaly, polydactyly, cleft lip/palate, rocker-bottom feet, death <1Y

## Cri-du-Chat (Chr 5 defect)
Sx: microcephaly, high-pitched cat-like cry

## Spinal Muscular Atrophy (Chr 5, SMN1 gene)
6mo losing motor milestones, fasciculations
Pathophys: destruction of anterior horn cells in spinal cord

## Retinoblastoma (Chr 13, Rb tumor suppressor)
White reflex/leukocoria — C/b osteosarcoma

## WAGR (Chr 11 deletion)
Wilms tumor + aniridia + genitourinary + mental retardation

## Beckwith-Wiedemann Syndrome
Wilms tumor, macroglossia, hemihypertrophy, hepatoblastoma
Seizures at birth d/t hypoglycemia ← overgrowth of pancreatic islet beta cells (insulin)

## Klinefelter Syndrome (47XXY)
25M infertility, 6', breast tissue, micropenis
Pathophys: gonads don't work → no inhibition @ pituitary → elevated FSH/LH (hypergonadotropic hypogonadism)

## Turner Syndrome (45XO)
Second X chromosome necessary for forming ovaries → streak ovaries → no estrogen → deficient secondary sexual characteristics
Sx: cubitus valgus, short stature, low posterior hairline, congenital lymphedema (cystic hygromas, webbed neck), primary amenorrhea
Coarctation of aorta (delayed radial-femoral pulse), Bicuspid aortic valve → early onset AS (*MCC of AS <70Y! After: senile calcification)
Horseshoe kidney, Streak ovary → gonadoblastoma
Hypergonadotropic hypogonadism
Vs: athlete → shut down HPG → hypogonadotropic hypogonadism
Vs: Hypothyroidism → elevated TSH → prolactin inhibits GnRH, FSH, LH
Vs: Kallmann syndrome → inhibits GnRH cells

# Genomic Imprinting / Uniparental Disomy
Prader-Willi: obesity, hypotonia, deletion of paternal chr15
Angelman: inappropriate laughter, deletion of maternal chr15

# Autosomal Recessive (enzyme defects, earlier sx, need 2 bad copies)

## Cystic Fibrosis (Chr 7, deltaF508, CFTR channel)
5th percentile weight, recurrent infections
Pathophys: Cl doesn't leave cell → attracts Na → water follows → thick secretions
Sx: Fat malabsorption ADEK (night blindness, secondary hyperPTH, cerebellar ataxia, acanthocytosis, bleeding), Recurrent sinus infections, Bronchiectasis
Pneumonia: 1) Staph aureus 2) Pseudomonas, Burkholderia cepacia → sudden death
Plugging of seminiferous tubules, agenesis of vas deferens → infertility, Meconium ileus, Secondary PBC
Tx: Ivacaftor
Dx: sweat chloride test, nasal epithelium conductance, low serum trypsinogen, DNA testing

## Phenylketonuria
Child w/ musty odor, ID
Et: deficiency of phenylalanine hydroxylase (BH4 cofactor)
Sx: Toxicity @ neurons, Musty odor (phenyl = benzene ring), Albinism (deficiency of tyrosine → melanin)
Tx: no phenylalanine in diet, avoid aspartame
Dx: newborn screen
Ppx: mom with PKU needs careful diet during pregnancy (phenylalanine crosses placenta)

## Alkaptonuria/Ochronosis
25Y, osteoarthritis, black joints, ear/nose discoloration
Et: deficiency of homogentisic acid oxidase — "cartilage fetish"

## Albinism
Et: deficiency of tyrosinase (tyrosine → melanin)
C/b BCC/SCC (melanin absorbs UV)

## Tay-Sachs Disease
Cherry-red spot on macula, loss of motor milestones, no HSM
Et: deficiency of hexosaminidase A → buildup of GM2 ganglioside (@CNS)
Sx: hyperreflexia, ID, cherry-red, death <2Y
RF: Ashkenazi-Jewish heritage

## Niemann-Pick Disease
Et: deficiency of sphingomyelinase → buildup of sphingomyelin (reticuloendothelial system + CNS)
Sx: cherry-red spot, hyperreflexia, ID, hepatosplenomegaly (c/b thrombocytopenia)

## Gaucher Disease
Pancytopenia, macrophages with crumpled paper appearance on BMBx
Et: deficiency of glucocerebrosidase @ macrophages
Sx: ineffective erythropoiesis, joint problems

## Hurler Syndrome
Corneal clouding, coarse facial features
Et: mutation in alpha-L-iduronidase → buildup of heparin + dermatan sulfate
Vs. Hunter syndrome (X-linked recessive): alpha-iduronate sulfatase — NO corneal clouding or coarse facial features

## Glycogen Storage Diseases
Von Gierke (Type 1): deficiency of glucose-6-phosphatase → G6P buildup in liver → hepatocytes expand → hypoglycemia between meals
Pompe (Type 2): deficiency of alpha-1,4-glucosidase/acid maltase → heart failure
Cori (Type 3): deficiency of debranching enzyme → HSM, muscle sx
McArdle's (Type 5): deficiency of glycogen myophosphorylase @muscle → muscle cramping with exercise

## I-Cell Disease
Inhibits phosphotransferase → cannot put mannose-phosphate on things → improper transport

## FA Oxidation Disorders
MCAD/LCAD: inhibits beta-oxidation → elevated acylcarnitine
Vs carnitine deficiency: low acylcarnitine
Very long chains @ peroxisome: Adrenoleukodystrophy → demyelination, cerebellar ataxia, death <2Y

## Hemophilia C
Inhibits Factor 11

## G6PD Deficiency
African missionary with oxidizing drug → hemolytic anemia
Sx: Heinz bodies, bite cells
Dx: G6PD assay weeks after episode

# X-Linked Recessive (sx @ boys)

## Wiskott-Aldrich Syndrome
Recurrent infections, hypopigmented skin (eczema), low platelets

## Chronic Granulomatous Disease
Et: deficiency of NADPH oxidase
Sx: recurrent Staph aureus abscesses
Dx: tetrazolium, dihydrorhodamine test
Tx: interferon-gamma
(Vs: interferon alpha → HepC; interferon beta → MS)

## Lesch-Nyhan Syndrome
Kiddo w/ gout, chews off fingers/toes
Et: deficiency of HGPRT (purine salvage pathway)
Sx: hyperuricemia → neuronal toxicity, self-mutilation

## Bruton's Agammaglobulinemia
Et: mutation in Bruton's tyrosine kinase (B-cells)
Sx: small tonsils, bacterial infections
Tx: IVIG

## Hemophilia A (Factor 8) / Hemophilia B (Factor 9)
Sx: elevated PTT
Dx: correct w mixing studies (but sometimes create Ab against factor)

## Menkes Disease/Kinky Hair Disease
Et: ATP7A mutation (copper metabolism) → death <2Y
Vs: ATP7B = Wilson's disease (copper @ basal ganglia, liver dz, psych, Kayser-Fleischer rings, CI copper IUD, tx penicillamine/trientine)
*penicillamine/trientine = chelators; AE: zinc deficiency

# Autosomal Dominant (structural protein/receptor defect, later sx)
If two copies of bad dominant allele: death in utero (i.e. lethal form of osteogenesis imperfecta)

## Familial Hypercholesterolemia
19Y, father died from MI @30Y, STEMI, xanthelasma/xanthomas
Et: mutation in LDL receptor
Type 1: No LDL receptor
Type 2: LDL receptors don't reach surface (inhibits exocytosis)
Type 3: LDL has trouble binding to receptor (MCC)
Type 4: LDL binds but not endocytosed
Type 5: Inhibits recycling LDL receptor
Tx: statin, PCSK9 inhibitors (-cumab)

## Marfan's Syndrome
6'5", hyperextensible joints, lens displacement, tearing chest pain radiating to back
Et: mutation on chr15 fibrillin/FBN1
Sx: MVP/myxomatous degeneration, Ascending aortic dissection → aortic regurgitation, Circle of Willis aneurysm → SAH
Lens subluxation: up and out

## Marfan's vs Homocystinuria
| | Marfan's | Homocystinuria |
|---|---|---|
| Inheritance | AD | AR |
| Defect | Fibrillin | Cystathionine beta-synthase |
| Lens | Up and out | Down and out |
| Intelligence | Normal | ID |
*B6/B9/B12 elevate homocysteine*

## Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome
Problems w/ wound healing, hyperextensible joints
Et: mutation in Type 3 collagen
Sx: aortic dissection, thoracic aortic aneurysm, wound dehiscence, rupture of colon

## Osteogenesis Imperfecta
Blue sclera, fractures, short
Et: defect in Type 1 collagen (COL1)

## Alport Syndrome
Hematuria, hearing problems, cataracts
Et: defect in Type 4 collagen COL4A5 (@eye, ear, kidney) — *X-linked dominant
Vs Goodpasture: Ab against Type 4 collagen

## Achondroplasia
Short stature, problems with long bone growth
Et: mutation in fibroblast-growth-factor-3

## NF1 (Chr 17, neurofibromin = tumor suppressor of p21/RAS)
Cafe au lait spots, neurofibromas, meningiomas, pheochromocytoma, Lisch nodules (hamartomas @ iris)

## NF2 (Chr 22, merlin = contact inhibition)
Bilateral acoustic neuromas

## VHL (Chr 3, VHL gene = ubiquitination of growth/proliferation proteins)
Posterior fossa mass, hematocrit 60%
Sx: hemangioblastoma (esp cerebellum), EPO production, cysts @ liver/pancreas, bilateral RCC

## FAP
Multiple polyps in colon — Ppx: colonoscopies <20Y
+ medulloblastoma = Turcot syndrome
+ soft tissue osteoma = Gardner syndrome

## Hereditary Spherocytosis
Hgb 8, paternal relatives with blood disorders
Et: mutation in ankyrin, spectrin, band 3.2
Tx: splenectomy

## Von Willebrand Disease
F with heavy menstrual bleeding
Et: deficiency of VWF
Dx: normal plt, elevated PTT

# Trinucleotide Repeat Disorders

## Autosomal Dominant
Myotonic dystrophy: CTG @ DMPK1 → trouble releasing grip, early balding
Huntington's: CAG @ chr4 with anticipation → choreiform movements, dementia, inappropriate behavior

DDx Choreiform Movements: Huntington's, Sydenham chorea (rheumatic fever), Subthalamic nucleus lesion (contralateral hemiballismus), PANDAS (GAS)

Vs Myoclonus: Neuroblastoma (opsoclonus-myoclonus), CJD (elevated 14-3-3, prion), Serotonin syndrome
Tx: haloperidol, tetrabenazine

## X-Linked Dominant (only 2: Fragile X + Alport!)
Fragile X: CGG repeat → big ears, testicles, long face, ADHD/autism

## Autosomal Recessive
Friedreich Ataxia: GAA repeat

# Mitochondrial Disorders (from mom!)
Leber optic neuropathy
MELAS = "ragged red fibers" → encephalopathy, lactic acidosis, stroke

# Multifactorial Inheritance
Neural tube defects, Congenital heart disease, T2DM

# CONGENITAL
Epispadias: dorsal surface groove; assoc: bladder exstrophy; don't circumcise (need foreskin for repair)
Hypospadias: ventral urethral opening; can have cryptorchidism; causes UTI

# PENILE CANCER
Biggest RF: HPV 16,18,30s, smoking, uncircumcised; Squamous cell (keratinization)
Bowen disease: SCC in situ (limited by basement membrane); 10% progress to invasive
SCC → PTHrP paraneoplastic → hypercalcemia

# CRYPTORCHIDISM
Testis didn't descend (androgen causes descent in 3rd trimester)
MC location: inguinal canal; assoc: inguinal hernia
Increased risk testicular cancer (even after orchiopexy of 1 testis)
Seen in androgen insensitivity syndrome (46XY, female phenotype → high risk seminoma)

# TESTICULAR CONDITIONS
Bilateral atrophy → Klinefelter (47XXY)
Testicular enlargement → Fragile X (CGG repeats), Alport syndrome, Mumps orchitis (25-35%)

## Epididymitis
Young: N. gonorrhea/Chlamydia (ceftriaxone + doxy/azithromycin)
Old: E. coli (bactrim/ciprofloxacin)

## Hydrocele
Fluid in tunica vaginalis (failed obliteration); DDx from cancer: transillumination (hydrocele +, cancer -)

## Varicocele
"Bag of worms"; dilation of pampiniform plexus → heat impairs spermatogenesis → infertility

## Testicular torsion
Sudden onset groin/testicular pain → do NOT do U/S → straight to exploratory surgery
Spermatic cord twists → stops venous drainage → testis dies in 6h (hemorrhagic infarction)
U/S: decreased blood flow; decreased cremasteric reflex
Tx: detorsion + bilateral orchiopexy

## Torsion of appendix testis
Upper pole pain + blue discoloration; vestigial structure

# TESTICULAR CANCER
95% germ cell tumors; Tx: orchiectomy (no biopsy first)

## Seminoma
No real tumor marker (no AFP, no b-hCG); human placental lactogen
Radiation + chemo sensitive; good prognosis

## Embryonal carcinoma
2nd most common; bad prognosis

## Yolk sac (endodermal sinus) tumor
Histology: Schiller-Duval bodies; Marker: AFP

## Choriocarcinoma
Histology: syncytiotrophoblast; Marker: b-hCG

## Leydig cell tumor
Kid with precocious puberty (excess androgen); Histology: Reinke crystals

# BPH
Acute Tx: alpha-1 blocker (prazosin, doxazosin; AE: orthostatic hypotension)
Tamsulosin: alpha-1a selective for prostatic urethra (no orthostatic hypotension)
Long-term: finasteride (5-alpha reductase inhibitor → shrinks prostate)
If nothing works → TURP

# PROSTATITIS
Chronic: months of urinary sx + lots of WBC but no bacteria + back/perineal pain
Acute: few days + bacteria + tender prostate

# PROSTATE CANCER
BPH does NOT increase risk; Biggest RF: age; Blastic metastasis
MC metastatic location: vertebral bodies

# Biostatistics

## Case Control vs Retrospective Cohort
Case control: Start at outcome, look back for exposure — susceptible to recall bias — data as odds ratio (crosses 1 = not significant)
Retrospective cohort: Start at exposure, go forward to outcome

## Clinical vs Statistical Significance
Drug A lowers SBP by 3 / DBP by 1.5 (vs 1.5/0.75 conventional), p <0.05 → NOT clinically significant (small absolute reduction)
Risk 10% vs 50%, p = 0.1 → likely underpowered (type II error)
Cannot extrapolate results to different population than study population

# Cardiology

## Aortic Dissection
Sudden-onset severe CP radiating to back
Dx: HDS → CT chest; HDUS → Bedside TEE
Stanford A (ascending ± descending) → Beta blocker + surgery
Stanford B (descending only) → Medical: beta blocker
Complications: Acute AR, RCA occlusion → inferior MI (II, III, aVF — preload dependent, no nitrates!)

# Neurology — Cervical Nerve Roots
Cervical nerve roots arise ABOVE corresponding vertebral bodies
C5 vertebral body crush → affects C6 nerve root
Other levels: nerve roots arise BELOW

# Immunodeficiency — No Thymic Shadow
DiGeorge: 3rd/4th pharyngeal pouches → no thymus (T cells) + no parathyroid (hypocalcemia, QT prolongation)
SCID: Adenosine deaminase or IL2-R mutation

# Psychiatry — Bipolar
Best chronic therapy: Lithium (decreases risk of death, takes weeks)
Acute mania: Atypical antipsychotic (quetiapine, ziprasidone, aripiprazole) or valproate
Not fully controlled on lithium → add atypical antipsychotic

# Mediastinal Masses
Posterior: Neurogenic (pheo, neuroblastoma)
Anterior: "Terrible T's" — Thymoma, Thyroid, Teratoma, "Terrible" lymphoma, Thoracic aorta
Middle: Lymphadenopathy, Bronchogenic cysts

# Acid-Base in PE
PaO2 low, PaCO2 low (hyperventilation), Respiratory alkalosis → pH high
Can cause hypocalcemia (H+ stripped from albumin → negatively charged albumin binds Ca++)

# Surgical Site Infection Prevention
Cefazolin 30-60 mins before incision
RFs: Emergency surgery, Obesity, DM

# Desmopressin Indications
Central DI, vWD (releases vWF from Weibel-Palade bodies), Nocturnal enuresis (dx ≥5 yo; bed alarms 1st, desmopressin 2nd, imipramine 3rd), Hemophilia A (protects factor VIII)
TCA overdose → widened QRS → Tx: sodium bicarb

# End-of-Life / Palliative
Stage 4 malignancy → avoid aggressive interventions with curative intent (e.g. AAA repair), usually can't be organ donor

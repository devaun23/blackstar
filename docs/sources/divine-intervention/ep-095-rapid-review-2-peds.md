# Cystic Fibrosis
Salty baby w/ malabsorption, dx with fecal elastase = CF
MCC of PNA until 21 = S. aureus (Also B. cepacia)
MCC of PNA >21 years old = Pseudomonas
DF508 mutation is the MC
Male infertility (congenital absence of vas deferens), rectal prolapse, nasal polyps
Tx nasal polyps with inhaled corticosteroids
Tx of PNA = coverage of Pseudomonas with tobramycin
Pancreatic enzyme replacement
Dornase alpha (breaks phosphodiesterase in mucous)
High calorie diet

# Pediatric Oncology
Calcified mass in abdominal CT scan or in posterior mediastinum = neuroblastoma
Crosses the midline vs Wilms tumor which is not calcified and does not cross midline
Opsoclonus myoclonus syndrome

# Seizure Disorders
Clustered seizures in child, contraction of neck and arms, EEG shows hypsarrhythmia, hypopigmented macules on skin
Tuberous sclerosis, the seizures are West syndrome (infantile spasms)
West syndrome tx = ACTH, also vigabatrin (GABAergic agent)
Intellectual disability, nodules in ventricular system (subependymal nodule), subependymal giant cell astrocytoma, cardiac rhabdomyoma, renal angiomyolipoma

Generalized tonic clonic seizures in morning (teenager) = Juvenile myoclonic epilepsy
Autosomal dominant, good prognosis

Kid w/ many kinds of seizures, bad cognitive dysfunction, EEG shows generalized spikes and slow waves = Lennox Gastaut syndrome

Kid not doing well in school, stares into space, no postictal state = Absence seizures
EEG = 3 hertz spike and slow waves
Tx = ethosuximide, T type Ca channel blocker

Diet to decrease recurrence of seizures = ketogenic diet

## Febrile Seizures
High fever, seizure for <15 min, no recurrence, generalized tonic clonic seizure = typical febrile seizure
Tx = antipyretics, increased risk of recurrence, no imaging, send home

Typical vs Atypical:
Atypical = > 15 min, recurrence within 24 hour span, focal seizure, no fever → Get imaging or EEG
Typical = generalized tonic clonic (NOT focal) → No imaging, no EEG

# Neurocutaneous Syndromes
Cafe au lait spots (hyperpigmented), chr 17 mutation = NF1
Gliomas, Lisch nodules on iris, Pheochromocytoma
Autosomal dominant inheritance

Triad: vestibular schwannomas, chr 22 defect, AD inheritance = NF2

# Down Syndrome
Alzheimer in 30s, increased risk of ALL, epicanthal folds = Down syndrome (tri 21)
Meconium ileus from Hirschsprung disease
Maternal nondisjunction potential cause
Atlantoaxial instability — Before starting sports, lateral neck x-ray
Same for pts with ankylosing spondylitis and RA prior to surgery
Increased risk of ALL

# Spinal Muscular Atrophy
6 month old kid, losing motor milestones, dies before age of 2, autosomal recessive = spinal muscular atrophy (Werdnig-Hoffman)
Chr 5 (SMN1 gene)
Hypotonic infant with fasciculations
Fasciculations in adult = ALS

# Genetics
Defect in chr 15, happy puppet syndrome in girls = Angelman syndrome in girls and Prader Willi in boys
Kid with large ears, large testicles, low IQ = Fragile X
CGG repeats, X-linked dominant

# Cerebral Palsy
UMN disease in a child secondary to initial insult to brain = cerebral palsy

# HUS
Triad: MAHA + low platelet + renal failure = HUS
Shigella, E. coli
No steroids or antibiotics in tx

# Pediatric Nephrology
Kid with HTN with preceding URI = postinfectious glomerulonephritis
Proteinuria = 2+ or less
UA shows dysmorphic RBCs
Low complement levels, ASO/Anti DNAse B titers are high
Low C3 + nephritic syndrome = C3 nephritic factor seen in type MPGN

IgA nephropathy and Henoch Schonlein Purpura
Avoid rotavirus vaccine d/t intussusception
Membranous nephropathy associated the most with renal vein thrombosis
Minimal change disease associated with hematologic malignancies (leukemias, lymphomas)
Light microscopy normal, EM shows effacement of foot processes

## Pediatric HTN
Kid with BP > 95th percentile = HTN in kid
Secondary HTN, different BP in arms vs legs, delayed femoral pulse = coarctation of the aorta
Associated with Turner (also bicuspid aortic valve → aortic dissection, early onset aortic stenosis)
Secondary HTN with flank mass crossing midline, increased catecholamines = neuroblastoma
Secondary HTN, episodic with headaches, increased metanephrines = pheochromocytoma
Flank mass not calcified, does not cross midline → Before surgery give alpha blocker (phentolamine, phenoxybenzamine)

# Pediatric Oncology — Renal
Kid with HTN, mom felt hard flank mass, kid has no iris = Wilms tumor
WAGR: Aniridia, GU anomalies, mental retardation
Tx dactinomycin
Denys-Drash = genital anomalies, Wilms tumor

# Beckwith-Wiedemann Syndrome
RUQ mass in a kid, large tongue, one side of body is larger = Beckwith-Wiedemann syndrome
Hepatoblastoma (RUQ mass)
Can present as newborn w/ seizures d/t increased insulin producing cells → hypoglycemia → seizures
Babies of diabetic moms are hyperinsulinemic in utero → after delivery, still hyperinsulinemia → hypoglycemia
Hypocalcemic seizures in infants of diabetic mothers
2 causes of seizures in infants of diabetic moms: hypoglycemia or hypocalcemia
Hypocalcemia also seen in DiGeorge syndrome pts

# Alport Syndrome
Kid has urine problems, visual problems, hearing problems = Alport syndrome
Collagen IV mutation
Inherited in AD fashion (COL4A5 mutation). However, X-linked is MC

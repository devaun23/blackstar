# ACUTE ANGLE CLOSURE GLAUCOMA

32 yo Asian M with severe R eye pain. PE reveals "rock hard" unreactive pupil → acute-angle glaucoma
Dx: Tonometry (eye pressures)
Tx:
Surgery: Laser iridotomy
Medical: Mannitol (lowers IOP by drawing water out of vitreous humor), Acetazolamide (decreases aqueous humor production), Timolol (decreases aqueous humor production), Pilocarpine (muscarinic agonist, constricts pupil)

# ORGANOPHOSPHATE POISONING

3 yo F using the potty continuously for 3 hrs. Pupillary miosis + profuse sweating. Played on farm → organophosphate poisoning
Pathophys: Organophosphates inhibit AChE → ACh levels rise → parasympathetic overactivation
Tx: Atropine (blocks AChR) + pralidoxime (regenerates AChE)

# BOTULISM vs TETANUS

## Botulism
Flaccid paralysis after consuming home canned goods → botulism
Pathophys: Botulinum toxin cleaves SNARE proteins → prevents release of ACh from presynaptic neuron
Tx: Botulinum immune globulin, intubate for respiratory support

## Tetanus
Spastic paralysis (contrast to botulism)
Pathophys: Tetanus toxin prevents release of glycine & GABA (inhibitory NTs) from Renshaw cells → spastic paralysis

# LAMBERT-EATON MYASTHENIC SYNDROME

Difficulty getting out of chair in patient with 45 pack year smoking history that improves with muscle use → LEMS
Pathophys: Ab to presynaptic voltage-gated Ca++ channel
Increment in muscle contraction with repetitive nerve stimulation
Associated malignancy: Small cell lung cancer

# MYASTHENIA GRAVIS

Difficulty swallowing and droopy eyelids in 35 yo F with anterior mediastinal mass → myasthenia gravis (thymoma)
Pathophys: Ab against nicotinic AChR
Decrement in muscle contraction with repetitive nerve stimulation
Dx: Anti-AChR antibodies (NOT Tensilon test anymore)
Tx: AChE inhibitors (e.g. pyridostigmine), Resection of thymoma
NBSM: CT chest (look for thymoma)
How differentiated from ChAT deficiency: ChAT deficiency sxs will NOT improve with AChE therapy

# ANTICHOLINERGIC TOXIDROME

"Hot as a desert, dry as a bone, red as a beet, blind as a bat, mad as a hatter"
Classic meds: Diphenhydramine, TCAs, 1st gen low potency antipsychotics (e.g. chlorpromazine)

# MOTOR NEURON DISEASES

## SMA (Spinal Muscular Atrophy)
Tongue fasciculations in a 6 mo → SMA
Inheritance: AR
Pathophys: Pure LMN disease
Mutated gene: SMN1
Chromosome: 5
Viruses that affect anterior horn cells: Polio, West Nile

## ALS (Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis)
Tongue fasciculations in a 65 yo M with asymmetric weakness → ALS
UMN + LMN problems
Pathophys: Destruction of UMNs (corticospinal/corticobulbar tract), LMNs (anterior horn of spinal cord), and cranial nerves
Genetic mutation in familial cases: SOD1, C9orf72
What is spared in ALS: Sensation, bowel/bladder function, extraocular muscles
EMG findings: Chronic denervation, fibrillation potentials
Tx that improves survival: Riluzole (NMDA receptor antagonist, reduces glutamate excitotoxicity), BiPAP
Tx for spasticity: Baclofen (GABA receptor agonist), Tizanidine (alpha-2 agonist), Botox injection

# INFLAMMATORY MYOPATHIES

## Dermatomyositis vs Polymyositis

| Feature | Dermatomyositis | Polymyositis |
|---------|----------------|--------------|
| Age group | Kids | Adults |
| T cells | CD4+ | CD8+ |
| Damage pattern | Perifascicular | Endomysial |

Ab: Anti-Jo1, anti-Mi-2, anti-SRP
Dx: 1st muscle MRI, 2nd muscle biopsy
Lab findings: Elevated creatine kinase
Associated malignancy: Lung cancer (especially small cell)

# PARANEOPLASTIC CEREBELLAR DEGENERATION

Dysarthria + truncal, gait, and limb ataxia in patient with lung cancer → paraneoplastic cerebellar degeneration
Associated malignancies: Lung, Breast
Ab: Anti-Hu, anti-Yo
Pathophys: Tumors express cerebellar proteins → immune system attacks tumor but also mounts response against cerebellum

# WERNICKE-KORSAKOFF

Confusion, ophthalmoplegia, and ataxia in an alcoholic → Wernicke's syndrome
With forgetfulness & confabulation → Korsakoff's syndrome
Tx: IV thiamine (Vit B1)

## Hepatic Encephalopathy
Tx: Lactulose (convert ammonia to ammonium, which you poop out), Rifaximin (a lot of ammonia comes from bacteria in GI tract)

# SEIZURE TYPES

## Simple Partial Seizures (Focal Seizure w/o Loss of Awareness)
NO loss of consciousness
4 types: motor, sensory, autonomic, psychic
Motor: Rigidity or jerking of extremity
Sensory: Hissing sound or can't see or smell burnt rubber
Autonomic: Sweating, mydriasis, rising sensation in abdomen
Auras that localize to temporal lobe: Smelling burnt rubber, Deja vu, Rising sensation in abdomen

# TRISOMY 21 — SPORTS CLEARANCE

Required imaging before recommending sports in 12 yo with Trisomy 21: High risk of atlanto-axial instability/subluxation
Dx: Lateral neck XR
Other populations at risk: RA, Ankylosing spondylitis

# GUILLAIN-BARRE SYNDROME

25 yo M in respiratory failure. Recently recovered from 7 day bloody diarrhea. Pronounced areflexia in LE bilaterally. Weakness "started in legs and progressed upward" → GBS
Alternative term: AIDP (acute inflammatory demyelinating polyneuropathy)
Pathophys: Peripheral demyelinating disease (Schwann cells affected)
Classically associated bug: Campylobacter jejuni
CSF findings: Albuminocytologic dissociation
Tx: IVIG, Plasmapheresis
GBS-like + nystagmus + ataxia → Miller-Fisher syndrome

# SUBCLAVIAN STEAL SYNDROME

Pt with syncope or neuro deficit when using arms → subclavian steal syndrome
Pathophys: Proximal subclavian stenosis → low pressure distal → reversal of blood flow in vertebral arteries → "stealing" blood from basilar → hypoperfusion to brainstem

# WEBER & RINNE TESTS

## Weber Test
Compares bone conduction between ears (normally same in both)
If Weber lateralized to R ear — 2 possibilities:
Conductive hearing loss in R ear
Sensorineural hearing loss in L ear

## Rinne Test
Compares air conduction to bone conduction for each ear (single ear test)
Normally: Air conduction better than bone conduction
If bone conduction > air conduction → conductive hearing loss

# HIV NEUROLOGIC COMPLICATIONS

## Cryptococcal Meningitis
Meningitis in HIV+ → Cryptococcus neoformans
ID: India ink stain, Latex agglutination test
Tx: Acutely: Amphotericin B + 5-flucytosine; Chronically: fluconazole

## Toxoplasmosis vs Primary CNS Lymphoma
Multiple ring enhancing lesions on MRI in HIV+:
Toxoplasmosis: Tx pyrimethamine & sulfadiazine; PPx for CD4 < 100: TMP-SMX
Primary CNS lymphoma: Usually single lesion; Infectious association: EBV

## HIV-Associated Dementia
MMSE 20/30 + CD4 25 + high viral load → HIV-associated dementia
Poor prognosis
Tx: HAART + Alzheimer's drugs (rivastigmine, galantamine, donepezil)

## Progressive Multifocal Leukoencephalopathy
HIV+ with neuro deficits and multiple areas of demyelination on MRI → PML
Infectious agent: JC virus
Cause of drug-induced PML: Natalizumab (indication: MS; mechanism: alpha-4 integrin inhibitor)

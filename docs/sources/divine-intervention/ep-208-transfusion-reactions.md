# ACUTE HEMOLYTIC TRANSFUSION REACTION
MCC: ABO incompatibility due to clerical errors (HY)
Ex: Type O person gets Type A or B blood
Presentation: immediately as transfusion is started — back/flank pain, hematuria
Dx: Direct Coombs test
Type 2 Hypersensitivity reaction: hemolysis due to preformed antibodies
Tx: give fluids (similar to rhabdomyolysis tx)
May go into DIC — give platelets, FFP

# DELAYED HEMOLYTIC TRANSFUSION REACTION
MCC: DUFFY/KEL/KIDD antigen present, not due to ABO incompatibility
Rxn does not occur during transfusion — usually days after!
+ direct Coombs test
Much less severe reactions
Type 2 HS rxn but days later

# ALLERGIC TRANSFUSION REACTION (ANAPHYLAXIS)
MCC: cross linking of IgE on the surface of mast cells
Onset: blood contains something you have IgE antibodies to
Presentation: airway problems — wheezing, bronchospasm, normal vitals
Type 1 HS reaction
Tx: give antihistamine or IM epinephrine if severe

# FEBRILE NONHEMOLYTIC TRANSFUSION REACTION
Pathophys (2 mechanisms):
1. Platelets stored at room temp contain WBCs that make cytokines and activate
2. Antibodies from RECIPIENT attack WBCs in DONOR blood → antigen-antibody complex (type 3 HS rxn) → release of cytokines
Presentation: during transfusion develop chills, fevers, mild tachy, no significant hemodynamic instability
Onset: during the transfusion OR 1-4 hrs after
Tx: fever reducer (cytokine mediated mechanism)

# TRALI (TRANSFUSION RELATED ACUTE LUNG INJURY)
Pathophys: Antibodies from DONOR blood attack WBCs in RECIPIENT blood → antigen-antibody complex
Basically identical to what happens in nephritic/nephrotic syndromes but in the lungs!
Non-cardiogenic pulmonary edema
Presentation: normal PCWP (<18mmHg)
Tx: pulmonary support — vents, fluids/pressors, corticosteroids
*NO DIURETICS

# TACO (TRANSFUSION ASSOCIATED CIRCULATORY OVERLOAD)
RF: bad heart — HF
Pt given large volume blood transfusion but heart can't handle the fluid → backs up in lungs → pulmonary edema
Cardiogenic pulmonary edema — PCWP >18mmHg
Tx: give diuretics
Everything goes up: ↑CVP, ↑PCWP, ↑BNP

# TRANSFUSION ASSOCIATED GRAFT VS HOST DISEASE
Pathophys: Donor T cells attack recipient tissue
Very high mortality
RF: recipient is immunocompromised
Symptoms: hepatosplenomegaly, diarrhea, pancytopenia, rash
Type 4 HS rxn
Prevention: irradiate the donor blood to kill the T cells

# LEUKOREDUCTION
Reduce WBCs in donor blood
Effective for febrile nonhemolytic rxn and possibly in allergic rxn

# BRUTON'S AGAMMAGLOBULINEMIA

3 yo M with multiple Strep pneumo PNA + giardia infections → Bruton's agammaglobulinemia
Recurrent infections after 6 months old (when maternal antibodies wane)
Cell affected: B-cells
Pathophys: Mutation in tyrosine kinase for B cell differentiation → no mature B cells → issues with humoral immunity
Classic organisms: Encapsulated organisms (Strep pneumo, H. flu)
Inheritance: X-linked dominant
Mutated gene: BTK
PE findings: Poorly developed tonsils, Tiny spleen, No lymphadenopathy with infections
Lab findings: NO B cells on peripheral smear, Low levels of immunoglobulin
Tx: Monthly IVIG

# CVID (COMMON VARIABLE IMMUNODEFICIENCY)

Similar presentation to Bruton's
Inheritance: AR or AD
Pathophys: Issues with B cell differentiation → low levels of immunoglobulins
Presentation: Boy or girl with recurrent respiratory or GI infections after age 10
Alternate presentation: Pt gets pneumococcal vaccine but still gets multiple pneumococcal infections. Check titers → not immune.
Associated problems: Skin disease (alopecia, vitiligo), Autoimmune dz (ITP, autoimmune hemolytic anemia, RA), Lymphoma
Lab findings: B cells present on peripheral smear (contrast with Bruton's)
Tx: IVIG

# IgA DEFICIENCY

Pt getting blood transfusion becomes hypoxic with stridor/wheezing → IgA deficiency
Presentation: Recurrent respiratory and GI infections
Pathophys: Failure of differentiation of IgA-producing B cells. IgA is the "guardian of the mucosa." Since deficient in IgA, can form Ab to IgA.
Associated problems: Food allergy, Atopic disorders
Lab findings: Normal WBC count, Low IgA levels (other Ig normal)
Tx: Keep treating recurrent infections, Washing of blood transfusions (to remove plasma)

# ATAXIA-TELANGIECTASIA

Girl with progressive neurologic impairment + problems walking + telangiectasias in skin/eye + mild ID → ataxia-telangiectasia
Classic presentation: Ataxia w/ negative Romberg → age 3-6 telangiectasias on skin & eye → then recurrent sinopulmonary infections
Note: Friedreich's ataxia tends to have positive Romberg
Inheritance: AR
Pathophys: ATM gene regulates response to ds DNA breaks → sensitivity to ionizing radiation
Mutated gene: ATM
Chromosome: 11
Lab testing: Low WBC count, Low IgA level
Associated problems: Increased risk of cancer, especially hematologic cancers

# SCID (SEVERE COMBINED IMMUNODEFICIENCY)

Presentation: Bad infections since birth
Pathophys:
Common cause: Deficiency of adenosine deaminase → apoptosis of lymphocytes
Another cause: Mutation in IL-2 receptor
Cells affected: B-cells & T-cells
Classic organisms: ALL (bacterial, fungal, viral)
Inheritance: Can vary (AR, X-linked)
Lab findings: Low WBC count, Low immunoglobulins
Imaging: Absent thymic shadow on CXR (also seen in DiGeorge)
Histology: Hypoplasia of the paracortex (T-cell zone) of lymph node
Tx: Abx, Bone marrow transplant
Vaccination restrictions: NO live-attenuated vaccines

# WISKOTT-ALDRICH SYNDROME

Boy with petechiae + platelet count 30k + eczematous lesions + recurrent infections → Wiskott-Aldrich syndrome
Inheritance: X-linked recessive
Mutated gene: WASP
Pathophys: WASP gene mutation → issues with signaling cascade for appropriate movement of actin filaments
Lab findings: Thrombocytopenia, Tiny platelets, High IgE (→ eczema), High IgA, Low IgM
Associated problems: Autoimmune disease

# LEUKOCYTE ADHESION DEFICIENCY

Skin infections w/o pus + delayed separation of umbilical cord → leukocyte adhesion deficiency
Pathophys: Neutrophils cannot adhere to endothelium & exit bloodstream
Mutated proteins: Integrins (e.g. CD-18), E-selectin, Sialyl Lewis X
Lab findings: Leukocytosis w/o signs of infection (large % leukocytes are in demarginated state)
Dx: Flow cytometry

# CGD (CHRONIC GRANULOMATOUS DISEASE)

Pt with lymphadenopathy + recurrent Staph aureus abscesses → CGD
Inheritance: X-linked recessive
Cells affected: Neutrophils
Pathophys: NADPH oxidase deficiency → neutrophils cannot generate oxidative burst
Classic organisms: Catalase-positive (Staph aureus, E. coli, Candida)
Dx: Dihydrorhodamine test (DHR); Nitroblue tetrazolium test (NBT — OLD!)
Tx: Interferon-gamma

# CHEDIAK-HIGASHI DISEASE

Recurrent infections + albinism ("diffuse skin hypopigmentation") with blond hair & blue eyes + intellectual disability → Chediak-Higashi disease
Inheritance: AR
Cells affected: Neutrophils
Mutated gene: LYST or CHS1
Pathophys: Problems with intracellular protein transport → problems transporting secretory granules
Issues with melanin granule transport → albinism
Issues with neutrophil granules → can't kill bacteria
Associated problems: Hematologic malignancies
Lab findings: Peripheral smear with giant granules in neutrophils

# DiGEORGE SYNDROME

Recurrent infections + no thymic shadow on CXR + hypocalcemia → DiGeorge
Pathophys: Failure of 3rd and 4th pharyngeal pouches to develop
No thymus → T cells can't mature
No parathyroids → hypocalcemia → prolonged QT & seizures
Gene: 22q11 deletion
Chromosome: 22
Dx: FISH
CATCH-22: Cardiac issues (tetralogy, truncus arteriosus, VSD), Abnormal facies, Thymic aplasia, Cleft palate, Hypoparathyroidism, chromosome 22
Most common cause of death: Heart disease
Velocardiofacial syndrome vs DiGeorge: doesn't have the hypoparathyroidism

# TERMINAL COMPLEMENT DEFICIENCY

Terminal complement deficiency (C5-C9) → susceptible to Neisseria meningitidis
Prevention: Neisseria meningitidis vaccine

# AGE-RELATED MACULAR DEGENERATION

Central scotomas, straight lines look wavy → age related macular degeneration
2 types: wet (exudative/neovascular) and dry (atrophic)

## Wet AMD
More progressive, but also more treatment options offered
Tx: VEGF inhibitors (Ranibizumab, Bevacizumab) or laser

## Dry AMD
Drusen on fundoscopy; more common than wet
Tx: Vitamins C, E, beta-carotene, zinc slow progression
Note: Careful in smoking pt — increased mortality rate from lung cancer with Vit E and beta-carotene

MCC of vision loss in developing world = macular degeneration
Other RFs: smoking, aspirin (chronic use)
Buzzword: neovascularization
Lose central vision (macula) — whereas in glaucoma lose peripheral vision

# RETINAL DETACHMENT

Punched in eye + floaters and bright streaks of light → retinal detachment
RFs: trauma, cataract surgery, metabolic disorders, myopia, degenerative diseases, vascular disorders
Tx: Laser photocoagulation (buzzword)
Ophtho emergency

# RETINOBLASTOMA

Newborn with R eye white reflex → retinoblastoma
Associated with: Osteosarcoma (sunburst/Codman's triangle on XR)

# AMBLYOPIA vs STRABISMUS

## Amblyopia
Cortical blindness → eye normal but brain doesn't recognize it
MCC: strabismus
Others: anything that obstructs light (cataracts, glaucoma)

## Strabismus
Misalignment of eye
Tx if persists past 3 months: patching of unaffected eye (makes the brain work the affected eye to align itself); eyepatch or "paralytic" drops with atropine
Corneal light reflex is uncentered in affected eye (not symmetric)
Red reflex will be brighter in affected eye

# GLAUCOMA

Asian male with sudden onset severe eye pain + nausea and vomiting following nasal decongestant use → acute angle closure glaucoma
Alpha-1 agonist (phenylephrine) will cause mydriasis
Tx: laser iridotomy (ophtho emergency), clonidine, muscarinic agonist (pilocarpine), mannitol or acetazolamide, latanoprost, beta-blockers
RFs: African American, diabetes, steroid use (can decrease outflow at anterior chambers)
Closed (aCute) think Asians, Open (chrOnic) think African Americans
MCRF = increase in intraocular pressure
Dx: Tonometry
Fundoscopic exam: increased optic cup:disc ratio

## Aqueous Humor Pathway
Ciliary epithelium makes aqueous humor (driven by sympathetic NS via beta2 receptors) → posterior chamber → anterior chamber → drain via canal of Schlemm/trabecular meshwork (M3 receptors) & some (~25%) through the uveoscleral outflow tract (controlled by prostaglandins)

## Treatment Targets
Aqueous humor production:
Beta blockers (Timolol): decrease aqueous humor synthesis (Beta2)
Alpha2 agonist (brimonidine): Gi coupled, inhibit adenylate cyclase, inhibit presynaptic release of NE — NOT in closed angle glaucoma (can precipitate closed/acute)
Carbonic anhydrase inhibitor (acetazolamide): decrease bicarb at ciliary epithelium → decrease Na+ and H2O transport → decrease synthesis of aqueous humor
Uveoscleral outflow:
Prostaglandin agonists (e.g. latanoprost): increase outflow through the uveoscleral tract by increasing permeability
SE: Permanent discoloration of iris
Trabecular outflow:
M3 agonists (Carbachol, Pilocarpine): increase drainage of meshwork/canal with increase episcleral vasculature

# ORBITAL CELLULITIS

2 days of severe R eye pain + blurry vision + sinusitis taking Benadryl + pain with eye movement and 20/200 vision and difficulty moving EOM → orbital cellulitis
Dx: Clinical +/- ocular CT scan
Differentiate from preseptal cellulitis by involvement of EOM and pain with movement in orbital cellulitis (orbital cellulitis = preseptal cellulitis + other worsening sxs)
Path: Polymicrobial infection
Tx: Clindamycin + penicillin family
Big RF: Sinusitis

# OPTIC NEURITIS

Female with severe L eye pain, can barely see, worse after hot shower → optic neuritis due to multiple sclerosis
Diagnostic imaging: MRI
Tx: IV steroid (PO steroid increases risk of recurrence of optic neuritis — very HY!)
Most likely sequelae: Resolve over time

# CATARACTS

76 yo F difficulty seeing while driving at night and reading road signs → cataracts
Path: Opacification of the lens
Metabolic RF: Diabetes (aldose reductase converts glucose to sorbitol, lens lacks sorbitol dehydrogenase to convert it to fructose so sorbitol accumulates in lens)
Drug associated RF: Steroids
Infectious RF: Congenital syphilis, congenital CMV, congenital toxo
Newborn with a bilateral presentation → classic galactosemia (GALT deficiency) — galactitol accumulates in lens and opacifies it
Red reflex: dark, dull, white
Most common cause of death in newborns with galactosemia: E. coli sepsis (HY)

# CONJUNCTIVITIS

## Neonatal Conjunctivitis
MCC conjunctivitis in first 24 hours of life: chemical conjunctivitis from silver nitrate

5 day old newborn with "super" purulent conjunctivitis:
Bug: Gonorrhea (within 1st week of life — most dangerous for blindness in US)
Tx: IV cefotaxime (3rd gen cephalosporin) — ceftriaxone causes kernicterus and cholestasis in newborns
Prophylaxis: Topical macrolide (erythromycin) — does NOT ppx against chlamydia

12 day old newborn with "watery" discharge conjunctivitis and eyelid swelling:
Bug: Chlamydia (serovars A-C) — appears 1-2 weeks after birth — leading cause of preventable blindness worldwide
Tx: PO (oral!) erythromycin — use oral to eliminate eye infection AND possible nasopharyngeal infection (otherwise will develop PNA at 1-3 months)
Topical erythromycin does NOT ppx against chlamydia
Pneumonia presentation: Newborn with "staccato cough"
"Down the line" nonbilious vomiting after tx (pear shaped mass) → pyloric stenosis from erythromycin

## Most Likely Conjunctivitis
Glued eyes in AM (unilateral) + watery discharge + rhinorrhea → viral
Tx: Warm/cold compress
HY bug: Adenovirus ("pharyngoconjunctivitis") — also causes gastroenteritis and hemorrhagic cystitis
MCC conjunctivitis = viral

Glued eyes in AM (unilateral → bilateral) + purulent discharge + no rhinorrhea → bacterial
MCC bacterial conjunctivitis: S. aureus
Tx: Antibiotic eye drop (erythromycin except contact-wearers use fluoroquinolone drops to cover for possible pseudomonas)

Hx of asthma + bilateral itchy red eye → allergic conjunctivitis
Tx: Antihistamine eye drops (Olopatadine, Azelastine)

# ANTERIOR UVEITIS

Eye pain + worsened in light in 23 yo male with hx of chronic LBP and morning stiffness → ankylosing spondylitis → anterior uveitis
Pulmonary disease association: Sarcoid
Pediatric rheumatology association: Oligoarticular JRA
If given JRA pt and asked NBS → slit lamp
If caused by HSV → give acyclovir +/- topical steroid
If caused by autoimmune phenomenon → give topical steroid

# PRESBYOPIA

70 yo F with difficulty reading books — has to move books away from her eyes → presbyopia
Path: Lens loses elasticity with age so can't accommodate (just like skin loses elasticity)

# RETINAL VASCULAR OCCLUSIONS

## CRAO (Central Retinal Artery Occlusion)
Acute, painless, monocular vision loss
Amaurosis fugax: curtain over eye
Associated with: Temporal arteritis, carotid artery stenosis
Presents as transient loss of vision for several minutes → eventually persistent vision loss (= CRAO)
Dx: Ocular U/S + fundoscopic exam → thin retinal vessels, fundal pallor (retinal swelling)
Tx: Ocular massage and high flow oxygen en route to hospital; once at hospital give tPA

## CRVO (Central Retinal Venous Occlusion)
Rapid, painless loss of vision of varied severity
Dx: Fundoscopic exam → optic DISC swelling (vs retinal swelling in CRAO), thick retinal vessels, blood and thunder retina, cotton wool spots, hard exudates
Fluorescein angiography (not U/S) if suspect venous occlusion
Tx: VEGF-I, photocoagulation

## Cherry Red Spot DDx
Tay-Sachs (hexosaminidase A deficiency): losing milestones and impaired startle, no HSM
Niemann-Pick (sphingomyelinase deficiency): similar to Tay-Sachs but with hepatosplenomegaly

## Diabetic Retinopathy
Presents very similar to CRVO
Nonproliferative: dot and blot hemorrhages, hard exudates, retinal edema, microaneurysm
Preproliferative: cotton wool spots
Proliferative: neovascularization (buzzword), macular edema
Do fundoscopic exam — repeat every year; definitive dx with fluorescein angiography (r/o CRVO)
Tx: VEGF-Inhibitor, laser photocoagulation

# CORNEAL ABRASION

19 yo F with pain and a "foreign body sensation" in the eye + wears contact lenses → corneal abrasion
Dx: Fluorescein slit lamp exam (can't see with naked eye)
Tx: REMOVE contact, topical broad spec antibiotic with pseudomonas coverage (fluoroquinolone), topical NSAID drop for eye

# Ep. 37: RISK FACTORS

## Cardiovascular
#1 RF for AFib → Mitral stenosis
MC arrhythmia in hyperthyroidism → AFib
#1 RF for Mitral stenosis → Rheumatic fever
#1 RF for AAA development → Smoking
Screen AAA → men + 65-75 yo + smoker. If >5.5 cm → surgery. Or >0.5 cm/6 mos or 1 cm/yr growth
Greatest predisposing RF to rupture of AAA → diameter of aneurysm
Most important modifiable RF for CAD → Smoking
#1 RF for stroke and aortic dissection → HTN
#1 RF for ED → CVD

## Cancer Risk Factors
#1 RF for SCC → cumulative sun exposure
#1 RF for ovarian cancer → FHx of ovarian cancer
#1 RF for prostate, breast Ca → Age
#1 RF for pancreatic Ca → Smoking
MCC cancer a/w asbestos exposure → Bronchogenic carcinoma (mesothelioma has no relation to smoking)
#1 RF for papillary thyroid cancer → prior chest/neck radiation
#1 RF for bladder cancer → Smoking
#1 RF for endometrial cancer → unopposed estrogen → endometrial hyperplasia
#1 RF for cervical cancer → HPV
RF esophageal adenocarcinoma → Barrett's esophagus (caused by GERD)

## GI
Biggest RF in iron deficiency anemia pt with h/o H. Pylori → PUD
MCC acute pancreatitis in America → Gallstones
MCC chronic pancreatitis → Alcoholism

## Renal/Metabolic
MCCOD in pt with kidney transplant → CV disease
MCCOD in acromegaly → Heart failure
MCCOD in CKD → CV disease/arrhythmia followed by infection
MCCOD in ADPKD → CV disease (not SAH!)
MCCOD in SLE → Ischemic heart disease

## Pulmonary
Which pneumoconiosis increases risk of TB → Silicosis

## MSK/Misc
#1 RF for OSA → Obesity
#1 modifiable RF for knee OA → Obesity
#1 RF for suicide → Prior suicide attempt

## Obstetric Risk Factors
#1 RF for endometritis → C-section
#1 RF for preeclampsia → Previous hx of preeclampsia/nulliparity
#1 RF for uterine inversion → Prior inversion
#1 RF for chorioamnionitis → Prolonged rupture of membranes
#1 RF for placenta previa → Prior C-section
HY RF to preterm labor → Bacterial vaginosis
#1 RF for ectopic pregnancy → Hx of ectopic pregnancy and smoking (affects cilia)
#1 RF for cervical incompetence → Cervical conization procedure or LEEP
#1 RF for fetal macrosomia → Gestational diabetes or preexisting diabetes
#1 RF for shoulder dystocia → Fetal macrosomia
MCC of placental abruption → Trauma/cocaine
RF urinary incontinence → age and multiple vaginal deliveries (stress), diabetes (overflow), MS (urge)
RF for Asherman's syndrome → History of uterine curettage

## DM
Controlling blood glucose only decreases microvascular risks (retinopathy, neuro, nephropathy), NOT macrovascular (CAD, stroke)

## Cancer Screening
Breast CA: Every year from 40 yo. USPSTF → every 2 years start at 50 until age 75
Cervical CA: Every 3 years 21-30 regardless of sexual activity. >30 → HPV + pap every 5 years
Hyperlipidemia: Men every 5y >35 yo; Women every 5y >45 yo
Colon CA: Colonoscopy q 10 years from 50-75 yo
  UC pts: starting 8 years after diagnosis then every 1-2 years
  FHx colon cancer: 10 years prior to family member's age at diagnosis
  FAP pts: q 1 year starting at 10-15 yo
Lung CA: Low dose CT, men and women, 55-80 who have >30 pack-year smoking hx, currently smoke + less than 15 yr since quitting

## Prenatal Screening
All pregnant women 1st prenatal visit → screen syphilis, asymptomatic bacteriuria, HIV
Rh immunoglobulin screen → 28-32 wks + within 72 hours of delivery. Determine dose → Kleihauer-Betke test

## Prognostic Factors
Melanoma most likely determination of spread → Breslow depth
Most important prognostic factor in cancer → Stage of tumor (TNM staging)
#1 prevention of HCC → Hep B vaccination
MCCOD in cervical cancer → Renal failure (esp ureters)

# SYRINGOMYELIA

Student touching lighted bunsen burner unknowingly until hand pulled away. Loss of pain and temperature in UEs bilaterally + mild hand muscle weakness → syringomyelia
Pathophys: Obstruction in central canal of spinal cord → cystic expansion caudally → compression of anterior white commissure → knocks out spinothalamic tract
Tracts involved:
Spinothalamic → loss of pain + temperature bilaterally
If untreated, will expand and affect ventral horn → motor sxs (UE first because these tracts are most medial)
Dx: MRI

# TB MENINGITIS

Nuchal rigidity in recent immigrant with hemoptysis and high fevers. Marked "enhancement" at the base of the brain → TB meningitis
CSF findings: Lymphocytic predominance, protein HIGH, glucose LOW, high OP
Tx: RIPE regimen + Vit B6

## Meningitis vs Encephalitis
Encephalitis → more neuro deficits, may not have nuchal rigidity
Meningitis → nuchal rigidity

## Meningitis in Kids
Most common neurologic sequelae of meningitis in kids → hearing loss
Top 3 causes in neonates: GBS, E. coli, Listeria
Empiric tx for neonatal meningitis: Cefotaxime + vanc + ampicillin (ceftriaxone can cause intrahepatic cholestasis in neonates)

## CSF Summary
Bacterial: Neutrophilic predominance, protein HIGH, glucose LOW
Fungal: Lymphocytic predominance, protein HIGH, glucose LOW
Viral: Lymphocytic predominance, protein high, glucose low/normal

# GUILLAIN-BARRE SYNDROME

Patient with symmetric ascending paralysis after recent bloody diarrheal infection → GBS after Campylobacter infection
CSF finding: Albuminocytologic dissociation (high protein but normal WBCs — usually WBC & protein go up together)

# MULTIPLE SCLEROSIS

30 yo F with afferent pupillary defect and other neuro findings → MS
CSF finding: Oligoclonal bands
Dx: MRI

## CSF with RBCs
HSV encephalitis
Xanthochromia 2/2 SAH

# MENINGITIS TREATMENT SUMMARY

General cocktail: Ceftriaxone + vancomycin + steroids (CVS)
Elderly, immunocompromised, or super young (infants/neonates): Add ampicillin
CSF with normal glucose, slightly elevated protein, lymphocytic pleocytosis → viral meningitis
HSV meningitis/encephalitis imaging: Enhancement of temporal lobes

# BRAIN ABSCESS

25 yo M with T of 103, severe headache, and 2 day LE muscle weakness. Recently treated for otitis media → brain abscess
Triad: Fever + HA + neurologic deficit (NO nuchal rigidity)
Dx: MRI
Tx: Drain abscess + steroids (decrease swelling) + abx
Classic bug with MRI enhancement at "base of brain": TB

# RABIES

35 yo zoologist brought to ED unresponsive. Studies cave-dwelling animals. Past 2 weeks has resisted water/shower attempts → rabies
Prevention: Rabies immunoglobulin + vaccine (at two different sites)
Pathognomonic histologic finding: Negri bodies

# NF-2

Bilateral acoustic neuromas → NF-2
Inheritance: AD
Gene: Merlin
Chromosome: 22
Involved CN: CN8
Classic brainstem location: Cerebellopontine angle
Dx: MRI

# STURGE-WEBER SYNDROME

Port wine stain + glaucoma + seizures + ID + tram track CT calcifications → Sturge-Weber syndrome
Inheritance: Non-heritable! Sporadic!
Gene: GNAQ activating mutation

# RESTLESS LEGS SYNDROME

Woman with trouble sleeping due to weird sensations in legs → RLS
Associated dz: IDA
Tx: Pramipexole/ropinirole, Primidone

# CARPAL TUNNEL SYNDROME

Paresthesias over palmar aspect of hand + thenar atrophy → carpal tunnel syndrome
Associations: RA, Pregnancy, Hypothyroidism
PE maneuvers: Tinel's sign (tapping over carpal tunnel), Phalen sign (flexed wrists)
Tx: Wrist splint, NSAID, Inject steroids, Carpal tunnel release
Confirm dx before surgery: Nerve conduction study!

# VACCINE TYPES
Live attenuated: weakened bug, T-cell response, no boosters needed (MMR, Varicella, Intranasal influenza)
Killed/Inactivated: destroyed bug + antigenic parts, humoral response, needs boosters
Toxoid: toxin generates antibody response

# SPECIAL SCENARIOS
Newborn to HBsAg+ mom → HepB vaccine AND HepB Ig (separate arms)
Rotavirus: live attenuated given at 2,4,6 months (exception to <1y/o rule)
Most dangerous complication of rotavirus vaccine: intussusception (c/I: prior intussusception, Meckel's, IgA nephropathy/HSP)

# PEDIATRIC VACCINE SCHEDULE
Most pediatric vaccines: 3 doses at 2, 4, 6 months
HepB: birth, within 2 months, 6-18 months
DTaP: 5 doses (2, 4, 6 months + 15-18 months + 4-6 yo); then TDaP (adolescent) + TD booster
H. flu Type B: 2, 4, 12-15 months
PCV-13: 2, 4, 6, 12-15 months
Polio: 2, 4, 6 months + 4-6 yo
Influenza: every year (no intranasal for <1 yo)
MMR + Varicella: 12-15 months + 4-6 yo
HepA: within 2 yo + 6 months after 1st dose
Meningococcal: 11-12 yo + 16 yo
HPV: 9-26 yo

# PNEUMOCOCCAL VACCINES
PCV-13 always FIRST (conjugated → B + T cell response); "13 comes before 23"
PPSV-23 (polysaccharide → humoral only): for >65 yo or chronic disease (smoker, DM, cochlear implant, asplenic, immunodeficient, chronic CSF leak)

# WHO DOES NOT GET LIVE ATTENUATED
Pregnant women, <1 yo (except rotavirus), CD4 <200

# SPECIAL POPULATIONS
Pregnancy: TDaP at 27-36 weeks
Splenectomy/Sickle cell: PCV-13 + PPSV-23 + S. pneumo + H. flu + N. meningitidis (wait 2 months between PCV-13 and PPSV-23); also amoxicillin/penicillin until age 5
Egg allergy: avoid Yellow fever only (everything else including MMR is fine)
Zoster: recombinant at age 50 (2 doses, 6 months apart)

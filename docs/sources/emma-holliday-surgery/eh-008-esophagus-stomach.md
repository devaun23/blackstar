# Esophagus

- Bad breath & snacks in the AM → **Zenker's diverticulum**. False diverticulum (only mucosa). Tx w/ surgery
- Dysphagia to liquids & solids → **Achalasia**. Tx w/ CCB, nitrates, botox, or Heller myotomy. Assoc w/ Chagas dz and esophageal cancer
- Dysphagia worse w/ hot & cold liquids + chest pain like MI w/ NO regurg → **Diffuse esophageal spasm**. Tx w/ CCB or nitrates
- Epigastric pain worse after eating or laying down, cough, wheeze, hoarse → **GERD**
  - Most sensitive test: 24-hr pH monitoring. Do endoscopy if "danger signs" present
  - Tx: behavioral mod 1st, then antacids, H2 block, PPI
  - Indications for surgery: bleeding, stricture, Barrett's, incompetent LES, max dose PPI w/ still sxs, or no want meds

## Esophageal Rupture

- Hematemesis after vomiting, w/ subQ emphysema. Can see pleural effusion w/ ↑amylase → **Boerhaave's**
  - Next best test: CXR, gastrografin esophagram. NO endoscopy
  - Tx: surgical repair if full thickness

## Gastric Varices

- Gross hematemesis unprovoked in a cirrhotic w/ portal HTN
- If in hypovolemic shock → ABCs, NG lavage, medical tx w/ octreotide or SS. Balloon tamponade only to stabilize for transport
- Tx of choice: Endoscopic sclerotherapy or banding
- *Don't prophylactically band asymptomatic varices. Give BB.*

## Esophageal Carcinoma

- Progressive dysphagia/weight loss
- SCC in smoker/drinkers in the middle 1/3
- Adeno in ppl with long standing GERD in the distal 1/3
- Best 1st test: barium swallow → endoscopy w/ bx → staging CT

# Stomach

## Hiatal Hernia

- Type 1 = Sliding. GE jxn herniates into thorax. Worse for GERD. Tx symptoms
- Type 2 = Paraesophageal. Abd pain, obstruction, strangulation → needs surgery

## Gastric Ulcers

- MEG pain worse w/ eating. H.pylori, NSAIDs, steroids
- Work up: Double-contrast barium swallow (punched out lesion w/ regular margins)
- Surgery if: EGD w/ bx shows lesion persists after 12wks of treatment

## Gastric Cancer

- Adeno most common. Esp in Japan
- **Krukenberg**: Gastric CA → ovaries
- **Virchow's node**: L supraclavicular fossa
- **Blummer's Shelf**: Mets felt on DRE
- **Sister Mary Joseph**: Umbilical node
- Lymphoma: HIV, MALT-lymphoma from H. pylori

## Randoms

- Menetrier's = protein losing enteropathy, enlarged rugae
- Gastric Varices = splenic vein thrombosis
- Dieulafoy's = massive hematemesis → mucosal artery erodes into stomach

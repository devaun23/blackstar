# Gallbladder

## Acute Cholecystitis

- RUQ pain → back, n/v, fever, worse s/p fatty foods
- Best 1st test: U/S
- Tx: Cholecystectomy. Perc cholecystostomy if unstable

## Choledocholithiasis

- RUQ pain, high bili and alk-phos
- Dx: U/S will show CBD stone
- Tx: Chole +/- ERCP to remove stone

## Ascending Cholangitis

- RUQ pain, fever, jaundice, ↓BP, AMS
- Tx: fluids & broad spec abx. ERCP and stone removal

## Choledochal Cysts

- Type 1: Fusiform dilation of CBD → Tx w/ excision
- Type 5: Caroli's Dz. Cysts in intrahepatic ducts → needs liver transplant

## Cholangiocarcinoma

- Rare. RF: Primary sclerosing cholangitis (UC), liver flukes and thorotrast exposure
- Tx w/ surgery +/- radiation

# Liver

## Hepatitis

- AST = 2x ALT → Alcoholic hepatitis (reversible)
- AST > ALT high (1000s) → Viral hepatitis
- AST & ALT high s/p hemorrhage, surg, or sepsis → Shock liver

## Cirrhosis and Portal HTN

- Tx: SS and VP vasoconstrict to decrease portal pressure, beta-blockers also decrease portal pressure
- Don't need to treat esophageal varices prophylactically, but band/burn them once they bleed once
- TIPS relieves portal HTN but worsens hepatic encephalopathy
  - Treat with: Lactulose (helps rid body of ammonia)

## Hepatocellular Carcinoma

- RF: chronic hepB carrier > hepC. Cirrhosis for any reason, plus aflatoxin or carbon tetrachloride
- Dx w/ high AFP (in 70%), CT/MRI
- Tx: surgically remove solitary mass, rads or cryoablation for palliation of multiple

## Hepatic Adenoma

- *Women on OCP → palpable abd mass or spontaneous rupture → hemorrhagic shock*
- Dx: U/S or MRI
- Tx: D/c OCPs. Resect if large or pregnancy is desired

## Focal Nodular Hyperplasia

- 2nd MC benign liver tumor. W>M but less likely to rupture
- No tx needed

## Bacterial Abscess

- Most common bugs: E. coli, Bacteroides, Enterococcus
- RUQ pain, profuse sweating and rigors, palpable liver
- Tx: Surgical drainage and IV abx

## Amebic Abscess

- Entamoeba histolytica
- Tx: Metronidazole. DON'T drain it

## Hydatid Cyst

- Patient from Mexico w/ RUQ and large liver cysts on U/S → Echinococcus
- Mode of transmission: Parasite from dog feces
- Lab findings: eosinophilia, +Casoni skin test
- Tx: albendazole and surgery to remove ENTIRE cyst. Rupture → anaphylaxis

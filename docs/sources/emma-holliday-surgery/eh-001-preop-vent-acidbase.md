# Pre-Op Evaluation

## Contraindications to Surgery

- Absolute?
- Poor nutrition? albumin <3, transferrin <200, weight loss <20%
- Severe liver failure? bili >2, PT >16, ammonia >150 or encephalopathy
- Diabetic Coma, DKA
- Smoker? stop smoking 8wks prior to surgery
  - If a CO2 retainer, go easy on the O2 in the post-op period. Can suppress respiratory drive.

## Goldman's Index

Tells you who is at greatest risk for surgery:
1. #1 = CHF — Check EF. If <35%, no surg.
2. #2 = MI w/in 6mo
3. #3 = Arrhythmia — Check EKG → stress test → cardiac cath → revasc
4. #4 = Old (age >70)
5. #5 = Surgery is emergent
6. #6 = AS, poor medical condition, surg in chest/abd
   - Listen for murmur of AS: Late systolic, crescendo-decrescendo murmur that radiates to carotids. ↑ with squatting, ↓ with decr preload

## Meds to Stop

- Aspirin, NSAIDs, vit E (2wks)
- Warfarin (5 days) — drop INR to <1.5 (can use vit K)
- Take 1/2 the morning dose of insulin, if diabetic

## CKD on Dialysis

- Dialyze 24 hours pre-op
- Why check BUN and Creatinine? If BUN >100: increased risk of post-op bleeding 2/2 uremic platelet dysfunction
- Coag panel: Normal platelets but prolonged bleeding time

# Vent Settings

- **Assist-control**: set TV and rate but if pt takes a breath, vent gives the volume
- **Pressure support**: pt rules rate but a boost of pressure is given (8-20). *Important for weaning.*
- **CPAP**: pt must breathe on own but + pressure given all the time
- **PEEP**: pressure given at end of cycle to keep alveoli open (5-20). *Used in ARDS or CHF*

## Vent Management

- Best test to evaluate management? **ABG**
- If PaO2 is low? increase FiO2
- If PaO2 is high? decrease FiO2
- If PaCO2 is high (pH is low)? Incr rate or TV
- If PaCO2 is low (pH is high)? Decr rate or TV
- Which is more efficient? **TV is more efficient to change.**
- *Remember minute ventilation equation & dead space*

# Acid Base Disorders

## Acidotic (pH <7.4)

Check HCO3 and pCO2:
- If HCO3 is high and pCO2 is high → **Respiratory Acidosis**
- If HCO3 is low and pCO2 is low → **Metabolic Acidosis**
  - Check anion gap (Na – [Cl + HCO3]), normal = 8-12
  - Gap acidosis = **MUDPILES**
  - Non-gap acidosis = diarrhea, diuretic, RTAs (I, II, IV)

## Alkalotic (pH >7.4)

Check HCO3 and pCO2:
- If HCO3 is low and pCO2 is low → **Respiratory Alkalosis**
- If HCO3 is high and pCO2 is high → **Metabolic Alkalosis**
  - Check urine [Cl]
  - If [Cl] <20: Vomiting/NG, antacids, diuretics
  - If [Cl] >20: Conn's, Bartter's, Gitelman's

# Sodium Abnormalities

## Hyponatremia (↓Na = Gain of water)

- Check osm, then check volume status
- ↑volume ↓Na: CHF, nephrotic, cirrhotic
- ↓volume ↓Na: diuretics or vomiting + free water
- Nl volume ↓Na: SIADH, Addisons, hypothyroidism
- Treatment: Fluid restriction & diuretics
- If hypovolemic: Normal Saline
- When to use 3% saline? Symptomatic (Seizures), <110
- Worry about: Central Pontine Myelinolysis

## Hypernatremia (↑Na = Loss of water)

- Treatment: Replace w/ D5W or hypotonic fluid
- Worry about: cerebral edema

# Other Electrolyte Abnormalities

- **↓Ca**: Numbness, Chvostek or Trousseau, prolonged QT interval
- **↑Ca**: Bones, stones, groans, psycho. Shortened QT interval
- **↓K**: Paralysis, ileus, ST depression, U waves. Tx: give K (kidneys!), max 40mEq/hr
- **↑K**: Peaked T waves, prolonged PR and QRS, sine waves. Tx: Ca-gluconate → insulin + glucose → kayexalate, albuterol, sodium bicarb. Last resort = dialysis

# Fluid and Nutrition

## Maintenance IVFs

- Up to 10kgs: 100mL/kg/day
- Next 10kgs: 50mL/kg/day
- All above 20: 20mL/kg/day
- Solution: D5 1/2NS + 20KCl (if peeing)

## Enteral vs TPN

- Enteral Feeds are best — keep gut mucosa intact and prevent bacterial translocation
- TPN indicated if gut can't absorb nutrients 2/2 physical or functional loss
  - Risks: *acalculous cholecystitis*, hyperglycemia, liver dysfunction, *zinc deficiency*, other lyte problems

# Burns

- Look for singed nose hairs, wheezing, soot in mouth/nose → Low threshold for intubation
- Patient w/ confusion, HA, cherry red skin? Check carboxyHb (pulse ox = worthless). Tx: 100% O2 (hyperbaric if CO-Hb is ↑↑↑)
- Circumferential burns? Consider escharotomy

## Burn Workup and Tx

- Rule of 9s
- **Parkland formula**: Kg x % BSA x 3-4 (Adults) | Kg x % BSA x 2-4 (Kiddos)
  - Give 1/2 over the 1st 8hrs and the rest over next 16hrs
- Ringers lactate or normal saline
- NO PO or IV abx. Give topical:
  - **Silver Sulfadiazine**: Doesn't penetrate eschar and can cause leukopenia
  - **Mafenide**: Penetrates eschar but hurts like hell
  - **Silver Nitrate**: Doesn't penetrate eschar and causes hypoK and hypoNa

## Other Burn Stuff

- Chemical burn → Irrigate >30min prior to ER
- Electrical Burn → best 1st step: **EKG!**
  - If abnormal → 48 hours of telemetry (also if LOC)
  - If urine dipstick + for blood but microscopic exam negative for RBCs → **Myoglobinuria → ATN**
  - Then check **K+!** (When cells break)
- If affected extremity is extremely tender, numb, white, cold with barely dopplerable pulses → **Compartment syndrome!!**
  - Criteria: 5 Ps or compartment pressure >30mmHg
  - Treatment: May require **fasciotomy** (at bedside!)

# Clotting & Bleeding

## Clotting

- In old people? Think cancer
- Edema, HTN, & foamy pee? Nephrotic syndrome
- Post op, ↓plts, clots → **HIT!** (If heparin w/in 5-14 days and schistocytes)
  - Treat w/ Lepirudin or argatroban
- In young person w/ +FH → Factor V Leiden
- What's special about ATIII def? Heparin won't work
- Young woman w/ mult. SABs → Lupus Anticoagulant

## Bleeding

- Isolated decr in plts → **ITP**
- Normal plts but incr bleeding time & PTT → **vWD**
- Low plts, Incr PT, PTT, BT, low fibrinogen, high D-dimer → **DIC!!**
  - Caused by gram – sepsis, carcinomatosis, OB stuff

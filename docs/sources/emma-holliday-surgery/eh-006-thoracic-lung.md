# Thoracic

## Pleural Effusions

- See fluid >1cm on lat decubitus → thoracentesis!
- Transudative → likely CHF, nephrotic, cirrhotic
- Exudative → likely parapneumonic, cancer, etc.
- If bloody → Malignant or Pulmonary Embolus
- If low pleural glucose → Rheumatoid Arthritis
- If high lymphocytes → Tuberculosis
- If complicated (+ gram or cx, pH <7.2, glc <60) → Insert chest tube for drainage

### Light's Criteria — transudative if:

- LDH <200
- LDH eff/serum <0.6
- Protein eff/serum <0.5

## Spontaneous Pneumothorax

- Subpleural bleb rupture → lung collapse
- Suspect in tall, thin young men w/ sudden dyspnea (or asthma or COPD-emphysema)
- Dx w/ CXR, Tx w/ chest tube placement
- Indications for surgery: ipsi or contra recurrence, bilateral, incomplete lung expansion, pilot, scuba, remote area → VATS, pleurodesis (bleo, iodine or talc)

## Lung Abscess

- Usually 2/2 aspiration (drunk, elderly, enteral feeds)
- Most often in posterior upper or superior lower lobes
- Tx initially w/ abx: IV PCN or clinda
- Indications for surgery: abx fail, abscess >6cm, or if empyema present

## Solitary Lung Nodule Workup

- 1st step: Find an old CXR to compare!
- **Benign**: Popcorn calcification (hamartoma, MC), Concentric calcification (old granuloma), Pt <40, <3cm, well circumscribed → CXR or CT q2mo
- **Malignant**: Risk factors (smoker, old), >3cm, eccentric calcification → Remove the nodule (bronc if central, open lung bx if peripheral)

## Lung Cancer

- MC in non-smokers → **Adenocarcinoma**. Occurs in scars of old pneumonia. Peripheral. Mets to liver, bone, brain, adrenals. Effusion: exudative w/ high hyaluronidase
- Kidney stones, constipation, malaise, low PTH, central mass → **Squamous cell**. Paraneoplastic: PTH-rP secretion. Low PO4, High Ca
- Shoulder pain, ptosis, constricted pupil, facial edema → **Small cell** (Superior Sulcus Syndrome)
- Ptosis better after 1 min of upward gaze → **Lambert Eaton Syndrome** from small cell (Ab to pre-syn Ca channels)
- Old smoker, Na = 125, moist mucus membranes, no JVD → **SIADH from small cell**. Euvolemic hyponatremia. Fluid restrict +/- 3% saline in <112
- Peripheral cavitation, distant mets on CT → **Large Cell Carcinoma**

## ARDS

- Pathophys: inflammation → impaired gas exchange, mediator release, hypoxemia
- Causes: Sepsis, gastric aspiration, trauma, low perfusion, pancreatitis
- Diagnosis:
  1. PaO2/FiO2 <200 (<300 means acute lung injury)
  2. Bilateral alveolar infiltrates on CXR
  3. PCWP <18 (means pulmonary edema is non-cardiogenic)
- Treatment: Mechanical ventilation w/ PEEP

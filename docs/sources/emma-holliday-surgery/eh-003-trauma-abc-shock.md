# Trauma Drama

## Airway

- Trauma patient comes in unconscious → Intubate!
- GCS <8 → Intubate!
- Guy stung by a bee, developing stridor and tripod posturing → Intubate!
- Guy stabbed in the neck, GCS = 15, expanding mass in lateral neck → Intubate!
- Guy stabbed in the neck, crackly sounds w/ palpating anterior neck tissues → fiberoptic bronchoscope
- Huge facial trauma, blood obscures oral and nasal airway, GCS of 7 → **cricothyroidotomy**

## Breathing

- After intubation, next best step → Check bilateral breath sounds
- If decr on the left → Intubated the right mainstem bronchus → Pull back ET tube
- Next step → Check pulse ox, keep it >90%

## Chest Trauma

- Inward mvmt of ribcage upon inspiration → **Flail chest** (>3 consec rib fractures). Tx: O2 and pain control
- Confusion, petechial rash in chest/axilla/neck, acute SOB → **Fat embolism**. Suspect after long bone fx (esp femur)
- Patient dies suddenly after central line removal → **Air embolism**. Also suspect with lung trauma, vent use, heart vessel surgery

## Cardiovascular — Shock

- Hypotensive, tachycardic, flat neck veins, normal CVP → **Hypovolemic/Hemorrhagic**
  - Next: 2 large bore periph IV — 2L NS or LR over 20min followed by blood
- Muffled heart sounds, JVD, electrical alternans, pulsus paradoxus → **Pericardial Tamponade**
  - Confirmatory: FAST scan
  - Tx: Needle decompression, pericardial window or median sternotomy
- Decr BS on one side, tracheal deviation AWAY from collapsed lung → **Tension Pneumothorax**
  - Next: Needle decompression, followed by chest tube. **DON'T do a CXR!!!**

## Shock Types Table

| Type | Causes | Physical Exam | Swan-Ganz | Treatment |
|------|--------|---------------|-----------|-----------|
| Hypovolemic | Hemorrhage, bowel obstruction, vomiting/diarrhea, polyuria, burn | Hypotensive, tachycardic, diaphoretic, cool/clammy | RAP/PCWP↓ SVR↑ CO↓ | Crystalloid resuscitation |
| Vasogenic | Sepsis (LPS), anaphylaxis (histamine) | AMS, hypotension, warm/dry (early), late looks hypovolemic | RAP/PCWP↓ SVR↓ CO↑ (EF↓) | Fluids + tx offending organism |
| Neurogenic | Spinal cord injury, spinal anesthesia, adrenal insufficiency | Hypotensive, bradycardic, warm/dry, absent reflexes. Adrenal insuf: hypoNa, hyperK | RAP/PCWP↓ SVR↓ CO↑ | Adrenal insuf: dexamethasone taper |
| Cardio-compressive | Cardiac tamponade | Hypotensive, tachycardic, JVD, ↓heart sounds, pulsus paradoxus | U/S: pericardial fluid | Pericardiocentesis |
| Cardiogenic | Arrhythmias, acute heart failure | SOB, clammy, rales bilat, S3, pleural effusion, ascites, periph edema | RAP/PCWP↑ SVR↑ CO↓ | Diuretics → rate control → vasopressor |
